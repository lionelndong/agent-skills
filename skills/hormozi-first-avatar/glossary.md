# Glossary — Your First Avatar

**Avatar** — The specific ideal customer you choose to serve (and, by exclusion, who you don't); a subset of choosing your market. (Ch 1)

**Buying Process (survey bucket)** — The questions that uncover *how* your best customers bought: biggest reason, trigger event, content/testimonials consumed, time from first-hear to buy, referral source. (Ch 1)

**Force multiplier** — Getting the avatar right amplifies the returns of everything else you do in the business. (Ch 2)

**Leading indicators (of high-value customers)** — The shared characteristics of your top 20% that predict a valuable customer before they buy. (Ch 2)

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — Total value of a customer over their lifetime; competitor avg ≈ $6–8K vs. Acquisition.com ≈ $45K+ in the example. (Ch 2)

**Pareto on steroids (80/20)** — 20% of customers bring ~80% of revenue; replacing the low-value 80% with high spenders can grow a business ~5x. (Ch 1)

**Qualifiers** — The fewest common characteristics your best customers all share (usually 3–5); the definition of your avatar. (Ch 1)

**Re-engineer the sales process** — Reverse-engineer the buying journey that produced your best customers, then reproduce it on purpose for every lead. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Top 20%** — The customers you like most, who spent the most, and stayed the longest; the group you study and clone. (Ch 1)

**Value Acceleration Method (VAM)** — Acquisition.com's method (adapted from Vista) of scoring customers by value/longevity, cutting low-value channels, and doubling down on high-value ones. (Ch 1)

**Vista method** — The PE approach Hormozi adopted: score a company's customers by who stays longest/pays most, then reallocate acquisition toward the best ones. (Ch 1)
