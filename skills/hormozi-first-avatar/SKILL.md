## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

**The avatar is a subset of choosing your market** — getting nuanced about exactly who you serve and who you don't. The goal: **more high-profit customers, fewer low-profit ones. Rinse. Repeat.**

- **Vista method → Value Acceleration Method (VAM)**: score customers by **who stays longest and pays most**; cut channels feeding low-value customers; double down on channels feeding the best. **80/20 on steroids** — replace the low-value 80% with high spenders → ~5x growth.
- **The Four Steps**: (1) **Survey** customers (Demographics · Business Stats before/current · Aspirations · Buying Process); (2) **Find your biggest spenders** — liked-most / spent-most / stayed-longest → keep the **top 20%**, ignore the rest; (3) **See what they have in common** — the fewest shared **qualifiers (usually 3–5)**; (4) **Execute** — speak your new avatar in all advertising (repel the bad, attract the good; *stop selling non-fits*) **and** re-engineer the sales process around how the best customers actually bought.
- **Re-engineer the buyer journey**: find the buying-process commonality of the top 20% (Gym Launch: **78% consumed ≥2 long-form content pieces before buying**) and force every lead through it (genuine value-in-advance).
- **Quality > Quantity**: removing qualification steps raises volume but lowers money. *"I'd rather pay $5,000 to acquire $45,000 than $1,000 to acquire $5,000."* Merge marketing + sales into one **acquisition department**. (The 70x-less-profit competitor "accepted anyone with a pulse and a credit card.")

**Bottom line**: growing a business = selling more customers OR making them worth more — picking the right avatar does **both**, and acts as a force multiplier on everything else.
