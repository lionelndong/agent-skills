# Patterns — Your First Avatar

## Pattern: Define Your First Avatar (the 4 steps)
**When to use**: You serve "anyone who'll pay," and want higher retention, margin, and lead quality.
**How**:
1. **Survey** customers (Demographics, Business Stats before/current, Aspirations, Buying Process).
2. **Find your biggest spenders** — sort by liked-most / spent-most / stayed-longest; keep the **top 20%**, ignore the rest.
3. **Find commonalities** — the fewest qualifiers they all share (**3–5**).
4. **Execute** — (a) speak directly to that avatar in all advertising (repel the bad, attract the good; stop selling non-fits); (b) re-engineer the sales process around how the best customers actually bought.
**Trade-offs**: Short-term revenue may dip during the change; long-term retention and profit rise. No competitor does it — durable advantage.

## Pattern: Reverse-Engineer the Ideal Buyer Journey
**When to use**: To lift conversion and customer value by reproducing what made your best customers buy.
**How**: Find the buying-process commonality of your top 20% (e.g., "78% consumed ≥2 long-form content pieces first"), then inject that step into *every* lead's journey; arm sales with a "greatest hits" content list to hand-select per prospect (genuine value-in-advance, never disguised pitches).
**Trade-offs**: Adds qualification steps that lower raw volume but raise money-per-lead.

## Pattern: Merge Marketing & Sales Into One Acquisition Department
**When to use**: When marketing and sales blame each other (leads vs. closing) and qualification steps keep getting cut for volume.
**How**: Combine them into a single acquisition department focused on closing lots of *valuable* deals; keep the optimal number of qualification steps for long-haul ROAS.
**Trade-offs**: Less volume, far more profit — "I'd rather pay $5,000 to acquire $45,000 than $1,000 to acquire $5,000."

## Pattern: Score & Reallocate (the Vista/VAM move)
**When to use**: Deciding where to spend acquisition effort across channels.
**How**: Score customers by who stays longest and pays most; cut the channels feeding low-value customers; double down on the channels feeding the best ones. Rinse and repeat.
**Trade-offs**: Requires honest data and the discipline to turn away poor-fit revenue.
