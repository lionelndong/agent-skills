# Chapter 1: Finding the Right Customers — The Avatar Method

## Core Idea
Choosing the perfect avatar is **a subset of picking your market** — getting nuanced about exactly *who* you serve and, more importantly, *who you do not*. The method: find the customers worth the most, then get more of them and fewer of everyone else. *"More of the high profit customers. Fewer low profit customers. Rinse. Repeat."*

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Vista method (the origin)** — how a renowned software PE fund grows companies: analyze current customers → score them by **who stays the longest and pays the most** → cut the channels that bring low-value customers → double down on the channels that bring the best ones. Hormozi adopted it as a pillar of Acquisition.com's **Value Acceleration Method (VAM)**.
- **Pareto on steroids (80/20)** — "Twenty percent of customers bring in eighty percent of revenue. If you replace the eighty percent with those high spenders, you grow the business 5x."

## The Four Steps to Install the Process
1. **Survey your customers** — a form (or live at an event/call for higher engagement; require proof of completion for a benefit). Ask every relevant detail:
   - **Demographics**: age, gender, political affiliation, geographic + digital location, single/divorced, partnered vs. solopreneur.
   - **Business Stats (Before & Current)**: revenue, profit, # employees, churn, pricing, products, customer LTV, # customers, niche, time in business.
   - **Aspirations**: their goal upon purchasing; the problem they were trying to solve.
   - **Buying Process**: the single biggest reason they bought; any trigger event; specific content/testimonial consumed; how many pieces; when they first heard of you vs. bought; where they first saw you; who referred them.
2. **Find your biggest spenders** — sort by the customers you **like the most, who spent the most, and stayed the longest**. Focus on the **top 20%**. Ignore the rest.
3. **See what they have in common** — read every answer and think. Goal: the **fewest qualifiers they all share** (usually **three to five**). "Your competitors won't do it — easy advantage."
4. **Execute** —
   - **(a) Speak your new avatar**: be upfront about customer requirements; make all advertising speak directly to them; **repel the bad, attract the good**; *stop selling anyone who doesn't meet your ideal-customer requirements*; increase effort on the channels these people come through.
   - **(b) Re-engineer the sales process**: reverse-engineer the buying process your best customers went through, then make it happen on purpose.

## Mental Models
- *Picking the avatar is a strategic decision about exclusion* — who you say "no" to matters as much as who you serve.
- *Quality of prospect determines quality of everything downstream* — retention, margin, pricing power, repeat business.

## Anti-patterns
- Accepting "anyone with a pulse and a credit card" → high churn, high CAC, low retention, generic advice.

## Key Takeaways
1. Score customers by who stays longest and pays most; get more of them, fewer of the rest.
2. Install it in four steps: Survey → Find biggest spenders (top 20%) → Find 3–5 common qualifiers → Execute (speak to them + re-engineer the sale).
3. No one does this — getting it right is a force multiplier on everything else.
