# Cheatsheet — Your First Avatar

## The core move
Score customers by **who stays longest and pays most** → get more of them, fewer of everyone else. "Rinse. Repeat." (Vista method → Acquisition.com's **VAM**.) **80/20 on steroids**: replace the low-value 80% with high spenders → ~**5x** growth.

## The 4 steps
1. **Survey** — Demographics · Business Stats (before/current) · Aspirations · Buying Process.
2. **Find biggest spenders** — liked-most / spent-most / stayed-longest → **top 20%**, ignore the rest.
3. **Find commonalities** — fewest shared **qualifiers (3–5)** = your avatar.
4. **Execute** — (a) **speak to the avatar** in all ads (repel bad, attract good; stop selling non-fits); (b) **re-engineer the sale** around how the best bought.

## Survey question buckets
| Bucket | Examples |
|---|---|
| Demographics | age, gender, location (geo + digital), marital, solo vs. partnered |
| Business stats | revenue, profit, employees, churn, pricing, LTV, # customers, niche, tenure |
| Aspirations | goal at purchase; problem they were solving |
| Buying process | biggest reason; trigger event; content/testimonials consumed; time-to-buy; referral source |

## Decision rules
- **Quality > Quantity** — removing qualification steps raises volume but **lowers money**. Keep the optimal steps.
- **The value trade** — *"I'd rather pay $5,000 to acquire $45,000 than $1,000 to acquire $5,000."*
- **Merge marketing + sales** into one acquisition department so neither blames the other.
- **Buying-journey clone** — find the buying-process commonality of the top 20% (e.g., consumed ≥2 long-form pieces) and force every lead through it.

## Tells
- If you accept "anyone with a pulse and a credit card" → high churn, high CAC, low retention, generic advice (the 70x-less-profit competitor).
- Same market + better segmentation = many multiples more profit. Competitor LTV ~$6–8K vs. ~$45K+.

## The one-line summary
Grow by **selling more customers OR making them worth more** — picking the right avatar does **both**.
