# Patterns — $100M GOATed Ads

## Pattern: Run the Ad Assembly Line (the master loop)
**When to use**: You want to scale paid ads and keep finding winners.
**How**: Dedicate a recurring ad day (Hormozi uses **every Friday**). Find your best ads → chop into **Hook / Meat / CTA** → produce in bulk: **50 hooks × 3–5 meat × 1–3 CTAs = 150–750 ads**. Make the parts separately, then combine.
**Trade-offs**: Volume is also a defense — at hundreds of ads/week, competitors can't identify your top performers.

## Pattern: Spend Prep 80/20/0
**When to use**: Allocating effort across an ad.
**How**: **80% Hooks, 20% Meat, ~0% CTAs.** "Ads are made in research, not in recording." If they don't pass the hook, nothing else matters.
**Trade-offs**: Under-investing in hooks caps the whole funnel; over-polishing meat/CTAs wastes time.

## Pattern: Write Winner Hooks (immediate results)
**When to use**: Weekly, bread-and-butter ad production.
**How**: Write hooks off your **previous winners**. Build a "stable" of reusable winning hooks — one good hook gives huge runway.
**Trade-offs**: Skip this on your very first ad run (no winners yet) — start with the other sources.

## Pattern: Mine the 5 Hook Sources
**When to use**: Sourcing high-converting hooks.
**How**: In order — (1) your previous ads, (2) your free content (chop a winning hook off a post/tweet/email), (3) others' ads (keep a saved album), (4) others' free content (high-view posts in your space), (5) platform ad libraries **last**. Winning hooks in one industry often work in another.
**Trade-offs**: Ad libraries show everything but hide what actually performs — use for ideas, not as a winner guarantee.

## Pattern: Write Expansion Hooks by Awareness
**When to use**: You've **"capped"** current ads (can't spend more profitably) and need a broader audience.
**How**: Map hook type to the audience's level of awareness — **Most Aware → offer**, **Product-Aware → proof**, **Solution-Aware → promise**, **Problem-Aware → pain**, **Completely Unaware → curiosity**. Spread your 50 hooks across the buckets instead of piling 90% in "Most Aware."
**Trade-offs**: Only do new when you must; "it's about doing more/better far more than doing something new." Offer-driven hooks to cold national audiences (as an unknown brand) burn money.

## Pattern: Go Wider With Memes
**When to use**: You want the largest share of an audience.
**How**: Use memes / meme-like content (or a niche audience's culture-specific memes). "When in doubt, go a little broader" — you still catch the warm audience and pick up colder ones.
**Trade-offs**: Test them; not every meme converts to your offer.

## Pattern: Build the Meat From the 5 Formats
**When to use**: Creating the body of the ad (3–5 per session).
**How**: Pick from **Demonstration, Testimonial, Education, Story, Faceless** — mix and match. Keep the creative aligned to the hook, and the hook aligned to awareness. Refer back to the list whenever you're stuck.
**Trade-offs**: Meat is only 20% of effort and rotates slowly (fewer people see it) — don't over-produce it.

## Pattern: Write a Clear CTA (Show AND Tell)
**When to use**: Closing every ad.
**How**: **Clear > Clever.** Spell out the next step ("Click this button," "Reply YES," "Scan this QR code"). Then **demonstrate** it — show the click, the form, the submit — for congruence. Cover the **5 elements**: what / how / when / what they get / what happens next.
**Trade-offs**: A sound CTA never breaks a campaign; no CTA does. Make 1–3; don't over-test.

## Pattern: Test the CTA in Isolation
**When to use**: Finding the best CTA.
**How**: Run **three identical hook + meat combos to the same audience, changing only the CTA.** Once one follows the fundamentals, stick with it.
**Trade-offs**: Lowest-leverage chunk — spend the least time here.

## Pattern: Layer Urgency / Scarcity / Guarantees / Bonuses
**When to use**: Squeezing more action out of any CTA.
**How**: Add urgency, scarcity, guarantees, and bonuses on top of a clear CTA. They apply "here, and everywhere else you tell your audience to do something."
**Trade-offs**: Enhancers, not substitutes — they sit on top of a clear, demonstrated next step.

## Pattern: Scale by Variation → Double-Down
**When to use**: Growing spend over time.
**How**: Early on, make many variations to hit different segments (you can't predict winners). When a few wildly outperform, **double down** and **reuse the winning hooks** to manufacture more winners.
**Trade-offs**: Don't get bored of a winner — new customers see it for the first time.

## Pattern: Treat the Quality Wall as the Real Wall
**When to use**: CAC spikes as you scale spend.
**How**: Refresh and multiply creative before concluding the market is saturated. "You've hit a wall with your ad quality, not with the market." Better ads convert larger audiences.
**Trade-offs**: This is the on-ramp to **Kaleidoscope Ads** — but find winners with this process first; "you can't do those without this."
