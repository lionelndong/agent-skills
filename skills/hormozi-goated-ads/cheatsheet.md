# Cheatsheet — $100M GOATed Ads

## The one idea
You hit a wall on **ad quality, not the market.** Scale past it by making **more, better ads** — and the way to make better ads is to make *way more.* Better ads convert a larger slice of a larger audience.

## The Ad Equation (assemble, don't create)
**50 hooks × 3–5 meat × 1–3 CTAs = 150 to 750 ads/week.** Make the parts separately, then combine. Dedicate a recurring ad day.

## The Prep Split
| Chunk | Effort | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Hooks** | **80%** | If they don't pass the hook, nothing else matters |
| **Meat** | **20%** | Seen by fewer people; rotated less; 3–5/session |
| **CTAs** | **~0%** | Cheap; never breaks a campaign; 1–3/session |

## Hormozi's own 5-step process (from the playbook's cheat sheet)
1. **Pick the level of awareness** you're targeting (Unaware → Most Aware).
2. **Write 50 hooks** — split across awareness buckets, or push broader than your current hooks. Find inspiration: your best ads → top ads in *other* industries (gold) → your top short content → others' short content → your saved-ads album.
3. **Write 3–5 "meats"** — pick from the 5 formats below.
4. **Write 1–3 CTAs** — show AND tell what to do next.
5. **Try to find a new winner** — repeat steps 1–4.

## The 5 Levels of Awareness → Hook Type (Eugene Schwartz)
| Level (warm→cold) | Hook is… | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Most Aware** | offer | "50% off our best-selling XYZ for the rest of the month." |
| **Product-Aware** | proof | "Discover why 10,000 people chose XYZ last month" |
| **Solution-Aware** | promise | "The fastest way to [result] - introducing XYZ" |
| **Problem-Aware** | pain | "Tired of [problem]? There's a better way" |
| **Completely Unaware** | curiosity | "The hidden danger… that's costing you [money/time/health]" |
*Larger audience = ads must be more varied/exceptional. Don't pile 90% in "Most Aware." When in doubt, go broader (memes pull the widest audience).*

## The 5 Hook Sources (in order)
1. Your **previous ads** *(skip on first run)* → 2. Your **free content** (chop a winning hook) → 3. **Others' ads** (saved album) → 4. **Others' free content** (high-view posts) → 5. **Platform ad libraries** (last — ideas, not proven winners).
*Winning hooks in one industry often work in others.*

## The 5 Ad Formats (the "meat") — mix & match
| Format | Think… |
|---|---|
| **Demonstration** | live use, unboxing, before/after, comparisons, high-production hero (Dollar Shave Club, Old Spice) |
| **Testimonial** | UGC, direct-to-camera, podcast, raw/pro testimonials, walk-'n-talk rant, group ("Parade Of Proof"), lifecycle, man-on-the-street, influencer |
| **Education** | explainer, how-to/listicle, whiteboard explainer, repurposed organic |
| **Story** | storytelling, lifestyle, documentary, skits, brand manifesto, problem-solution |
| **Faceless** | screenshots of comments/texts, text-only, slideshows, animation, cartoon, VFX |

## The CTA — Clear > Clever
**Spell it out, then SHOW it.** A good CTA shows or tells:
1. **What** to do → 2. **How** to do it → 3. **When** to do it → 4. **What they get** → 5. **What happens next** *(optional; matters most for lead magnets/multi-step)*.
- **Show AND tell**: demonstrate the click/form/submit → "the ultimate congruence."
- **Test**: run 3 identical hook+meat combos to the same audience, change *only* the CTA.
- **Layer on**: urgency, scarcity, guarantees, bonuses.

## Scale It
- Early: make **many variations** (can't predict winners) → a few wildly outperform.
- Then: **double down on winners** and **reuse winning hooks** to make more winners.
- Don't get bored of a winner — new customers see it for the first time.
- **Double your leads → double your business.**
