## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The big idea: it's a quality wall, not a market wall
Ads stop scaling because the **creative** capped out, not because the market saturated. *"The better your ads, the bigger the audience they'll convert."* You scale by making **more, better ads** — and you make better ads only by making *way more*. (Old Spice converted ~70% of the market because it was good enough to convert everyone — an ad that "wasn't capped.")

### Assemble, don't create — The Ad Assembly Process
Chunk every ad into **Hook → Meat → CTA** and mass-produce the parts separately, then combine:
**`50 hooks × 3–5 meat × 1–3 CTAs = 150 to 750 ads per week.`**
*"I wasn't really creating ads at all. I was assembling them."* Volume is also defense — no one can spot your top performers.

### The Prep Split (where the effort goes)
**80% Hooks · 20% Meat · ~0% CTAs.** *"Ads are made in research, not in recording."* If they don't pass the hook, nothing else matters.

### The Five Levels of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz) → hook type
Warmest/smallest → coldest/largest. Bigger audience ⇒ ads must be more varied and exceptional.
1. **Most Aware** — knows your product, needs the deal → **offer**-driven hook.
2. **Product-Aware** — knows what you sell, unsure it fits → **proof**-driven.
3. **Solution-Aware** — wants the result, doesn't know you deliver it → **promise**-driven.
4. **Problem-Aware** — feels the problem, doesn't know a solution → **pain**-driven.
5. **Completely Unaware** — doesn't know they have a problem → **curiosity**-driven.
Hormozi treats this as a **continuum**, not rigid buckets. Don't pile 90% of hooks in "Most Aware"; when in doubt, go broader (memes pull the widest audience).

### Hooks — two types + five sources
- **Winner Hooks** (weekly, off your previous winners) vs. **Expansion Hooks** (to reach broader audiences once you've **"capped"** current spend). "It's about doing more/better far more than doing something new."
- **The 5 Hook Sources** (in order): (1) your previous ads → (2) your free content → (3) others' ads → (4) others' free content → (5) platform ad libraries **last**. *Winning hooks in one industry often work in others.*

### The Ad Meat — five proven formats (mix & match)
The creative that **fulfills the hook**; aligns creative → hook → awareness. Only 20% effort, 3–5/session.
**Demonstration · Testimonial · Education · Story · Faceless.** (Testimonials include the "Parade Of Proof" group style.)

### The CTA — Clear > Clever
Spell out the next step, then **Show AND Tell** it (demonstrate the click/form/submit → "the ultimate congruence"). A good CTA shows or tells **what / how / when / what they get / what happens next** (last is optional). Make 1–3; test by running identical hook+meat, changing only the CTA; layer on **urgency, scarcity, guarantees, bonuses.**

### Scale It
Make **many variations** (you can't predict winners) → a few wildly outperform → **double down** and **reuse winning hooks** to make more. Don't get bored of a winner; new customers see it for the first time. **Double your leads → double your business.** Next step after winners: **Kaleidoscope Ads** ("you can't do those without this").
