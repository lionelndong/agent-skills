# Section I: Why Ads Hit A Wall And How To Scale Past It

## Core Idea
Ads don't stop scaling because the market is saturated — they stop because the **creative** hit a wall. *"You haven't saturated Facebook. But you have gotten all the low hanging fruit. You've hit a wall with your ad quality, not with the market. The better your ads, the bigger the audience they'll convert."* The fix is more and better ads: *"That's how you make ads that scale - you make better ads. And you only do that by making more…way more."*

> Disclaimer (Hormozi's own): this playbook is meant to be used **on top of** the paid ads chapters in $100M Leads — *"Not, instead of. This takes those ideas and puts them on steroids."*

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Five Levels of Awareness** (Eugene Schwartz) — audiences run from warmest/smallest (bottom) to coldest/largest (top).
- **The Awareness Continuum** — Hormozi's adaptation of Schwartz's "chunked" model into a **continuous** one.

## Key Concepts

### The Five Levels of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)
Listed bottom (warmest, smallest) to top (coldest, largest):
1. **Most Aware** *(bottom)* — the customer knows your product and only needs to know the deal.
2. **Product-Aware** — the customer knows what you sell but isn't sure it's right for them.
3. **Solution-Aware** — the customer knows the result they want but doesn't know your product can provide it.
4. **Problem-Aware** — the customer senses they have a problem but doesn't know there's a solution.
5. **Completely Unaware** *(top)* — the customer doesn't know they have a problem or need.

### The Awareness Continuum (Hormozi's adaptation)
*"Labels are clean. Reality is messy."* He has never met the clean archetypes in Schwartz's pyramid, so he treats awareness as continuous rather than five discrete buckets. The operating rule: **the larger the audience you reach, the more varied and exceptional your ads must be.** Ads start profitably at the bottom; the stronger the creative (the **hook**, the **offer**, and the **person delivering it**), the colder the traffic it converts and the higher up the pyramid it scales.

### The scaling logic
To scale ad spend you must *"make ads that convert the highest percentage of the largest audience possible."* That requires a repeatable **process that consistently creates bangers** — not luck, not theory. The process took 13 years to develop and is how Acquisition.com, Skool.com, and the portfolio run paid ads.

## Worked Example: The Portfolio Founder Who "Saturated" His Market
A portfolio founder selling weight loss on Facebook spent **$200,000/month** on ads. Every scale attempt hit a wall: after **$5–8k/day** in spend, cost-to-acquire shot through the roof. The cause wasn't the market — he made ads only **once a month**, **5 (sometimes a few more)** at a time. Hormozi's prescription: *"you're gonna take at least five and change it to at least 500."* Devote **every Friday** to advertising → find best ads → chop into **hook, meat, CTA** → run them through the **kaleidoscope** to make 50 more hooks and 3–5 meat variations → **150 ads/week**, i.e. from **5 ads/month to 600 ads/month**. Result: *"The company doubled in two quarters."*

### The Old Spice proof
The Old Spice "dude on the horse" ads converted ~**70% of the market** — including Hormozi, who wasn't a buyer. *"That's an ad that wasn't capped. It was good enough to convert everyone."* A great enough ad scales to the whole audience; that's the target.

## Mental Models
- **Quality wall, not market wall** — when CAC spikes at scale, refresh creative before blaming the market.
- **Better = more** — you only produce better ads by producing far more ads.
- **Volume is also defense** — at 150–750 ads/week, *"no one being able to figure out your top performing ads"* (good offense AND a defense against copycats).

## Anti-patterns
- Recording ~5 new ads a month and concluding the market is saturated.
- Treating Schwartz's levels as rigid real-world archetypes instead of a continuum.
- Reaching for theory; this playbook *"isn't about theory, it's about doing."*

## Key Takeaways
1. Ad ceilings are creative ceilings — scale past them with more, better ads.
2. Audiences run Most Aware → Completely Unaware (warm/small → cold/large); colder + larger audiences demand more varied, exceptional creative.
3. The whole system rests on a repeatable assembly process that reliably produces "bangers."
