# Section III: The Ad Meat

## Core Idea
The **meat** is the creative — *"the part of the ad that fulfills your hook."* It's the body of the ad. Alignment chain: **the creative aligns with the hook; the hook aligns with the audience's awareness level.** So the meat educates the customer about the **offer, the product, the solution, the problem**, or makes them aware of the phenomenon that is your product. The meat earns only **20%** of your attention and gets rotated **less often** than hooks (fewer people see it, so you don't "use it up"). **3–5 meats per weekly recording session is more than enough.**

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Five Ad Formats (Meat Types)** — the five major formats Hormozi's own ads used, proven across services, education, physical products, brick-and-mortar, and software. Mix and match.

## Key Concepts

### The Five Ad Formats
*"I'm not saying these are the only formats… these are the ones I've used that worked."*

1. **Demonstration Ads** — live use or reactions, unboxing, comparisons (before-and-afters), and high-production hero ads (think **Dollar Shave Club** and **Old Spice**). Covers product demonstrations (literally show the lead magnet / product & results) and service demonstrations (show the result of the service — e.g. *a packed gym*).

2. **Testimonial Ads** — user-generated content (UGC), founder direct-to-camera, podcast style, professional testimonials, raw iPhone-style testimonials, **walk 'n talk rants**, **group testimonials** (many people in frame), **lifecycle ads** (see Marketing Machine Playbook), man-on-the-street interviews, or celebrity/influencer collabs. His favorite group style: a **"Parade Of Proof."**

3. **Education Ads** — explainer videos, how-to / tutorial, **whiteboard explainer**, listicle videos, and high-performing organic content repurposed.

4. **Story Ads** — storytelling / narrative, **lifestyle**, warnings and opportunities, documentary style, skits, humor/comedy, **brand manifestos**, and problem-solution. *(His one lifestyle/"flaunt wealth" ad performed well even though he hated it — and he returned the car within 6 months.)*

5. **Faceless Ads** — screenshots of customer comments/texts, text-only, slide shows, animations, cartoon ads, or visual-effect-based ads. *(He ran screenshot compilations on their own as ads.)*

### Rotation logic
Because the body is seen by fewer people than the hook, it gets *"rotated less often."* You don't burn through meats the way you burn through hooks — hence only 3–5 per session, vs. 50 hooks.

### Reference-list discipline
*"Whenever I get stuck on what type of ad or format I'm going to use for the 'meat', I refer back to this list as an easy reference."* The five formats are a fallback menu when creativity stalls.

## Worked Example: Hormozi's Own Ad Library Mapped to the Five Formats
The playbook walks through real Acquisition.com / Skool / HormoziAlex ads, each tagged to a format:
- **Demonstration** — a product-demo ad that literally shows the lead magnet while explaining it; high-production "hero" ads that are fast-paced and switch scenes showing product & results; a service-demo ad showing a **packed gym**.
- **Testimonial** — a podcast clip pulled from a recording session that became **one of the top ads for Skool**; a **"Parade Of Proof"** group testimonial (his favorite); a lifecycle ad; raw and walk-'n-talk-rant testimonials shot at an actual location.
- **Education** — two **whiteboard explainer** ads.
- **Story** — a lifestyle ad he disliked but that performed; a storytelling ad that *"slayed."*
- **Faceless** — two **screenshot compilation** ads run on their own.

The lesson: one founder's entire winning ad set is just these five formats, recombined.

## Mental Models
- **Meat fulfills the hook** — the body must deliver on the promise the hook made.
- **20% effort, slower rotation** — don't over-invest; the meat lasts longer than the hook.
- **Format menu** — when stuck, pick from the five proven formats and mix/match.
- **Formats are industry-agnostic** — the same five worked across every industry he tried.

## Anti-patterns
- Spending the bulk of your time perfecting the meat (it's only 20%).
- Building meat that doesn't match the hook's promise or the audience's awareness.
- Recording dozens of meats per session (3–5 is enough).

## Key Takeaways
1. The meat is the creative that **fulfills the hook**; align creative → hook → awareness.
2. Use the **five proven formats**: Demonstration, Testimonial, Education, Story, Faceless — mix and match.
3. Make only **3–5 meats per session** and rotate them less often than hooks.
