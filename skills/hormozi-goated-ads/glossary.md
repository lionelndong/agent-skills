# Glossary — $100M GOATed Ads

**GOATed Ads** — "GOAT" = Greatest Of All Time; the playbook's name for making ads that get the right people to click, and the system for producing winners at volume. (Intro)

**Ad Assembly Process** — Chunking ad creation into an assembly line: build **Hooks**, **Meat**, and **CTAs** in parts, then combine. "I wasn't really creating ads at all. I was assembling them." (Ch 2)

**Hook** — The opening of an ad; the gate. If they don't make it through the hook, nothing else matters, so it gets ~80% of prep time. (Ch 2)

**Meat** — The creative/body of the ad — "the part of the ad that fulfills your hook." Gets 20% of attention; rotated less often than hooks. (Ch 3)

**CTA (Call To Action)** — The instruction telling the audience exactly what to do next; made last; "Clear > Clever." (Ch 4)

**The Ad Equation** — `50 hooks × 3–5 meat × 1–3 CTAs = 150 to 750 ads per week`. (Ch 2)

**The Prep Split** — Time allocation for ad prep: **80% Hooks / 20% Meat / ~0% CTAs.** (Ch 2)

**Banger** — A high-performing ad; the goal is a process that "consistently creates bangers" — "banger after banger." (Ch 1)

**The Five Levels of Awareness** — Eugene Schwartz's ladder, warmest/smallest to coldest/largest: Most Aware, Product-Aware, Solution-Aware, Problem-Aware, Completely Unaware. (Ch 1)

**Most Aware** — Knows your product; only needs to know the deal. Hooks are **offer-driven**. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Product-Aware** — Knows what you sell but unsure it's right for them. Hooks are **proof-driven**. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Solution-Aware** — Knows the result they want but not that your product provides it. Hooks are **promise-driven**. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Problem-Aware** — Senses a problem but doesn't know a solution exists. Hooks are **pain-driven**. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**Completely Unaware** — Doesn't know they have a problem or need. Hooks are **curiosity-driven**. (Ch 1, Ch 2)

**The Awareness Continuum** — Hormozi's adaptation of Schwartz's chunked pyramid into a *continuous* model: the larger the audience, the more varied and exceptional the ads must be. (Ch 1)

**Winner Hooks** — Bread-and-butter hooks written off previous winners for immediate weekly results. (Ch 2)

**Expansion Hooks** — Hooks written to enter new markets / reach broader audiences once existing ad capacity is "capped." (Ch 2)

**Capped** — When you can't spend more money profitably on your current ads even after refreshing creative; the trigger to write expansion hooks. (Ch 2)

**The 5 Hook Sources** — Where to find high-converting hooks, in order: (1) your previous ads, (2) your free content, (3) others' ads, (4) others' free content, (5) platform ad libraries (last). (Ch 2)

**Platform-Specific Ad Libraries** — Public libraries of competitors' ads; the *last* place to look — good for ideas, but you can't tell which ads actually perform. (Ch 2)

**The Widest Hooks Possible (Pro Tip)** — Use memes / meme-like content to attract the largest share of an audience; "a moth to a flame." When in doubt, go broader. (Ch 2)

**The Five Ad Formats** — The five meat types Hormozi has used that worked across all industries: Demonstration, Testimonial, Education, Story, Faceless. (Ch 3)

**Demonstration Ads** — Live use/reactions, unboxing, comparisons, before-and-afters, high-production hero ads (Dollar Shave Club, Old Spice). (Ch 3)

**Testimonial Ads** — UGC, direct-to-camera, podcast style, professional/raw testimonials, walk-'n-talk rants, group testimonials, lifecycle ads, man-on-the-street, influencer collabs. (Ch 3)

**Parade Of Proof** — Hormozi's favorite group-testimonial style: many testimonials/people paraded in the frame. (Ch 3)

**Education Ads** — Explainer/how-to/listicle/whiteboard-explainer videos and high-performing organic content. (Ch 3)

**Story Ads** — Storytelling, lifestyle, warnings/opportunities, documentary, skits, brand manifestos, problem-solution. (Ch 3)

**Faceless Ads** — Screenshots of customer comments/texts, text-only, slide shows, animations, cartoons, visual-effect ads. (Ch 3)

**Clear > Clever** — The CTA principle: spell out exactly what to do next rather than being clever. (Ch 4)

**Show AND Tell** — Don't just tell the next step — demonstrate it (show the click, the form, the submit) for "the ultimate congruence." (Ch 4)

**The 5 CTA Elements** — A good CTA shows or tells: what to do, how to do it, when to do it, what they get, what happens next (optional). (Ch 4)

**Kaleidoscope (Ad Kaleidoscope / Kaleidoscope Ads)** — Hormozi's "most prized advertising possession": the method that takes one ad and multiplies its hooks/variations to scale; the *next* step after this playbook finds winners. (Intro, Ch 4)

**Double your leads, double your business** — The scaling thesis: more, better ads → more leads → more business. (Ch 4)
