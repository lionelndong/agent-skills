# Chapter 3: Pillar II — Speed

## Core Idea
"Money loves speed. Wealth loves time. Poverty loves indecision." The faster you contact a lead and the sooner you get them into the sales meeting, the more they schedule and show — and the **less** you have to work them. Speed is the second-biggest lever after Availability.

## The Stats (why speed)
- **391% increase** in sales conversions when leads are contacted within the first **60 seconds** (Velocify).
- Contact within an hour → **7x** more likely to qualify the lead than waiting one more hour; **60x** more likely than waiting 24 hours (Harvard Business Review).
- **78% of customers buy from the company that responds first** (Lead Connect).

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Three Elements of Speed**:
  1. **Speed To First Contact** — time to call/message after a lead expresses interest. **Gold standard: contact all leads in 5 minutes or less** (extra credit: under 60 seconds). Key insight: *the slower you contact, the MORE follow-ups you need; the faster you contact, the FEWER* — "work less to make more" is literally true here.
  2. **Speed To First Appointment** — time between *scheduling* and *having* the appointment. Shorter delay = higher show rate. Two rules: **limit scheduling to 3 days out max** (5 if you must — "always 72 hours in advance"; closer-scheduled = higher-showing), and **pull appointments forward, preferably to the same day** (same-day shows higher; "show rates for *I'm talking to you right now* are 100%").
  3. **Speed Of Response** — how fast you reply *after* they schedule and before the appointment. This is where leads "fall through the cracks." Leads often decide *before* the call, "and that makes it the competitor's sale to lose."
  - When to use: every lead, every stage between opt-in and the meeting.
  - How: staff **excess capacity** so fresh leads are never waiting; use the calendar gaps that Availability created to contact instantly.

- **The 5 Pull-Forward Outcomes** — when you call a self-scheduled lead to pull the appointment forward, exactly five things happen:
  1. **Doesn't respond** — remove from calendar (or double-book if in-person).
  2. **Contacted & unqualified** — remove; give free useful stuff/references, or offer a downsell if you have one.
  3. **Contacted, qualified, free right now** — best case; **guaranteed show**. Same person sets & closes → go for the close; if not → **hot handoff** to an active closer.
  4. **Contacted, qualified, pulled appointment forward** — moved sooner (ideally same day).
  5. **Contacted, qualified, appointment confirmed** — re-confirmed the existing time.

- **Hot Handoff** — connect the qualified, ready-now lead to a closer via a **three-way message**, and **edify** the closer ("you're going to speak with one of our top experts on X… helped Y achieve [thing they want]").

## Key Concepts
- **Speed to first contact** — opt-in → first touch (target <5 min).
- **Speed to first appointment** — scheduled → held (target ≤72 hrs, ideally same day).
- **Speed of response** — reply latency between booking and the call.
- **Ghosting** — a scheduled lead skipping the appointment; rises sharply when booked too far out.
- **Excess capacity** — slack staffing so no lead waits; the cost of true speed.
- **Edify** — build up the closer to the lead before the handoff.

## Worked Example: The Pull-Forward Script
Calling a self-scheduled lead (when no fresh leads remain, this is priority #2):
> "Hey, this is Jim… calling from XYZ on a recorded line… I saw that you just booked a time for our X on Monday. Just calling to confirm some details before your appointment. **Is now a terrible time?**"
> **(WHO)** "So I see you are a [qualification 1], is that right?"
> **(WHAT THEY WANT)** "And you're looking to [qualification 2], correct?"
> **(WHAT THEY'RE STRUGGLING WITH)** "What have you tried so far?… So this is a priority to fix or [bad thing] happens — fair?"
> "Two things. First, good news: I think we can help you out. Second, **better news: I have an opening that canceled later today at 4pm**… unless now is a good time?"

Three outcomes: go into the sale, **move the appointment up**, or (worst case) re-confirm:
> "No worries — we always like to offer more times to make it convenient… So we're all good for two days from now at 4pm. Any reason you think you won't make it?"

Then text a confirmation, **or** do the hot handoff (three-way message + edify the closer).

## Mental Models
- **Faster contact = fewer touches.** Speed is the rare "work less, make more."
- **The pre-call window is the competitor's sale to lose** — own it with instant responses.
- **Pull forward toward "now"** — every hour closer raises the show rate; "right now" is 100%.

## Anti-patterns
- **Letting leads schedule far out** — more schedules, but more ghosts (a "triple loss": the ghost, killed sales-team morale, and a stolen convenient slot).
- **Slow replies between booking and call** — "you may as well communicate by carrier pigeon"; if they don't show up for *you*, why show up for *them*?
- **Treating slow first-contact as fine** — it directly burns the 391% / 7x / 78% advantages.

## Pro Tips
- **Speed Litmus Test**: if you're not hearing "man, that was fast — you guys are on top of it!" **at least once a day**, you're not calling leads fast enough.
- **Get Leads To Schedule Sooner**: ask what days they *planned* to do the thing (e.g., work out), then frame this appointment as the **first step** toward that — justifies booking within 72 hrs.
- Watch time stamps (calls + messages); if show rates drop, **look here first**, especially per-rep.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Contact in <5 minutes** (ideally <60 sec) — fast contact means fewer follow-ups.
2. **Book inside 72 hours** (same day > next day > day after) and **pull appointments forward**.
3. Respond **instantly** in the pre-call window; it's the competitor's sale to lose.
4. Run the pull-forward call against the **5 outcomes**; hot-handoff the ready-now buyers.
