# Chapter 1: Lead Nurture & The Four Pillars

## Core Idea
This playbook solves ONE problem: **maximizing 30-day show rates** — the medium-term nurture that happens *after advertising and before sales*. "If they do not show, they cannot buy." Show-ups have four main drivers ("the Four Pillars"): **Availability, Speed, Personalization, Volume**.

## Origin Story (why these four)
Born from **ALAN** (Artificial Lead Automation & Nurture), Hormozi's "pay per show" software. When COVID killed brick-and-mortar gyms (June 2020), he pivoted to phone/video businesses and his data scientist **Vlad** mined ~1M rows of appointment data. Four data points correlated with positive (shown) appointments:
1. The **more time slots available**, the greater the show rate.
2. The **fewer days out** leads scheduled, the greater the show rate.
3. The **more follow-ups**, the greater the show rate.
4. The **more often leads responded to follow-ups**, the greater the show rate.

These four findings became the Four Pillars. "There are no silver bullets, only golden BBs."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Four Pillars of Lead Nurture** — the four drivers of show-ups:
  - **1) Availability** — the number of open appointment slots you have (days/week, hours/day, slots/hour) and how far out leads can schedule.
  - **2) Speed (to contact)** — how fast you respond to leads and how soon they can have the appointment.
  - **3) Personalization** — making communication useful and relevant to each lead. "The more personal the follow-up, the more likely they'll respond. And the more often they respond, the more likely they'll show up."
  - **4) Volume** — the number of times you reach out before giving up.
  - When to use: any appointment-based business converting engaged leads into shown sales appointments.
  - How: "Reach out the moment they express interest, make it convenient to talk, frame the conversation as beneficial to them, and do it as many times as you can." Then execute (a 5th element of *doing* it).

- **The Three Metrics** (shared language for the whole playbook):
  - **Schedule Rate** — % of *engaged leads* who schedule an appointment.
  - **Show Rate** — % of *scheduled leads* who show up.
  - **Throughput** — total % of *engaged leads* who show up (Schedule Rate × Show Rate).

## Key Concepts
- **Schedule Rate** — engaged → scheduled (a percentage).
- **Show Rate** — scheduled → shown (a percentage).
- **Throughput** — engaged → shown (the end-to-end conversion that matters most).
- **Medium-term lead nurture** — the window between showing interest and buying; this playbook's sole scope.
- **Long-term follow-up** — emails, podcasts, content to re-engage cold leads; covered *elsewhere*, not here.
- **Golden BBs** — many small improvements stacked; the opposite of one "silver bullet."

## Worked Example: Schedule / Show / Throughput
```
Leads:                 100
Schedule Rate:         50%   → Scheduled Appointments: 50
Show Rate:             50%   → Shown Appointments:     25
Throughput:            25%   (25 shows out of 100 leads)
```
A 20–40% increase in revenue, at 20% margins, is **2x–3x the profit** of the business — in year one alone. "Once you learn to see the money you didn't make as money you've lost, the show rate becomes one of the most important numbers in your business."

## Mental Models
- "You must first become consistent before you can become exceptional."
- Treat lost shows as **money you've lost**, not money you never had — that reframe makes show rate a top metric.
- Most owners ignore show rate for three reasons (1: can't do anything, 2: don't know what, 3: more important things). Only #3 is ever acceptable — and it's rarely true.
- Any business can become **best in class within its industry** at getting leads to show, even if some industries have higher baseline show rates than others.

## Anti-patterns
- **Believing show rate is unfixable** — it's one of the highest-ROI levers available, risk-free.
- **Treating nurture as "one thing"** — it's many small things (golden BBs) compounding.
- **Confusing this with long-term follow-up** — different problem, different playbook.

## Key Takeaways
1. The whole playbook optimizes **30-day show rate** — the gap between interest and purchase.
2. Memorize the four drivers in order: **Availability → Speed → Personalization → Volume** (then Execution).
3. Track **Schedule Rate, Show Rate, Throughput** — Throughput is the number that compounds into revenue.
4. A 20–40% show-rate lift ≈ 2x–3x profit at 20% margins.
