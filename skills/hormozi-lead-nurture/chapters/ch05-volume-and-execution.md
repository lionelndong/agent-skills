# Chapter 5: Pillar IV — Volume (and Execution)

## Core Idea
"Volume negates luck." The more often you reach out, the more leads show — because most communication attempts fail (bad timing, forgot, missed, didn't need it then). Reaching out again makes business *and* ethical sense: "if they opted in, they **asked** to be contacted." The problem: **nearly half of salespeople give up after the first attempt** (avg 1.3 attempts; 44% stop after the first). Then: **Execution** — none of it works unless you actually do it, which comes down to **culture**.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Three Parts of Volume**:
  1. **Scheduling Appointments** — the front-loaded multi-touch reach-out cadence (below).
  2. **Reminding Them To Show** — automated + manual reminders.
  3. **Booking Their Next Appointment At The Current Appointment** — **BAMFAM**.
  - When to use: every lead from opt-in until they buy or hit long-term nurture.
  - How: front-load touches (more days that pass = less likely to schedule), then remind, then BAMFAM.

- **BAMFAM — "Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting"** (from Sharran Srivatsaa): NEVER end a call with "we'll circle back" or "I'll propose times." "You're both there. Right now. Pull up your darn calendar. Pick a darn time." People show more to appointments *you scheduled with them in real time*. Someone dodgy about the next call **is an objection — handle it.** "BAMFAM as a way of life." (Fixed an acquired insurance company's first-to-second-call drop-off → second-call show rates skyrocketed.)

- **Acknowledge – Compliment – Ask The Next Question** — the manual reminder / response pattern: keep the conversation going, use it to **qualify** and **pull appointments forward** ("I've actually got an opening today at 4 if you're available… if not, no worries"). Once they respond — **keep nurturing**, keep responding quickly.

- **Culture of Execution** — "Culture refers to the rules that govern good and bad behavior." Accept the behaviors that make you **available, fast, personal, persistent**; reject **unavailable, slow, boring, fragile**. Rewards: **money** (a 5–30% commission bump for show rate) + **non-money** (attention, affection, approval — kudos to top show rates). Rank the team on **show rate, close rate, and total call-to-close %** (nurture list = work ethic; closing list = skill).

## The Reach-Out Cadence (Scheduling Appointments)
1. **Call within 5 min** of opting in.
2. **Double-dial** — if the first call gets nothing, call again immediately (many phones block the first unknown call, let the second through; they gave permission — contact them like you mean it).
3. If nothing, **leave a voicemail**.
4. **Send a text** immediately after the voicemail.
5. **Double-dial + text two more times that day** (hours apart).
6. **Call twice/day for the next two days** (once early, once late); text after the second call each day.
7. **Call + text once/day for the next four days.**
8. **After week one → long-term nurture** (free value + soft CTAs to re-engage; when they do, start back at the top).
> Front-load reach-outs (steps 6–7 are flexible). "Follow the laws in your area." **Pro Tip:** get an **automatic dialer** (2–3x team efficiency; surfaces highest-likelihood leads, keeps the cadence).

## Reminding Them To Show
- **Automated reminders** (make it clear they're automated — "people don't mind reminders; they mind being lied to"):
  - **Immediately** — confirm time, date, the number you'll call from, and who they're meeting.
  - **24 hours before · 12 hours out · 3 hours from the appointment.**
  - Tips: add the **area code** you'll call from (2x pick-up rates); use the lead's **local time zone**.
- **Manual reminders** from a real cell phone: **the night before, the morning of, and ~60 min prior.** Send **multiple short messages**, not one big block (more natural). Keep it personal; use **Acknowledge–Compliment–Ask the next question**.

## Worked Example: Execution Culture — "Jacob the Garbage Man"
A 15-year-old neighbor (Jacob) who "wanted to make money" took a cold-call seat: "look at what the top guy does, then do **double** that. He makes 200 calls, make 400. He works five days, you work six." Reframe for unqualified leads: they're **"free practice"** — "swings of the hammer to chisel our skillset." Jacob offered to take everyone's **yellow** (less-qualified) leads in off-hours: **"Yellows are the new gold."** He finished **#1 of 26** salesmen at Gym Launch, then became Director of Sales at Acquisition.com. The team -isms that encode the culture:
- **Volume negates luck** — do more than everyone else.
- **Yellows are the new gold** — don't sleep on less-qualified leads.
- **Be a garbage man** — don't judge a book by its cover; be an equal-opportunity salesman; every sale is free practice.
- **BAMFAM as a way of life.**
- **Process > all** — no one is above the process.

## Key Concepts
- **Volume** — number of reach-outs before giving up (target **7+** in multiple ways).
- **Double-dial** — back-to-back calls to beat first-call blocking.
- **Automated vs manual reminders** — software cadence vs real-phone, personal touches.
- **BAMFAM** — book the next meeting *during* the current one.
- **Culture** — the rules governing good/bad behavior; here, a culture of execution.
- **Yellows** — less-qualified leads (vs reds = unqualified, greens = prime) that still convert.

## Mental Models
- **Volume negates luck** — most touches fail, so add more touches; front-load them.
- **Reaching out again is a service** — they asked for help by opting in.
- **Schedule the next step while you're both present** — limbo kills shows (BAMFAM).
- **"Do the boring work"** — tedious to work leads, but it gets "very unboring results."

## Anti-patterns
- **Giving up after ~1 attempt** — where 44% of salespeople lose the sale.
- **"We'll circle back later."** — the BAMFAM cardinal sin; they put you in their rearview mirror.
- **Cherry-picking only green leads** — ignores the yellows that close in volume.
- **Obsessing over close rate alone** — track all three (show, close, call-to-close); conversion on the leads you *have* is what matters.
- **One big blocky reminder** — send several short, natural messages instead.

## Key Takeaways
1. Reach out **7+ times in multiple ways**, **front-loaded**; transition to long-term nurture after week one.
2. **Automated reminders** at 24h / 12h / 3h; **manual** ones night-before / morning-of / 60-min-prior, as short personal messages.
3. **BAMFAM** every time — never let a lead hit no-man's-land.
4. **Execute via culture**: reward show rate (5–30% bump + attention/affection/approval); rank on show, close, and call-to-close. **"None of this will work unless you do."**
