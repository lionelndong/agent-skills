# Patterns — $100M Lead Nurture

## Pattern: Run The Four Pillars (in order)
**When to use**: Any appointment-based business converting engaged leads into shown sales appointments.
**How**: **Availability** (open more days/hours/slots + all booking channels) → **Speed** (contact <5 min, book ≤72 hrs, respond instantly) → **Personalization** (qualify, score, route, segment, incentivize, prove) → **Volume** (7+ front-loaded touches, reminders, BAMFAM) → **Execute** (build the culture).
**Trade-offs**: Availability and Speed are the two biggest levers; do them first. Each pillar costs capacity but returns 20–40% (sometimes 200%+) more shows.

## Pattern: Maximize Availability
**When to use**: First — it's the #1 lever on show rate.
**How**: Take appointments **7 days/week** (+40% available), **more hours/day** (6am–6pm PST for nationwide), **4 slots/hour** (every 15 min, same appointment length), and offer **inbound + outbound + self-scheduling**. Strip steps from the self-scheduler; pre-fill known fields.
**Trade-offs**: Diminishing returns at the top quartiles, but large gains remain. Needs excess capacity / extra setters.

## Pattern: Friction As A Dial
**When to use**: Calendar quality is off.
**How**: Too *few* appointments → **remove** friction (fewer steps, obvious times). Too many *bad* appointments → **add** friction (video to watch, sales letter, price on page, delayed scheduler).
**Trade-offs**: Adding friction lowers quantity to raise quality — match the lever to the actual problem.

## Pattern: Speed To Contact (<5 minutes)
**When to use**: Every new lead.
**How**: Use the calendar gaps Availability created to contact instantly; "contact all leads in 5 minutes or less" (extra credit <60 sec). Faster contact = fewer follow-ups needed.
**Trade-offs**: Requires excess capacity so fresh leads never wait; pays back via 391% / 7x / 78% advantages.

## Pattern: Speed To First Appointment + Pull-Forward
**When to use**: After a lead schedules.
**How**: Limit scheduling to **3 days out** (5 max); always 72 hrs in advance. Call self-scheduled leads to **pull the appointment forward** (ideally same day) using the script; run the **5 outcomes** (no response / unqualified / ready-now / pulled-forward / re-confirmed). Hot-handoff the ready-now buyers.
**Trade-offs**: Booking far out gets more schedules but more ghosts (a triple loss).

## Pattern: Qualify + Score + Route (Best Leads → Best Closers)
**When to use**: You have variable lead quality and multiple closers.
**How**: (1) Study best customers (actions + demographics); (2) ask those questions in opt-ins/applications/pre-call; (3) score **1–5 or red/yellow/green**; (4) route best leads to best closers; (5) cancel poor-fit appointments to free capacity.
**Trade-offs**: Deliberately *cancels* some appointments — counterintuitive, but concentrates time on likely buyers.

## Pattern: Segment Your Messaging
**When to use**: One generic sequence underperforms.
**How**: **Easy** — research each good lead's site/socials, mention it early ("I see your traffic is down 18%…"). **Hard** — build separate text/email sequences per lead category (revenue level, goal weight) aligned to their specific pains.
**Trade-offs**: "Hard" takes a heavy weekend upfront, far less ongoing — and lifts response (HubSpot 7x).

## Pattern: Push vs Pull Incentives
**When to use**: To raise the value of showing up.
**How**: **Push** = a no-strings **gift** *before* arrival → reciprocity ("$5 gift card — check your email"). **Pull** = an **A/B** reward *after* arrival they choose, both options assuming attendance ("face cream or hand lotion, waiting for you tomorrow"). A/B needs: they want it, it connects to the offer, you really prepared it.
**Trade-offs**: Push works even when people know what you're doing; both also lift close rate.

## Pattern: Match Proof To The Lead
**When to use**: Across all follow-up stages.
**How**: Send testimonials/case studies of people matching the lead's age/ethnicity/situation. Weave proof **after schedule + before show**, **between calls**, and **every 90 days for ghosts** (results of people who started when they ghosted). Stay compliant by state/country.
**Trade-offs**: Generic proof doesn't excite; the closer the demographic match, the better.

## Pattern: The Volume Cadence (7+ touches, front-loaded)
**When to use**: From opt-in until purchase or long-term nurture.
**How**: Call <5 min → **double-dial** → voicemail → text → 2 more double-dial+text that day → 2 calls/day for 2 days (text after 2nd) → 1 call+text/day for 4 days → week-two: long-term nurture. Use an **auto-dialer** (2–3x efficiency).
**Trade-offs**: More days passing = lower schedule odds, so front-load; obey local contact laws.

## Pattern: Reminders (Automated + Manual)
**When to use**: After a lead is scheduled.
**How**: **Automated** (clearly labeled): immediate confirmation + **24h / 12h / 3h** out; include the area code you'll call from (2x pick-up) and the lead's local time zone. **Manual** from a real phone: **night before / morning of / 60 min prior**, as several short messages; Acknowledge–Compliment–Ask the next question.
**Trade-offs**: With good Speed you need fewer reminders; never disguise automation ("they mind being lied to").

## Pattern: BAMFAM (Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting)
**When to use**: End of every call, especially first-to-second-call handoffs.
**How**: Never say "we'll circle back." Open the calendar and book the next step in real time; treat dodging the next call as an objection to handle.
**Trade-offs**: None worth noting — it directly raises next-appointment show rates.

## Pattern: Build A Culture Of Execution
**When to use**: To make the team actually work the leads.
**How**: Reward show rate with a **5–30% commission bump** + non-money (attention/affection/approval). Rank on **show rate, close rate, call-to-close %**. Encode -isms: "volume negates luck," "yellows are the new gold," "be a garbage man," "process > all."
**Trade-offs**: "None of this will work unless you do." Treat new setters as low-risk future closers (same skill set).
