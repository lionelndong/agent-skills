## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What this playbook solves
**"If they do not show, they cannot buy."** This is **medium-term lead nurture** — maximizing **30-day show rates** in the window *after advertising and before sales*. (Long-term follow-up — emails, podcasts, content — is covered elsewhere.) "There are no silver bullets, only golden BBs." A 20–40% show-rate lift ≈ **2x–3x profit** at 20% margins.

### The Three Metrics (shared language)
- **Schedule Rate** — % of *engaged leads* who schedule.
- **Show Rate** — % of *scheduled leads* who show.
- **Throughput** — % of *engaged leads* who show (Schedule × Show). *Ex: 100 leads × 50% × 50% = 25% throughput.*

### The Four Pillars of Lead Nurture (drivers of show-ups; do in order — Availability & Speed are the biggest levers)
1. **Availability** — "If leads cannot schedule, they do not show." The number of slots (days/week, hours/day, slots/hour) and how far out they can book. Tactics: take appointments **7 days/week** (+40% available), **more hours/day** (6am–6pm PST nationwide), **4 slots/hour** (every 15 min, same length), and offer **inbound + outbound + self-scheduling**. **Friction is a dial**: remove it for more bookings; add it (video, sales letter, price, delayed scheduler) when you have too many *bad* appointments.
2. **Speed** — "Money loves speed." Three elements: **Speed to First Contact** (target **<5 min**, extra credit <60 sec — faster contact = *fewer* follow-ups), **Speed to First Appointment** (book **≤72 hrs / 3 days out max**; **pull forward to same day** — "right now" shows at 100%), **Speed of Response** (reply instantly pre-call — it's "the competitor's sale to lose"). Stats: **391%** lift if <60 sec · **7x** qualify if <1 hr · **78%** buy from whoever responds first.
3. **Personalization** — "Personalize, don't pressure." Find what they want, get it for them, give it *on the condition they show* (risk down, opportunity cost up). Six tactics: use their **preferred channel** (open multi-channel, continue where they respond) · **qualify** (application; cancel poor fits) · **best leads → best closers** (score **1–5 or red/yellow/green** from your best customers) · **segment messaging** · **incentivize** (Push vs Pull) · **proof** (matched to the lead).
4. **Volume** — "Volume negates luck." Reach out **7+ times, front-loaded** (most touches fail; ~44% of salespeople quit after one). Then **remind** (automated 24h/12h/3h + manual night-before/morning-of/60-min-prior) and **BAMFAM**.

### Incentives — Push vs Pull
- **Push (gift)**: a no-strings gift *before* arrival → **reciprocity** ("$5 gift card — check your email"; they keep it whether they show or not). Works even when people know what you're doing.
- **Pull (A/B)**: a reward *after* arrival the lead **chooses**, where **both options assume they'll show** ("face cream or hand lotion, waiting for you tomorrow"). A/B works only if they want it, it connects to your offer, and you really incurred the cost.

### BAMFAM — "Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting"
Never end a call with "we'll circle back." Open the calendar and book the next step **in real time**. People show more to appointments scheduled *with them, now*. Dodging the next call is an objection — handle it.

### The pull-forward call → 5 outcomes
When you call a self-scheduled lead to move it sooner: (1) no response → remove; (2) unqualified → remove/downsell; (3) **qualified + free now → guaranteed show** (close or **hot handoff**); (4) qualified → **pull forward**; (5) qualified → **re-confirm**. *Hot handoff = three-way message + edify the closer.*

### Execution (the 5th element)
"None of this will work unless you do." It comes down to **culture** — reward show rate (a **5–30%** commission bump + attention/affection/approval); rank the team on **show rate, close rate, and call-to-close %**. -isms: "volume negates luck," **"yellows are the new gold"** (less-qualified leads still convert), "be a garbage man" (don't judge — every lead is free practice), "process > all."
