# Glossary — $100M Lead Nurture

**A/B Incentive ("Pull")** — A reward the lead chooses (A or B) that they get *after* showing up; both options assume attendance and signal you incurred a cost on their behalf. Needs three conditions: they want it, it connects to your offer, you really prepared it. (Ch 4)

**Acknowledge – Compliment – Ask The Next Question** — The pattern for manual reminders/responses; keeps the conversation going and is used to qualify and pull appointments forward. (Ch 5)

**ALAN** — "Artificial Lead Automation & Nurture," Hormozi's pay-per-show software whose appointment data produced the Four Pillars. (Ch 1)

**Availability** — Pillar I: how many appointment slots you have (days/week, hours/day, slots/hour) and how far out leads can schedule; the single biggest lever on show rate. (Ch 2)

**BAMFAM** — "Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting" (from Sharran Srivatsaa): always schedule the next appointment *during* the current one. (Ch 5)

**Double-dial** — Two back-to-back calls; many phones block the first unknown call and let the second through. (Ch 5)

**Edify** — Build up the closer to the lead before a hot handoff ("you'll speak with one of our top experts on X…"). (Ch 3)

**Excess capacity** — Deliberate staffing/scheduling slack so fresh leads never wait; the cost of true Availability and Speed. (Ch 2, Ch 3)

**Four Pillars of Lead Nurture** — Availability, Speed, Personalization, Volume — the four drivers of show-ups (plus Execution as a fifth, "do it" element). (Ch 1)

**Friction control** — Two-way dial: *remove* friction to book more appointments; *add* friction (video, sales letter, price, delayed scheduler) when you have too many bad appointments. (Ch 2)

**Ghosting** — A scheduled lead skipping the appointment; rises sharply when booked too far out (a "triple loss"). (Ch 3)

**Golden BBs** — Many small improvements stacked; "there are no silver bullets, only golden BBs." (Ch 1)

**Header–Headline–Subheadline** — Top of a self-scheduling page; Header = "Attention [Avatar]" confirms they're in the right place, then say the next thing to do and why. (Ch 2)

**Hot Handoff** — Connecting a qualified, ready-now lead to a closer via a three-way message, with edification. (Ch 3)

**Lead Scoring** — Rating leads 1–5 or red/yellow/green from criteria derived from your best customers. (Ch 4)

**Long-term follow-up / nurture** — Re-engaging cold leads with free value + soft CTAs (emails, podcasts, content); covered *elsewhere*, not in this playbook. (Ch 1, Ch 5)

**Medium-term lead nurture** — The window *after advertising and before sales* — interest to purchase; the sole scope of this playbook (maximizing 30-day show rates). (Ch 1)

**Personalization** — Pillar III: making communication useful, relevant, and pleasant to each lead. "Personalize, don't pressure." (Ch 4)

**Proof** — Case studies/testimonials matched to the lead's demographics, woven across all follow-up stages (and every 90 days for ghosts). (Ch 4)

**Pull-Forward** — Calling a self-scheduled lead to move their appointment sooner (ideally same day); resolves to 5 outcomes. (Ch 3)

**Push Incentive (Gift)** — A no-strings gift given *before* arrival to evoke reciprocity (e.g., a $5 gift card they keep whether they show or not). (Ch 4)

**Qualify The Leads** — Collect data (an application before the call), then remove poor-fit leads from the calendar to protect capacity. (Ch 4)

**Schedule Rate** — % of *engaged leads* who schedule an appointment. (Ch 1)

**Segment Your Messaging** — Send the right message per lead category (research each lead, or build per-segment sequences). (Ch 4)

**Show Rate** — % of *scheduled leads* who show up to the appointment. (Ch 1)

**Speed** — Pillar II: speed to first contact (<5 min, ideally <60 sec), speed to first appointment (≤72 hrs, ideally same day), and speed of response. "Money loves speed." (Ch 3)

**Speed Litmus Test** — If you don't hear "wow, that was fast!" at least once a day, you aren't contacting leads fast enough. (Ch 3)

**Throughput** — Total % of *engaged leads* who show up (Schedule Rate × Show Rate); the end-to-end conversion that matters most. (Ch 1)

**Volume** — Pillar IV: the number of times you reach out before giving up (target 7+). "Volume negates luck." (Ch 5)

**Yellows are the new gold** — Less-qualified ("yellow") leads still convert in volume; don't cherry-pick only "greens." (Ch 5)
