# Chapter 13: Enhancing the Offer — Urgency

## Core Idea
"Scarcity is a function of quantity. Urgency is a function of time." Urgency limits *when* people can act (rather than how many), giving a deadline that drives the decision. "Deadlines drive decisions."

## Frameworks Introduced — The Four Types of Urgency
1. **Cohort-Based Rolling Urgency** — start clients on a regular cadence: "If you sign up today, I can get you in with our next group that kicks off Monday; otherwise you wait until our next kickoff." Less frequent kickoffs = more powerful. (Bonus operational benefit: choreographed onboarding.)
2. **Rolling Seasonal Urgency** — real date countdowns on the *same* core promotion, re-wrapped by season: "New Year Promotion ends Jan 30" → "Valentines Lovers Promo" → "Sexy By Spring Special," etc. Same promo, a "real" start/finish via the seasonal name. (Hormozi's #1 strategy for local businesses.)
3. **Pricing- or Bonus-Based Urgency** — let the *promotion/price/bonus* be the thing they could miss, not the service: "let's get you started today so you can take advantage of the discount… I'm not sure how long we'll run it." Lets year-round businesses keep integrity.
4. **Exploding Opportunity** — the opportunity itself decays with time (arbitrage, a stock, a new ad platform before competitors, an exploding job offer). "Every second someone delays, they miss out on disproportionate gains."

## Key Concepts
- **The deadline effect is real and illogical** — "The biggest sales on a week-long campaign happen in the last 4 hours of the last day (up to 50–60%)." The last 3% of the time creates the majority of sales.
- **You lose almost nothing by turning business away** — the fear of lost sales is "unfounded"; the people who "miss out" were rarely going to buy anyway.
- **Make deadlines real** — fake countdowns destroy credibility ("you'll just look like every other wannabe marketer").
- **Clean your pipeline with every price change** — "The price is going up! So get in now!" shows strength and pulls fence-sitters off the fence. Never raise prices without telling people.

## Worked Example: The Two Cohort Nudges
Base nudge: *"If you sign up today, I can get you in with our next group that kicks off Monday, otherwise you'll have to wait until our next kickoff date."*
Juiced version: *"I actually had a client who signed up a few weeks ago drop out, so I have an opening for our next cohort that kicks off Monday. If you're pretty sure you're gonna do this sooner or later, might as well get in now so you can start reaping the rewards sooner rather than paying the same and waiting."*
These two tweaks "have pushed so many sales over the edge" by reminding the prospect that acting now means starting Monday, not next week.

## Mental Models
- *Deadlines drive decisions* — without a real cut-off, prospects default to "later" (which becomes never).
- *Re-wrap, don't rebuild* — seasonal urgency is a five-minute landing-page/date change over the same core offer.

## Anti-patterns
- Fake or resetting countdowns (credibility suicide).
- No deadline at all — leaving the decision open-ended.

## Key Takeaways
1. Add a real deadline using Cohorts, Seasonal, Pricing/Bonus, or Exploding Opportunity.
2. Most sales cluster at the deadline — that's human, not logical.
3. Turning business away (and price-increase notices) increases, not decreases, net sales.
