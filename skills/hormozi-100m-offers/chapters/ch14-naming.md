# Chapter 16: Enhancing the Offer — Naming

## Core Idea
"Like the tree that falls in the forest that no one hears, having a Grand Slam Offer will not make you money if no one finds out about it." The name determines whether the right prospects pay attention. And renaming an offer over time ("changing the wrapping paper," not the offer) gives you **evergreen lead generation**.

## Framework Introduced — The M-A-G-I-C Naming Formula
Use **three to five** of these components (not necessarily in order; shorter and punchier is better):
- **M — Make a Magnetic "Reason Why"**: the reason for the promotion / what's in it for them. Examples: *Free, 88% off, Giveaway, Spring, Back To School, Grand Opening, New Management, Anniversary, New Year.* "It really doesn't matter so long as you believe it" (the fraternity-party-planner mindset).
- **A — Announce Your Avatar**: call out exactly who it's for (and who it isn't). Go hyper-local where relevant ("Not Baltimore but Towson, MD"). Examples: *Bee Cave Dentists, Rolling Hills Moms, Salon Owners, Retired Athletes.*
- **G — Give Them a Goal**: the prospect's dream outcome — a word, feeling, event, or result. Examples: *Pain Free, Celebrity Smile, Double Your Profit, First Client, 7-Figure, Little Black Dress.*
- **I — Indicate a Time Interval**: how long results take. Examples: *4-Hour, 21-Day, 6-Week, 3-Month.* (Compliance note: many ad platforms reject a quantifiable claim paired with a duration since it implies a guarantee — e.g., use "Make Your First Sale in 10 Days" rather than "$10,000 in 10 days" where restricted.)
- **C — Complete with a Container Word**: signals it's a *bundle/system* (can't be commoditized). Examples: *Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Incubator, Masterclass, Accelerator, Sprint, System, Mastermind, Deep Dive, Reset, Shortcut.*

(Author's marketing-theory mapping: M = Attention, A = Discrimination, G = Purpose, I = Timeline, C = Method.)

## Key Concepts
- **You're changing the wrapper, not the offer** — services, pricing, and money model stay the same; only the exterior perception changes.
- **Rhyme & alliteration (nice-to-haves, don't force)**: *Six-Pack Fast Track*, *Marriage Thrive Deep Dive* (rhyme); *Make Money Masterclass*, *Debt Detox*, *Big Booty Bootcamp* (alliteration).
- **Name sub-items & bonuses too** — applying MAGIC to each stack item raises its perceived value.
- **Order of variation when offers fatigue** (change top-down; lower items are more operationally heavy):
  1. Change the **creative** (images/video).
  2. Change the **body copy**.
  3. Change the **headline/wrapper** (e.g., *Six-Week Stress Release Challenge* → *42-Day Relaxing Holidays Challenge*).
  4. Change the **duration**.
  5. Change the **enhancer** (the free/discount component).
  6. Change the **monetization structure** — last resort.

## Worked Example: Named Offers Across Industries
- **Wellness**: *Free Six-Week Lean-By-Halloween Challenge*; *88% Off 12-Week Bikini Blueprint*; *Free 21-Day Mommy Makeover*.
- **Doctors**: *$2,000-Off Celebrity Smile Transformation*; *Lakeway Moms — 12 Months To A Perfect Smile*; *Back Sore No More! 90-Day Rapid Healing Intensive (81% off!)*.
- **Coaching**: *5 Clients in 5 Days Blueprint*; *14-Day Find Your Perfect Product Launch*; *Fill Your Gym in 30 Days (Free!)*.
Each blends Magnet + Avatar + Goal + Interval + Container.

## Mental Models
- *People do judge a book by its cover* — half-assed naming ruins conversions; the same offer renamed can get 2×–10× response.
- *Once monetized, rarely change the offer itself* — point your "entrepreneurial ADD" at the wrapper, not the machine. Change deeper only as a last resort.

## Anti-patterns
- Lazy/vague names (the "Float Tank Center Session" vs. "Free Six-Week Stress Release Challenge" contrast).
- Changing the whole offer/business when only the creative or copy needed refreshing.

## Key Takeaways
1. Name with MAGIC (Magnet, Avatar, Goal, Interval, Container) — use 3–5, test them.
2. Refresh the wrapper over time for evergreen lead flow — don't touch the core offer.
3. When fatigued, vary top-down: creative → copy → headline → duration → enhancer → structure.
