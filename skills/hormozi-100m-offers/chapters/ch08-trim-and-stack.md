# Chapter 10: Creating Your Grand Slam Offer, Part II — Trim & Stack

## Core Idea
"The meatiest section in the book." After listing solutions, decide *how* you'll actually deliver them (the delivery vehicles), then **trim** away low-value/high-cost items and **stack** the high-value items into one incomparable bundle. Goal: the prospect thinks, "I get all this, for only that?"

## Frameworks Introduced — Steps 4 & 5
- **Step #4: Create Your Solutions Delivery Vehicles ("The How")** — for each problem, brainstorm every possible way to solve it (divergent thinking). "This is the most important step." Group by how many people you serve at once (one-on-one, small group, one-to-many).
- **Step #5a: Trim** — list the cost-to-deliver of each vehicle. Remove **high-cost / low-value** first, then **low-cost / low-value**. Keep only **(1) low-cost, high-value** and **(2) high-cost, high-value**.
- **Step #5b: Stack** — bundle the survivors into "the ultimate high value deliverable," each piece named with a benefit and assigned a dollar value.

## The Delivery Cube — Product Delivery "Cheat Codes"
Vary any solution along these dimensions to find high-value/low-cost versions:
1. **Personal attention**: one-on-one, small group, one-to-many.
2. **Effort expected from them**: DIY (do it themselves), DWY (done *with* you), DFY (done *for* them).
3. **Live medium**: in-person, phone, email, text, Zoom, chat support.
4. **Recording consumption**: audio, video, or written.
5. **Speed/responsiveness**: which days/hours? 24/7, 9–5, within 5 min, within an hour, within 24 hrs?
6. **The 10×-to-1/10th test**: "If they paid me 10× (or $100,000), what would I provide? If they paid 1/10th and I had to make it *more* valuable, how?" Stretching both directions yields wildly different solutions.

## Key Concepts
- **Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum** — a trade-off between ease of sales and ease of fulfillment. Doing more makes it easy to sell but hard to fulfill; doing less, the reverse. The goal is the **sweet spot**: easy to sell *and* easy to fulfill.
- **"Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction."** — generate demand first; get them to say yes; only *then* offer less for the same price or add friction.
- **Favor high-value "one-to-many" solutions** — biggest discrepancy between cost and value (high one-time creation cost, near-zero marginal cost — "exactly why software becomes so valuable"). His macro-calculating Excel meal-plan tool: ~100 hrs once, then sold personalized plans that took ~15 min each.
- **Solve every perceived problem** — the eating-out-guide anecdote: one unsolved objection ("but what about eating out?") repeatedly killed sales until he built a solution for it.

## Worked Example: The Stacked Weight-Loss Offer
Each problem-bundle is named with a benefit and a stated value:
- **Foolproof Bargain Grocery System** — ($1,000 value)
- **Busy Parent "Ready-in-5-min" Cooking Guide** — ($600 value)
- **Personalized "Lick Your Fingers Good" Meal Plan** — ($500 value)
- **Fat-Burning Workouts** — ($699 value)
- **Ultimate Tone-Up-While-You-Travel Blueprint** — ($199 value)
- **"Never Fall Off" Accountability System** — ($1,000 value)
- **"Live It Up While Slimming Down" Eating-Out System** — ($349 value)
**Total value: $4,351 — all for only $599.** The bundle (1) solves *all* perceived problems, (2) gives conviction it's one-of-a-kind, (3) makes it impossible to compare to the gym down the street.

## Mental Models
- *Over-deliver like crazy at first* (especially your first GSO); use the cash flow to later make fulfillment efficient — value to the customer can stay the same while your cost drops.
- *Create the asset once* — high up-front, infinitely low marginal effort; these become the bedrock of a scalable business.

## Anti-patterns
- Optimizing/adding friction before you have demand flowing ("you have no idea whether what you have is good").
- Getting "romantic" about how you solve a problem — refusing to solve the customer's actual objection and losing the sale.

## Key Takeaways
1. Brainstorm every delivery vehicle, then trim to high-value (low- and high-cost) only.
2. Use the Delivery Cube cheat codes to find high-value/low-cost versions.
3. Stack the survivors into one named, value-anchored, incomparable bundle.
