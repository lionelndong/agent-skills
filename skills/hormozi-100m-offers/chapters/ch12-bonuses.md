# Chapter 14: Enhancing the Offer — Bonuses

## Core Idea
"A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into its component parts and stacked as bonuses." Bonuses widen the **price-to-value discrepancy** by *adding value* (not cutting price) — you anchor the price to the core offer, then expand value with each bonus "until it's too big to bear."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Add bonuses instead of discounting** — never discount the core offer (it teaches customers your prices are negotiable). Adding value to close "puts you in a position of strength and goodwill rather than weakness."
- **Presenting bonuses, 1-on-1 vs. group**:
  - **1-on-1**: ask for the sale *first*; if yes, reveal bonuses *after* signup (a "wow" that reinforces the decision). If they say no, present a bonus that matches their specific objection, then ask again — and again ("Fair enough?"). Reciprocity makes rejection hard.
- **Bonus Bullets — the rules for each bonus**:
  1. Always offer them.
  2. Give each a **special name with a benefit in the title**.
  3. Tell them: (a) how it relates to their issue, (b) what it is, (c) how you discovered it / what it took to create, (d) how it specifically improves their life — faster, easier, less effort/sacrifice (the Value Equation).
  4. Provide **proof** of its value (stat, past client, personal experience).
  5. Paint a **vivid mental image** of life already using it.
  6. Always **ascribe a price tag** and justify it.
  7. **Tools & checklists beat additional trainings** (lower effort/time → higher value).
  8. Each should **address a specific obstacle** in the prospect's mind (prove the limiting belief wrong).
  9. Solve their **next** problem before they hit it.
  10. The **value of the bonuses should eclipse the core offer** (this keeps expanding the price-to-value discrepancy and implies the core must be even more valuable).
  11. Enhance bonuses with their own **scarcity and urgency**.

## Key Concepts
- **Bonuses with Scarcity**: "Only people who sign up get Bonus #1, 2, 3 — never sold anywhere else." / "I have 3 tickets left to my $5,000 event; buy this and get one as a bonus."
- **Bonuses with Urgency**: "If you buy *today*, I'll add in this $1,000 bonus free — to reward action takers."
- **Other People's Products & Services** — get non-competing businesses to provide products/services as your bonuses in exchange for free exposure to your customers. You can even negotiate a **group discount + a referral commission to yourself** (each bonus becomes an indirect *and* direct revenue stream).

## Worked Example: The Pain-Clinic Bonus Stack
A $400 offer enhanced with partner bonuses:
- Massage therapist: 1–2 free massages
- Chiropractor: 2 free adjustments ($100)
- Low-inflammation food company discount ($50)
- Braces/orthotics discount ($150)
- Local health club: free PT session + free pool month ($100)
- Pharmacy discounts ($100/mo savings)
The free bonuses *alone* exceed the $400 price. Then negotiate affiliate commissions (chiro $100/referral, orthotics $100, health club $50, pharmacy $100…) and the $400 offer can generate an *extra ~$350 in pure profit* — the partners pay you, and you "don't have to do anything but refer customers you've already paid to acquire."

## Mental Models
- *"But wait… there's more"* — every infomercial stacks because it works; each added bonus snaps "the rubber band in their mind that is holding their wallet in their pocket."
- *Wow factor decides core vs. bonus* — pull out the distinct, stand-alone, high-value-but-short pieces (checklists, infographics) and spotlight them as bonuses.

## Anti-patterns
- Discounting the core offer to close (trains negotiation, signals weakness).
- Leaving deliverables un-enumerated — "until I enumerate them, they are unknown."

## Key Takeaways
1. Stack named, proven, price-tagged bonuses — each killing a specific objection.
2. Add value instead of cutting price; let bonus value eclipse the core offer.
3. Use partners' products as bonuses (and negotiate discounts + commissions).
