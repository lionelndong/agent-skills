# Chapter 3: Pricing — The Commodity Problem

## Core Idea
A commodity is "a product available from many places," so it is bought on **price** instead of **value** — a race to the bottom at the point of market efficiency. A Grand Slam Offer differentiates you so purchases become **value-driven**, in "a category of one."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Three Ways to Grow** (the only ones): (1) Get more customers, (2) Increase their average purchase value, (3) Get them to buy more times.
  - Author's simplification: there are really **two** ways — get more customers, and increase each customer's value (value = profit per purchase × number of purchases).
- **Commoditized vs. Differentiated**:
  - Commoditized = price-driven purchases (race to the bottom).
  - Differentiated = value-driven purchases (sell in a category of one, no comparison).
- **What a GSO multiplies** (the "fancy marketing terms"): (1) Increased Response Rates (clicks), (2) Increased Conversion (sales), (3) Premium Prices.

## Key Concepts
- **Gross Profit** — revenue minus the direct cost of servicing *one additional* customer (e.g., $1,000/mo service costing $100/mo labor = $900 / 90%). Not net profit.
- **Lifetime Value (LTV)** — gross profit accrued over a customer's lifetime = gross profit × number of purchases. (Hormozi uses gross-profit-based LTV; sometimes calls it **LTGP**, Lifetime Gross Profit.) Example: $1,000/mo × 90% × 5 months = **$4,500 LTV**.
- **Market efficiency** — competition drives commodity prices down until margins are "just enough to keep the lights on."

## Worked Example: The 22.4x Money Math
Same eyeballs, same ad spend — but a GSO replaces a commoditized agency offer:
- **Old (commoditized)**: "$1,000 down, then $1,000/mo retainer." At 0.5–1 ROAS you lose money acquiring customers and only break even later.
- **New (GSO)**: "Pay one time. No retainer. Just cover ad spend. I'll generate and work your leads. Only pay me if people show up. I guarantee 20 people in your first month or your next month free. Plus the entire playbook — daily sales coaching, tested scripts, tested price points, sales recordings — free for becoming a client."
- Result: ~2.5× more respond × 2.5× more close × 4× higher price = **22.4× more cash collected up front** ($10,000 spent → $112,000). "You just made money getting new customers."

## Mental Models
- *"Grow or Die" — maintenance is a myth.* The market grows ~9%/yr; standing still is falling behind.
- *Same work, different perceived product.* Fulfillment is identical; the GSO changes whether the buyer decides on value or price.

## Anti-patterns
- Being commoditized: prospect thinks "these are pretty much the same, I'll buy the cheaper one."
- Staying "competitive" on price — margins vanish, the business dies or the owner quits.

## Key Takeaways
1. Don't let buyers compare you — incomparability is the whole point.
2. There are only three (really two) levers to grow; a GSO pulls all of them.
3. The same fulfillment, sold as a GSO, can be many multiples more profitable.
