# Patterns — $100M Offers

## Pattern: Build a Grand Slam Offer (the full sequence)
**When to use**: Creating or rebuilding an offer that is being commoditized / competing on price.
**How**:
1. Pick the **market** first (Ch 4): score on Massive Pain, Purchasing Power, Easy to Target, Growing; stay in Health/Wealth/Relationships; niche down.
2. Commit to **premium pricing** (Ch 5): widen the price-to-value discrepancy; enter the virtuous cycle.
3. Identify the **Dream Outcome** (Ch 9).
4. List **every problem** between prospect and outcome, in sequence, in insane detail (Ch 9).
5. Turn each problem into a **solution** (Ch 9), then into **delivery vehicles** (Ch 10).
6. **Trim & Stack** (Ch 10): cut low-value items; bundle high-value ones; name and value-tag each.
7. **Enhance** (Ch 11–16): Scarcity → Urgency → Bonuses → Guarantees → Naming.
**Why it works**: The result is incomparable, so the decision becomes value-based, not price-based.

## Pattern: Apply the Value Equation
**When to use**: Making any offer more valuable.
**How** — work all four levers:
- ↑ **Dream Outcome** (bigger, vivid, status-framed, in their words).
- ↑ **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** (proof, track record, guarantees).
- ↓ **Time Delay** (faster outcome; engineer early fast wins).
- ↓ **Effort & Sacrifice** (do more for them; "done for you").
**Trade-offs**: The top is easy to claim (least unique); the durable advantage is winning the bottom two. Communicate every lever — perception is the product.

## Pattern: Choose the Market First (Starving Crowd)
**When to use**: Before perfecting an offer or copy.
**How**: Score candidates on the four indicators; confirm Health/Wealth/Relationships; niche down and **commit** ("don't make me niche slap you").
**Trade-offs**: Under ~$10M/yr, narrower usually earns more; broaden only when TAM caps you.

## Pattern: Charge a Premium (Virtuous Cycle)
**When to use**: Setting or raising price.
**How**: Raise price *only after* raising value (keep it "money at a discount"); price so it "stings a little." Never be the second-cheapest.
**Trade-offs**: Requires conviction and real delivery; premium funds the results that justify it.

## Pattern: Trim & Stack with the Delivery Cube
**When to use**: Deciding what goes into the offer.
**How**: Generate delivery vehicles via the cheat codes (attention level; DIY/DWY/DFY; medium; format; speed; 10×-to-1/10th test). Trim high-cost/low-value and low-value items; keep low-cost/high-value and high-cost/high-value. Favor high-value one-to-many assets.
**Trade-offs**: One-to-many assets cost a lot up front, near-zero after — the basis of scale.

## Pattern: Manufacture Scarcity (quantity)
**When to use**: After the core offer exists, to raise price and demand.
**How**: Cap real quantity — seats/slots, bonuses, or "never again." For services: Total Business Cap, Growth-Rate Cap, or Cohort Cap. Always sell out, and announce it.
**Trade-offs**: Must be honest; faking it kills credibility. Compounds in effectiveness over time.

## Pattern: Add Urgency (time)
**When to use**: To lower the action threshold and drive a decision now.
**How**: Use Cohort-Based Rolling, Rolling Seasonal, Pricing/Bonus-Based, or Exploding Opportunity. Make deadlines real.
**Trade-offs**: Most sales cluster in the final hours — turning business away nets more, not less.

## Pattern: Stack Bonuses (instead of discounting)
**When to use**: To widen the price-to-value discrepancy and close fence-sitters.
**How**: Present each bonus named, related to a specific objection, explained, proven, vividly imagined, and price-tagged. Let total bonus value eclipse the core. Add scarcity/urgency to bonuses. Use partners' products (negotiate discounts + referral commissions).
**Trade-offs**: 1-on-1 — ask for the sale first, reveal bonuses after yes; if no, add an objection-matching bonus and ask again.

## Pattern: Reverse Risk with a Guarantee
**When to use**: To attack the #1 objection (risk) and lift conversion.
**How**: Structure "If not X in Y, then Z." Pick a type — Unconditional (strongest, lower-ticket), Conditional ("better than money back," tie to success actions), Anti-Guarantee (all sales final + reason why), Implied (performance/revshare). Name it; hit it boldly; stack types.
**Trade-offs**: Do the math (stronger guarantee usually raises *net* sales). Guarantees can't fix a weak product; guarantee-only buyers can be poor customers.

## Pattern: Name with MAGIC
**When to use**: Naming an offer (or its bonuses), and refreshing it for evergreen leads.
**How**: Assemble 3–5 of Magnet, Avatar, Goal, Interval, Container. Keep it short/punchy; optional rhyme/alliteration; test names. When offers fatigue, vary top-down: creative → copy → headline → duration → enhancer → monetization.
**Trade-offs**: You're changing the wrapper, not the offer; once monetized, rarely change the core.
