# Glossary — $100M Offers

**Anti-Guarantee** — A guarantee that states "all sales are final," owned with a creative "reason why" / damaging admission; attracts serious buyers and suits exclusive or high-cost offers. (Ch 15)

**Avatar** — The specific person an offer is for (and who it is not for); the "A" in MAGIC. (Ch 4, Ch 16)

**Bonuses** — Component deliverables (or partner products) stacked onto the core offer, each named, proven, and price-tagged, to widen the price-to-value discrepancy without discounting. (Ch 14)

**Category of One** — A differentiated position where your offer can't be compared to alternatives, so price is set by perceived value, not comparison ("sell in a vacuum"). (Ch 3, Ch 5)

**Commodity** — "A product available from many places," prone to price-driven purchases and a race to the bottom. (Ch 3)

**Conditional Guarantee** — A guarantee with terms/conditions, ideally "better than money back," often tied to the actions required for success. (Ch 15)

**Container Word** — The MAGIC word signaling the offer is a bundle/system (Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Intensive, Accelerator…). (Ch 16)

**Convergent Thinking** — Many known variables, fixed conditions, one right answer (math-style). (Ch 8)

**Delicate Dance of Desire** — Managing supply and demand so satisfaction stays under generated demand, keeping desire ravenous. (Ch 11)

**Delivery Cube / Product Delivery Cheat Codes** — Dimensions for varying how a solution is delivered: personal attention (1-on-1 / small group / one-to-many), effort (DIY/DWY/DFY), live medium, recording format, speed, and the 10×-to-1/10th test. (Ch 10)

**Divergent Thinking** — Many variables (known & unknown), dynamic conditions, multiple right answers; the mode for building offers (the Brick Exercise). (Ch 8)

**Dream Outcome** — "The gap between their current reality and their dreams"; the result the prospect most wants. First numerator of the Value Equation (increase). Most valued when it raises status. (Ch 6)

**Effort & Sacrifice** — The ancillary costs (tangible/intangible) to get the outcome; a denominator of the Value Equation (decrease). (Ch 6)

**Enhancers (The Five)** — Scarcity, Urgency, Bonuses, Guarantees, Naming — levers that raise desire/price without changing the core offer. (Ch 11–16)

**Fast Beats Free** — "The only thing that beats 'free' is 'fast.'" People pay for speed. (Ch 6)

**Grand Slam Offer (GSO)** — An incomparable offer combining an attractive promotion, an unmatchable value proposition, a premium price, and an unbeatable guarantee with a money model — "an offer so good people feel stupid saying no." (Ch 1–2)

**Gross Profit** — Revenue minus the direct cost of servicing one additional customer (not net profit). (Ch 3)

**Honest Scarcity** — Stating your real capacity ("81% to capacity"); the most ethical scarcity, with built-in social proof. (Ch 12)

**Hormozi's Law** — "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make." (Ch 11)

**Implied Guarantee** — A performance-based structure (revshare, profit-share, triggers, ratchets): "if I don't perform, I don't get paid." (Ch 15)

**Interval** — The MAGIC time-frame component (4-Hour, 21-Day, 6-Week). (Ch 16)

**Lifetime Value (LTV / LTGP)** — Gross profit accrued over a customer's lifetime = gross profit × number of purchases (Hormozi uses gross-profit-based LTV; "Lifetime Gross Profit"). (Ch 3)

**Magnet (Reason Why)** — The MAGIC attention component / reason for the promotion (Free, 88% off, Grand Opening, Spring). (Ch 16)

**MAGIC Formula** — Offer-naming formula: Magnet, Avatar, Goal, Interval, Container (use 3–5). (Ch 16)

**Market > Offer > Persuasion** — The order of importance of the three levers on success; the market (starving crowd) is the highest. (Ch 4)

**Niche Slap** — Hormozi's term for the mistake of hopping niche-to-niche instead of committing. (Ch 4)

**Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** — The prospect's belief they will get the result ("people pay for certainty"); a numerator of the Value Equation (increase). Raised by proof and guarantees. (Ch 6)

**Perception Is Reality** — Value exists only once the prospect perceives the increased likelihood and decreased time/effort (the London tube dotted map). (Ch 6)

**Price-to-Value Discrepancy** — The gap between perceived value and price; widen it by raising value (and price), never by discounting. (Ch 5, Ch 14)

**Scarcity** — Limited *quantity* that triggers fear of missing out; fear of loss > desire for gain. Three types: limited seats/slots, limited bonuses, never available again. (Ch 12)

**Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum** — The trade-off between ease of sales and ease of fulfillment; aim for the sweet spot of both. (Ch 10)

**Starving Crowd** — A large, desperate market; the most important success factor. "Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction." (Ch 4)

**Three Markets** — Health, Wealth, Relationships — enduring desires every niche sits within. (Ch 4)

**Three Ways to Grow** — Get more customers, increase average purchase value, get them to buy more times (really two: more customers, and more value per customer). (Ch 3)

**Time Delay** — Time between purchase and receiving the outcome; a denominator of the Value Equation (decrease). Includes long-term outcome and short-term experience (fast wins). (Ch 6)

**Trim & Stack** — Cut low-value (high- and low-cost) deliverables, then stack the high-value survivors into one incomparable bundle. (Ch 10)

**Unconditional Guarantee** — The strongest guarantee: no-questions-asked / satisfaction-based refund. (Ch 15)

**Urgency** — Limited *time* (a deadline) that lowers the action threshold. Four types: Cohort-Based Rolling, Rolling Seasonal, Pricing/Bonus-Based, Exploding Opportunity. (Ch 13)

**Value Equation** — Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). (Ch 6)

**Virtuous Cycle of Price** — Raising prices → more emotional investment, perceived value, results, better clients, and multiplied margin. Opposite of the discounting **Vicious Cycle**. (Ch 5)
