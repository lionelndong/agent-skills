# Cheatsheet — $100M Playbook: Proof Checklist

Decision-ready. The rule is always: **push every piece of proof toward the LEFT side of each `>`.**

## The One-Page Proof Checklist (all 13 rules)
| # | Prefer | Over | Tell / why |
|---|--------|------|-----------|
| 1 | In-Person | Virtual | Closest to the result happening in front of them |
| 2 | Live | Recorded | A live customer is "living proof" |
| 3 | Raw | Processed | Edited = looks like an ad; raw = believable |
| 4 | Show | Tell | Visual before/after beats description (unboxing effect) |
| 5 | Other people praise you | You praise you | You're the most biased person alive |
| 6 | Identical to them | Opposite of them | "This is me" — match the avatar/demographics |
| 7 | Personal | Generic | Specific, vivid pain/benefit makes them identify |
| 8 | Big results | Small > none | Lead with the biggest; small still beats zero |
| 9 | Newer | Older | Stale-only proof signals no recent wins |
| 10 | More | Less | Conquer with volume (page / reel / wall) |
| 11 | Third-party verified | Zero verification | "Don't believe me, believe them" |
| 12 | With numbers | Without numbers | Plain numbers (+ how & when) are hard to argue |
| 13 | Metaphors | Technical jargon | Connect the unknown to what they already know |

## Core decision rules
- **If you're tempted to improve your *claim*, stop** → improve your *proof* instead. "Your promise is not a differentiator. Your proof is."
- **If you're about to make a claim** → collect the data first, then claim only what it supports ("claim your proof"). Always state *how* and *when* you collected it.
- **If you can deliver the result before purchase** (live demo / free work) → do it; that's the most compelling proof on the Belief Continuum.
- **If you have a big result** → spend money/time to capture it in as many formats as possible; it pays dividends for years.
- **If a breakthrough moment is coming** (weigh-in, acceptance letter, score) → pre-build the capture; "proof is an animal in the wild — capture it."
- **If your data sucks** → fix the product until it's good; don't hide the method.
- **If your platform is low-trust** → import proof from a high-trust source (Amazon reviews onto a non-Amazon page; BBB / NSF / awards).
- **If a customer won't document/leave a review** → add an incentive; bake the ask into the customer journey.
- **If a mechanism is too complex to explain** → give 2–3 metaphors matched to the audience; teach them to sales staff.

## Highest-leverage first (when time/budget is limited)
1. Big Results (capture your biggest) → 2. Show + In-Person/Live → 3. Other-People + Personal (specific) → 4. Numbers (with method) → 5. More (volume) → 6. Third-Party + Newer + Metaphors.

## Tells & smells (you're doing proof wrong)
- Your ad talks about *you / your services / your "unique mechanism"* — not the customer's result.
- Your best asset is educational, offer-centric, or founder-center-stage (the losing pattern).
- Testimonials only *describe* results (no show), are all old, or are all from one platform.
- You make claims you can't back, instead of claiming what data already proves.

## Proof works everywhere (funnel)
Click the ad → opt in → buy (live or recorded) → reassure post-purchase so they keep going. You can **never have too much proof**.
