# Glossary — $100M Playbook: Proof Checklist

All terms scoped to this one playbook. Format: **Term** — definition (Ch).

- **Belief Continuum** — the 0%–100% scale of how much a prospect believes your thing will work for them; proof moves them along it (Ch 2).
- **Big Results > Small Results > No Results** — checklist rule: lead with your biggest result; small beats none, but a big one beats both (Ch 3).
- **Bucketing (theme buckets)** — sorting top-performing assets by theme so you get a repeatable framework, not a one-off list (Ch 1).
- **Capture (proof capture)** — proactively recording proof the moment it occurs ("an animal in the wild"); built into the customer journey (Ch 3, Ch 4).
- **Claim your proof** — collect data first, then make only the claims the data supports — the reverse of trying to prove a claim (Ch 2).
- **Compelling proof** — proof that moves the prospect closer to absolute certainty; strength scales with how close it gets them (Ch 2).
- **Floor-to-ceiling proof / wall of results** — saturating a space (in-person room or web page) with proof; e.g., printing every 5-star review and framing it lobby-wide (Ch 3).
- **Get the result before they buy** — the most persuasive proof: the prospect experiences the maximum achievable result pre-purchase (live demo, free work) (Ch 2).
- **Identical to Them > Opposite of Them** — checklist rule: show proof from customers who match the prospect's avatar/demographics (Ch 3).
- **In-Person > Virtual** — checklist rule: make proof in-person whenever possible (Ch 3).
- **Live > Recorded** — checklist rule: use live testimonials over recorded ones; customers on stage are "living proof" (Ch 3).
- **Living proof** — a live customer telling their transformation in person (Ch 3).
- **Match Your Claims To Your Proof** — the pro-tip / principle behind "claim your proof" (Ch 2).
- **Metaphors > Technical Jargon** — checklist rule: explain complex/unique mechanisms via metaphors matched to what the audience already knows (Ch 3).
- **More Proof > Less Proof** — checklist rule: conquer with volume — proof pages, testimonial reels, walls of results (Ch 3).
- **Newer Proof > Older Proof** — checklist rule: keep proof recent; stale-only proof signals no recent success (Ch 3).
- **Other People Saying Your Stuff's Great > You Saying Your Stuff's Great** — checklist rule: third-party voices beat self-praise; you're the most biased person in existence (Ch 3).
- **Overwhelming proof** — accumulating so much evidence that belief is inevitable regardless of market position; the thing that can grow any business (Ch 1).
- **Personal > Generic** — checklist rule: specific, vivid pain/benefit makes the prospect think "this is me" (Ch 3).
- **Prescription / probing questions** — interview technique to draw out specific before/after detail ("why was this so important to you?") (Ch 3).
- **Proof** — evidence that your product works for someone like the prospect; the one differentiator competitors can't copy (Ch 1).
- **Proof With Numbers > Proof Without Numbers** — checklist rule: plainly stated, understandable numbers (with how + when collected) are hard to argue with (Ch 3).
- **Promise vs. proof** — "Your promise is not a differentiator. Your proof is." Claims get copied; results don't (Ch 1).
- **Raw > Processed** — checklist rule: unedited proof beats polished, edited proof (Ch 3).
- **Show > Tell** — checklist rule: visually showing the result (before/after, unboxing) beats describing it (Ch 3).
- **Testimonial prompting** — building reminders/incentives to collect testimonials into the customer journey (Ch 3).
- **Third Party Verification > Zero Verification** — checklist rule: accreditations, awards, certifications, and trusted-brand endorsements amplify existing proof (Ch 3).
- **Top 50 ads** — Hormozi's audit method: rank best-performing assets, then bucket by theme to find the winning pattern (Ch 1).
- **Unique mechanism (anti-pattern framing)** — your proprietary process/tech; talking about it (instead of the customer's result) is what low-performing ads do (Ch 1, Ch 3).
