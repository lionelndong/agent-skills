## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The thesis: proof is the differentiator
**"Everyone can make the same promise, but no one can have the same proof."** Anyone can copy an advertising claim; no one can produce the exact same results to prove it. So **"Your promise is not a differentiator. Your proof is."** Proof is the one thing that grows any business — and for an up-and-comer, it's your biggest weapon. (When Hormozi audited his top vs. bottom ads, the only difference was proof: the 80% winners had no educational content, weren't offer-focused, didn't put him center-stage, and told one story — *This. Product. Works.*)

### The Belief Continuum
Belief isn't all-or-nothing — **"it's how much do they believe."** Prospects sit somewhere on a 0%–100% certainty scale; proof's job is to move them toward 100%. The closer your proof gets them to certainty, the more compelling it is. The strongest form is **getting the result before they buy** (live demonstration, or working for free to the extent you can afford it) — "it's tough to say something works for you after it already has."

### Match Your Claims To Your Proof ("claim your proof")
Most marketers find proof to fit a claim — backwards. **Collect your data first, then make only the claims the data already supports.** State *how* and *when* you collected it; hold nothing back. "Instead of trying to prove your claim — claim your proof."

### The Proof Checklist — 13 comparison rules
Some proof works better than other proof. Push every piece toward the **left** side of each rule:
1. **In-Person > Virtual** — proof in-person whenever possible.
2. **Live > Recorded** — live testimonials are "living proof."
3. **Raw > Processed** — unedited beats polished.
4. **Show > Tell** — visual before/after beats describing it (unboxing effect).
5. **Other People Saying Your Stuff's Great > You Saying Your Stuff's Great** — you're the most biased person alive.
6. **Identical to Them > Opposite of Them** — match the prospect's avatar/demographics → "this is me."
7. **Personal > Generic** — specific, vivid pain/benefit makes them identify.
8. **Big Results > Small Results > No Results** — lead with your biggest; small still beats none.
9. **Newer Proof > Older Proof** — stale-only proof signals no recent wins.
10. **More Proof > Less Proof** — conquer with volume (proof page, testimonial reel, wall of results).
11. **Third Party Verification > Zero Verification** — accreditations, awards, certifications: "don't believe me, believe them."
12. **Proof With Numbers > Proof Without Numbers** — plain, understandable numbers (with method + date) are hard to argue with; visualize with charts.
13. **Metaphors > Technical Jargon** — connect the unknown to what the audience already knows (2–3 metaphors per mechanism).

### Capture it — proof is fleeting
**"Proof is like an animal in the wild. It may only be there for a moment. You have to capture it."** Be proactive; build capture into the customer journey (record breakthrough moments before they happen, prompt testimonials at milestones, incentivize documentation). Proof works at every funnel step — click, opt-in, the buy (live or recorded), and post-purchase reassurance — and **you can never have too much of it.**
