# Glossary — $100M Pricing Playbook

**Annual Billing** — billing the full year (or quarter) up front; less frequent billing → lower churn → higher LTV (1x/yr ≈ 2% churn ≈ 5.35x monthly LTV) (Ch 4, Play #5).

**Annual CPI Increase** — a fixed annual price increase (5–15%) built into contracts so prices keep pace with inflation; +3–10% (Ch 4, Play #4).

**Annual Renewal Fee** — a once-a-year fee (1–3x the monthly rate) charged on top of an advertised low monthly; lifts effective monthly rate ~+10–20% (Ch 4, Play #7).

**Anchor / Price Anchor** — a much higher-priced option presented first that makes the core offer look affordable; see Ultra High Ticket Anchor (Ch 4, Play #9).

**Automatic Continuity (Continued Access)** — a paired-down, high-margin version of your offer (priced 5–20% of the main thing) bolted onto every front-end purchase that recurs automatically, agreed up front (Ch 4, Play #8).

**CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)** — what it costs to win one customer; doubling customers for the same spend halves CAC (Ch 1).

**Churn** — % of customers lost per period; rises when price rises (non-luxury) and falls with longer billing cycles (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 4).

**Competitor Based Pricing** — pricing at the average of what everyone else charges; "Be Cinderella, not the stepsisters" (Ch 2).

**Cost Plus Pricing** — costs plus an arbitrary margin; "your costs don't matter to [customers]" (Ch 2).

**Customer Surplus (goodwill)** — the gap between value delivered and price charged; drives word-of-mouth and repurchase (Ch 2).

**Effective Monthly Rate** — true monthly cost once annual fees/renewals are spread over 12 months (e.g. $39/mo + $99/yr = $47/mo effective) (Ch 4, Play #7).

**Genie / Three Options** — the book's framing device: double customers, purchases, or price (price wins at 6x profit) (Ch 1).

**Gross Margin** — (price − delivery cost) ÷ price; rises mechanically when price rises (Ch 1).

**Guarantee / Warranty Upsell** — a guarantee sold for 5–30% of price (often = cost of goods); "buying and selling risk"; near-pure profit (Ch 4, Play #10).

**Instant Profit Pricing Playbook** — the master list of 10 low-effort plays totaling a 26.8%–63.8% price increase (Ch 3, Ch 4).

**Involuntary Churn** — 1.2–1.7% monthly loss from card declines/changes; recovered with a second form of payment (Ch 4, Play #2).

**Lookback Window of Value** — Hormozi's theory that customers judge value within their last billing cycle, not the whole relationship → longer cycles reduce churn (Ch 2).

**LTV / Lifetime Revenue** — total a customer pays over their life (price × duration); the number pricing should maximize (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Luxury vs Premium** — premium price *reflects* product value; luxury value comes *from* the high/round price. Don't add .99 to luxury (Ch 4, Play #6).

**Net Margin** — profit ÷ revenue; explodes when price rises and costs hold flat (Ch 1).

**NPS Score** — objective measure of whether price went too high ("value for the cost") (Ch 2).

**ProfitWell Study** — 512-company dataset: +1% pricing ≈ 2x retention ≈ 4x acquisition in profit impact; frequent price-testers grow faster (Ch 1).

**Processing Fee** — a 3.99% card fee added after price agreement; waivable in exchange for a second form of payment (Ch 4, Play #2).

**Round Up** — change prices ending in 7 → 9 and add .99; +1–3% (real-world 4–11%), no conversion drop (except luxury) (Ch 4, Play #6).

**Sales Tax (pass-through)** — adding tax you pay as a separate invoice line item rather than absorbing it; "You cannot charge taxes you do not pay" (Ch 4, Play #3).

**Sunk Cost Fallacy** — the lever behind automatic continuity: customers keep paying a small fee to not "lose" what they already bought (Ch 4, Play #8).

**Three Big Pricing Models** — Cost Plus, Competitor Based, and Value Based pricing (Ch 2).

**Three Metrics (value-driven pricing)** — conversion rate and churn at the current price; used to find the price with the highest Total Return (Ch 2).

**Total Return** — lifetime gross profit at a given price point; the column you optimize in the pricing metrics table (not sales count) (Ch 2).

**Ultra High Ticket Anchor** — a 10x+ "mac daddy" option that anchors price and occasionally sells; +10–15% LTV (Ch 4, Play #9).

**Value Based Pricing** — pricing on the customer's willingness to pay (WTP); enables charging 2–5x+ market and raising prices as value grows (Ch 2).

**Willingness To Pay (WTP)** — what a customer will actually pay; the basis of value-based pricing; can vary 5–10x across avatars (Ch 2).

**28-Day Billing Cycle** — billing every 4 weeks for 13 cycles/year instead of 12; instant +8.3% (Ch 4, Play #1).
