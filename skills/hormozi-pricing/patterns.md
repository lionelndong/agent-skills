# Patterns — $100M Pricing Playbook

## Pattern: Pick the Highest-Profit Lever (the Genie)
**When to use**: Deciding where to focus to grow profit.
**How**: Compare doubling # customers (3.5x profit) vs # purchases (3.5x) vs **price (6x)**. Price wins because it raises revenue while holding CAC and delivery cost flat. Focus on pricing first — lowest effort, highest return.
**Trade-offs**: Higher price means fewer new customers and more "no"s; net profit still rises 9/10 times.

## Pattern: Value-Based Pricing
**When to use**: You can keep adding value over time and have the operational chops to deliver.
**How**: Price on customer willingness-to-pay (WTP), not cost or competitors. Raise prices continuously as you raise value; talk to customers to learn what to build.
**Trade-offs**: More (and different) work; optimizes value-per-customer over raw customer count.

## Pattern: The Pricing Metrics Table
**When to use**: Choosing the optimal price point for an offer.
**How**: Tabulate Price | Conv Rate | Sales | Churn | LTV | **Total Return**; raise price and watch the columns. Pick the row with the highest Total Return (lifetime gross profit), not the most sales.
**Trade-offs**: Requires testing; non-luxury demand falls as price rises, so the peak is interior.

## Pattern: Raise the Price (the rules)
**When to use**: Close rate consistently >50%, at/over capacity, or fixed capacity sold out.
**How**: Raise price until extra revenue from new sales no longer offsets lost sales ("hear more nos, make more money"). Usually raise value to match. Don't revert at the first "no."
**Trade-offs**: You'll hear ~40% more "no"s; tolerable because profit rises. Too far = sales stop or NPS drops.

## Pattern: Tier by Avatar
**When to use**: You have 2–3 customer types with different WTP (often 5–10x apart).
**How**: Offer multiple prices/service levels; name each tier after an aspirational title the customer wants; make sure each knows which is theirs.
**Trade-offs**: Advanced — needs operations to deliver at different levels.

## Pattern: Monthly → 28-Day Billing (Play #1)
**When to use**: Any recurring subscription. **+8.3%**.
**How**: Bill every 4 weeks (13 cycles/yr, not 12). Display price weekly (low perceived price), bill every 4 weeks (or 12-week+).
**Trade-offs**: Weekly/bi-weekly creates pause/billing hassle — stick to 4-week or longer.

## Pattern: Processing Fee + Second Form of Payment (Play #2)
**When to use**: Card-based recurring billing. **+3–4% and/or big LTV gain**.
**How**: After price agreement, add a 3.99% card fee; waive it for a backup card. Re-run failed charges next day on the backup.
**Trade-offs**: The fee gain ≈ cancels if waived — but you capture a backup card that recovers 1.2–1.7% involuntary churn (worth far more).

## Pattern: Pass-Through Sales Tax (Play #3)
**When to use**: You actually pay sales tax on your offering. **+0–10%**.
**How**: Agree on price, then add tax as a separate invoice line before the total, stated dryly via tax code. If they balk, "get in the angry boat" with them.
**Trade-offs**: Legally cannot charge tax you don't pay; alternative is reincorporating in a no-tax state.

## Pattern: Annual CPI Increase (Play #4)
**When to use**: Long-term contracts you want inflation-protected. **+3–10%**.
**How**: Build a fixed 5–15% annual increase into contracts; raise it at paperwork (not during the sale) with a CPI/quality "reason why."
**Trade-offs**: Few balk under 15%; over 15% risks pushback.

## Pattern: Annual Billing (Play #5)
**When to use**: Subscriptions where you can deliver value over a long term. **5x LTV potential**.
**How**: Always offer the highest (annual) price first; downsell to quarterly, then monthly, with prepay discounts (~16–17% annual, ~8% quarterly). Or sell 6–12 weeks first, then upsell to annual prepay.
**Trade-offs**: Lowers conversion (price is 12x monthly); test that it doesn't drop conversion more than 5x. Zero downside — revert anytime.

## Pattern: Round Up (Play #6)
**When to use**: High transaction volume, non-luxury. **+1–3% (often 4–11%)**.
**How**: Change prices ending in 7 → 9; add .99. No conversion change.
**Trade-offs**: NOT for luxury (ends in 0/5). Test — sometimes shorter numbers win.

## Pattern: Annual Renewal Fee On Top of Monthly (Play #7)
**When to use**: Price-conscious markets where customers fixate on the monthly. **+~10%**.
**How**: Advertise a low monthly; add a renewal fee of 1–3x the monthly on the contract anniversary. Reason-why = "rate protection." Have them initial both. Optionally pair with a setup fee so you can waive one for the other.
**Trade-offs**: If they balk, drop it (they lose the benefit) — no lost sale.

## Pattern: Automatic Continuity (Play #8)
**When to use**: Almost any business, including one-time-transaction ones. **+~10%, +32% LTV in the worked example**.
**How**: Build a high-margin, low-work feature priced 5–20% of the main offer; bolt onto every front-end purchase as the default, earned after X time; remarket to the pool.
**Trade-offs**: Must be disclosed and agreed up front — never undisclosed/forced.

## Pattern: Ultra High Ticket Anchor (Play #9)
**When to use**: You want the core offer to look affordable and to occasionally sell a premium. **+10–15% LTV**.
**How**: Add a 10x+ "mac daddy" option at a price you'd happily deliver for; present it first to "get the gasp," then bring out the main offer.
**Trade-offs**: Must genuinely be willing to fulfill it; if it stresses you, raise the price until it makes you smile.

## Pattern: Guarantee / Warranty Upsell (Play #10)
**When to use**: Any price point; especially main-street/traditional businesses. **+5–20%**.
**How**: After purchase, offer a guarantee/warranty for 5–30% of price (often = COGS, so you never lose). Re-deliver rather than refund; check at claim time whether they bought it.
**Trade-offs**: You're underwriting risk — guarantee revenue must exceed cost-to-fix; know your refund/claim rate.
