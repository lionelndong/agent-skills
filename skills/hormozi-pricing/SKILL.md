## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The Genie: price is the 6x lever
A business genie doubles ONE thing: # customers, # purchases, or price. All look like "2x revenue," but on **profit**: **2x customers = 3.5x**, **2x purchases = 3.5x**, **2x price = 6x**. Price wins because it raises revenue while **holding CAC and delivery cost flat**. Backed by ProfitWell's 512-company study: **+1% pricing ≈ 2x retention ≈ 4x acquisition** in profit impact; frequent price-testers grow faster. Focus on pricing first — lowest effort, highest return. *"Profit is unnatural. You must force it into existence."*

### The Three Big Pricing Models → use Value-Based
1. **Cost Plus** — costs + arbitrary margin. *"Your costs don't matter to [customers]."*
2. **Competitor Based** — the average everyone charges. *"Be Cinderella, not the stepsisters."*
3. **Value Based (WTP)** ← the goal. Price on **willingness to pay**, not competitors' willingness to charge. Charge 2–5x+ market and **raise prices continuously as you add value.**

### Finding the price (3 metrics)
To adjust a price, look at **conversion rate** and **churn** at the current price. Build a table (`Price · Conv · Sales · Churn · LTV · Total Return`) and **pick the row with the highest Total Return** (lifetime gross profit) — **"the perfect price is the one that makes you the most money, not gets you the most customers."**

### Rules of Pricing (the foundation)
- **Raising price makes money two ways**: more gross profit/customer AND lower delivery cost (fewer customers). *Gym: $99→$299, lost 30% (200→140); revenue >doubled, costs −30%.*
- **High close rate (>50%) = price too low. Full/fixed capacity = price too low.**
- **Keep raising until extra revenue from new sales no longer offsets lost sales.** "Hear more nos, make more money" — don't cave at the first no. **Too far** = sales stop / NPS drops.
- **Raising price usually means raising value.** **Believe in your price** (or make it better).
- **Multiple avatars → multiple prices** (WTP can vary 5–10x); name tiers aspirationally.
- **Bill on the longest cycle (less churn), display on the smallest increment.** Separate one-time value (access fee) from on-going value (recurring fee).

### Small Percentages, Big Changes
A small % added to revenue carries **no extra cost**, so it drops to the bottom line. On a **10%-margin** business, **+10% price ≈ double profit**; a 4% revenue bump on 9% margins ≈ **+44% profit**. *(Restaurant's 4% processing fee: $90k → $130k profit.)*

### The 10 Instant-Profit Pricing Plays → total **26.8%–63.8%** (you only need one)
| # | Play | Lift | Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Monthly → 28-Day Cycles** | 8.3% | Bill every 4 wks (13 cycles/yr); display weekly, bill 4-weekly |
| 2 | **Processing Fee + 2nd Form of Payment** | 3–4% | "+3.99% card fee"; waive for a backup card (recovers involuntary churn) |
| 3 | **Sales Tax** | 0–10% | Pass tax through as a line item; can't charge tax you don't pay |
| 4 | **Annual CPI Increase** | 3–10% | Bake 5–15%/yr into contracts; raise at paperwork |
| 5 | **Annual Billing** | →5x LTV | Offer annual first; prepay discount ~16–17%; less billing = less churn |
| 6 | **Round Up** | 1–3% | 7→9 and add .99 (NOT luxury) |
| 7 | **Annual Renewal Fee On Top of Monthly** | ~10% | Low monthly + 1–3x-monthly fee yearly ("rate protection") |
| 8 | **Automatic Continuity** | ~10% | High-margin feature at 5–20% of price, auto-recurs, agreed up front |
| 9 | **Ultra High Ticket Anchor** | 10–15% | Add a 10x+ "mac daddy"; show it first for "the gasp" |
| 10 | **Guarantee/Warranty Upsell** | 5–20% | Post-sale, sell guarantee for 5–30% of price (often = COGS) |
