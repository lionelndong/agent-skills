# Cheatsheet — $100M Pricing Playbook

## The one truth
**Doubling price = 6x profit** (vs 3.5x for 2x customers or 2x purchases), because price raises revenue while costs stay flat. **+1% pricing ≈ 2x retention ≈ 4x acquisition** in profit impact (512-company ProfitWell data). *"Profit is unnatural. You must force it into existence."*

## Tells your price is TOO LOW (raise it)
| Tell | Action |
|---|---|
| Close rate consistently **>50%** | Raise price |
| **Full capacity** / sold out | Raise price |
| **Fixed capacity** + selling out (e.g. 10 paintings/mo) | Raise price |
| Haven't changed price in years | You've already lowered it (inflation) — raise |

## Tells your price is TOO HIGH (back off)
- Sales **stop**, or customers complain about **value-for-cost** (measure with **NPS**).

## Pricing model: pick value-based
| Model | Verdict |
|---|---|
| Cost Plus | ❌ "Your costs don't matter to customers" |
| Competitor Based | ❌ "Be Cinderella, not the stepsisters" |
| **Value Based (WTP)** | ✅ charge 2–5x+, raise as you add value |

## How to set the price
Build the table → pick the row with the highest **Total Return** (lifetime gross profit), NOT the most sales. Keep raising until extra revenue from new sales no longer offsets lost sales. **Hear more nos, make more money** — don't cave at the first "no."

## The 10 Instant-Profit Plays → total **26.8%–63.8%**
| # | Play | Lift | One-line move |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Month → 28-Day Cycles | 8.3% | Bill every 4 wks = 13 cycles/yr; display weekly, bill 4-weekly |
| 2 | Processing Fee + 2nd Card | 3–4% | "+3.99% card fee" — waive for a backup card (recovers involuntary churn) |
| 3 | Sales Tax | 0–10% | Pass tax through as a separate line; can't charge tax you don't pay |
| 4 | Annual CPI Increase | 3–10% | Bake 5–15%/yr into contracts; raise at paperwork, not the sale |
| 5 | Annual Billing | →5x LTV | Offer annual first; prepay discount ~16–17%; less billing = less churn |
| 6 | Round Up | 1–3% | 7→9 and add .99 (NOT luxury) |
| 7 | Annual Renewal Fee | ~10% | Low monthly + 1–3x-monthly fee yearly ("rate protection") |
| 8 | Automatic Continuity | ~10% | High-margin feature at 5–20% of price, auto-recurs, agreed up front |
| 9 | Ultra High Ticket Anchor | 10–15% | Add a 10x+ "mac daddy"; show it first for "the gasp" |
| 10 | Guarantee/Warranty Upsell | 5–20% | Post-sale, sell guarantee for 5–30% of price (often = COGS) |

## Key thresholds & defaults
- **Billing cycle**: every **4 weeks** (or 12+); never weekly/bi-weekly (pause hassles).
- **Processing fee**: **3.99%**; involuntary churn from cards = **1.2–1.7%/mo**.
- **CPI increase**: **5–15%/yr** (few balk under 15%).
- **Annual prepay discount**: **16–17%**; quarterly **~8%**. Selection: on-page 10–15%, default 30%, phone 35–40%.
- **Renewal fee**: **1–3x monthly** (1x = +8.3%, 3x = +24.9%).
- **Continuity price**: **5–20%** of main offer (worked example: 10% → +32% LTV).
- **Anchor**: **10x+** core price.
- **Guarantee**: **5–30%** of price; set = **cost of goods** to never lose; revenue must exceed cost-to-fix.
- **Round Up**: skip for luxury (ends in 0 or 5).

## Anchoring rule (Plays #5, #9)
**Always say the highest price first** — "the first number out of your mouth anchors the entire conversation." Then downsell discounts as *benefits*, not the high price as a *penalty*.

## Display vs bill (Plays #1, #7)
Display price in the **smallest increment** ($5/day), bill on the **longest** ($1,825/yr). Separate one-time value (access fee) from on-going value (smaller recurring fee).

## Margin-multiplier intuition
On a **10% margin** business, **+10% price ≈ double profit**. A 4% revenue bump on a 9%-margin business ≈ **+44% profit**. Small % = big profit because it carries no extra cost.

## Disclosure / ethics guardrails
- Continuity (Play #8): **agreed up front**, never undisclosed/forced — "Don't be a sneak."
- Sales tax (Play #3): **only charge what you pay.**
- Premium ≠ luxury: premium price reflects value; luxury value comes from the price.
