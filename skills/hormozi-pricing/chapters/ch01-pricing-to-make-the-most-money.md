# Chapter 1: Pricing To Make The Most Money

## Core Idea
Of the three things you could double in a business — number of customers, number of purchases, or price — **doubling price 6x's your profit** (vs ~3.5x for the other two), because a price increase raises revenue and *keeps your costs flat*. Pricing is the highest-leverage, lowest-effort profit lever you have. Guiding principle of the book: **"Profit is unnatural. You must force it into existence."**

## Frameworks Introduced

- **The Genie / Three Options** *(the book's framing device)*: A business genie offers to double exactly ONE thing and keep the rest the same. The three options all *look* like "2x hypothetical max revenue," but they are not equal on profit:
  - **Option #1 — Double # of new customers per month** (aka cut CAC in half) → **3.5x profit**
  - **Option #2 — Double # of purchases** (how many times they buy / how long they stay; cuts churn in half) → **3.5x profit**
  - **Option #3 — Double your price** (charge more for the same thing) → **6x profit** ← pick this.
  - When to use: as the mental model for *where to focus first*. Price wins because the extra revenue drops almost entirely to the bottom line (same CAC, same delivery cost).

- **The three profit levers**: `# new customers × price × # of purchases`. Any one doubled doubles "hypothetical max revenue," but they differ wildly in *profit* impact once costs are held constant.

## Key Concepts
- **CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)** — what it costs to get one customer; doubling customers for the same spend halves CAC.
- **Churn** — % of customers lost per period; doubling # of purchases ≈ halving churn (e.g. 33%/3mo → 16.5%/6mo).
- **LTV / Lifetime Revenue** — total a customer pays over their life (price × duration); the number pricing should maximize.
- **Net margin** — profit ÷ revenue; the lever that explodes when price rises but costs hold.
- **Gross margin** — (price − delivery cost) ÷ price; rises mechanically when you raise price.

## Mental Models
- **Think of price as the only lever that cuts costs while raising revenue** — fewer customers at a higher price means less to deliver *and* more per sale.
- Use the genie story whenever you're tempted to chase more leads first: **"focus on [pricing] first. It has the lowest effort and highest returns."**
- **"Pluck the juicy profit fruit from the low-hanging price tree while it's right in front of you."**

## Anti-patterns
- **Pricing to maximize customers or the first purchase**: optimizes the wrong number; you want most money over the customer *lifetime*, not the most bodies or the biggest first sale.
- **Assuming the three options are equivalent because the revenue math matches**: they tie on revenue, not on profit — costs are what break the tie.

## Worked Example: The $100/mo Business Through All Three Options
Stable starting business: **30 new clients/mo · 33% churn (3 mos) · $100/mo · 100 total clients · 20% net margin · $10,000 rev/mo · $2,000 profit/mo · CAC $100 · lifetime revenue $300 · gross margin 50%.**

- **Option #1 (2x customers):** 30→60 sales/mo; stabilizes at 200 clients (60 ÷ 33%); CAC halves $100→$50; revenue $10k→$20k; the $3,000 saved CAC pushes margin to 35% → **profit $2k→$7k = 3.5x.**
- **Option #2 (2x purchases):** churn 33%→16.5%, customers stay 3→6 months; lifetime revenue $300→$600; clients grow to 200; revenue $10k→$20k, costs $13k → **profit $7k = 3.5x.**
- **Option #3 (2x price):** same 100 clients, same CAC, same delivery; gross profit $150→$450, gross margin 50%→75%; revenue $10k→$20k; same $8k costs → **profit $12k, net margin 60% = 6x.**

**Conclusion:** tell the genie **Option #3**. "Yes, that is how powerful pricing is on the profit of your business."

## Proof: The ProfitWell 512-Company Study
Data from 500+ membership companies (ProfitWell): **improving pricing by 1% is ~2x as efficient at increasing profit as improving retention, and ~4x as efficient as improving acquisition.** Companies that **test pricing more frequently make more profit and grow continually faster.** This study is what inspired the genie story.

## Key Takeaways
1. Doubling price 6x's profit; doubling customers or purchases each only 3.5x's it — because price holds costs flat.
2. Price to make the most money over the *lifetime*, not the most customers or the biggest first sale.
3. Pricing is the lowest-effort, highest-return lever — focus there first (1% price ≈ 2x retention ≈ 4x acquisition).
4. Test pricing often: frequent testers make more profit and grow faster (512-company data).
