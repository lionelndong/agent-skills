# Chapter 3: Just Raise It

## Core Idea
Tiny pricing changes that barely touch conversion drop **straight to the bottom line** — and because most businesses run thin net margins (the average U.S. small business runs 7–10%), a small % added to revenue can multiply profit. This chapter is the bridge: the mindset ("just raise it"), the math of why small % = big profit, and the **master table of all 10 instant-profit plays** that follow.

## Frameworks Introduced

- **Small Percentages, Big Changes** *(the core leverage principle)*: "If you raise price by 10% and you run 10% margins… you double your profit." Because added revenue carries no extra cost, it lands almost entirely as profit. The 10 plays in this book stack to a **26.8%–63.8%** total price increase — designed to affect sales *minimally if at all*.
  - When to use: any time you're deciding whether a "small" pricing tweak is worth it. It almost always is.

- **The *Instant Profit* Pricing Playbook (master table)** — the 10 plays and what each provides. **You only need ONE to make more money immediately.** Pick the one or two with the biggest impact for the least work/risk.

## Key Concepts
- **Pricing easter eggs** — the "tiny little pricing… hidden under little stones" that Hormozi collects; each one is a low-effort profit hack.
- **Drops straight to the bottom line** — added revenue with no added cost = nearly pure profit; the mechanism behind every play.
- **Instant-profit (vs bigger plays)** — these are chosen for speed and low risk; "There are other pricing strategies that create bigger swings, but they require more work/change/risk. So, I excluded them."

## Mental Models
- **Think in margin multiples, not revenue percentages**: a 4% revenue bump on a 9%-margin business is ~44% more profit, not "just 4%."
- **Use "just raise it" as the default disposition** — the friction is psychological, not financial. "Changing prices, more than anything, requires guts. Have some."
- "How many things can you do to a business, **overnight**, that can increase profit by 50%+ without any major change in operations?"

## Anti-patterns
- **Dismissing small increases as "won't make a difference"**: on thin margins they often *double* profit — the owner who "hasn't adjusted prices in five years" has already moved price, just downward (via inflation).
- **Holding out for big, risky pricing overhauls** while ignoring the free, instant wins sitting in front of you.

## Worked Example: The Restaurant's 4% Processing Fee
At a mall sit-down restaurant, Hormozi spots a menu note: *"All prices subject to 4% processing fee at check out."* The company was private-equity owned. The imagined boardroom logic: **"We're running 8% net margins… add a 4% processing fee… it won't affect sales… by adding 4% to our revenue, we'll add 50% to our profit."**

The math on an average single-location restaurant (≈9% net margins, $1,000,000 revenue → $90,000 profit): adding a **4% fee → +$40,000 revenue**, which drops straight to the bottom line: revenue $1,000,000 → $1,040,000, but **profit $90,000 → $130,000 — a 44% increase from one small change.** Cost to implement: reprint menus and add one line to checkout. "Efficient."

*Scaling it:* add 26.8% to that same business's revenue and profit goes $90,000 → $358,000 (~4x). **"This. Small. Stuff. Makes. A. Big. Difference."**

## Key Takeaways
1. Small % price increases multiply profit because they carry no extra cost — on a 10%-margin business, +10% price ≈ double profit.
2. The 10 plays stack to a 26.8%–63.8% price increase, each engineered to barely move conversion.
3. You only need to implement **one** to make more money immediately — pick by biggest impact ÷ least work/risk.
4. The barrier is guts, not economics: "have some, and reap your reward."
