# Section II: Attraction Offers

## Core Idea
**"Attraction Offers generate leads and convert them into customers"** by offering something **free or at a discount** — because everyone wants a great deal. Free and discount exist on a continuum ("free" = a 100% discount); anywhere Hormozi says "free," you can substitute "discount" or "$1." Most businesses spend too much to get customers and make too little — Attraction Offers solve that cash constraint first.

## The Five Attraction Offers

### 1) Win Your Money Back
*"If you do X within Y time within Z rules, you can get it free."* Customer puts money down; if they **get the result, take the actions, or both** (make them easy to track), they win it back as cash or store credit.
- Generated **$1B+ in sales industry-wide**; the first Grand Slam Offer Gym Launch taught.
- **The money is in the people who succeed** (and have something else to buy) — not the people who fail. Have an upsell ready to apply their winnings (see **Rollover Upsell**).
- Good criteria: easy to track · gets customers results · **advertises the business** (posts, tags, referrals, reviews). Only run it if your refund rate is **below ~5–10%**.
- **Make everyone a winner** (in private): about halfway through, make your next offer as if they already won.

### 2) Giveaways
Advertise a chance to win a big **Grand Prize** in exchange for contact info; then offer **everyone else** a discounted "promotional offer" (the "partial scholarship"). Steps: pick a Grand Prize (assign a $ value = price anchor) → pick your promotional offer → ask for contact info + eligibility → qualifying actions → **deadline for urgency** → announce winner + privately contact everyone else (who all "qualify"). Rule of thumb: core-offer discount = **10–30% of gross margins**; framed against the Grand Prize value, a 10% discount can read as a 64% cost-to-value difference.

### 3) Decoy Offer
Advertise a free/discounted **decoy**; when leads engage, present a **premium offer** side-by-side so they see how much more valuable it is. *"Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?"* — most say results → go to premium. Make the **contrast huge** (strip the decoy bare, no guarantee; make premium awesome). **Advertise benefits, not features.** Note: **Discount offers have higher show-up rates than free offers** (use discounts where no-shows are costly — doctors, dentists, lawyers). Four ways to advertise a discount: % off · absolute $ off · free portion · total package price.

### 4) Buy X Get Y Free
When customers buy, they get other stuff free — "free" gets far more attention than a discount. Reframe pricing: *3 shirts at $10 = $30*, or *"buy 1 shirt for $30, get 2 free"* — same price, more free stuff. **Always give more free than paid** ("buy 1 get 2," not "buy 2 get 1"). **Raise prices before giving stuff away** to preserve profit. (Boot Factory: "Buy 1 Pair, Get 2 Free" — tripled the price of one pair.) Free things can differ from paid things; can be used for **fast cash** with existing customers (cap at ~10%).

### 5) Pay Less Now or Pay More Later
Give a choice: **pay full-price later** (delayed payment + conditional guarantee — advertise it as "free," get the card on file) **OR pay a discounted price now** (20–50% off + bonuses). Removes all risk → tons of engaged leads. Promise a clear **yes/no, measurable result**; make a **conditional satisfaction guarantee** (they qualify only by doing the value-getting actions — e.g., attending). If >10% of "pay later" cancel, you over-promised or under-conditioned. (Examples: the 3-day real-estate workshop; the 14-Day Clothing Trial.)

## Mental Models
- *More and better free stuff beats one free thing* — maybe to get the free things, they have to buy one. "That's how we make money on free stuff."
- *We don't get customers to make a sale; we make sales to get customers.*

## Key Takeaways
1. Lead with free/discount to beat the cash constraint and convert strangers.
2. Five plays: Win Your Money Back, Giveaways, Decoy, Buy X Get Y Free, Pay Less Now or Pay More Later.
3. Always have something to sell next — Attraction Offers reveal the next problem.
