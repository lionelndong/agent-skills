# Section V: Continuity Offers

## Core Idea
*"You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once."* **Continuity Offers provide ongoing value that customers make ongoing payments for — until they cancel.** "You sell once, but get paid again and again." They boost the profit from every customer and give you one last thing to sell.

## Worked Example: $10,000 Now vs. $42,000 Over Time
Offer a **$1,000 thing** to 100 people → 10 buy → **$10,000 now, $0 later**.
Offer the same value at **$50/month** → 40 of 100 buy → keep them ~20 months → still $1,000 each → **$2,000 now and $40,000 over time** — *and* you now have **4x the customers to upsell later**.
The trade-off: continuity attracts more customers but brings far less cash *now* — which is why it's hard to use as an Attraction Offer on its own. The fix: **do continuity last** (after the big-cash Attraction/Upsell/Downsell offers), then offer a prepay discount to pull future cash forward.

## The Three Continuity Offers
You can't get customers to *keep* buying unless they *start* — so these get them started by combining **bonuses, discounts, and fees**.

### 1) Continuity Bonus Offers
Give an awesome **bonus** (worth more than the first continuity payment) if they sign up today. (The gym: "Want it free? Become a member and it's free — plus member-exclusive bonuses: better class times, tanning, VIP events." Tripled membership sales, kept the up-front challenge cash, then upsold a discounted prepaid membership.) Combine **bonus (add value)** + **discount (lower cost)** + **a dash of urgency**.

### 2) Continuity Discount Offers
Lower the cost to start (and to stay). Anything you offer free you can offer as a discount — use both for the benefits of both. Frontloading free/discounted time (e.g., to cover the time it takes to pay off a first offer) keeps payments **continuous** so the customer rolls straight into recurring billing.

### 3) Waived Fee Offer
Waive a setup/onboarding/initiation **fee** as the incentive to start continuity — a low-cost lever (you give up a fee, not margin) to get the subscription going.

## Getting People to Start (examples)
- **Physical product**: "Get every dog toy free ($800 value) when you sign up for $59/mo food" (+ a new toy monthly).
- **Service**: "Short-Term Accelerator ($1,000) free when you become a member for $100/mo."
- **Digital**: bundle a one-time deliverable free with the subscription.

## Mental Models
- *If customers get ongoing value, it makes sense for them to make ongoing payments.* (Conversely, don't charge a one-time price for a service delivered forever, and don't charge forever for a one-day workshop — that's a payment plan.)
- *Continuity is the last sale in your 30-day window and the engine of recurring cash.*

## Key Takeaways
1. Continuity = sell once, get paid repeatedly; it maximizes each customer's total spend.
2. Three plays: Continuity Bonus, Continuity Discount, Waived Fee.
3. Do continuity *last*, then add a prepay discount to pull cash forward into the 30-day window.
