# Section I: What's A Money Model? + The Four Types of Offers

## Core Idea
**"A Money Model is a sequence of offers."** You find every opportunity to solve a customer's problem… then offer to solve it. Done right, you make enough money from one customer to get and service more customers fast — removing cash as the constraint on growth.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Money Model** = a deliberate sequence of offers — *what* you offer, *when*, and *how*, to make as much money as fast as possible. Ideal: make enough from one customer to get and service **at least two more in under 30 days**.
- **The Four Types of Offers** (the building blocks):
  1. **Attraction Offers** — turn strangers into customers.
  2. **Upsell Offers** — get people to spend more cash.
  3. **Downsell Offers** — get people to say yes when they'd have said no.
  4. **Continuity Offers** — keep people buying.
  "They work great on their own, but together they make your business unstoppable."

## Key Concepts
- **Bad Money Models kill businesses** — if it costs more to get a customer than you make from them, you're cash-constrained: you can only get lots of customers if you already have lots of customers. Big/funded companies survive this; bootstrappers don't.
- **Two options** when a customer takes 2 years to pay back: (1) wait and pray you don't run out of money, or (2) **get paid fast and grow as much as you please**. A good Money Model is option 2.
- **Compounding growth**: make customers 2x more valuable, get 2x as many, at 2x the speed → the business grows **8x faster** (2×2×2). Triple each → **27x**.

## Worked Example: The Rental Car Money Model
A $19/day reservation became **$100/day** (5x) through a *sequence* of offers, each solving a problem: Vehicle Upgrade (big man, small car) → Late Return (missing checkout) → Premium Insurance (dinging the car) → Minimum Insurance **Downsell** → Prepaid Gas (rushing to a flight). "I paid more money faster… and now we see why the car rental industry brings in billions per month."

## The 7 Important Notes (rules of the game)
1. **All businesses have Money Models** — swap "this won't work for my business" → "how will I make this work?"
2. Some models **work better in some businesses** than others — design your own, don't just copy.
3. **If a customer asks for their money back — give it back.** Avoid the headache.
4. **Hard selling is for weak products** — make offers available at the time of the problem; if they don't want it, find someone who does.
5. **Obey the law** (especially around "free") — check with lawyers; this book is *inspiration*.
6. **Be transparent** — state the facts; don't lie (you can't bankrupt a bad reputation).
7. **Any offer can be used on its own, at any time, in any order** — sequenced right, they make a $100M Money Model.

## Key Takeaways
1. A Money Model is a sequence of offers, each solving the next problem.
2. The four offer types: Attraction, Upsell, Downsell, Continuity.
3. The goal: get paid fast enough that one customer funds the next two within 30 days.
