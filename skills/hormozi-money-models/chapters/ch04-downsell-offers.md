# Section IV: Downsell Offers

## Core Idea
**"What to offer when they say no."** Downselling tweaks the original offer to find the highest-value solution for the customer's budget. *"Any offer you make after someone says 'no' is a downsell."* You downsell in two ways: change **how they pay** or **what they get**.

## The Rules of Downselling
- **They said no to *this* offer, not all offers.** "No means no for this thing, not no for everything." Make another offer.
- **Downsells are trades** — if you give something, get something.
- **Personalize, don't pressure** — offer more of what they like, less of what they don't, at a matching price.
- **Offer the same things in new ways** — don't invent a hundred new products; find a hundred ways to offer what you have.
- **Don't drop your price just to get someone to buy** — that's discounting, not downselling. (You *can* offer a payment plan.)
- **Customers talk about price** — test prices deliberately and in advance, never cave in the moment; a "just because" discount upsets full-price buyers and is an ethics problem.

## The Three Downsell Processes

### 1) Payment Plan Downsell (how they pay)
Offer the **same product**, but spread the cost — some up front, the rest in scheduled payments. *"'It costs too much' usually means 'it costs too much up front.'"* Payment plans **make money** when more customers buy and complete payments; they **lose money** when people cancel before you profit (worst case: someone who'd have paid in full takes a plan and cancels early). "I take the bet when I know I'll win." (A desperate first month: "Just put half down now…" → eventually "I can pay the whole thing on the first" → his first-ever payment plan worked.)

### 2) Trial With Penalty (how they pay)
A trial where the customer faces a penalty/charge if they don't return the product or meet the condition. Hormozi uses **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later** as an attraction offer for first-time and one-time services, and **Trial With Penalty** as the *downsell* version of the same idea — it keeps the price intact while lowering the up-front commitment.

### 3) Feature Downsell (what they get)
Change **what they get** — lower quantity or quality, or "peel" features out of a bundle, to hit their budget. (Supplements: Big Bundle → Small Bundle → Monthly Subscription.) Best of all, you can **alternate** between payment-based and feature-based downsells within the same sale — the more flexible the offer, the more people buy.

## Mental Models
- *A downsell is a personalized trade*, not a price cut — keep the price, change the terms or the package.
- *More ways to offer the same thing* beats more things to offer.

## Key Takeaways
1. Any offer after a "no" is a downsell; "no" is information about what they really want.
2. Three processes: Payment Plan & Trial With Penalty (change how they pay), Feature Downsell (change what they get).
3. Don't discount in the moment to save a sale — change the terms, not the price.
