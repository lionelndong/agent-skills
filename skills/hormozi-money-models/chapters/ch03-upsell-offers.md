# Section III: Upsell Offers

## Core Idea
"Upsells just mean **whatever we offer next**." With an Attraction Offer in place you have customers and cash; now **maximize 30-day profits**. When an offer solves a problem, another appears — you upsell the solution to *that* problem. **"Your first offer doesn't always make the profit."**

## Worked Example: The McDonald's 11.6x
A burger nets $0.25 profit — you'd need ~10,000/day to survive. "Do you want fries with that?" (+$0.75) → "Make it a meal?" (+$1.75, an **8x** jump) → "Supersize for a buck?" → **$3.00 profit, an 11.6x increase**. "If McDonald's didn't upsell fries and soda, there wouldn't be a McDonald's. Figure out your version of 'Do you want fries with that?'"

Upsells offer: **More** (quantity) · **Better** (quality) · **New/complementary** (different). They fail when you offer the wrong thing, at the wrong time (before they feel the problem), or the wrong way (they don't believe you).

## The Four Upsell Offers

### 1) The Classic Upsell — "You can't have X without Y!"
Solve the customer's **next problem the moment they become aware of it**. Relies on knowing their problem better than they do. (Car wash → sealant; bicycle → helmet/lights/tires; exercise course → nutrition course.)
- **Say "no" to say "yes"**: *"You don't want anything else, do you?"* — people are trained to answer "no," which becomes a yes.
- **Faster access = more value** — put it in their hands before they've said yes ("harder to give something back than to say no").
- **Hyper Buying Cycle** — buyers spend a lot in a short window when starting something new (weddings, babies, moving). Keep offering.
- **Name your bundles**; **BAMFAM** (Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting); upsell as many times as makes sense; you can even **charge for guarantees/warranties/insurance** (add 5–50%) instead of giving them free.

### 2) Menu Upsell — "You don't need that… you need this"
Tell customers what they *don't* need, then what they *do*. Combines four tactics:
- **Unselling** — cross out what they don't need (builds goodwill to upsell what they do).
- **Prescription Upsell** — tell them exactly what they need and how to use it (as if they already bought it); removes the "buy or not" option.
- **A/B Upsell** — ask their *preference* (A or B), not whether they want it ("chocolate or vanilla?"). Anything can be A/B (quantity, start date, flavor, time slot). Add a **nudge** ("this is my favorite").
- **Card On File** — "Want to just use the card on file?" — removes the hidden costs of buying.

### 3) Anchor Upsell
Present a **premium offer 5–10x the price first**; expect "the gasp"; come to the rescue with your main offer, which now looks like a great deal. (The $16,000 suit → the $2,200 suit felt cheap.) Bonuses: anchored customers spend more, and **some still buy the expensive thing**. Works best when the cheaper offer has the same *core function* as the premium.

### 4) Rollover Upsell — "Wanna just roll it forward?"
Credit some or all of a customer's **previous purchase** toward your next (more expensive) offer. Figure out **who** (re-engage old customers / rescue your upset customers / steal competitors' upset customers / upsell current customers), **what** (more, better, new), **how** (full or partial credit, up front or spread over time). Price the upsell at **≥4x the rollover credit** (so even a full credit = ≤25% discount). *(Winback campaign: 200 personalized videos offering $4,000 credit → ~20% took it → ~$1.9M annual revenue from one day of recording.)*

## Key Takeaways
1. Upsells (whatever you offer next) make or break the Money Model — often the majority of profit.
2. Four plays: Classic ("can't have X without Y"), Menu (unsell/prescribe/A-B/card), Anchor (premium-first), Rollover (credit forward).
3. Offer the more profitable upsell first; give faster access; BAMFAM.
