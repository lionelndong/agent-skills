# Glossary — $100M Money Models

**A/B Upsell** — A Menu Upsell tactic: ask the customer's *preference* (A or B) rather than whether they want the product; either choice is a sale. (Ch 3)

**Anchor Upsell** — Present a premium offer 5–10x the price first; after "the gasp," your main offer looks like a great deal. (Ch 3)

**Attraction Offer** — An offer (free or discounted) that turns strangers into customers; Stage I of a Money Model. (Ch 2)

**BAMFAM** — "Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting"; end every appointment by scheduling the next. (Ch 3)

**Buy X Get Y Free** — An Attraction Offer where buying something comes with free stuff; "free" reframes a discount and attracts more customers. Give more free than paid. (Ch 2)

**Card On File** — A Menu Upsell tactic: "Want to use the card on file?" — removes the hidden costs of buying. (Ch 3)

**Classic Upsell** — "You can't have X without Y!" — solve the next problem the moment the customer becomes aware of it. (Ch 3)

**Continuity Bonus Offer** — Give a bonus (worth more than the first payment) to get customers to start a subscription. (Ch 5)

**Continuity Discount Offer** — Lower the cost to start/stay on continuity. (Ch 5)

**Continuity Offer** — Ongoing value for ongoing payments until cancellation; sell once, get paid repeatedly; Stage III. (Ch 5)

**Decoy Offer** — Advertise a free/discounted decoy, then present a premium offer beside it so the premium looks far more valuable. (Ch 2)

**Downsell Offer** — Any offer made after a "no"; changes how they pay or what they get to fit the budget; part of Stage II. (Ch 4)

**Feature Downsell** — Change *what they get* (lower quantity/quality, peel features from a bundle) to hit a budget. (Ch 4)

**Giveaway** — Advertise a chance to win a Grand Prize for contact info, then offer everyone else a discounted "promotional offer." (Ch 2)

**Grand Prize** — The thing you want everyone to buy, used as the Giveaway's price anchor. (Ch 2)

**Hyper Buying Cycle** — A short window when a customer starting something new spends heavily (weddings, babies, moving). (Ch 3)

**Menu Upsell** — "You don't need that… you need this." Combines Unselling, Prescription Upsell, A/B Upsell, and Card On File. (Ch 3)

**Money Model** — A deliberate sequence of offers — what/when/how you offer — to make as much money as fast as possible. (Ch 1)

**Payment Plan Downsell** — Offer the *same* product but spread the cost (some up front, rest scheduled); keeps price, lowers up-front cost. (Ch 4)

**Pay Less Now or Pay More Later** — An Attraction Offer: pay full-price later (delayed payment + conditional guarantee) or a discounted price now (+ bonuses). (Ch 2)

**Prescription Upsell** — A Menu Upsell tactic: tell the customer exactly what they need and how to use it, as if they've already bought it. (Ch 3)

**Rollover Upsell** — Credit some/all of a previous purchase toward a more expensive next offer; price the upsell ≥4x the credit. (Ch 3)

**The Four Types of Offers** — Attraction, Upsell, Downsell, Continuity. (Ch 1)

**The Three Stages** — Get Cash (Attraction) → Get More Cash (Upsell/Downsell) → Get The Most Cash (Continuity). (Ch 6)

**Trial With Penalty** — A downsell where a trial carries a penalty/charge if conditions aren't met; lowers up-front commitment while keeping price. (Ch 4)

**Unselling** — A Menu Upsell tactic: tell customers what they *don't* need (builds goodwill to upsell what they do). (Ch 3)

**Upsell Offer** — Whatever you offer next (more/better/new) to maximize 30-day profit; Stage II. (Ch 3)

**Waived Fee Offer** — Waive a setup/initiation fee as the incentive to start continuity. (Ch 5)

**Win Your Money Back** — An Attraction Offer: do X within Y time within Z rules to get your money back (cash or store credit). (Ch 2)
