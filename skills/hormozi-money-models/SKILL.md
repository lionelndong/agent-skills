## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What a Money Model is
**"A Money Model is a sequence of offers."** You find every chance to solve a customer's problem, then offer to solve it. The goal: **make enough money from one customer to get and service at least two more in under 30 days** — so cash stops constraining growth. Bad money models (where acquisition costs more than first-purchase profit) starve a business; good ones get paid fast. Make customers 2x more valuable, get 2x as many, 2x faster → **8x** growth (3x each → 27x).

### The Four Types of Offers
1. **Attraction Offers** — turn strangers into customers (free/discount).
2. **Upsell Offers** — get people to spend more (whatever you offer next).
3. **Downsell Offers** — turn "no" into "yes" (offer made after a no).
4. **Continuity Offers** — keep people buying (ongoing value for ongoing payments).

### The Three Stages (how a Money Model is sequenced)
- **Stage I — Get Cash**: Attraction Offers.
- **Stage II — Get More Cash**: Upsell & Downsell Offers.
- **Stage III — Get The Most Cash**: Continuity Offers.
Build **one offer at a time** (reliable → automatic → next stage); each stage funds the next. "When your Money Model starts working, your business starts breaking."

### Attraction Offers (Stage I)
Free/discount on a continuum ("free" = 100% off). Five plays: **Win Your Money Back** (do X in Y by Z rules → free; criteria must advertise the business) · **Giveaway** (Grand Prize anchor → discounted promo offer for everyone else) · **Decoy Offer** (stripped decoy beside a premium; "free stuff or lasting results?") · **Buy X Get Y Free** (reframe a discount as free; give more free than paid) · **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later** (pay later with conditional guarantee, or pay now discounted + bonuses). *Discount offers get fewer leads but higher show-up rates than free.*

### Upsell Offers (Stage II) — more / better / new
"Your first offer doesn't always make the profit" (the McDonald's 11.6x). Four plays: **Classic Upsell** ("you can't have X without Y") · **Menu Upsell** (Unsell → Prescribe → A/B → Card On File) · **Anchor Upsell** (premium 5–10x first → "the gasp" → main offer looks cheap) · **Rollover Upsell** (credit a prior purchase toward a pricier offer; price it ≥4x the credit). Tells: say "no" to say "yes" · faster access = more value · BAMFAM · charge for guarantees/warranties.

### Downsell Offers (Stage II) — turn the "no" into "yes"
Any offer after a no; change **how they pay** or **what they get**. Three plays: **Payment Plan Downsell** · **Trial With Penalty** · **Feature Downsell**. Rules: they said no to *this* offer, not all · downsells are trades · personalize, don't pressure · never discount "just because" (customers talk) · alternate plays in one sale.

### Continuity Offers (Stage III) — sell once, get paid repeatedly
Do continuity **last** (after the big-cash offers), then add a prepay discount to pull cash into 30 days. Three plays: **Continuity Bonus** (bonus worth > first payment) · **Continuity Discount** · **Waived Fee**. Math: $1,000 once → 10 buyers ($10k); same value at $50/mo → 40 buyers × 20 mo = $2k now + $40k later + 4x customers to upsell.

### Make Your Own (and the laws)
Steps: Attraction → Upsell → Downsell → Continuity. **Raise price in stages** (start cheap for yeses + feedback, raise until you make *less*). **Simple Scales, Fancy Fails** — "100 ways to offer your product," not 100 products. **Affiliate products fill gaps**. **Turn Attraction into Continuity** via auto-renewal. Mix and match — there are no rules.
