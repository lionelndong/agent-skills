# Patterns — $100M Money Models

## Pattern: Build a Money Model (the 4 steps / 3 stages)
**When to use**: Designing how to make money from customers, fast.
**How**: Stage I — start with an **Attraction Offer** (get customers + cover cost). Stage II — add an **Upsell** (push 30-day profit above acquisition+delivery cost), then a **Downsell** (convert the nos). Stage III — add a **Continuity Offer** (recurring cash). Build one offer at a time; make each reliable, then automatic, then advance.
**Trade-offs**: A "finished" model collapses a zero-stage startup; let each stage pay for the next. Measure in quarters.

## Pattern: Win Your Money Back (Attraction)
**When to use**: Products requiring continuous customer effort (fitness, skills, weight loss).
**How**: Customer pays; wins it back (cash/store credit) by getting the result, taking the actions, or both — criteria easy to track, results-aligned, and that **advertise the business**. Have an upsell ready to apply winnings (Rollover).
**Trade-offs**: Only if refund rate <~5–10%; the profit is in those who succeed and buy again.

## Pattern: Giveaway (Attraction)
**When to use**: Generate many qualified leads around your most expensive product.
**How**: Grand Prize (with a $ value as anchor) → discounted "promotional offer" for everyone else → contact info + qualifying actions → deadline → announce winner, privately contact the rest.
**Trade-offs**: Core-offer discount ≈ 10–30% of gross margins; cap to what you can service.

## Pattern: Decoy Offer (Attraction)
**When to use**: Close everyone — premium buyers now, decoy buyers later.
**How**: Advertise a stripped free/discounted decoy; present a far-more-valuable premium beside it. "Free stuff or lasting results?" to go premium-first. Make the contrast huge; advertise benefits not features.
**Trade-offs**: Discount offers get fewer leads but higher show-up rates than free.

## Pattern: Buy X Get Y Free (Attraction)
**When to use**: Stuff worth buying more of / longer access; fast cash.
**How**: Reframe a discount as free ("buy 1 get 2," same price as buying 3). Give more free than paid. Raise prices before giving stuff away.
**Trade-offs**: Manage the cash — you must deliver the full term you collected for.

## Pattern: Pay Less Now or Pay More Later (Attraction)
**When to use**: Removing risk to engage lots of leads; first-time/one-time services.
**How**: Pay later (delayed payment + conditional guarantee, card on file) OR pay now (20–50% off + bonuses). Promise a clear yes/no measurable result; qualify the guarantee by value-getting actions.
**Trade-offs**: If >10% of "pay later" cancel, you over-promised or under-conditioned.

## Pattern: Classic Upsell
**When to use**: An immediate next problem your offer just revealed.
**How**: "You can't have X without Y." Offer the more profitable upsell first; give fast access; "say no to say yes"; BAMFAM; charge for guarantees/warranties.
**Trade-offs**: The only downside is a "no"; the worse outcome is never asking.

## Pattern: Menu Upsell
**When to use**: You have multiple offers; consultative selling.
**How**: Unsell (cross out what they don't need) → Prescribe (exactly what they need + how to use it) → A/B (preference, not yes/no) → Card On File. Nudge new customers.
**Trade-offs**: Unsell lower-margin items to incentivize higher-margin ones.

## Pattern: Anchor Upsell
**When to use**: Get more people to take your main offer (and some to buy premium).
**How**: Present the premium (5–10x) first → "the gasp" → rescue with the main offer (now a deal). Lower offer should share the premium's core function.
**Trade-offs**: Anchored customers spend more; needs a believable premium.

## Pattern: Rollover Upsell
**When to use**: Re-engage old customers, rescue upset ones (yours or competitors'), upsell current ones.
**How**: Credit some/all of a prior purchase toward a more expensive offer (full/partial, up front/over time). Price upsell ≥4x the credit. Add one-time urgency.
**Trade-offs**: Do rollovers before refunding; test how much credit to give.

## Pattern: Downsell (convert the "no")
**When to use**: After any rejected offer.
**How**: Change **how they pay** (Payment Plan / Trial With Penalty) or **what they get** (Feature Downsell). Alternate between them in one sale. Trades, not price cuts.
**Trade-offs**: Never discount "just because" in the moment — customers talk.

## Pattern: Continuity Offer
**When to use**: Maximize each customer's total spend; recurring cash.
**How**: Get them to *start* with a Continuity **Bonus**, **Discount**, or **Waived Fee** (+ urgency). Do continuity after the big-cash offers; add a prepay discount to pull cash into 30 days.
**Trade-offs**: Less cash now, much more later; turn Attraction Offers into Continuity via auto-renewal.

## Pattern: Fill Gaps With Affiliate Products
**When to use**: You lack an offer for a slot in your Money Model.
**How**: Sell other businesses' products for a commission; they deliver. Works at any size.
**Trade-offs**: More offers/money with no extra delivery — but vet what you promote.
