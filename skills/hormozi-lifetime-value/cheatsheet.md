# Cheatsheet — $100M Playbook: Lifetime Value

## The one definition
**LTV = LTGP = CLV = the gross profit collected over the lifespan of a customer** (total money minus delivery cost). *"He who can make his customer more valuable than his competition, wins."* Advertising is the machine; **LTV is the fuel.**

## Calculate the baseline (3 steps)
| Step | Do this |
|---|---|
| 1. Gross Profit | Revenue − delivery cost (NOT net). Get GP and GP% per product + overall. |
| 2. Frequency or Churn | Transactional → avg transactions/customer. Recurring → **Churn = left ÷ original** (new signups don't count). |
| 3. Combine | Transactional: **GP × transactions**. Recurring: **GP ÷ Churn**. |

Worked: widget $80 GP × 4 = **$360**. Service $2,400 GP ÷ 5% churn = **$48,000**.

## Decision rules
- **Start with price.** It moves gross profit more than anything → 10% business + 20% price (sales held) ≈ **triples** profit.
- **Optimize price by `conversion × LTGP`, not units.** "The goal isn't to sell the most stuff, it's to make the most money."
- **Start low, then +20% every ~10 sales** until a dramatic drop, then revert to the sweet spot. Overshot? Add bonuses or refund the difference.
- **Test price every quarter.** (Profitwell: pricing-test frequency tracks profitability.)
- **To increase purchases, pick ≥1 of exactly three:** add recurring · decrease churn · follow up.
- **Lower churn is the compounding lever:** `Price ÷ Churn` → **10%→5% churn doubles LTV.**
- **Offer the bigger/better version FIRST, then downsell to standard** → "20%+ lifts in cash collected overnight."
- **Downsell ONLY unqualified buyers — never a qualified one.** "I forbid my sales team from selling a qualified person a downsell" (prevents cannibalization).
- **Cross-sell only what's seamless** to your infrastructure/expertise; "don't break your business to pick up some extra change."
- **What to sell next? Survey the customers** and build the winner.

## Quick math
| Lever | Formula |
|---|---|
| Cross-sell added LTV | conversion rate × gross profit of cross-sell |
| Recurring LTV (service) | Price ÷ Churn |
| Price sweet spot | maximize (conversion rate × LTGP) |
| Quantity multipliers | bulk (prepay yr = 12x) · more often (1.33x) · bigger (3x) |

## Thresholds & defaults
- Price nudge: **+20% per ~10 sales**.
- Vendor prepay/bulk discount: **10–20%** ("guaranteed return on investment").
- Offshoring can cut cost **80%+**; doubling a rep's clients (5→10) **halves** that cost.
- Reported quantity/quality upsell lift: **~20%+** cash collected.

## #6 quality dimensions (sell up) ⇄ #8 (reverse to sell down)
Speed of response/delivery · availability (days/hours/times) · service ratio (1-on-1 ↔ many-to-one) · provider seniority · live vs recorded · in-person vs remote · **DIY/DWY/DFY** · personalization (generic ↔ made-for-you) · guarantee (full ↔ none) · ingredients/materials (sirloin vs chuck; steel vs iron). **Up = premium; reverse the same list = lower-quality downsell.**

## Tells & smells
- Customers are "one and done," one marketing channel → **"sitting ducks"**; add a back-end/recurring offer.
- A proposed new product "feels too far from the core" → it won't solve the customer's *next* problem; don't build it.
- "Some products you spend a lot of time on don't make as much profit as you thought" → recompute GP per product.
- Spending too little to acquire customers → you haven't maxed LTV; "those who can spend the most, get the most."
