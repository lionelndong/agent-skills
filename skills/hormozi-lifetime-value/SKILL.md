## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What LTV is
**"Lifetime value (LTV) is the gross profit collected over the lifespan of a customer"** — total money from a customer minus everything it costs to deliver. Hormozi also calls it **lifetime gross profit (LTGP)**; some call it **customer lifetime value (CLV)** — "the same idea." **Advertising is the machine that makes a business grow; LTV is the fuel.** The owner who makes a customer more valuable than the competition can outbid everyone for attention → "a veritable monopoly of attention." Cost to acquire can only hit zero; revenue per customer "can go infinitely high."

### How to calculate it (baseline before you optimize)
1. **Gross Profit** = revenue − delivery cost (NOT net profit). Get GP and GP% per product and overall.
2. **Average transactions** per customer — OR, for recurring, **Churn** = `people who left ÷ original amount` (new signups during the period do **not** affect churn).
3. Combine: transactional → **`GP × transactions`**; recurring → **`GP ÷ Churn`**.
- Worked: widget $80 GP × 4 = **$360 LTGP**; service $2,400 GP ÷ 5% churn = **$48,000 LTGP**.

### The Crazy Eight — the eight ways to make a customer worth more
Hormozi's authoritative list (verbatim): **1) Raise prices · 2) Lower the cost of delivering the thing · 3) Upsell Frequency (buy more again later) · 4) Upsell Quantity (buy more now) · 5) Upsell Quality (premium version) · 6) Downsell Quantity (fewer rather than nothing) · 7) Downsell Quality (lower-cost rather than nothing) · 8) Cross-Sell (a different thing on top).** Run them in your head on every product until you're **"fluent in monetization."** *(The body chapters head these as #1 Increase Prices, #2 Decrease Costs, #3 Increase # of Purchases, #4 Cross-Sell, #5 Sell More, #6 Sell Better, #7 Downsell Fewer, #8 Downsell Lower Quality — same eight, the source's two numberings preserved.)*

- **#1 Increase Prices** — charge more for the same thing; moves gross profit most, so do it first. A 10%-profit business that raises price 20% (sales held) **triples** profit. Optimize **`conversion rate × LTGP`** (make the most money, not sell the most units); test every quarter. **Start low, then +20% every ~10 sales** to the sweet spot.
- **#2 Decrease Costs** — deliver the same thing cheaper (price has no ceiling; cost only falls to zero). Tactics: raise employee:customer ratio · offshore (−80%+) · productize delivery · **DFY→DWY** · cap usage · lifetime→annual · in-person→remote · cut meetings · buy in bulk & prepay (10–20% off).
- **#3 Increase # of Purchases** — exactly three ways: **add recurring**, **decrease churn** (`Price ÷ Churn` → 10%→5% churn **doubles LTV**), **follow up** (quarterly promotion + value between).
- **#4 Cross-Sell** — a complementary different product; added LTV = **conversion × gross profit** of the cross-sell. Keep it seamless — "don't break your business to pick up some extra change."
- **#5 Sell More (Quantity)** — bulk (prepay a year, 12x) · more often (1.33x) · bigger (3x).
- **#6 Sell Better (Quality)** — a newer or premium version (better ingredients/materials/people; or ~17 service dimensions: speed, availability, ratio, personalization, DIY/DWY/DFY…). For #5 & #6: **offer the bigger/better version first, then downsell to standard** → "20%+ lifts in cash collected overnight."
- **#7 Downsell Fewer (Quantity)** & **#8 Downsell Lower Quality** — capture buyers who'd otherwise buy nothing ("this beats nothing"). #8 = **reverse the #6 quality list**. **Rule: downsell ONLY unqualified buyers — never a qualified one** (prevents cannibalizing the main offer).

### The whole point
*"If you make more than anyone else does from the same customer then you can spend more than anyone else to get them."* LTV is the **arms race** of getting customers; work the eight as a process, not by inspiration. Hormozi's favorite upsell: **more of — or more help with — what they just bought, with faster results, less risk, effort, and hassle, for more money.**
