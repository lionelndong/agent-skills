# Glossary — $100M Playbook: Lifetime Value

**Add Recurring** — Adding a weekly/monthly/quarterly/annual version of an offer so a one-time buyer becomes a repeat buyer; one of three ways to increase # of purchases. (Ch 3)

**Bigger** — A quantity upsell (#5): more in the same package (e.g. 1 hour → 3 hours; normal → bigger burger). (Ch 3)

**Break-even conversion rate** — The conversion rate at which a new higher price yields the same profit as the old price; stay above it to win on a price test. (Ch 2)

**Bulk** — A quantity upsell (#5): selling more of the same thing at once, e.g. prepaying a year (12x). (Ch 3)

**Cannibalization** — When a customer who'd have paid full price takes a cheaper downsell instead; avoided by downselling only unqualified buyers. (Ch 4)

**Churn** — "The percentage of customers that leave between time periods"; `people who left ÷ original amount`. New signups in the period do not affect it. (Ch 1)

**CLV (Customer Lifetime Value)** — Another name for LTV/LTGP; "the same idea." (Ch 1)

**Crazy Eight (The)** — Hormozi's eight ways to make a customer worth more: raise prices, lower cost, upsell frequency, upsell quantity, upsell quality, downsell quantity, downsell quality, cross-sell. (Ch 2)

**Cross-Sell** — Selling a different, complementary product on top of the first; added LTV = conversion rate × gross profit of the cross-sell. (#4 in body; #8 in summary list.) (Ch 3)

**Decrease Churn** — Getting recurring customers to stay and pay longer; 10%→5% churn doubles LTV via `Price ÷ Churn`. (Ch 3)

**Done For You → Done With You (DFY→DWY)** — A cost-decrease tactic: shift from "I'll do it for you" to "I'll help you do it" to raise the customer-to-employee ratio. (Ch 2)

**DIY / DWY / DFY** — Do It Yourself vs Done With You vs Done For You; a quality dimension that can be sold up (#6) or down (#8). (Ch 3, Ch 4)

**Downsell Lower Quality (#8)** — Sell something that gets the same result with a lower-quality experience; "take the list for a quality upsell and reverse it." (Ch 4)

**Downsell Fewer / Lower Quantity (#7)** — Sell a smaller amount, smaller version, or less-frequent doses of the same thing rather than nothing. (Ch 4)

**Follow Up** — Reactivation of a list/audience; Hormozi's preferred method is a quarterly promotion plus value the rest of the time. (Ch 3)

**Give:Ask Ratio** — Balancing value provided against requests made, so follow-up promotions land and you stay top of mind. (Ch 3)

**Gross Profit** — "What's leftover from a purchase after you deliver the goods or service"; NOT net profit. Used to pay the rest of the bills. (Ch 1)

**Gross Profit %** — Gross profit ÷ revenue (e.g. $24,000 ÷ $30,000 = 80%). (Ch 1)

**Increase # of Purchases (#3)** — Get people to buy the same thing more times; three ways only — add recurring, decrease churn, follow up. (Ch 3)

**Increase Prices (#1)** — Charge more for the same thing; affects gross profit more than any other of the eight, so it goes first. (Ch 2)

**Increase Quality / Sell Better (#6)** — Sell a newer or premium version of the same thing at a higher price; ~17 service dimensions. (Ch 3)

**Increase Quantity / Sell More (#5)** — Sell more of the same thing at once: bulk, more often, or bigger. (Ch 3)

**LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)** — Hormozi's preferred name for LTV: gross profit collected over a customer's lifespan. (Ch 1)

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — "The gross profit collected over the lifespan of a customer"; total money from a customer minus delivery cost. = LTGP = CLV. (Ch 1)

**Money Math** — Hormozi's term for the arithmetic of profit/LTV; "the more I did, the more money I made." (Ch 1)

**More Often** — A quantity upsell (#5): increased frequency of delivery (e.g. monthly → every three weeks = 1.33x). (Ch 3)

**Premium Version** — A higher-quality version (better ingredients/materials/people or faster/more available service); the basis of #6. (Ch 3)

**Productize** — Sell more similar customers with similar problems so the same templates/automation serve them; a cost-decrease tactic. (Ch 2)

**Reactivation Campaign** — A follow-up to get old leads/customers to re-engage (e.g. a quarterly promotion or competition). (Ch 3)

**Sales conversion rate × LTGP** — The quantity that price optimization maximizes; "the price that gets the most people to buy with the highest gross profit." (Ch 2)

**Unqualified buyer** — A prospect who doesn't qualify for the main offer; the *only* person who should be offered a downsell. (Ch 4)

**3-Step LTGP Calculation** — Step 1 gross profit → Step 2 average transactions OR churn → Step 3 multiply (transactional) or divide by churn (recurring). (Ch 1)
