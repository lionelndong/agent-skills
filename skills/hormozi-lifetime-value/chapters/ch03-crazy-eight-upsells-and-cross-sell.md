# Chapter 3: The Upsells & Cross-Sell (#3 Purchases, #4 Cross-Sell, #5 Quantity, #6 Quality)

## Core Idea
Four of the Crazy Eight raise **average order value or purchase frequency** by getting customers to buy *more again*, *more now*, *better*, or *something different*. Hormozi's favorite upsell of all time: **"More of — or more help with — what they just bought… but with faster results, less risk, less effort, less hassle — for more money."**

## Frameworks Introduced
- **#3 Increase # of Purchases** — *"You get people to buy the same thing more times."* Exactly three ways (pick at least one):
  - **Add recurring** — add a weekly/monthly/quarterly/annual version. Buying once → three times = you tripled purchase count, *even with high churn*. (Plumbing membership; mug-of-the-month; monthly accountability calls.)
  - **Decrease churn** — if you already recur, get them to "stay and pay longer." `Price ÷ Churn = Lifetime revenue`, so **10% → 5% churn doubles LTV**. (Customer-appreciation events; let customers change quantity/frequency by text; add a community feature.) *Hormozi defers the depth to the Churn Checklist Playbook.*
  - **Follow Up** — run a **reactivation campaign**; his preferred long-term method is a **quarterly promotion**, providing value the rest of the time to balance the **give:ask ratio** and stay top of mind. ("Best lawn" competition; quarterly weight-loss challenge; quarterly student cohort or a new module "launch.")
- **#4 Cross-Sell Something Different** — *"You sell another product that complements — or goes with — your first product."* Added LTV = **`conversion rate × gross profit of the cross-sell`**, added to your original LTV. (Lawn care → snow blowing; burgers → fries + soda; course → community.) Pick the cross-sell that **"does not dramatically change who you serve or what you do every day."**
- **#5 Sell More (Increase Quantity)** — *"You sell more of the same thing at once."* Three sub-types:
  - **Bulk** ("more"): prepay a year (12x); one burger → two.
  - **More often** (frequency of delivery): monthly → every three weeks (1.33x).
  - **Bigger** (more in the same package): 1 hour → 3 hours each time (3x); normal → bigger burger.
- **#6 Sell Better (Increase Quality)** — *"You sell a better version of the same thing"* at a higher price. Two flavors: a **newer** version (new process / new recipe / updated model) and a **premium** version (better ingredients, materials, or people). The premium-service lever list (17 dimensions): faster response times; time availability; days of week; times of day; amount of time per call; location availability; cancellations (fees vs free); speed of response; speed of delivery; service ratio (1-on-1 vs 1-to-many vs many-to-one); communication method; provider qualifications; live vs recorded; in-person vs remote; **DIY/DWY/DFY**; expirations; personalization (generic vs 3–5 avatars vs made just for you). Physical premium = better ingredients (sirloin vs ground chuck) / stronger materials (steel vs iron).

## Key Concepts
- **Give:ask ratio** — keep providing value between asks so follow-up promotions land.
- **Recurring** — any subscription/membership version of an existing offer; multiplies purchase count.
- **The quality dimensions list** — a reusable menu (#6) you can also *reverse* into a quality downsell (#8).
- **Cross-sell vs upsell** — cross-sell is a *different complementary* product; upsells (#5/#6) are *more/better of the same*.

## Mental Models
- **"Sell first, then downsell your standard offer"** — for quantity (#5) and quality (#6), *offer the bigger/better version first on the call*, then fall back to standard; Hormozi reports **"20%+ lifts in cash collected overnight."**
- **The Hyper Buying logic of follow-up** — "sometimes it's not the right time"; reminding customers you exist makes more money when they're ready.
- **Seamless add-on test (cross-sell)** — choose "the easiest thing you could add in seamlessly with your existing infrastructure, resources and expertise."

## Worked Example: Quantifying a cross-sell, and quantity multipliers
**Cross-sell math:** LTV starts at **$100**. You get **20%** of people to buy a **$100** all-profit cross-sell → added LTV = 20% × $100 = **$20**. New LTV = $100 + $20 = **$120**.

**Quantity multipliers (pest control, 1x/month base):**
- **Bulk** → prepay for a year = **12x**.
- **More often** → monthly to every three weeks = **1.33x**.
- **Bigger** → 1 hour to 3 hours each visit = **3x**.
Action: offer each quantity upsell *first* on sales calls, then downsell to the standard offer → "20%+ lifts in cash collected overnight."

**Recurring multiplier:** a customer who went from buying **once** to buying **three times** (because you added a subscription) tripled their purchase count — *true even with high churn*.

## Anti-patterns
- **Cross-selling something that breaks your business**: *"you don't wanna break your business to pick up some extra change"* — don't change who you serve or your daily work.
- **Relying on inspiration for upsells**: *"My highest converting upsells have rarely come from moments of inspiration. They usually come from… following a process I know works."*
- **Ignoring churn while adding recurring**: recurring multiplies purchases, but lowering churn is the compounding lever (`Price ÷ Churn`).
- **Selling premium/bulk *after* the standard offer by default**: lead with the bigger/better version first, then downsell.

## Key Takeaways
1. Increase purchases only three ways: **add recurring, decrease churn, follow up** — pick at least one.
2. Cross-sell value = **conversion × gross profit**, added to LTV; keep it seamless to your core.
3. Quantity (#5) = bulk / more often / bigger; Quality (#6) = newer / premium across ~17 dimensions.
4. Offer the bigger or better version **first**, then downsell to standard → ~20% overnight cash lifts.
5. Hormozi's favorite upsell: more of / more help with what they just bought — faster, less risk/effort/hassle, for more money.
