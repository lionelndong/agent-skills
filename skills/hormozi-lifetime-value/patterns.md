# Patterns ‚Äî $100M Playbook: Lifetime Value

## Pattern: Calculate LTGP (establish the baseline)
**When to use**: Before trying to raise LTV ‚Äî you can't increase what you haven't measured.
**How**: Step 1 ‚Äî find **gross profit** (revenue ‚àí delivery cost) and gross profit % for each product and overall. Step 2 ‚Äî find **average transactions per customer** (export data, average the count) OR, for recurring, **churn** (`left √∑ original`). Step 3 ‚Äî transactional: `Gross Profit √ó avg transactions`; recurring: `Gross Profit √∑ Churn`.
**Trade-offs**: Transaction counts are always estimates and rise as the business ages; double-check CRM figures with a back-of-napkin pass.

## Pattern: #1 Increase Prices
**When to use**: Almost always first ‚Äî pricing moves gross profit more than any other lever.
**How**: Maximize **conversion rate √ó LTGP**. Set a new price, compute the break-even conversion vs the old price, track the new conversion; if above break-even, keep going and test again until conversion √ó LTGP drops. Test every quarter.
**Trade-offs**: A few months of testing cost is real, but "under-monetized for life" costs more. If you overshoot, add bonuses or refund the difference.

## Pattern: Start Low, Then Go Up (price discovery)
**When to use**: New offer or unproven demand.
**How**: Launch low enough to make sales and surface "leaks." Once humming, nudge **+20% every ~10 sales** until a dramatic drop, then return to the prior sweet spot.
**Trade-offs**: Early low prices leave money on the table short-term; the point is validated demand and a known ceiling.

## Pattern: #2 Decrease Costs
**When to use**: To raise gross profit and scalability without touching price.
**How**: Pick the top 1‚Äì2 of: raise employee:customer ratio ¬∑ offshore talent ¬∑ productize delivery ¬∑ DFY‚ÜíDWY ¬∑ cap usage ¬∑ lifetime‚Üíannual ¬∑ in-person‚Üíremote ¬∑ cut meeting times ¬∑ buy in bulk & prepay (10‚Äì20% off).
**Trade-offs**: Cost can only fall to zero (price has no ceiling). Cut cost to deliver *the same thing* ‚Äî don't degrade the deliverable (that's a downsell).

## Pattern: #3 Increase # of Purchases
**When to use**: Get the same customer to buy the same thing more times.
**How**: Choose at least one ‚Äî **add recurring** (subscription/membership), **decrease churn** (stay-and-pay-longer; see Churn Checklist), **follow up** (quarterly promotion + value between).
**Trade-offs**: Recurring multiplies purchases even with high churn, but `Price √∑ Churn` means lowering churn compounds hardest.

## Pattern: #4 Cross-Sell Something Different
**When to use**: Add a complementary product without changing who you serve.
**How**: Pick a seamless add-on; quantify added LTV as **conversion rate √ó gross profit** of the cross-sell, added to the original LTV.
**Trade-offs**: "Don't break your business to pick up some extra change" ‚Äî must fit existing infrastructure/expertise.

## Pattern: #5 Sell More (Increase Quantity)
**When to use**: Lift order value with the same product.
**How**: Sell **bulk** (prepay a year, 12x), **more often** (monthly‚Üíevery 3 weeks, 1.33x), or **bigger** (1hr‚Üí3hr, 3x). Offer the bigger version **first**, then downsell to standard.
**Trade-offs**: ~20%+ overnight cash lifts reported; lead with the upsell or you leave the lift on the table.

## Pattern: #6 Sell Better (Increase Quality)
**When to use**: Lift order value with a newer or premium version.
**How**: Offer **newer** (new process/recipe/model) or **premium** (better ingredients/materials/people; or any of ~17 service dimensions ‚Äî speed, availability, ratio, personalization, DIY/DWY/DFY, etc.). Offer premium first, then downsell; or upgrade existing customers in a follow-up.
**Trade-offs**: ~20%+ lifts in upfront cash and overall LTV reported.

## Pattern: #7 Downsell Fewer (Lower Quantity)
**When to use**: Capture buyers who'd otherwise buy nothing.
**How**: Offer fewer / smaller / less-frequent of the same thing ‚Äî **only to prospects who don't qualify** for the main offer.
**Trade-offs**: You lose money if a qualified buyer takes it instead of the main offer ‚Äî "I forbid my sales team from selling a qualified person a downsell."

## Pattern: #8 Downsell Lower Quality
**When to use**: Same as #7, on the quality axis.
**How**: Offer the same *result* with a lower-quality experience (slower, riskier, more hassle) ‚Äî **reverse the #6 premium list** (junior staff, recorded vs live, remote vs in-person, no/worse guarantee, etc.). Offer only to unqualified buyers.
**Trade-offs**: Low operational drag ("you already do or make the stuff"); only the catch is cannibalization if mis-targeted.

## Pattern: Survey The Customers (resolve "what to sell next")
**When to use**: Disagreement over which back-end/upsell product to add.
**How**: Offer both candidates to existing customers and let them choose; build the winner. "The business serves the customer, not the other way around."
**Trade-offs**: In the source story this 2.2x'd LTV and enabled 5x ad spend; avoids "too far from the core" products that don't solve the next problem.
