# Patterns — $100M Branding Playbook

Every reusable technique in the playbook, as patterns. Each one stays in Hormozi's terms.

## Pair With What They Like
**When to use**: starting or strengthening any brand.
**How**: pair your business with something a majority of your *ideal* audience likes; avoid what they dislike; never settle for "no branding" (invisible). Score every pairing by money, not opinion.
**Trade-offs**: the same pairing pulls one audience toward and another away — so define the audience first.

## Measure Reach, Influence, Direction
**When to use**: diagnosing your brand or a competitor's.
**How**: track **reach** (how many know you), **influence** (chance it changes behavior), **direction** (toward/away). Map yourself among the **8 brand positions**.
**Trade-offs**: high influence is good only if direction is "toward" your *buyers*; high reach with wrong direction repels.

## Transfer-and-Sell (Brand → Money)
**When to use**: converting brand into profit.
**How**: pair the brand with liked things → it absorbs their influence/reach → add the brand to a generic product (now premium) → present it to the audience with an **offer**. Customers pay to claim the association.
**Trade-offs**: works only while premium-pricing profit exceeds pairing cost (Branding Break-Even Rule); takes time.

## Pick the Right Avatar (4 Criteria)
**When to use**: Step 1 of building/pivoting a brand.
**How**: choose a market that is **Growing**, **Has money**, **Easy to target**, **In Pain**. Narrow to provide more value per person; competition is a *plus* (proves demand).
**Trade-offs**: narrowing shrinks the raw pool but raises value-per-person and targeting efficiency.

## Find What They Like, Then Make + Distribute + Iterate
**When to use**: Steps 2–3.
**How**: list the audience's wants and pains; make content/products (free and paid) from *their* perspective; distribute; iterate on their responses. Give value away so you become top-of-mind.
**Trade-offs**: requires sustained output; results follow the audience's feedback, not your taste.

## Climb the Levels of Authority
**When to use**: turning attention into durable brand.
**How**: progress from **What We Say** (content/ads/outreach) → **What Other People Say** (affiliates, customers) → **What They Experience Themselves** (most authority). Engineer for real delivery so experience and word of mouth carry the brand.
**Trade-offs**: advertising moves brand fastest *short-term*; product/experience dominates *long-term*. Premium prices demand exceptional delivery.

## Grow in One of 5 Directions
**When to use**: Step 4 — enlarging the addressable market.
**How**: move **Up / Down / Adjacent / Broader / Narrower**; then associate with what the *new* avatar likes (the core audience, and the brand, changes accordingly).
**Trade-offs**: every move is a *bet* — aim to gain more than you lose; one-for-one swaps "only happen on paper."

## Tend the Bouquet
**When to use**: shaping or pivoting brand composition.
**How**: treat products/values/experiences/people as flowers; include only what fits the desired bouquet (narrow = red roses; broad = many flowers; adjacent = yellow roses). Prune what ideal customers dislike; add what they like.
**Trade-offs**: adding "sticks and weeds" (off-brand pairings) distorts clarity and weakens the whole.

## Overwhelm a Branding Mistake
**When to use**: after a bad pairing (it *will* happen).
**How**: don't try to erase the "dead rose" — overwhelm it with far more of the stuff the majority likes until the bad pairing "shrinks into irrelevance" (Kanye keeps selling post-cancellation via successive good pairings).
**Trade-offs**: recovery is slow and additive, not a one-time fix.

## Make Promises and Keep Them (Virtuous Cycle)
**When to use**: growing a *good* brand.
**How**: advertise → win first sale → deliver on promises → strong positive association → repeat purchase + referral. Make sure the product matches the promise so brand compounds upward.
**Trade-offs**: the same loop runs in reverse if you fail to deliver (vicious cycle, negative word of mouth compounds fastest of all marketing).

## Be Patient (Default Growth vs. Brand Bets)
**When to use**: choosing growth strategy.
**How**: low-risk path = "do more of what already works, do it better, show it to more people." Higher-risk/higher-reward path = *change* (a brand bet). Expect the payoff over years: "Wait five years and don't give up."
**Trade-offs**: brand under-earns direct response short-term, out-earns it long-term — which is why few do it.
