# Cheatsheet — $100M Branding Playbook

Decision rules and judgment from the playbook. Keep beside you while building a brand.

## The whole playbook in one line
Branding = **deliberately pairing your thing with what your ideal audience likes**, until they pay a premium to associate with you. Do the 4 steps, then prune forever.

## The 4 steps (do in order)
1. **Who** — figure out your ideal customer.
2. **What** — figure out what they like.
3. **Associate** — pair your stuff with those things (make → distribute → iterate).
4. **Optimize** — adjust associations for max profit (the 5 directions).

## Pick the avatar — all 4 boxes must be checked
| Box | Want | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Growing** | a rising market | you grow even staying the same size |
| **Has money** | can afford you | so you can charge a lot |
| **Easy to target** | findable with ads | uncatchable customers don't count |
| **In Pain** | desperately wants it | competition proves demand; most owners are beatable |

## Diagnose any brand: Reach × Influence × Direction
- **Reach** = do they know you? (output of *advertising*) — required; a brand can't exist without it.
- **Influence** = does seeing you change behavior?
- **Direction** = **toward** (buy/love) or **away** (avoid/hate)? — depends on the audience.
- Decision: **score brand with money.** Good = ideal audience likes *and buys*. Bad = they hate it and don't.

## Advertising vs. Branding (don't confuse them)
- Advertising = letting people know about your stuff (lets them know the cow belongs to *someone*).
- Branding = *what* they learn (the cow belongs to *you*). You can have great one and poor other.

## Climb the Levels of Authority (weak → strong)
1. **What We Say** (content, ads, outreach) — least influential.
2. **What Other People Say** (affiliates, customers) — matters way more.
3. **What They Experience** — most authority; governs word of mouth and long-run results.
- Rule: **advertising moves brand short-term; the product moves it long-term.** Premium price → product *must* be exceptional.

## Grow / pivot in 5 directions (each is a *bet*)
| Direction | Means | Bouquet |
|---|---|---|
| **Up market** | larger customers | — |
| **Down market** | smaller customers | — |
| **Adjacent** | similar-but-different | red roses → yellow roses |
| **Broader** | the whole category | add many flowers |
| **Narrower** | a slice of your market | only red roses |
- Rule: **gain more than you lose.** "One-for-one swaps only happen on paper." Don't let 5 mean comments cost you 500 new fans.

## Money math (the brand premium)
- Pricing power (Buffett): can you raise price 10% **without a prayer session**? If not, weak business.
- Break-even rule: **branded well when premium-pricing profit > cost of the pairings.**
- Reference example — same product, same $2k ad spend: branded vs unbranded ≈ **4.5x sales, 11x return on ad** (and likely repurchases vs. losing to a 10% undercut).

## Tells & smells
- **Accidental brand** = takes every podcast, sells any customer, chases every trend → "no one has any idea what they're about."
- **Wrong reach** = views go up but the *wrong* audience shows up (Hormozi's "wantrepreneurs") → recenter.
- **Bad pairing** = one rotten flower → "this bouquet sucks now."
- **Premium without delivery** = you get the first sale, never the repeat.

## When things go wrong / myths
- Made a bad pairing? **Don't erase it — overwhelm it** with more of what they like until it's irrelevant.
- "No such thing as bad publicity"? False. **Good beats bad. Bad beats none. Lots of good beats everything.**
- "Must be polarizing"? No. Be **widely positive** (Taylor Swift, Mother Teresa, Apple); just sell, and polarization takes care of itself.

## Final rule
**Patience.** Brand under-earns direct response short-term, out-earns it long-term. **Wait five years and don't give up.**
