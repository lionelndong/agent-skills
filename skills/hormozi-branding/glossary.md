# Glossary — $100M Branding Playbook

Terms, named frameworks, and Hormozi's working definitions from this playbook. Chapter references in parentheses.

- **Advertising** — "Letting people know about your stuff." Produces *reach*. Distinct from branding. (Ch 1)
- **Adjacent (market)** — A growth direction: a similar market, slightly different (e.g. rehab facilities for an assisted-living firm). Bouquet: red roses → yellow roses. (Ch 3)
- **Association / Pairing** — The core act of branding: tying your business to people, values, experiences, products the audience already feels something about. (Ch 1)
- **Bad branding** — Pairing your business with something a majority of your audience *dislikes*; they hate it and don't buy. (Ch 1)
- **Bad publicity** — Real, but better than none: "Good beats bad. Bad beats none. Lots of good beats everything." (Ch 1)
- **Bouquet Metaphor** — A brand is an assembled bouquet; products/values/experiences/people are the flowers. What you include (and exclude) defines the brand. Avoid "sticks and weeds." (Ch 3)
- **Brand** — The pairing that results when people associate you with things. Measured by *influence* and *direction*. (Ch 1)
- **Brand Goes Both Ways** — Brand affects whether they buy the product; the product then affects how they see the brand next time. (Ch 3)
- **Branding** — "Pairing two or more things together." "What you let them know in the advertisement." (Ch 1)
- **Branding Cycle** — See *Levels of Authority*. The virtuous (deliver → repeat + referral) or vicious (fail → never buy + warn others) loop. (Ch 3)
- **Branding Break-Even Rule** — You branded well whenever "the increased profit from your premium pricing covers the cost to make the pairings." (Ch 2)
- **Broader (market)** — A growth direction: the entire category (e.g. all senior living). Bouquet: add all kinds of flowers. (Ch 3)
- **CAC (lower)** — Benefit of brand: more customers for less; convert a higher % from advertising; new products sell "right out the gate." (Ch 2)
- **Cool factor** — The intangible association Hormozi's supplements lacked; why a friend wouldn't switch despite better pay and product. (Ch 2)
- **Direction** — "How their behavior changes — towards or away." Audience-dependent. One of two branding measures. (Ch 1)
- **Down Market** — A growth direction: smaller customers (e.g. at-home/mobile care). (Ch 3)
- **Eight (8) Brand Positions** — Reach × Influence × Direction, each high/low / towards-away = 8 positions; one brand can occupy any depending on who you ask. (Ch 1)
- **Good branding** — "Pairing your business with something a majority of your ideal audience likes"; they like and buy. (Ch 1)
- **Ideal-Avatar Criteria** — A target market that is Growing, Has enough money, Easy to target, and In Pain. (Ch 3)
- **Influence** — "The chance it changes behavior when they see it." One of two branding measures. (Ch 1)
- **Learning** — "Same condition, new behavior." The litmus test for whether content (incl. this playbook) worked. (Ch 1)
- **Levels of Authority** — Three camps, weakest→strongest: **What We Say** (content/ads/outreach) → **What Other People Say** (affiliates, customers) → **What They Experience Themselves** (most authority). (Ch 3)
- **LTV (raise)** — Benefit of brand: "Charge 2x, 5x, or 10x more than your competition for the exact same thing." (Ch 2)
- **Lower Risk** — Benefit of brand: customers buy again and again "without considering competition." (Ch 2)
- **Narrower (market)** — A growth direction: a slice of your existing market (e.g. centers with 100–500 residents). Bouquet: only red roses. (Ch 3)
- **Net Changes in Audience** — A brand pivot is a *bet*; only two outcomes — gain more than you lose, or lose more than you gain. "One-for-one swaps only happen on paper." (Ch 3)
- **No branding** — Exposure with no behavior change; "You're practically invisible." (Ch 1)
- **Offer** — How you deliberately pair the (now-premium) product with the audience to make money. (Ch 2)
- **One Page Branding Checklist** — The tear-off roadmap summarizing all four steps. (Ch 3 / cheatsheet)
- **Patience** — The final step; brand "has higher returns than direct response advertising… over a longer period." "Wait five years and don't give up." (Ch 3)
- **Pricing Power** (Buffett) — "If you've got the power to raise prices without losing business to a competitor, you've got a very good business." (Ch 2)
- **Polarization** — Selling polarizes people on its own; deliberately polarizing "interferes with doing business." You don't have to be polar to be strong (Taylor Swift, Mother Teresa, Apple). (Ch 1)
- **Reach** — "The amount of people who know about my stuff"; the output of advertising and "the primary element of a brand." (Ch 1)
- **Towards / Away** — The two values of *direction*: behavior moving toward (love/buy) or away (avoid/hate) the brand. (Ch 1)
- **Up Market** — A growth direction: larger customers (e.g. multi-location owners). (Ch 3)
- **Virtuous / Vicious Cycle** — Good branding compounds (deliver → repeat → referral); bad branding compounds the other way (fail → no repeat → negative word of mouth). (Ch 3)
- **What They Experience Themselves** — The highest level of authority; governs word of mouth and long-run advertising. (Ch 3)
