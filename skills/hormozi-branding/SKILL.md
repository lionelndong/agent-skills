## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What branding is
**"Branding means pairing two or more things together."** It happens whether you want it or not — people associate you with what you say, do, appear next to, and look like. The job is to do it on purpose and well.
- **Good branding** — pair your business with something **a majority of your ideal audience likes** → they like and buy.
- **Bad branding** — pair with something they **dislike** → they don't buy (net loss).
- **No branding** — exposure with no behavior change; "You're practically invisible."

### Branding vs. Advertising
"Advertising is letting people know about your stuff. Branding is **what** you let them know." Advertising says the cow belongs to *someone*; branding says it belongs to *you* (and your relative value). "You don't want them to buy products like yours, you want them to buy *your* products." The two are independent — great one + poor other, any combination.

### The three measurements
- **Reach** — how many people know about your stuff (output of advertising). "Reach is the primary element of a brand" — a brand can't exist without it.
- **Influence** — "the chance it changes behavior when they see it."
- **Direction** — behavior moves **towards** or **away**; audience-dependent. (Politicians = high influence, opposite directions.)
- Reach × Influence × Direction → **8 brand positions**; one brand can occupy any, depending on who you ask. **Measure your brand with money**: good = ideal audience likes *and buys*.

### Why brand makes money — the three benefits
Brand is the lowest-cost, longest-horizon way to do the only three things that grow a business:
1. **Lower CAC** — more customers for less; new launches sell "right out the gate" (The Rock).
2. **Raise LTV** — "Charge 2x, 5x, or 10x more… for the exact same thing" (Starbucks $5 vs $1).
3. **Lower Risk** — they buy again and again without considering competition (Mac users).

### How brand turns into money
"You **transfer** some of the influence and reach of what you pair with your product into the product itself," then pair the product with the audience via an **offer**. A strong brand turns a commodity (a $10 hat) into a *premium* product people pay to associate with. **Break-even rule**: you branded well whenever premium-pricing profit exceeds the cost of the pairings. **Pricing power** (Buffett): if you can raise price 10% without a "prayer session," you've got a good business.

### The 4-step process (start, grow, pivot)
1. **Who** — figure out your ideal customer.
2. **What** — figure out what they like.
3. **Associate** — pair your stuff with those things (**make → distribute → iterate** content/products, free and paid).
4. **Optimize** — adjust associations for max profit.
**Ideal-avatar criteria** (all four): **Growing · Has money · Easy to target · In Pain**. Narrow to "provide more value per person"; competition is a *plus*.

### Levels of Authority (the Branding Cycle)
Weakest → strongest source of authority:
1. **What We Say** — content, ads, outreach (least influential).
2. **What Other People Say** — affiliates, customers ("matters way more").
3. **What They Experience Themselves** — most authority; governs word of mouth long-term.
**Brand goes both ways**: brand drives the purchase; the product then drives the brand. "Advertising has a stronger influence short term; the product long term." Deliver, or the premium brand "won't keep the business."

### Optimize: the 5 directions + the Bouquet
Grow the addressable market by moving **Up / Down / Adjacent / Broader / Narrower** — then associate with what the *new* avatar likes. **The Bouquet Metaphor**: a brand is an assembled bouquet of products/values/experiences/people; narrow = only red roses, broad = many flowers, adjacent = yellow roses; never add "sticks and weeds." **Pivots are bets** — gain more than you lose ("one-for-one swaps only happen on paper"). Fix mistakes by **overwhelming** the bad pairing, not erasing it. Final step: **Patience** — brand out-earns direct response over the long run; "Wait five years and don't give up."
