# Chapter 22: The Wedding Cake Gym Profit Model

## Core Idea
The most advanced model — "minting new $1 million+ facilities every quarter." Two drivers maximize **profit per customer**, and every tier has both **Icing** (front-end cash) and **Cake** (recurring cash). Three tiers = **six revenue streams**.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Two Main Drivers**:
  - **Driver #1 — Accommodating Buying Curve**: let higher-spenders give you more. With a typical 80/20 split, the 2 strong customers can pay 3x–10x the 8 weak ones. Example: 10 customers × $100 = $1,000 (avg $100). vs. 8 × $100 + 2 × $600 = **$2,000** (avg $200) — "just adding this second level created 2x the revenue."
  - **Driver #2 — Maximize Buying Curves Per Customer**: dip into different **spending wallets/verticals** (fitness service, supplements, gear, meal prep). "The more of these value centers you can tap into, the more money you can make and… the more aggressively you can profitably spend and acquire customers."
- **Icing vs. Cake** (each tier has both):
  - **Icing** = front-end cash flow from new customers — lets you acquire profitably and "buy eyeballs." But "you cannot have a cake made entirely of icing" (an ad-account shutdown stops the flow).
  - **Cake** = recurring cash flow (EFT) from existing customers ascending and re-buying — "the backbone and stability." "When that hot day comes and the icing melts thin (and it inevitably will), you'll want… a consistent group of paying regulars."
- **The Three Tiers (the trifecta)**: 1) **Large-Group Training** · 2) **Semi-Privates** · 3) **Supplements** — "in that order." Plus **Internal Plays** as the "sprinkles" (a traffic source feeding all tiers, not a tier itself).
- **The Innovation Process** (how the model keeps improving): 1) find a problem many owners struggle with (lead gen / lead nurture / sales / fulfillment / ascension-retention) · 2) grab 20 gyms in 20 markets with the WORST of it · 3) study the top 10 CRUSHING it, itemize their techniques · 4) implement in the weaker gyms; keep if it works in **80%+** · 5) systematize and roll out to Gym Lords. (Gym Launch spends **$100,000+/month** testing.)

## Worked Example: The Wedding Cake Real Numbers (~$108K/month)
Deliberately conservative assumptions: 30 new customers/month + 4 high-ticket/month from client-financed acquisition, **65% conversion** to recurring, **8% attrition** (semis + large group), 23% supplement attrition.
- **Tier #1 Large Group**: Icing 30 × $500 = $15,000; Cake = 30 × 0.65 ÷ 0.08 = 243 recurring × $167 = $40,581 → **$55,581**.
- **Tier #2 Semi-Privates**: Icing 4 × $2,500 = $10,000; Cake = 4 × 0.65 ÷ 0.08 = 32 × $550 = $17,600 → **$27,600**.
- **Tier #3 Supplements**: Icing 34 × 0.85 = 29 buyers × $200 = $5,800 (feeds recurring); Cake = 29 ÷ 0.23 = 126 × $200 = **$25,200**.
- **Total (3 tiers, no sprinkles) = $108,381/month.** "These numbers are real and happen every single day."

## Mental Models
- "Information is the most valuable thing in the world. Because it buys you TIME." Owners don't sprout wings — "they just acquired a new skill set based on better information."
- "If your gym isn't growing, it's not that you need to work harder — you need to apply your work ethic to a better model."
- A good cake needs both icing and cake; neither survives alone.

## Anti-patterns
- Trying to live entirely off front-end cash (icing) with no recurring base.
- Starting five companies — the **WARNING**: most facilities lack the capacity/bandwidth/staff to truly run more than their main service; use the streams to feed the business without out-of-pocket risk.

## Key Takeaways
1. Maximize profit per customer two ways: accommodating buying curve + stacking verticals.
2. Every tier needs Icing (front-end cash) and Cake (recurring) — 3 tiers = 6 revenue streams.
3. Build the tiers in order (large group → semis → supplements) using conservative, real numbers (~$108K/month).
