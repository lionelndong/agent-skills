# Chapter 16: PROCESS — The Five Horsemen Of Retention

## Core Idea
Five simple "retention weapons" — the **silver bullets**, not the golden BBs — that move customers along the milestones and crush churn. "We have taken gyms at 10%, 15%, and higher monthly churn… down to under 5% within a month and under 3% within two to three months by just using these tactics."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Five Horsemen Of Retention**: 1) **Reach-Outs/Touch Points** · 2) **Attendance Tracking** · 3) **Handwritten Cards** · 4) **Member Events** · 5) **Exit Interviews.** "These five actions are your silver bullets… the small hinges that open up the vault door to lifetime value."
- **#1 Reach-Outs**: personally text/Messenger **every customer every 14 days** — praise progress, solve small issues. "SYSTEMATIZE communication, but never AUTOMATE it." **Leading indicator**: if someone takes >24 hours to respond, escalate to high priority. ("Communication is the best content." — Hormozi)
- **#2 Attendance Tracking**: run a no-show report **weekly, ideally by Wednesday**. **The #1 leading indicator of cancellation: making it to the gym 2 times or less in a week.** Intercept early ("I noticed you haven't been coming… I miss you").
- **#3 Handwritten Cards**: send at sign-up (with swag + event invite), to ask for referrals, and at milestones (3, 6, 12 months). **Ratio: every other card is an ask**; the card body is praise, only the **P.S.** is the ask. "The secret is not the card, it's what you do AFTER you send the card."
- **#4 Member Events**: regular social events (putt-putt, BBQ, pool party). "Hold events at regular intervals. Don't announce the intervals, just hold the events. **Every 21 days** works well." Secret sauce: pay for the babysitter, members get in free if they bring a friend, **require all new customers to attend**, send handwritten invites. Events lower churn (deeper connections) AND create warm referrals.
- **#5 Exit Interviews**: "If you do these right, you can save half… of your cancellations." Mention them on the contract (have new members initial). The script is a **re-sell run through C-L-O-S-E-R** — find the current obstacle and solve it. "Half the time, you haven't actually lost them. They just need to vent and be heard."

## Worked Example: The Referral Card System (10 hot leads/week)
Step 1: split customers into 4 groups (A/B/C/D). Step 2: names + phone + address into a spreadsheet. Step 3: one column per trainer. Step 4: split each trainer's list into 4 weekly groups. Step 5: trainer writes a card with the P.S.: *"Take a picture of this card and text it to a friend… who lives close to the gym, and their first month is on us."* Step 6: when the client comes in, the trainer asks, "Who did you send my card to?" (they forgot). Step 7: "Don't worry, I'm not heartbroken. But who would you have sent it to? Go grab your phone right now." Step 8: trainer records a 20-second video with the client for that friend. Step 9: group-text it to the prospect with the trainer's cell. Step 10: trainer follows up, adds the lead, schedules them. **The card gives the relational capital to make them feel a little guilty they didn't send it.**

## Key Concepts
- **Why personal training clients stay forever: relationships.** "The moment someone realizes a piece of communication is automated, they stop paying attention, because attention is not being spent on them. It's an exchange."
- **Churn math reminder**: 9% → 3% isn't a 6% gain, it's a **300% increase in LTV**. Saving 25% of cancellations = a **33% LTV increase**.
- **The decay pattern**: Week 1: 3 visits → Wk 2: 2 → Wk 3: 2 → Wk 4: 1 → Wk 5: CANCEL. Intercept at Week 2.
- **Five workouts in the first month** is the magic stick number (per Orangetheory's 2017 MBO Conference stats); swag worn to those 5 workouts helps.

## Mental Models
- "Be exceptional by doing the boring stuff that no one else wants to."
- "Lots of golden BBs but only a few silver bullets" — do the five, especially the first two.
- Scale personal touch with spreadsheets/CRM (even at 1,000+ clients, Gym Lords still use Google Sheets + client concierge roles).

## Anti-patterns
- Automating current-customer communication (kills the felt attention).
- Running attendance reports monthly ("way too late").
- Letting members cancel without an exit interview / in-person conversation.

## Key Takeaways
1. Reach out to every member every 14 days; never automate it.
2. Track attendance weekly — 2-or-fewer visits/week is the cancellation flag.
3. Handwritten cards (every other an ask), events every 21 days, and CLOSER-style exit interviews complete the system.
