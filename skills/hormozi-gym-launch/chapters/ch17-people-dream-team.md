# Chapter 17: PEOPLE — How To Build Your Dream Team

## Core Idea
**"Treat your team like gold, and they will treat your clients like gold."** Hire by an **org/compensation chart that grows with your EFT count**, hire *before* you need the role, and do **not** open a second location until you hit strict benchmarks.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Org Structure & Compensation Chart (by EFT count)**: roles are added left to right as membership grows. Positions: Owner · Trainers (hourly $15–$20) · Operations Manager · Challenge Manager · Retention Manager · Sales Manager · Admin. Rough ladder:
  - **0–100 EFTs**: Owner + 1–2 trainers + Ops Mgr (20 hrs, ~$1,300/mo).
  - **100–175**: + Ops Mgr full (~$1,700) + Challenge Mgr (20 hrs, ~$1,300).
  - **175–250**: Ops Mgr 40+ hrs (~$3,400).
  - **250–325**: + Retention Mgr (20 hrs, ~$1,300).
  - **400+**: + Sales Mgr(s) (~$1K + commission) + 2×20-hr managers + Admin ($10/hr); Ops Mgr gets profit share.
- **Hire BEFORE you need the position**: gives time to find, hire, train, and test (may take a few tries). "Plateaus happen when there is a missing person or process… causing a leak."
- **Sales Manager hired LAST**: not because of pay but **lost opportunity** — "Rarely will someone sell as well as the owner/entrepreneur does," so the business loses money on missed sales while you train them. Hire after a healthy margin and money in the bank.
- **Second-Location Benchmarks** (don't even THINK about it until ALL are true): 1) **$20K/month profit for six straight months** · 2) staff handling all operations (including sales) during that time · 3) the gym grew during those six months · 4) **$200,000 minimum in the bank.** "Gym owners with multiple locations often make LESS money, because they scaled problems not processes."

## Key Concepts
- **One super-profitable gym > many break-even gyms**: Joey Huber grew from 250 to **800+ EFTs at $200+/month from one location.**
- **More vs. Better**: "Most owners only open a second or third location because they don't know how to make more with their existing gym. They mistakenly believe the only opportunity for growth is MORE instead of BETTER."
- **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs)**: as you scale, layer specialists (operations, phone sales, high-ticket sales, retention, automation) — cross-train first, then specialize.

## Worked Example: Why The Org Grows
Gym Launch started with **one** support rep who "did everything for everyone." Now: **37+ support reps**, each cross-trained initially, then specialized (ads, automation, funnels), with SMEs layered on top. "This should explain why the organization grows as the membership grows" — large corporations subdivide and specialize because they have so much business.

## Mental Models
- "The first person you should put on payroll is YOU."
- A business is "an organic entity… people are not machines — they must be watered and tended to like a garden."
- "I have never fired someone and not found someone better to replace them."

## Anti-patterns
- Treating the team poorly and wondering why clients are unhappy.
- Hiring hastily because you need someone *now* (you won't choose wisely).
- Opening a second location before the four benchmarks (scaling problems, not processes).

## Key Takeaways
1. Treat the team like gold; staff up by the EFT-based org chart, hiring ahead of need.
2. Hire the sales manager last (highest lost-opportunity cost).
3. One super-profitable gym beats many break-even ones; gate the second location behind $20K/mo × 6 months + $200K in the bank.
