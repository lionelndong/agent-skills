# Chapter 26: Sprinkles — Internal Plays

## Core Idea
An **internal play** is a marketing campaign aimed at your **existing** customers. Not a tier — a **traffic source / second funnel** that feeds all the other tiers. "A lack of these is usually the biggest gaping hole that I come across in gym businesses." ~Only 5% of gyms do this right.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Internal Play**: an added service/benefit beyond current membership, marketed to existing customers. The hardest part is a good **HOOK** — "something NEW OR DIFFERENT that invokes curiosity or desire."
- **The Three Internal Play Hook Types**:
  - **Nutrition-Based** (hardest to make, highest price, best for upselling ancillary services): e.g. "Lose While You Booze," "Lean By Halloween," "21-Day Detox," "Eat More To Lose More," "Eat Fat To Lose Fat."
  - **Fitness-Based** (body-part focused, easy to sell/execute, lower price): "Big Booty Bootcamp," "Cellulite Dynamite," "6-Week 6-Pack," "Buns & Guns," "Sexy Back."
  - **Community/Experience** (easiest to fulfill, hardest to show value — value = external motivation via teams/competition): "Bring-A-Friend Challenge," "Spouse Challenge," "Battle Of The Sexes," "Coworker Challenge." Pair with charity + an end event.
- **Seasons/Holidays answer the "why"**: tie a challenge to a holiday ("Slim Down For Santa," "Turkey Tone-Up") — it answers "why are they doing this?" and adds urgency. **Manufacturing events and deadlines** + FOMO (jerseys, banners, photo shoots, fake podiums) drive sign-ups.
- **The Three Monetization Paths**: 1) **Increase average large-group customer value** (retention + blended EFT) · 2) **Funnel into HTP** (25–35% of participants, ~8–10 people, maintains semis without a big external pop) · 3) **Conversion point for supplements** (the play becomes a paid-for sales consultation). "Internal plays satisfy all three" of Acquire / Ascend / Resell (Ch 2).

## Worked Example: Big Booty Bootcamp → $4,500 (and stacking to $11,580)
("Why that name? Because my '6-Week Deadlift and Squat Seminar' really doesn't convert well with the primary audience — women. Marketing matters.") 120 EFTs → ~25% take internal offers = 30 people. Price $150 (low-ticket, **$0 marketing cost** — you own the contacts). 30 × $150 = **$4,500** (~90% margin). Fulfillment: one 60-min "specialty" session/week, 1-on-10, on a weekend = 18 sessions × 10 people. Pay the trainer $25/session, keep $225 → 90% margin.
**Stacking the ancillaries** turns a $99 play into far more — take rate / cost / vertical / added ticket: 100% / $99 / large-group cleanse program / +$99 · 90% / $167 / supplements cleanse bundle / +$150 · 25% / $550 / semi-private / +$137 (month 1) = **$386 total per customer.** 30 people: from 30 × $99 = $2,970 → 30 × $386 = **$11,580.**

## Key Concepts
- **Why internal plays aren't a tier**: "they are really more of a traffic source. They feed the rest of the tiers but aren't another distinct profit center."
- **Member engagement as a health barometer**: ~25% take rate is normal (best gyms hit **60%**) — "a great barometer of gym health" and goodwill.
- **"Advertainment"**: a 10-day countdown of funny daily videos + emails + texts so it doesn't feel annoying.
- **~A third of sign-ups can come from outside** (free, via bring-a-friend pricing).
- "You could do this tomorrow, with zero prep. That, in my mind, is a grand slam."

## Mental Models
- Sprinkles make the whole cake more delicious — they strengthen every tier when combined.
- Internal plays are a low-effort, ~90%-margin, all-profit way to keep members engaged AND a second funnel into HTP and supplements.
- "You can be a purist or a realist. I choose the latter" (hence "Big Booty Bootcamp" over "Deadlift Seminar").

## Anti-patterns
- Not running internal plays at all (the biggest gaping hole in most gyms).
- Treating the play as a standalone profit center instead of a funnel into the tiers.

## Key Takeaways
1. Internal plays market to existing customers via a great hook (Nutrition / Fitness / Community), ideally tied to a holiday.
2. They're a traffic source feeding all tiers, monetized three ways (blended EFT, HTP funnel, supplement conversion).
3. ~90% margin, ~zero prep, ~$3K–$4.5K every six weeks — and stacking ancillaries can ~4x per-customer revenue.
