# Chapter 7: The Broken Profit Model

## Core Idea
The microgym's core problem: **the number of members needed to be profitable at industry-average prices usually exceeds the gym's reasonable capacity.** The gym hits its ceiling *before* it ever reaches adequate profit. Fix it with **three macro profit levers**, each containing **micro levers**.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Three Macro Profit Levers** (Gym Profit Levers™): **#1 Price Levers** (pricing, packages, memberships) · **#2 Capacity Levers** (the "beefiest" — all the ways to increase facility capacity) · **#3 Overhead Levers** (compensation, fulfillment, streamlining — "it's not what you make, it's what you take home").
- **Macro vs. Micro Levers**: macro levers are the major initiatives; within each are smaller **micro levers** you pull to move the macro lever. "Think of these levers like the handles on the side of a slot machine (your gym). Pull them all and pull them right, and cash will start flowing out."
- **Micro levers per macro lever**:
  - **Price**: number of members · billing cycle (weekly vs. monthly) · price.
  - **Capacity**: sessions/week · session duration · membership levels · attendance · physical space · exercise selection.
  - **Overhead**: number of sessions fulfilled · total work hours · cost of those hours.

## Worked Example: The Maxed-Out-But-Broke Gym
A large-group facility caps at 16/session, runs 7 sessions/day. 16 × 7 = 112/day at perfect fit; at 85% industry attendance you could carry **131 members** (112 ÷ 0.85). But even **fully maxed at 131 members** you gross only **$15,720/month**, and after rent ($4,000), payroll ($4,000 for 163 sessions @ $25), utilities/cleaning ($800), CRM/software/processing ($2,000), marketing ($1,000), misc ($1,000), you net **$2,920/month** — with **$0 for admin, $0 for sales commissions, $0 for staff education.** Pay an admin $1,000 → **$1,920/month**. At 80+ hours/week that's an effective wage of **$9.13/hour** ($2,920 ÷ 320 hrs). "This is where most gym owners live… and eventually burn out."

## Mental Models
- The levers are "all interrelated… one massive profit system" but Hormozi separates them to teach. They work best **pulled all at once** ("cut once, cut deep" — Ch 8/11).
- "It reaches capacity BEFORE the point of adequate profitability, which means that something needs to change or the business will not survive."

## Anti-patterns
- Believing "if I just fill the gym I'll be rich" — a maxed gym at average prices still barely profits.
- Running a gym as a "nonprofit" that breaks even while you feed yourself from side personal training.

## Key Takeaways
1. Capacity caps out before profitability at average prices — the model itself is broken.
2. Three macro levers (Price, Capacity, Overhead) each have specific micro levers to pull.
3. Pulling levers in unison produces the biggest, fastest turnaround.
