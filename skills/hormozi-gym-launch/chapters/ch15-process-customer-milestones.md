# Chapter 15: PROCESS — How To Give Consistently Amazing Customer Experiences

## Core Idea
Define the **"ideal scene"** — the four **customer milestones** every member should hit. "It's really hard to hit a target when you don't know where it is." Members sign up for the little black dress but **stay for the relationships.**

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Four Customer Milestones (Ideal Customer Life Cycle)**: **Connect → Refer/Testify → Testify/Refer → Ascend.** (Refer and Testify swap order because some people refer friends to join *with* them, others only refer *after* a good experience.) Each builds on the prior — someone who isn't connected won't testify; someone who won't testify won't spend relational capital to refer.
  - **Connect**: the first few weeks decide how a member feels forever. "Once they feel they have a good relationship with your business, they'll cut you some slack" — this is why OGs stay while new members leave. Get connection formed **as quickly as possible**.
  - **Refer/Testify**: "If you are doing something remarkable, they'll 'remark' to others." Hormozi's own business is **40%+ referral-driven** — his main KPI for how well he's serving.
  - **Testify/Refer**: get reviews on Yelp/Facebook/Google. **Three methods**: (1) **Inspiration** — frame it as helping others; take their "after" photo and have them post immediately; (2) **Requirement** — make completing the program include leaving a review (you can require *a* review, never a *5-star* one — that's deceptive trade practice); (3) **Ethical Bribe** — free swag for screenshotting all three reviews. "A $5 t-shirt can make you thousands."
  - **Ascend**: "what you sell someone after you've already sold them something" — an **upsell** (large group → semi-private) or **cross-sell** (supplements). "The money is always in the back end."
- **Category Economics / Category King**: "all the money in a category naturally flows to a category king… leaving very little for anyone else." Being anything less than spectacular won't get you there. ("Half the people reading this are below average" — mathematically, one in two gyms is.)

## Key Concepts
- **A few bad reviews help**: 900 reviews at 4.6 stars beats 29 reviews at 5.0 — "There is certainty in 'the herd.'" A few bad reviews make the good ones believable.
- **Ideal scene**: define the ideal customer experience, then hit it consistently by isolating variables and executing until the holes are plugged.

## Mental Models
- Connection is the gateway — without it, no testimonial, no referral, no ascension.
- "If you are simply doing a good, average, or slightly above-average job, then you are unlikely to get many referrals except from influencer-type people who can't help themselves."

## Anti-patterns
- Letting new members fade "with a whimper… into the random-reason cancellation pile" because connection never formed.
- Coercing 5-star reviews (illegal/unethical) instead of making a review part of the optional program.

## Key Takeaways
1. Define the ideal customer life cycle: Connect → Refer → Testify → Ascend.
2. Connect fast — the first few weeks set the whole relationship.
3. Engineer referrals and testimonials deliberately; aim to be the category king.
