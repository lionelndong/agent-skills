# Chapter 9: Capacity-Profit Levers

## Core Idea
The "beefiest" lever. Most gyms cap their own capacity through one bottleneck (a single exercise, unlimited memberships, 60-minute sessions). **Five micro levers** let you get MORE from LESS without opening another location.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Five Capacity Micro Levers**: 1) **Membership Levels** (how many sessions someone can attend) · 2) **Attendance** (% of scheduled sessions actually attended + how evenly spread) · 3) **Duration** (session length + breaks) · 4) **Physical Space** (usable fitness space) · 5) **Exercise Selection** (because walking barbell lunges take more room than sit-ups).
- **The "Unlimited" Problem → Solutions**: "Anything that's unlimited is perceived as less valuable" (no one offers unlimited personal training). Solutions: (a) **call them sessions, not classes** — language matters; (b) **go from unlimited to 3x/week** (one tweak that **doubles capacity overnight**) — assign Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat, and **always offer the less-popular option first**; (c) **charge more** (limited resources are valued more). Script if asked: "If you can come whenever you want, it really becomes never."
- **No-Show Fee** (from Orangetheory): ~$15/no-show. Does three things at once — increases attendance, decreases attrition (more attendance = longer stay), and becomes a separate revenue stream. Sell it as "your extra nudge to get to the gym when you don't feel like it."
- **Attendance Spread Tactic**: offer session times **in reverse order of popularity** (start with the least-crowded slot, e.g. Tue/Thu/Sat 8am). "People will make things work if they are asked to solve a problem" — you add a member without adding to busy times.
- **45-Minute Back-To-Back Sessions** (Duration): replace standard 60-min sessions + 15-min breaks with **45-min back-to-back** sessions. Splits a packed hour into two with **no payroll increase** → +33% capacity alone, **+66%** combined with the 3x/week change. (Small facilities ≤15: switch to **30-min sessions**.)
- **Partners** (Physical Space): pair everyone up — one rests/spots/cheers while the other works. "Partners DOUBLE the number of people you can have in your space" — plus safety, community, encouragement.
- **Convert Office To Fitness Space**: kill the office; the "sales room" becomes a portable cubby in the lobby. Often adds 4 people/session → over 7 daily sessions + 3x/week = **+56 EFT max capacity (~$11,000/month)**. "Work ON the business, not IN it."

## Worked Example: The Double-Under Jump Rope
Josh Price's gym (3,000 sq ft, 70 EFTs @ $100) insisted it could only service **14 people/class** — because of **double unders** (they take huge space, people whip each other, most can't do them, trip risk). Hormozi grabbed a jump rope, held it to their noses: "This is strangling your business. Are you going to let a jump rope kill your gym?" One year later (after removing the bottleneck), the gym averaged **$45,000/month** with $30,000+ recurring EFT. The lesson: one space-hungry exercise can bottleneck the entire business.

## Mental Models
- The intent of the Attendance lever is **NOT** to get people to come less (that spikes attrition) — it's to **spread** attendance evenly and maximize "fullness" of each session.
- "Effort is a COST, not a benefit" — shorter, harder sessions are easier to sell and people are happier.
- Two-Tier Programming (regressive parallel workouts) solves the **Onboard Problem** and **Jump Rope Problem** at once — see Ch 10's bridge / the regressive-programming play.

## Anti-patterns
- Selling unlimited memberships (perceived as low-value, caps your math).
- Letting one complex/space-hungry exercise (double unders, Olympic lifts) dictate class size.
- Moving to a bigger location before maximizing every capacity lever ("lots of headache for not a lot of benefit").

## Key Takeaways
1. Switch unlimited → 3x/week and call them "sessions" — doubles capacity instantly.
2. Spread attendance (reverse-order offers + no-show fee) and run 45-min back-to-back sessions.
3. Partner people up and convert your office to floor space before ever leasing more.
