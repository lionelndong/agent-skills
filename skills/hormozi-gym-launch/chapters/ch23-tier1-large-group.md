# Chapter 23: Tier 1 — Large-Group Training

## Core Idea
The base of the cake. The **Icing** is your high-ticket defined-end front-end program (the irresistible offer); the **Cake** is the EFT, sold via **three conversion attempts** during a member's first six weeks.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Large-Group Icing**: the front-end, defined-end program you market (a 6-week / 42-day / 28-day challenge) at $400–$1,000. It's also your **indoctrination "get-them-to-love-you" program** — put everyone (even referrals) through it because it's automated, starts them in a cohort (camaraderie), and takes a big up-front cash commitment (less fall-off). Top converter: the **6-Week Challenge** (42 days — "just enough time for people to believe a significant outcome can occur, but short enough that they think they can accomplish it").
- **The Three EFT Conversion Attempts**:
  1. **Bribe** (Day 1, hyper-responsive buyers): price-anchor the program high ($599+), then "we'll give you the entire program FREE if you become a member today." They prepay 6 weeks ($294 at $49/wk) and go straight onto recurring — making the mental shift from front-end customer to member. (Anchor at $799+ and 90% take the membership; some still take the $799 — "great customers who will buy EVERYTHING.")
  2. **Abandon Hope Sale** (Week 3 / halfway — the **biggest** conversion point if you skipped #1): a "mandatory halfway meeting" where you provide value/encouragement, re-ask the original goal/sustainability/permanent-weight-loss questions, then make an irresistible offer — "I'll credit all the money you paid as credit off a year membership, finish the program on me, and give you a lifetime lower rate." An **assumed close** that can close **95%** at the halfway point.
  3. **Last Chance** (weigh-out / final day): give credit based on weight/body-fat lost, then **change the terms** (not the price — different prices for the same service is illegal **price discrimination**) to back into your target EFT. "Sell to goal" — set the membership duration yourself and work backward.
- **"Manipulating numbers, not people"**: "It's in their best interest to sign up… so you have to sell the plan in a way that makes sense to them."

## Worked Example: Abandon Hope Sales Math
Week 3 of a 6-week, $600 challenge. Offer: remaining 3 weeks free if they sign a 12-month EFT. Desired EFT $49/week (explained as ~$199/month); inflated EFT $249/month. Script: "I'll personally credit your $600 toward our year-long program… instead of our normal $249/month, we'll knock off $50, bringing you to about $200 a month. We bill weekly, so you'll only pay $49/week… we'll honor that rate for the duration you're with us. But this is the only time you get this offer. Want to just use the card we already have on file? Sign here, initial the price." **"If you follow the money, all that really happened was they signed up for $600 and three weeks later signed up for a recurring weekly EFT at the full price."** ~90% of EFTs come from this halfway sale.

## Key Concepts
- **Last Chance "terms" math**: a man loses 13 lbs → $25/lb = $325 credit. Spread over 6 months = $54/month off → $250 − $54 = **$196/month** (right back to target). "If you can control the terms around the credit, then you can back into whatever number you want."
- **"Sell to goal" / Maintenance phase** (from Cris Cawley): make the membership duration up to you — e.g. "15 lbs at your rate = 4 months, then recomposition = 3 months, then maintenance the same length you dieted = 14 months total." Justifies the terms you want.
- **No Excuses (Last Chance)**: "No one needs to think about it… talk to their spouse… check their budget. They just had six weeks of the service… If they don't say yes immediately, they just don't want to make a decision." Counterintuitively, **people who haven't hit their goal are MORE likely to sign up** than those who hit it but haven't converted.

## Mental Models
- "No one wants a membership. They want a result and a deadline" — so sell the defined-end program first (Ch 2's downsell-your-upsell).
- "The amount of money you make is directly proportional to the amount of goodwill you have… times the number of offers you make." (Frank Kern) — so the offers (3 conversions) must crush.

## Anti-patterns
- Offering different prices to different people for the same service (illegal price discrimination — only change *terms*).
- Skipping the indoctrination program for referrals (breaks price uniformity + the love-you onboarding).
- Letting anyone walk out "undecided" on the Last Chance — "this is very much a sale."

## Key Takeaways
1. Icing = a high-ticket defined-end program (6-Week Challenge converts best); it's also indoctrination.
2. Convert to EFT in three attempts: Bribe (Day 1) → Abandon Hope (Week 3, closes ~95%) → Last Chance (weigh-out).
3. Manipulate numbers/terms, never people or prices — sell to goal and back into your target EFT.
