# Chapter 21: PRODUCT — How To Run An Amazing Session Every Time

## Core Idea
Each training session is your product — your **hamburger** — and layering the "ingredients" in the right order makes a great experience near-automatic. "Even a poor trainer who uses this… will still leave most people feeling better when they leave than when they came in."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Hamburger Workout**: a fixed session structure (the ingredients) that produces a consistent great experience regardless of trainer skill.
  - **Prep Work**: clean bathrooms (where people change); **fresh, FIRE playlist every day** — clean/no profanity, two set volumes (between vs. during session).
  - **When They Walk In**: greet by name **within 10 seconds**; check them into the system; encourage a **social-media check-in**; introduce new clients to OGs every workout of their **first week** (color name tags / wristbands); **pair each new person with an OG** for week one; **use a mic** (saves trainers' voices/CNS, lets everyone hear); make announcements **during warm-up**; use an **automated timer** (trainers should COACH, not count reps); demonstrate **no more than two exercises** before setting people loose.
  - **During**: call out each person **twice by name** for something good; give each person **two correctional cues** ("so they feel like they're getting their money's worth").
  - **Close**: reset stations → meet in the middle → **hands-in moment**; repeat announcements + pass clipboards for **internal promotions** (easy commission sales); call out 1–2 people (especially new ones); end with a **3-2-1 cheer**.
- **"Live What You Lead"**: "If you are not the type of person who parks your car across the street to let your customers have the best parking spaces, then your employees won't be either. A business is always a reflection of the owner."

## Key Concepts
- **A great trainer in a crappy structure beats a crappy trainer in a great structure** — "but you might as well have a great structure… so even mediocre trainers seem great."
- **Product = workout structure + the people executing them.** "Their energy, vibe, and smiles will dramatically attract customers."
- **Internal-promo sign-ups during the close** let trainers "mop up" easy commission sales every session.

## Worked Example: The Brian Story (and its lesson)
Brian, Hormozi's first trainer, was "too consistent" — he had clients do **sleds 38 days straight** (a woman counted) and induced vomiting in newbies with post-sled burpees, cranking gangster rap in a 75%-female Orange County gym. The fix wasn't a personality; it was a **structure** (the Hamburger Workout) that any trainer can execute the same way every time, so the experience doesn't depend on the trainer's instincts or mood.

## Mental Models
- Think of the session as a stack of ingredients layered for a predictable, repeatable result — "The fundamentals will always be the same."
- "Treat EVERYONE like royalty. And you'll live like royalty."

## Anti-patterns
- Trainers counting reps/tracking time instead of coaching (use an automated timer).
- Demonstrating many exercises at once (people forget, ask the trainer to repeat).
- No first-week integration (new people never connect → churn).

## Key Takeaways
1. Standardize the session (the Hamburger Workout) so great experiences don't depend on trainer talent.
2. Greet by name in 10 sec, pair newbies with OGs week one, two callouts + two cues per person, end with a hands-in + 3-2-1 cheer.
3. Live what you lead — the gym mirrors the owner.
