# Chapter 24: Tier 2 — Semi-Privates (1-on-4)

## Core Idea
The most profitable tier — **HTP** (hybrid training protocol to clients / high-ticket program to you). Priced like personal training ($500–$700/mo) at 4x the revenue per slot. Sold with a **two-step setter→closer process** because **"the set is the close."**

## Frameworks Introduced
- **HTP (Hybrid Training Protocol / High-Ticket Program)**: 1-on-4 semis at $500–$700/month ($125–$175/week) for three sessions. "You get 4x the revenue per session with the same costs." (Hormozi accepts a **75% margin** here vs. 80%+ for large group.)
- **Creating Demand ("dig your well before you're thirsty")**: start semis **second**, only after large group is **85% full** (packed times full, off-peak 60–70%). Once large group is full it "flows over" into the next level. With 200+ EFTs you'll easily get **10% (~20 people) to upgrade** from a small internal campaign (+$8,000/month net).
- **Semi-Private Icing — the Two-Step Sales Process**: HTP is a 12-week transformation sold for **$2,000–$3,000**. Because that's too much for a single cold visit, use a **setter** (phone) → **closer** (in person). **"The longer the runway, the bigger the takeoff."**
  - **The set is the close**: "If you close the $100 [consult fee] over the phone, you'll close them when they come in. If you do NOT… there's a 90% chance you will not close them in person." Close the **$50–$100 nonrefundable consult fee** or don't bother scheduling.
  - The setter **edifies the closer** (acts as a gatekeeper, treats "Brad" the master trainer like a demigod), and confirms a **decision-maker** is present and a payment method is brought.
  - "The sales PROCESS always has to match the PROMOTION and the PROSPECT."
- **Semi-Private Cake**: price the front-end so $2,500 ≈ $200/week and $3,000 ≈ $250/week — so you can **downsell your upsell** into ~$125/week ongoing (almost half price). Back-end retention is **near 100%** ("the lowest maintenance despite paying the most" — the virtuous price cycle).

## Worked Example: The High-Ticket Consult Script (and Ryan's numbers)
The script: small talk → "what motivated you to schedule? why me, why now?" → future-pace ("take me 12 months into the future, describe your perfect life") → drill into the emotional WHY ("what's important about that? how will it affect other areas of your life?") → "if I could take you quickly to losing that weight… how long would you want to wait to start?" (if not "right now," challenge once, then disqualify) → "I have something that might do just that. Let me know if you'd like me to tell you about it." Then **SHUT UP** and let them ask. Reveal the program, get a yes, **do NOT volunteer the price** — make them ask. Then: "the total investment is $2,497, but because you showed up today and sound like such a good fit, I can do it for $1,997." Then "How do you want to make that investment?"
**Ryan Karas** ran HTP exactly as outlined: paid **$42/lead**, closed **50%** onto a $2,500 program, made **$135K in three weeks** — the next time, **$300K+ in three weeks.** "Do you think he cared about the $40 leads? Yeah, me neither." Cool math: pay $4,000 for 100 leads → sell 55 on a $100 nonrefundable appointment fee (ad cost now covered) → close 50/55 on a $2,500 package.

## Key Concepts
- **Higher-quality leads close higher** — copy is geared to the affluent, the promo never says "free," so they expect to pay.
- **Clumpy distribution**: experienced owners run HTP only every quarter or six months because the stick rate is so high — they may have run it only 3–4 times total.
- **Targeting + sales process** are the two differences from the large-group front end: copy calls out wealthier folks; the two-step process replaces the no-qualification show.

## Mental Models
- "The set is the close. Keep saying it in your sleep."
- "Going from large group to small group automatically makes everyone feel special, and it's not nearly as hard as you may think."

## Anti-patterns
- Launching semis before large group is full (no demand; "exclusive" 1-on-4 means nothing if your average class is 1-on-8).
- Scheduling a high-ticket consult without closing the consult fee (90% won't close in person).
- Volunteering the price before the prospect asks.

## Key Takeaways
1. HTP = 1-on-4 at $500–$700/mo; start it only after large group is 85% full.
2. Sell the $2K–$3K front end with a setter→closer two-step — the set is the close (close the $50–$100 consult fee).
3. Price it so you can downsell into ~$125/week ongoing; back-end retention nears 100%.
