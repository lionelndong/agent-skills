# Chapter 20: PRODUCT — How To Motivate Unmotivated Trainers

## Core Idea
Trainers **are the product** ("the widget you are selling"). "If people are leaving your gym, it's because your products or services are subpar… it's your fault." Motivate trainers **extrinsically** (money) first to build trust, then **intrinsically** (the six human needs).

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Extrinsic vs. Intrinsic Motivation**: extrinsic = external rewards (money) — start here before they trust you. Intrinsic = a higher cause/internal purpose — "where people would jump on a sword for you." 
- **The Three Trainer Income Streams (extrinsic)**: 1) **selling supplements** · 2) **selling internal challenges** (to existing members) · 3) **fulfilling/retaining semi-private clients (25% of gross revenue)**. "Sales is the primary way they can make more money."
- **The Six Human Needs (intrinsic motivation)** — three pairs:
  - **Significance & Connection**: praise + publicly recognize (trainer of the month); involve them in events and handwritten cards so they build empathy for clients.
  - **Variety & Certainty**: let them help design the workouts (they hate having programming taken away); for a lazy trainer, dictate movement patterns/rep schemes but let them pick exercises/finishers. **Certainty = pay them on time, every time** ("the moment you are late… that trust is broken forever") + show up as a steady, even-keeled leader.
  - **Growth & Contribution**: trainers are growth-oriented — ask "what book can I buy you?", pay for 30 min/week of personal development/certs; tie them into the business vision and ask what they'd do to better serve clients ("they will totally 'own' any ideas they come up with themselves").
- **"You cannot lead what you do not live"**: "If you feel like your trainers do not respect you, then there is no one to blame but yourself. The culture is your fault."

## Worked Example: The Entrepreneurial Trainer's Income Ladder
Base: 20 hrs/week large group × $15 = $300/wk = **$15,600/yr**. Add 9 semi-private clients ($150 × 0.25 × 9 = $337.50/wk) = **$17,550/yr** → already **$33,150/yr** (2x base). Add supplements (sell 80% of ~15 new clients/month × $15 avg commission = $180/mo) → **$35,310/yr**. Add internal plays (sell 25% of 65 clients into a $150 play at $25–$50 commission = ~$600/mo) → **$42,510/yr**. "A good living for wearing shorts and short sleeves… and hanging out with people you like." Double the semis (9→18) → **$60,000+/yr**. "Your outcome and their outcome do not need to be mutually exclusive… they're intertwined."

## Mental Models
- "When presenting a new pay structure… sell the future of what is possible" — trainers will WANT to run large groups to recruit/upsell into high-ticket semis. Win-win.
- "Three positives for every piece of corrective feedback."
- Brian the gangster-rap trainer (sleds 38 days straight) had "the right attitude but not aptitude and… was not coachable" fast enough — a cautionary tale on coachability.

## Anti-patterns
- Taking session programming entirely away from trainers (kills their Variety).
- Paying trainers late (irreparably breaks Certainty/trust).
- Blaming trainers/culture instead of yourself ("if you do, you give someone else the power over your business").

## Key Takeaways
1. Trainers are the product — start with extrinsic motivators (supplements, internal challenges, 25% semi rev-share).
2. Then satisfy the six human needs (Significance/Connection, Variety/Certainty, Growth/Contribution).
3. You can't lead what you don't live; pay on time, praise publicly, invest in their growth.
