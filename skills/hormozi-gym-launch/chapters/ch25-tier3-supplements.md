# Chapter 25: Tier 3 — Supplement Sales

## Core Idea
The third tier — the first **"stacking" buying curve** into a new wallet. "Within 14 to 21 days, we can add an average $1,000 in sales on recurring for every 7 EFTs." Sold as a **prescriptive assumed-close upsell**, the most powerful kind of sale.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Supplements as a new wallet**: the first tier that taps a *different* spending vertical (their "supplement wallet," after their "fitness classes" wallet). "The more wallets we can access… the more money we can make from the same customer."
- **The Two Guiding Principles** (restated as the book's thesis): "**The business owner who makes his prospect more valuable to his business than to that of his competition wins.**" A more tactical take on "He who can spend the most to acquire a customer will win." You make them more valuable via high-ticket service AND by satisfying as many needs as possible.
- **The Supplement Company Criteria** (use a company that does ALL of these): **drop-ship** (no inventory/up-front cost), 3/6/12-month packages to sell up front, **0% financing** on $2,000–$5,000 packages (client pays $200–$400/month, you get paid immediately), solid **autoship** (recurring cash), **sticky commissions**, rep-level data tracking, site price **40% higher** than your sell price (never get price-shopped), premium positioning + celebrity/PhD endorsements, proprietary blends, high-margin products only, beautiful retail selection (no inventory cash), genuinely best product, follow-up marketing (+30% ticket), **bimonthly payouts**. (No company did all this, so Hormozi built **Prestige Labs** — 8 figures invested, buying 50,000+ units per flavor for economies of scale.)
- **The Four Ways To Sell Supplements**: 1) existing-client-base launch (commemorative internal campaign) · 2) bolt-on for new customers · 3) part of ongoing internal plays · 4) sampling sprees.
- **Prescriptive Assumed-Close Upsell**: it's not a traditional sale — it's an **upsell to customers** (who already trust you). "That's what pharmaceutical companies have been using since the dawn of time: a person of authority prescribes pills and you go buy them. Price doesn't matter."

## Worked Example: Supplement Pie-Equation Stacking
Sell to 29 new customers/month (34 × 85% take rate) at $200/month. At **23% Prestige Labs attrition**, each $200 subscription = an **$869 lifetime sale** ($200 ÷ 0.23). The recurring base stacks until inflow = outflow:
- Month 1: 29 sign up, 0 drop → 29 subscribers.
- Month 2: 29 + (29 − 7) = 51.
- Month 3: 29 + 40 = 69.
- Stabilizes at **126 subscribers** (because 126 × 23% = 29 = monthly sign-ups). 126 × $200 = **$25,201/month** — at a 40% margin, **~$120,000/year in profit.** "Note: This is the pie equation I showed earlier."
**Front-end icing**: 29 buyers × $200 × 12 = **$69,600/year**, at 40–50% margin → $80–$100 immediate profit per new customer ("just money for a few extra words" — no fulfillment, no sessions).

## Key Concepts
- **Sell on the second meeting, not the first**: sell supplements at the **nutrition orientation** (a few days after the fitness sale) — "You need to give people time to cool off after the first sale."
- **85%+ close on supplements** (some Gym Lords near 100%) because these are customers, not prospects — they already gave you money.
- **High-ticket supplement packages** ($2,000–$5,000) exist too (process from Cris Cawley) but aren't included in the average-ticket numbers.

## Mental Models
- "They might as well buy supplements from you, because you can at least point them in the right direction" — and you reinvest the money into acquisition/service.
- Supplements are "so easy to do that it's nonsense not to" — a core offering for every gym.

## Anti-patterns
- Refusing supplements over limiting beliefs about money ("Everyone is doing it as soon as they find out how").
- Selling supplements on the first visit ("That never works well").
- Using a drop-ship company that lacks the criteria (gets you price-shopped or pays you slowly).

## Key Takeaways
1. Supplements are the first stacked wallet — sold as a prescriptive assumed-close upsell to existing customers.
2. Use a company meeting all the criteria (drop-ship, autoship, sticky commissions, 40%-higher site price, bimonthly payouts).
3. The same pie equation stacks subscribers to ~$25K/month (~$120K/year profit); sell on the second meeting.
