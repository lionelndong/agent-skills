# Chapter 3: Get Paid To Acquire Customers

## Core Idea
With a high-ticket front-end offer, you can make **more on front-end sales than you spend on ads** — a **negative client-acquisition cost**. You never need a marketing budget again; you spend until your gym is full, not until you run out of cash.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Client-Financed Acquisition®**: create a high-ticket front-end product so the cash from new sales exceeds lead/ad cost. "It's like going to the casino and only playing with winnings."
- **Negative Client-Acquisition Cost**: you MAKE money getting each new customer. "So why would you ever stop?" You keep spending and making money until the gym is full — "not until you run out of cash… but until you darn well please."
- **The Lead-Gen Scrambler**: a system to never run out of ad ideas. Generate many **headlines, images, body copy, page names** and **scramble** them into fresh combinations (e.g. Headline 1 + Image 8 + Video 16). Variable buckets include duration/gender/benefit/curiosity/scarcity/urgency/implied authority hooks. Gives near-endless variations even in a tiny **7-mile radius** (the sweet spot for a local gym).
- **The 12 Copy Commandments** (checked on every ad):
  1. Headline comes first (curiosity is king; never run an ad without one).
  2. Say what only you can say.
  3. Always call out who you're looking for (and who you're NOT — even more powerful).
  4. Reason why ("because" is one of the most influential words).
  5. Damaging admission (own your flaws → makes you believable and authentic).
  6. Show, don't tell (description + emotion).
  7. Tie benefits to status.
  8. Use urgency and scarcity (make it legitimate).
  9. Implied authority.
  10. P.S. = power sentence (most-read words after the headline).
  11. Clear next steps ("Make. It. Stupid. Simple." — Step 1, 2, 3).
  12. Third-grade reading level ("Fancy = friction. Simple = sales").

## Worked Example: The Day-by-Day Cash Cycle
Day 1: put **$100** into ads → **10 leads** at $10/lead → schedule 7 → 3–4 show → **sell 2 @ $600** = **$1,200** (a **12:1 return**, 20% of leads closed). By Day 4 you've made $3,600. Payment processors hold funds ~2–3 business days, so by Day 4 the first 1–2 days of sales deposit: $2,400 − $400 ad spend = **$2,000 net**. That $2,000 covers acquisition cost and everything after is pure profit. The cycle repeats until the gym is full.

## Mental Models
- "The big secret to making great ads is simply testing a lot of different ideas." No magic genie, no silver bullet — test, keep winners, test more. (Hormozi tests 150 ads to find 5 "home runs" and 15 "triples," spending ~$100K of his own money across 100+ markets, then hands winners to top gyms — **Gym Lords**.)
- Lead cost being "high" doesn't matter if front-end revenue dwarfs it.

## Anti-patterns
- Throwing personal money into marketing, then having nothing left to operate the gym (the bootstrapper trap).
- Stopping ad spend because an agency or revenue-share company tells you to, rather than because you're full.

## Key Takeaways
1. A high-ticket front end flips acquisition cost negative — ads pay for themselves immediately.
2. Use the Lead-Gen Scrambler + 12 Copy Commandments to mass-produce converting ads without a pricey agency.
3. Keep spending profitably until the gym is full; the only reason to stop is hitting capacity (fix that in Section II).
