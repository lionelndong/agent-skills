# Chapter 19: PEOPLE — Five Things Every Role In Your Gym Needs

## Core Idea
Every role needs the same **five processes** — and they **mirror your sales pipeline**, just aimed at future employees instead of future customers. This keeps you from being held "hostage" by any one employee.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Employee Pipeline (mirrors the customer pipeline)**:
  - Customer: **Lead Generation → Lead Nurture → Sales → Fulfillment → Retention/Ascension**
  - Employee: **Application → Applicant Nurture → Interviewing → Training → Management**
- **Application**: always keep an inflow for each position. "This keeps the leverage in your pocket… no one is irreplaceable." Use **ad copy** to generate applicants (the book supplies swipeable Operations Manager, Salesperson, and Trainer ads — heavy on personality, GSD ("get-sh*t-done"), and disqualifiers like "Your ego is bigger than Kim Kardashian's backside").
- **Application Nurture**: "Work these like you work leads." Don't assume the right person will find you — follow up fast, show you care, get them on the phone.
- **Interviewing**: For trainers — feel them out in 30 min, have them shadow a class, then lead one, give feedback on **ONE thing**; if they improve next class they're **coachable**, if not, cut it. For salespeople, look for **five things**: **Work Ethic** (fast response time), **Past Success**, **Curiosity** (how many questions they ask), **Coachability** (have them sell you, then give feedback; provide a script beforehand), **Energy** (upbeat — "you gotta like them"). Ops/GM: decision-making + detail-orientation ("Mother Goose role").
- **Onboarding/Training** (do with every new hire): introduce to team + customers, write the first-week agenda, have them read your core values/mission, prepare a growth path (with comp), explain the communication cycle, describe a perfect day, and make sure they know the numbers they must hit.
- **Management/Ascension**: "You need to PRAISE people to success." Ascension = promotion; find people who buy into the vision and paint a growth path (trainers → their own semi-private base; admin/ops → shared revenue; salespeople → more sales + skill + paychecks).

## Worked Example: The Trainer Ad
*"[YOUR AREA] FITNESS ROCK STAR NEEDED — $30K–$60K/yr depending on work ethic. Overcrowded bootcamp/semi-private gym… Must be a cheerleader at heart but a drill sergeant in execution. Must love people. Must love sweet beats… Must be comfortable giving sweaty high fives… Must be on time. Always. DO NOT APPLY IF… You can't play in a team. You take yourself too seriously. You're too cool for Wacky Sock Wednesday… Your ego is bigger than Kim Kardashian's backside."* The disqualifiers screen for fit and make the ad memorable. The ads mirror customer ad copy — call out who you want and who you don't.

## Mental Models
- The pipelines are "mirror images" — apply the same rigor to hiring that you apply to selling.
- An inflow of applicants means no employee can hold you hostage ("not to be used or hung over someone's head. But everyone should know — no one is irreplaceable").

## Anti-patterns
- "If they're the right type of person, they'll find me" — "That's dumb. People don't live to serve you."
- Hiring without a defined onboarding (the new hire never learns the numbers they must hit).

## Key Takeaways
1. Build all five role processes: Application → Applicant Nurture → Interviewing → Training → Management.
2. Interview salespeople for Work Ethic, Past Success, Curiosity, Coachability, Energy; test trainers' coachability with one feedback note.
3. Always keep an applicant inflow so no employee is irreplaceable; praise people to success.
