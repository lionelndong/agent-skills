# Chapter 6: The C-L-O-S-E-R Formula

## Core Idea
**C-L-O-S-E-R** is the sales framework for closing cold traffic — "talking someone who is vaguely interested… into giving you money. That is sales." Learn it, use it, profit, then teach it to your team.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The C-L-O-S-E-R Formula**:
  1. **C — Clarify** why they're there. Ask "What made you come in today?" / "What's your goal?" / "Why did you sign up for [program] online?" The point: **establish a GAP** (something they want and haven't achieved). If they're generic ("just wanted info"), recover with: "I should have asked a better question… what are you hoping to accomplish or improve as a result of coming here?"
  2. **L — Label** them with the problem you plan to solve. Recap current state vs. desired state; get their stats ("So you're 180 lbs at 28% body fat and need to lose 40 lbs — sound right?").
  3. **O — Overview** their past pains & experiences. "What have you tried so far? I can't help you get where you're going until I know where you've been." Use the **Problem Pain Cycle chart** to categorize each prior attempt — align yourself with what worked, repel from what didn't — to show it **wasn't their fault**, it was a faulty plan.
  4. **S — Sell** them the vacation, not the flight. "No one wants to hear about TSA lines… they want Maui." Sell the fun/outcome, not your programming. Tie core benefits to their past pain; explain failures came from missing one of the three pillars.
  5. **E — Explain** away their concerns. There are only **three obstacles** (+ one sort-of): **Stalls** ("I need to think about it"), **Decision-Making** ("I need to talk to my spouse"), **Price** ("I can't afford it"). Plus fitness-specific non-obstacles ("I have a bad back") — "they still want to buy." (Full scripts in the Bonus, Ch 27.)
  6. **R — Reinforce** their decision / WOW them — prevents back-outs and begins the relationship.
- **The Belief-Breaking Formula** (from Linh Trinh): used inside the **Sell** step. 1) Say what they believe · 2) say why it's wrong · 3) say what is right · 4) say WHY it's right · 5) show proof (wall of testimonials behind you).
- **The Trifecta** (the three reinforcement moves): 1) a **handwritten invitation** to your next event · 2) a **call from the owner** that night or first thing next day · 3) a **t-shirt or swag**. "You only get one [first impression], so it might as well be spectacular."

## Key Concepts
- **The three pillars**: fitness, nutrition, accountability. "You need all three… like an engine — if even one important part is missing, it won't work." The Sell step proves each past failure missed a pillar.
- **Micro-commitment close**: "Do you have your ID on you?" (getting the wallet out kills the "I forgot my wallet" excuse), then "let me trade you your ID for the payment card you want to use."
- **If you sell well, you won't need the Explain closes.** Constant battling = something went wrong earlier (usually not enough time building the gap and pain cycle).

## Worked Example: The "Sell The Vacation" Scripts
For a Weight Watchers/Jenny Craig prospect (nutrition + accountability only): "They gave you a diet and checked in, so you lost some weight. The only problem was you didn't keep it off, right? That came down to two things: 1) the diet wasn't sustainable (or you'd still be on it), and 2) when you lost weight your metabolism went down because you weren't doing resistance training. Did you know you lose muscle when you diet? That's why people gain it back plus more… If you diet the right way, with a sustainable plan AND resistance training, you keep it off. That's what was missing before." → then build them back up by qualifying them on all three pillars ("Can you work out here 3 days a week? Can you follow a meal plan? The last part is accountability…").

## Mental Models
- "Sell in the imaginary realm." Don't hand over the meal plan/grocery list — flash it, say it's just a sample, put it away. Showing details adds time and gives them things to disagree with. If they ask to see it, that's a red flag.
- Eminem read the dictionary so he had every "weapon"; your list of closes is your weapon — "bring your A game."

## Anti-patterns
- Talking about programming/exercise selection/nutrition science during the sale — "They. Don't. Care. They just want Maui."
- Handing the prospect your fulfillment materials (adds time, invites irrelevant objections).
- Skipping the Overview/pain-cycle work, then having to "battle" on price later.

## Key Takeaways
1. Clarify → Label → Overview → Sell → Explain → Reinforce, in order.
2. Establish the GAP early; the deeper the pain cycle, the easier the close.
3. Sell the vacation (outcome), break beliefs with proof, and reinforce with the Trifecta to prevent buyer's remorse.
