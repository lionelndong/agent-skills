# Chapter 4: Get Them To Show Up

## Core Idea
Leads who scheduled aren't customers yet. **Show rate is everything**: book same-day/next-day only, hit prospects with many reach-outs, and make scarcity/urgency *real* — because a no-show's odds of rescheduling, showing, and closing are only ~10%.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Scheduling Best Practices**: allow **same-day or next-day appointments only**. Show rates drop dramatically after 3+ days out. A no-show must then (1) reschedule, (2) show after no-showing, (3) buy — "Make a straight line to the sale."
- **The Integrity Tie-Down**: a labeling script. "Do you consider yourself a person of integrity?" → "Great. Because we only do business with people who have the same values… are you the type of person who keeps their commitments?" People act consistently with positive traits they've claimed (psychological **labeling**).
- **Number Of Outreach Attempts** (a "sales law"): contact frequency is **directly correlated** to walk-ins. Recommended: **15 outreach attempts over the first 3 days** (text + call + email), then ≥1 daily reminder until the appointment.
- **Video Texts** (from Grant Cardone): a 15–20-second personalized video using the prospect's name, sent the morning of the appointment → immediate lift in show rates. Plus a second text: a goofy-face photo at the facility with a landmark ("Across from the 7-Eleven on 8th").

## Key Concepts
- **Scarcity/urgency must be true to work**: "the human mind is really good at picking out BS." Hormozi's belief that makes it real: if someone doesn't make it in, it's genuinely hard to help them — so missing the appointment really can be their last chance.
- **Hard-line no-reschedule policy**: "We don't do reschedules because we're simply too booked up. And we only work with people who are serious and keep their word."
- **70% of thank-you-page visitors** will self-schedule if you put an automated calendar app there.

## Worked Example: The Conviction-Changes-Show-Rates Story
An 8-person traveling sales team always did **twice the sales in the last 5 days** of a 21-day campaign than in the first 2 weeks combined. Why? Near the deadline they *needed* the commission, so their **tones changed**, scarcity/urgency became REAL, they closed faster — and **show rates doubled**. The lesson: "that was a change of conviction, not scripting." If your scarcity isn't working, you don't believe it yet.

## Mental Models
- Treat the booking like getting them in **before they get cold feet** — they clicked on a whim and a minute later forgot.
- "If people forget their kids at daycare occasionally, they can easily forget your appointment. Don't be too proud to send multiple reminders."
- The whole show-up process is **100% automatable** (saves ~$30,000/year in payroll on average).

## Anti-patterns
- Letting people schedule 3+ days out for convenience (kills show rate).
- Being too shy/proud to send reminders ("I only want people who don't need reminding") — "that's silly."
- Faking scarcity you don't believe — prospects feel it.

## Key Takeaways
1. Book same-day/next-day; never schedule far out.
2. 15 reach-outs in 3 days, then daily reminders; the number of attempts drives walk-ins.
3. Make scarcity/urgency genuinely true and your conviction will carry the show rate.
