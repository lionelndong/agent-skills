# Chapter 14: How Retention Multiplies Revenue

## Core Idea
The **Pie Equation** governs how big your gym can grow: **sign-ups ÷ churn = max EFTs.** You grow a gym by manipulating *both* pieces — raise sign-ups AND cut churn — and the three churn drivers are organized as **People, Process, Product**.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Pie Equation / Hypothetical Gym Max (HGM)**: **Number of new sign-ups (referrals + marketing) ÷ % leaving = HGM (max EFTs).** Lets you predict your ceiling and model improvements.
- **People / Process / Product** (from Marcus Lemonis, *The Profit*): the three components of any business, used to organize the retention fix. Hormozi covers them in this order:
  - **PROCESS** → How to treat your customers (Ideal Customer Life Cycle + Five Horsemen of Retention).
  - **PEOPLE** → How to treat your employees (org structure/comp + communication cycles + 5 things per role).
  - **PRODUCT** → How to have amazing sessions (motivate trainers + session structure).
- **The Three Growth Elements** examined in any turnaround: retaining existing customers · getting more referrals · bringing more in via marketing.

## Worked Example: Manipulating Both Pieces Of The Pie
Start: 10 sign-ups/month (2 referral + 8 marketing), 10% churn.
- **#1 — No changes:** 10 ÷ 0.1 = **100 max EFTs.**
- **#2 — Increase sign-ups** to 20/month (better marketing): 20 ÷ 0.1 = **200 max** (doubled).
- **#3 — Decrease churn** to 5% (sign-ups unchanged): 10 ÷ 0.05 = **200 max** (also doubled).
- **#4 — "SUPAH GYM" (both):** 20 sign-ups ÷ 0.05 churn = **400 max EFTs** (quadrupled).
"But, as my dad always says, 'Anyone can get rich on Excel.'"

## Key Concepts
- **Why growth stalls**: it's a function of business maturing — you went from underdog to "just another business," service from new-and-exciting to "not like it used to be," community from "all family" to "OGs vs. everyone," traffic from referrals to cold/promo traffic. "It's a function of time, not how much you care or how hard you work."
- **Deteriorating service delivery**: as membership grew, service dropped, new members couldn't assimilate (cliques formed), so no matter what you feed the front end, they don't stick.
- **A different skill set**: "What it took you to get to 100 members is not what it takes to get past 200 or past 400."

## Mental Models
- Most owners obsess over getting people in the door but "few even think about keeping those people over the long term." Grow by **both** acquisition and retention.
- The Pie Equation is the same math that later governs **supplement** subscriber stacking (Ch 25).

## Anti-patterns
- Trying to out-acquire your churn (rolling the boulder uphill — Ch 13's Sisyphus).
- Treating the 100→400 journey as "more of the same" rather than a new skill set.

## Key Takeaways
1. Max EFTs = sign-ups ÷ churn; improve both numerator and denominator.
2. Organize retention work as People / Process / Product.
3. Churn becomes the biggest issue as the gym grows — master it or it masters you.
