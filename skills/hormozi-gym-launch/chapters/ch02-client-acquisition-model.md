# Chapter 2: A Client-Acquisition Model That Works

## Core Idea
There are **only three ways to grow any business** — and the fix for the broken acquisition model is a 4-step process that starts with a high-ticket, decommoditized **irresistible offer** sold with **8 specific steps**.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Only 3 Ways To Grow Your Business** (trademarked as **Gym Profit Levers™**): **Acquire** (get more clients) · **Ascend** (increase average purchase via price + cross-sells/upsells) · **Resell** (get people to buy more frequently — in gyms, retention). Three lever types map to these: price levers, capacity levers, overhead levers (see Section II).
- **The 4-Step Acquisition Fix**: 1) Create a high-ticket/premium front-end offer · 2) Get paid to acquire customers · 3) Get them to show up · 4) Get them to buy.
- **Decommoditization**: make your offer so unique it can't be price-compared. "By combining your services and products in such a way that they become unique, you become immune to price comparison." The easiest way: an **irresistible offer** (his favorite: a **free six-week challenge** that actually costs $500–$600).
- **The 8 Steps To Sell An Irresistible Offer**:
  1. **Pre-Frame** — on the phone, position the gym as busy/desirable (authority + social proof); have them fill out an **intake questionnaire** that surfaces and repeats their pain points.
  2. **Price Anchor** — drop a higher price (your regular rate, competitors', or DIY cost) before revealing the real price.
  3. **Splinter Stack** — "unbundle" things you already do into a long list of named perks/bonuses; a longer list feels like more value even if contents are identical.
  4. **Scarcity** — limited supply ("X spots left" / popular session times).
  5. **Urgency** — limited time ("this program starts tomorrow"). Scarcity = units; urgency = TIME. They're different and both needed.
  6. **Crazy Guarantee** — bold satisfaction guarantee ("I'll write you a refund check myself… worst case, you worked out for free. You have no risk").
  7. **Bribe** — give a normally-paid item free for signing up today; an either/or "assumed close" (one high-ticket choice, one low-ticket continuity choice).
  8. **Downsell Your Upsell** — sell the expensive defined-end program first, then the cheaper continuity. "No one wants a membership. They want a result and a deadline."

## Key Concepts
- **DEP (Defined-End Program)**: a program that lasts X days / Y weeks. People pay 2–4x more for something NOT on continuity, so charge the most when excitement and pain are highest.
- **Downsell-your-upsell math**: e.g. $100/week for 12 weeks → then $50/week ongoing. Charging double up front makes the $200 membership "seem like a total bargain." Gyms using it keep **double the industry-average back end at a 58% higher price**.

## Worked Example: The Splinter Stack
"Which is more appealing: a bootcamp membership, or a six-week challenge with: personalized meal plan, personal grocery list, restaurant guide, matched recipes, accountability coach, 3 workouts/week with a trainer, online accountability group, weight-loss education portal, super shake guide, bomb-ass breakfasts, lick-your-fingers lunches, delectable dinners?" Same contents, longer list → feels like far more. If you don't have the assets, **go make them** (Hormozi built all of his in ~a week).

## Mental Models
- "Sell normal stuff for normal prices and irresistible things for irresistible prices."
- A free 6-week challenge that costs $500–$600: you let them earn their money back as cash/credit if they hit X lbs or Y% body fat — different, results-driven, decommoditized.

## Anti-patterns
- Selling a commodity at a cheap price that invites side-by-side comparison.
- Giving everything away free up front and only trying to upsell *after* excitement wears off — "we were all taught backward."

## Key Takeaways
1. Memorize the 3 ways to grow (Acquire / Ascend / Resell) — every tactic maps to one.
2. The irresistible offer is step one of fixing acquisition; you need something unique to command unique prices.
3. Irresistible pricing generates the up-front cash needed to buy ads (Ch 3).
