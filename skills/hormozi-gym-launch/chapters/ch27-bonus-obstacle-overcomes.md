# Chapter 27 (BONUS): Obstacle Overcomes

## Core Idea
The full library of closes for the **Explain** step of C-L-O-S-E-R. Every objection is one of **four types**, and the closes are **stacked** like a boxing combination ("Jab–Jab–Uppercut–Hook"). "If you internalize how to overcome these four primary objections… you give yourself a skill that will feed you for life."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Four Objection Types**: 1) **Stalls/Delays** · 2) **Decision-Maker** · 3) **Price** · 4) **Fitness/Program-Related** (mostly questions, not real obstacles).
- **ALWAYS AGREE**: transition every close with "I agree" or "I totally understand," then "Let me ask you a question…" Never say "but" or "I disagree" — that puts them on the defensive. "They need to conclude on their own that they should do this."
- **The core argument behind every stall close**: "You should make the decision NOW." "People hate making decisions because they hate making mistakes. So your responsibility is to convince them that the REAL mistake is NOT making a decision."
- **Stack closes** ("the more times you can hit the ball back, the more likely you are to win") — each close "dents the prospect's apprehension."

## Named Closes (by objection type)
**Stalls/Delays:**
- **"What's your main concern?"** — the #1 most-used overcome; surfaces the real objection. Then "Let's consider the options" and walk through Option 1/2/3 (their gym pass they don't use, a $600–$1,000 personal trainer, another diet-only program) vs. the Last Option (you).
- **Rocking Chair Close** — "When we say 'I'll think about it,' we don't go home and sit in our rocking chairs and stare at the wall… you'll get filled with groceries and laundry and kids… and in those two seconds you'll have made the decision." "Decisions don't take time, they take information, because thinking is instantaneous." There are only three reasons people don't buy: **the program, the person, or the price** — "Which is it?"
- **The 1–10 Scale** — "On a scale from 1 to 10, where are you? Why is it a 7 and not a 2? What would it take to get from a 6 to a 10?" Then address that one thing.
- **The Three-Questions Close** — "Do you need two or three days, or two or three months?… Either way you'll just have to answer three questions: 1) Do you think this will help you lose weight? 2) Do you have $X available / getting paid in 30 days? 3) Am I the person and is this the business to support you?"
- **Same-Day-Decision Preset** — set up front (great for group pitches): "We have one rule: people must decide same-day, because the people who delay tend to be wishy-washy and fall off."
- **Unicorn Close** — "What would your unicorn program have that we're missing? Because you'll need [the full splinter-stack list]… So what is this program missing? The only thing getting in the way is YOU."

**Decision-Maker** ("I need to talk to my spouse"): three angles — (1) past-agreements ("Does he know you're here? Does he want you healthy? Then is there any reason he'd say no?"), (2) divert to a different obstacle ("What would your spouse object to?"), (3) "you're your own person." **Delayed Payment Close** — "I'll reserve your spot without money down and delay your deposit to the start date, so if your spouse says no, I can tear this up." "It's better to ask forgiveness than permission."

**Price** ("I can't afford it"): "People will ALWAYS find a way to get money if they see sufficient value. If you offered them a $500,000 house for $2,000, they'd find the money." Closes:
- **Best Case / Worst Case** — best case you lose 26 lbs and get your money back; worst case you get a refund check. "Out of thousands… only two have taken me up on my refund."
- **Expensive Clothing** — "$1,000 heels worn 8 times = $33/hour. If you spend $2,500 on this program, how many times will you walk out wearing your body?"
- **Identity Swap** (the makeup-store story) — "We vote with our dollars… you're going from a woman unsatisfied with how she looks to one who IS satisfied — and that new identity comes with new priorities for where you spend."
- **Gonna Spend It Anyways** · **Delayed Payment Close** · **If This Means A Lot To You, I Know You'll Succeed** · **Cheap Comparison Close** ("I could go to LA Fitness for $29" → "If all else were equal, six weeks of gym access or our program — which would you do? Why?") · **It's Expensive Because Of The Time It Will Save You** · **For Men Who Say "I Can't Afford It"** ("Really? I wouldn't have taken you as someone financially stressed about this" — they'll name a different obstacle).

**Fitness/Program-Related** (questions, not obstacles): prepared answers for "bad back / I'm too old," "What if I get hurt/bored?," "Can I try a workout first?" (no — "it doesn't matter"), start-date concerns ("I can't start until next week" → "we have a new group starting that date"), "What if I fall off?" ("That's what we're here for"), certifications ("a cert is just $500 and a weekend… we have our own in-house Master Trainer certification — 1,000+ sessions"), and condition-specific ("I have low thyroid" — **Caution: never claim to cure/treat disease**). On diet: **"We eliminate before we moderate."**

## Worked Example: The "Consider The Options" Close
After "What's your main concern?" doesn't fully close: "If you walk out now, you'll be actively choosing to stay where you were when you came in, which you don't want — that's why you came in. And when people leave, they don't come back. **Option 1**: use your 24 Hour Fitness pass (but you haven't gone in a year and get no results). **Option 2**: hire a personal trainer ($600–$1,000/month, 2–3 workouts/week at $60 each — do you have that cash? Me neither). **Option 3**: another diet-only program like Weight Watchers (won't work long-term without muscle). **Last Option**: you get all the accountability, a nutrition plan that teaches you, a trainer at each workout, a community, and a money-back guarantee. Let us do what we do best and help you. Let's do this."

## Mental Models
- "Sales is a lot like tennis — the more times you can hit the ball back, the more likely you are to win."
- Tonality is much of selling and is lost in text (Hormozi recommends the audiobook for these).
- "The only people who fail the program are ones who had to think about it, because they weren't committed enough in the beginning to make a decision."

## Anti-patterns
- Saying "but" or "I disagree" (puts the prospect on the defensive).
- Claiming to cure/treat a medical condition (legal risk).
- Treating fitness questions as real obstacles — "they still want to buy."

## Key Takeaways
1. Every objection is a Stall, Decision-Maker, Price, or Fitness question.
2. Always agree, then stack named closes like a punching combination.
3. The real mistake is *not* deciding — your job is to lead them through the decision (decisions take information, not time).
