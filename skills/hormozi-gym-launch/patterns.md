# Patterns — Gym Launch Secrets

Each play in When-to-use / How / Trade-offs form. Hormozi's examples and numbers preserved.

## Client-Financed Acquisition
**When to use**: you're bootstrapped and can't out-spend big-box gyms on ads.
**How**: create a high-ticket front-end offer (free 6-week challenge that costs $500–$600); spend ad money, make more on front-end sales than on leads. Day 1: $100 ads → 10 leads → 2 sales @ $600 = $1,200 (12:1). Keep spending until full.
**Trade-offs**: requires an irresistible, decommoditized offer first; processors hold funds 2–3 days so you carry a few days of float.

## The Irresistible Offer (8 steps)
**When to use**: building any front-end offer that must command premium prices.
**How**: Pre-Frame → Price Anchor → Splinter Stack → Scarcity → Urgency → Crazy Guarantee → Bribe → Downsell Your Upsell.
**Trade-offs**: you must build the splinter-stack assets (meal plans, guides) if you don't have them; takes setup work.

## Lead-Gen Scrambler + 12 Copy Commandments
**When to use**: you keep running out of fresh ad ideas in a small (7-mile) radius.
**How**: build buckets of headlines/images/body copy/page names; scramble combinations; check every ad against the 12 commandments (headline first, call-out, reason why, damaging admission, show-don't-tell, third-grade level…).
**Trade-offs**: still requires testing volume (Hormozi tests 150 ads for 5 winners); no silver bullet.

## Show-Up System
**When to use**: leads schedule but don't show.
**How**: same-day/next-day booking only; Integrity Tie-Down script; 15 reach-outs over 3 days then daily reminders; morning video text (name) + goofy landmark photo; make scarcity/urgency genuinely true.
**Trade-offs**: labor-intensive (but 100% automatable, saves ~$30K/yr); requires real conviction or prospects feel the BS.

## C-L-O-S-E-R Formula
**When to use**: closing cold traffic in person.
**How**: Clarify (establish the gap) → Label (current vs. desired state) → Overview (past pains, Problem Pain Cycle chart) → Sell the vacation (not the flight; Belief-Breaking Formula) → Explain concerns (3 obstacles) → Reinforce (the Trifecta).
**Trade-offs**: if you sell well you won't need the Explain closes; battling on price means you under-built the gap earlier.

## Raise Your Prices (Virtuous Cycle)
**When to use**: margins are thin; you're competing on a cheap commodity price.
**How**: charge more for the same service; move to a two-tier model ($167–$225 large group, $500–$700 semi-private) priced by buyer type; never >3 levels.
**Trade-offs**: expect ~10% (occasionally up to 30%) customer loss; "cut once, cut deep" — but you make more from fewer.

## Billing-Cycle Switch (Sellability Combo)
**When to use**: you bill monthly and want ~8% more revenue at zero added cost.
**How**: advertise weekly ($39–$49/week) but bill on a 28-day cycle. Equal inflow/outflow, weekly sellability, same admin as monthly.
**Trade-offs**: you lose the "shearing" effect (avoiding a big card hit) vs. true weekly; more transactions than monthly if you bill weekly.

## Capacity Maximization (5 micro levers)
**When to use**: you're hitting capacity and considering a second location.
**How**: unlimited → 3x/week (call them "sessions"); spread attendance (reverse-order offers + no-show fee); 45-min back-to-back sessions; partner everyone up; convert office to floor space; swap space-hungry exercises + regressive two-tier programming.
**Trade-offs**: OG kickback on changes (switch all at once); requires removing beloved-but-bottlenecking exercises (double unders, Olympic lifts).

## Overhead Cut (80% Service Margin)
**When to use**: you have many half-empty sessions / margin below 80%.
**How**: compute (Revenue − Trainer Cost) ÷ Revenue; cut sessions to members ÷ capacity ÷ attendance; only open sessions you can fill; calibrate trainer pay if essential.
**Trade-offs**: cutting pay risks trust (last resort); fewer sessions may crowd remaining ones — size for headroom.

## Two-Tier (Regressive) Programming
**When to use**: new "pre-beginners" can't integrate without weird off-hour sessions (Onboard + Jump Rope problems).
**How**: for every workout, create a parallel easier Tier 2 (same muscles/patterns, regressed); have OGs spot/partner newbies; don't announce exercise changes.
**Trade-offs**: requires programming two parallel workouts; relies on OG goodwill ("the strong help the weak").

## The Five Horsemen Of Retention
**When to use**: monthly churn above 3–5%.
**How**: Reach-Outs every 14 days (systematize, never automate); Attendance Tracking weekly (2-or-fewer visits = flag); Handwritten Cards (every other an ask, P.S. only); Member Events every 21 days (pay babysitter, require new members, bring-a-friend free); Exit Interviews (CLOSER re-sell).
**Trade-offs**: labor-intensive personal touch at scale (use spreadsheets/CRM); cards/events cost time and small money.

## Communication Cycles
**When to use**: scaling the team / leaving growth to chance.
**How**: Daily Huddles (≤10 min, numbers + wins + announcements); Weekly Team Meetings (scripted, timed slots, end early); Weekly One-on-Ones (30 min, you listen: what's going well / not / how can I help you win more).
**Trade-offs**: you pay people to attend; requires rigid consistency ("as the sun rising").

## Employee Pipeline
**When to use**: you're held hostage by a key employee / can't find good people.
**How**: Application (always-on ad inflow) → Applicant Nurture (work like leads) → Interviewing (coachability test; salespeople = work ethic/past success/curiosity/coachability/energy) → Training (8-step onboarding) → Management (praise to success, ascension/promotion).
**Trade-offs**: mirrors the sales pipeline — same ongoing effort; hire before you need the role (may take tries).

## Trainer Motivation (Extrinsic → Intrinsic)
**When to use**: trainers underperform / you can't afford to pay them more.
**How**: extrinsic first — supplements, internal challenges, 25% semi rev-share (can take a trainer from $15.6K to $42K+/yr); then intrinsic via the Six Human Needs (praise/trainer-of-the-month, let them design workouts, pay on time, fund their growth/certs).
**Trade-offs**: rev-share gives up some margin; you must actually live the culture ("you cannot lead what you do not live").

## The Hamburger Workout
**When to use**: session quality is inconsistent / depends on the trainer.
**How**: fixed ingredient stack — fresh playlist, 10-sec name greeting, social check-in, OG pairing week one, mic, automated timer, ≤2 demos, 2 name-callouts + 2 cues per person, hands-in + 3-2-1 cheer, internal-promo clipboard.
**Trade-offs**: structure over spontaneity (intentional); great trainer in crappy structure still beats the reverse.

## The Wedding Cake (Icing + Cake)
**When to use**: maximizing profit per customer across the whole model.
**How**: build 3 tiers in order — Large Group → Semi-Privates → Supplements; each has Icing (front-end cash) + Cake (recurring) = 6 streams; add Sprinkles (internal plays) as a feeding funnel.
**Trade-offs**: WARNING — most gyms lack bandwidth to run all streams; don't start five companies; sequence it.

## Three EFT Conversion Attempts (Large Group)
**When to use**: converting a front-end challenger into recurring EFT.
**How**: Bribe (Day 1, anchor high, free program with membership) → Abandon Hope Sale (Week 3 halfway, credit the front-end, lifetime rate, ~95% close) → Last Chance (weigh-out, give result-based credit, change terms not price).
**Trade-offs**: never offer different prices for the same service (illegal price discrimination); only change terms.

## Setter → Closer (High-Ticket / HTP)
**When to use**: selling a $2K–$3K semi-private program (too big for a cold single visit).
**How**: setter qualifies on the phone (future-pace, emotional WHY, "how long would you wait?"), edifies the closer, and closes the $50–$100 nonrefundable consult fee; closer drops the high ticket in person.
**Trade-offs**: "the set is the close" — no consult fee closed = 90% won't close in person; needs two people (or you do both).

## Supplement Stacking
**When to use**: adding a recurring profit center with ~zero fulfillment.
**How**: use a drop-ship company meeting all criteria; sell as a prescriptive assumed-close upsell to *customers* on the second meeting (nutrition orientation); subscribers stack via the pie equation to ~$25K/month.
**Trade-offs**: 23% attrition on supplements; requires the right company (financing, autoship, sticky commissions, bimonthly payouts).

## Internal Plays (Sprinkles)
**When to use**: re-engaging existing customers + funneling into HTP/supplements.
**How**: pick a hook (Nutrition / Fitness / Community), tie to a holiday, run a 10-day "advertainment" countdown, price $150–$199, fulfill on weekends, upsell 25–35% into HTP + supplements.
**Trade-offs**: ~90% margin and ~zero prep, but not a standalone profit center — it's a funnel; needs a strong hook.

## Communicating The Shift
**When to use**: rolling out price/structure changes without a revolt.
**How**: sell trainers individually (frame problem → thought process → solution + their upside); announce to members over many channels (mail/email/video w/ comments off/text) with a service-upgrade frame + Refer-a-Friend; handle the business side in person only.
**Trade-offs**: expect loud customers for weeks (it's noise); you'll have hard conversations either way.
