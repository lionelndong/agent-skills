## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The Three Problems (the book's spine)
1. **Broken Acquisition** → **Client-Financed Acquisition** (Sections I).
2. **Broken Profit Model** → **Gym Profit Levers** (Section II).
3. **Hole-in-the-Bucket (attrition)** → **Process / People / Product** retention system (Section III).
Then **Section IV** stacks it all into the **Wedding Cake Gym Profit Model**.

### The Only 3 Ways To Grow (Gym Profit Levers™)
**Acquire** (more clients) · **Ascend** (increase average purchase) · **Resell** (buy more often = retention). Three macro levers map to these: **Price · Capacity · Overhead**, each with micro levers.

### Client-Financed Acquisition® (Section I)
Make **more on front-end sales than you spend on ads** → **negative client-acquisition cost**; spend until the gym is full, not until cash runs out. The **4-step fix**: high-ticket front-end offer → get paid to acquire → get them to show up → get them to buy. Day-1 math: $100 ads → 10 leads → 2 sales @ $600 = **$1,200 (12:1)**.

### The Irresistible Offer — 8 steps to sell it
**Pre-Frame · Price Anchor · Splinter Stack · Scarcity · Urgency · Crazy Guarantee · Bribe · Downsell Your Upsell.** Decommoditize so you can't be price-compared. "No one wants a membership. They want a result and a deadline" → sell a **Defined-End Program (DEP)** first.

### The C-L-O-S-E-R Formula (selling cold traffic)
**C**larify (establish the gap) · **L**abel (the problem you'll solve) · **O**verview (past pains; Problem Pain Cycle) · **S**ell the vacation, not the flight (Belief-Breaking Formula) · **E**xplain concerns (only 3 obstacles: Stalls, Decision-Maker, Price) · **R**einforce (the Trifecta). Answer "How much?" with **"It depends on your goal,"** never a number. **Selling is serving.**

### Gym Profit Levers (Section II)
- **Price**: charge more for the same service (**virtuous vs. vicious price cycle**); price by **buyer type** in two tiers ($167–$225 large group, $500–$700 semi-private); avoid **No Man's Land** ($250–$399, 5–15 people); ≤3 levels; bill weekly-advertised on a **28-day cycle** (+8%).
- **Capacity** (5 micro levers): unlimited → **3x/week** (~2x capacity); **45-min back-to-back** sessions; **partner up**; convert office → floor; swap space-hungry exercises + regressive two-tier programming.
- **Overhead**: hit the **80% service margin** = (Revenue − Trainer Cost) ÷ Revenue; cut empty sessions; calibrate pay last. Pull all levers at once — "**cut once, cut deep**."

### Retention (Section III): "You are NOT in the fitness business"
You're in the **accountability / relationship / community / coaching** business — that's what people stay and pay for. **Weapons-Grade Compassion** (100% responsibility) drives LTV + premium pricing. **Pie Equation**: max EFTs = sign-ups ÷ churn. Cutting churn **10% → 3% = 3.3x LTV** at ~$10/customer/month (11:1+ ROI).
- **PROCESS**: milestones **Connect → Refer → Testify → Ascend**; the **Five Horsemen** = Reach-Outs (every 14 days) · Attendance Tracking (weekly) · Handwritten Cards · Member Events (every 21 days) · Exit Interviews (a CLOSER re-sell).
- **PEOPLE**: EFT-based org chart; hire before you need it; **3 communication cycles** (Daily Huddles · Weekly Team Meetings · Weekly One-on-Ones); employee pipeline mirrors the sales pipeline.
- **PRODUCT**: motivate trainers extrinsically (25% semi rev-share + supplements) then intrinsically (**Six Human Needs**); run the **Hamburger Workout** session structure.

### The Wedding Cake Gym Profit Model (Section IV)
Maximize **profit per customer** two ways: **accommodating buying curve** (let the 20% pay 3x–10x) + **stacking wallets**. Every tier has **Icing** (front-end cash) + **Cake** (recurring) → 3 tiers = **6 revenue streams**: **Large Group → Semi-Privates (HTP) → Supplements**, fed by **Internal Plays (Sprinkles)**. Conservative real numbers ≈ **$108K/month**. EFT conversion (large group): **Bribe → Abandon Hope Sale (~95%) → Last Chance**. High-ticket: setter→closer, "**the set is the close**."
