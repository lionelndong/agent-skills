# Glossary — Affiliate Black Book ($100M Money Models Launch)

**Affiliate Prize Ladder** — the tiered referral rewards: 10 referrals → 70 Profit Tactics Audiobook + Vegas HQ drawing; Top 50 → guaranteed Vegas HQ day; Top 10 → fireside chat with Alex (Ch 1).

**Affiliate Referral Loop** — register via the affiliate's link and both the affiliate and the registrant unlock shared prizes; "we both win when you use my link" (Ch 1).

**Avoid Adverbs** — Hormozi writing rule #4: cut adverbs; they usually mean lame verbs ("shut really hard" → "slammed") (Ch 6).

**Benefit line** — the campaign's signature promise: "get more customers to spend more money, faster, over and over again" (Ch 2, Ch 6).

**Countdown Cadence** — the email schedule keyed to days remaining: 21 / 14 / 7 / 4 / 1 days, then live (Ch 3).

**Curiosity Hook** — the secret project teased as "better than an NFT, less than a Bitcoin," never named (Ch 2, Ch 6).

**Direct Benefit (Ad 6)** — ad angle: "How to get customers to spend more money" → the answer is Money Models (Ch 2).

**Event Details block** — the reusable Aug 16th / 9am PT–12pm ET / Live on YouTube / FREE summary inserted across assets (Ch 1).

**Friend Text Scripts** — two text-message versions (Direct, Benefit-Focused) for inviting friends with the referral link (Ch 4).

**Greenscreen style** — visual element: screen-sharing Alex's social posts behind the affiliate to borrow authority (Ch 5).

**Live-only gating** — the secret project goes only to people who watch live; the campaign's master scarcity lever (Ch 1, Ch 2).

**Magic Link** — the free link emailed on sign-up that grants access to the virtual event; the affiliate's is the tracked referral version (Ch 1).

**Meta Ad Copy Arsenal** — the six ready-to-run Meta ads, one per angle (Ch 2).

**Money Models** — the launch's subject: a series/sequence of offers that get customers to buy more, faster, repeatedly; "the 4 types" are the teased payoff (Ch 2).

**Mystery Headliners** — 5 mystery speakers (some billionaires) promised to live attendees (Ch 3, Ch 5).

**Organic Social Posts** — two personal-brand post versions (Personal Story Hook, Value-Forward) (Ch 4).

**Positive Language** — Hormozi writing rule #6: say what to do, not what to avoid ("Don't Stop" → "Keep Going") (Ch 6).

**Present Voice** — Hormozi writing rule #2: "When you create" not "When you're creating" (Ch 6).

**Preview line** — the email preview text paired with each subject line (Ch 3).

**Problem/Solution (Ad 4)** — ad angle: "Your business needs a Money Model"; without one you leave money on the table (Ch 2).

**Profit Tactics Audiobook (70)** — the bonus unlocked at 10 referrals (Ch 1, Ch 3).

**Promotion Timeline** — the campaign schedule, realized as the email Countdown Cadence (Ch 3).

**Secret Project** — Alex's 4-year project given free to live attendees; "better than an NFT, less than a Bitcoin"; you "literally can't get it any other way" (Ch 1, Ch 2, Ch 5).

**3rd-Grade Reading Level** — Hormozi writing rule #1: plain words over jargon ("utilize leverage" → "borrow money") (Ch 6).

**Slack Channel Messages** — two team-oriented versions (Team Benefit, Casual Invite) for coworker outreach (Ch 4).

**SMS Reminder** — the one-line live-day text driving to the affiliate's unique live link (Ch 4).

**Social Proof** — recurring hook: "Over 1 million people registered" / "biggest entrepreneur event of the year" (Ch 2, Ch 3, Ch 6).

**Social Media Group Posts** — two versions (Value-First, Social Proof) for posting in groups; link in comments/bio (Ch 4).

**Two-Comma Test** — Hormozi writing rule #5 heuristic: more than one comma → cut the middle or split the sentence (Ch 6).

**Two-Version Rule** — most short-form assets ship in two tonal versions to match channel and relationship (Ch 4).

**Urgency (Ad 5)** — ad angle: "This only happens once"; miss it and you miss it forever (Ch 2, Ch 6).

**Vegas HQ day** — the prize for Top 50 affiliates: a day at Alex's Vegas HQ to ask the audience's questions + recording (Ch 1).

**Writing Guidelines (Alex Hormozi's)** — the 8 copy rules: 3rd-grade level, present voice, active voice, no adverbs, short sentences, positive language, remove redundant words, remove unnecessary words (Ch 6).
