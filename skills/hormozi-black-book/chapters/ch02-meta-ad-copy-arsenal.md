# Chapter 2: Meta Ad Copy Arsenal (6 Versions)

## Core Idea
Six ready-to-run **Meta ads**, each built on a *different angle* for the same event. Rotate angles to find what your audience responds to — every ad carries the same offer (free live event + secret project) and a one-line CTA.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **One Event, Six Angles**: the same launch sold through six distinct emotional entry points. Each = Headline (the hook) + Body (the angle) + CTA (register free).
  - **Ad 1 — Secret Project Hook**: *"What's better than an NFT but less than a Bitcoin?"* → the free 4-year secret project for live attendees. CTA: *Register free now*.
  - **Ad 2 — Money Models Explained**: *"Most businesses lose money on ads. Here's why."* → they spend more getting customers than they make; the fix is Money Models; learn the 4 types. CTA: *Save your spot (free)*.
  - **Ad 3 — Social Proof**: *"Over 1 million people registered for this event"* → biggest entrepreneur event of the year + secret-project giveaway. CTA: *Register now*.
  - **Ad 4 — Problem/Solution**: *"Your business needs a Money Model"* → without one you leave money on the table; it's a *series of offers* that get customers to buy more, faster, repeatedly. CTA: *Get your spot*.
  - **Ad 5 — Urgency**: *"This only happens once"* → live, free, secret project only to people who show up live; *miss it and you miss it forever*. CTA: *Register free*.
  - **Ad 6 — Direct Benefit**: *"How to get customers to spend more money"* → the answer is Money Models, 4 types of offers that maximize what each customer is worth. CTA: *Save your spot*.

## Key Concepts
- **The repeated value promise**: *"get more customers to spend more money, faster, over and over again"* — the campaign's signature benefit line, recurring across ads.
- **Curiosity anchor**: the *"better than an NFT, less than a Bitcoin"* riddle defines the secret project's value without naming it.
- **The 4 types of Money Models**: named as the teachable payoff ("learn all 4 types live") but never enumerated in the ads — the enumeration is the reason to attend.

## Mental Models
- Treat the six ads as a **split-test set**, not a sequence: launch several angles, scale the winner.
- Match angle to **audience temperature**: skeptics → Ad 2 / Ad 4 (problem-aware); warm fans → Ad 1 / Ad 5 (curiosity + urgency); cold/broad → Ad 3 (social proof).

## Anti-patterns
- **Explaining the secret project**: naming or describing it kills the curiosity that drives the click — keep the riddle.
- **Listing the 4 Money Models in the ad**: that's the live payoff; give it away and you remove the reason to register.
- **Running one angle only**: the whole point of six versions is to test entry points.

## Key Takeaways
1. Six angles — Secret Project, Money Models Explained, Social Proof, Problem/Solution, Urgency, Direct Benefit — for one event.
2. Each ad = hook headline + single-angle body + one-line free CTA.
3. The benefit line ("spend more money, faster, over and over again") and the NFT/Bitcoin riddle are reused across all six.
4. Tease the 4 types and the secret project; never reveal them in the ad.
