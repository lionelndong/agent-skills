# Chapter 6: Copy Hooks, Messaging Frameworks & Alex Hormozi's Writing Guidelines

## Core Idea
The **messaging engine** behind the whole kit: the recurring hooks/angles that every asset is built from, plus **Alex Hormozi's Writing Guidelines** — a concrete set of rules (with ❌/✅ examples) for writing copy that lands.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Recurring Hooks** (the angles that repeat across ads, emails, posts, and video):
  - **Curiosity** — *"better than an NFT but less than a Bitcoin"* (the unnamed secret project).
  - **Social proof / scale** — *"Over 1 million people registered" / "the biggest entrepreneur event of the year."*
  - **Urgency + scarcity** — *"This only happens once" / "miss it and you miss it forever"* + live-only access.
  - **Problem/solution** — *"Most businesses lose money on ads. Here's why."*
  - **Direct benefit** — *"get more customers to spend more money, faster, over and over again."*
  - **FREE** — the event and the secret project are free; "free" is stated relentlessly.
- **Alex Hormozi's Writing Guidelines** (preserve each rule and its examples):
  1. **Keep everything below 3rd-grade reading level** — ❌ *"We utilize leverage to gain higher returns on our equity checks"* ✅ *"We borrow money so we can buy bigger stuff (and make more) than we could with our cash alone."*
  2. **Present voice** — ❌ *"When you're creating your sales page"* ✅ *"When you create your sales page."*
  3. **Active not passive** — ❌ *"The body was carried out of the room"* ✅ *"We carried the body out of the room."*
  4. **Avoid adverbs whenever possible** (adverbs usually mean lame verbs) — ❌ *"We shut the door really hard"* ✅ *"We slammed the door."* Avoid *"very, super, way, actually, etc."*
  5. **Simple/short sentences** — usually one comma; if two commas, cut the middle or make 2 sentences. ❌ *"We tried to escape but the man, with the red mask, chased us until we lost our breath."* ✅ *"The red-masked man chased us until we lost our breath. We were trapped."*
  6. **Positive language** — ❌ *"Don't Stop"* ✅ *"Keep Going"*; ❌ *"Don't leave the facility"* ✅ *"Stay inside."*
  7. **Remove redundant words** — ❌ *"We simultaneously left the building at the same time"* ✅ *"We left the building at the same time."*
  8. **Remove unnecessary words** (if they don't change or add meaning) — ❌ *"8 Guidelines For Writing I Live By"* ✅ *"8 Writing Guidelines I Live By"*; ❌ *"He was able to get out of his car."* ✅ *"He exited his car."*

## Key Concepts
- **Hooks are reusable building blocks**: the same six-ish angles recombine across every channel — pick the hook, then dress it for the platform.
- **The writing rules are an editing pass**: draft the copy, then run it through the 8 guidelines to tighten it.
- **"Adverbs usually mean lame verbs"**: the tell that you should swap a verb (shut hard → slammed), not add a modifier.
- **The two-comma test**: more than one comma signals a sentence that should be cut or split.

## Worked Example: editing a line through the guidelines
- Draft: *"We were actually able to very quickly get more customers."*
- Apply #4 (kill adverbs/"actually," "very") + #8 (cut "able to") + #1 (plain words):
- Result: *"We got more customers fast."*

## Mental Models
- Write at a **3rd-grade reading level on purpose** — clarity converts; cleverness doesn't.
- Prefer **strong verbs over modifiers** (exited > was able to get out; slammed > shut really hard).
- Phrase instructions as **what to do, not what to avoid** (Keep Going > Don't Stop).

## Anti-patterns
- **Jargon / "utilize"-speak** — violates rule #1; replace with plain words.
- **Passive voice and present-continuous** — "was carried," "creating" → "carried," "create."
- **Adverb padding and filler** — "really hard," "very," "actually," "able to" all get cut.
- **Negative phrasing** — "Don't leave" reads worse than "Stay inside."

## Key Takeaways
1. The kit recombines a fixed set of **hooks**: curiosity, social proof, urgency/scarcity, problem/solution, direct benefit, FREE.
2. **Hormozi's 8 writing guidelines** are the editing standard: 3rd-grade level, present + active voice, no adverbs, short sentences, positive language, cut redundant/unnecessary words.
3. **Adverbs signal weak verbs**; the **two-comma test** signals a sentence to split.
4. Run every line through the guidelines as a final tightening pass.
