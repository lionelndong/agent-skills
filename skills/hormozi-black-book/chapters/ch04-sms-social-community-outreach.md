# Chapter 4: SMS, Friend Texts, Social Posts & Community Outreach

## Core Idea
The **short-form outreach pack** — a swipe file of texts, social posts, and team/community messages, each shipped in **two versions** so the affiliate can pick the tone that fits the channel and relationship.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Two-Version Rule**: nearly every short asset comes in a *Direct* version and a *softer/benefit or social-proof* version. Pick by audience closeness.
- **SMS Reminder** (live-day): *"Hey [Name]! Alex Hormozi's $100M Money Models launch is LIVE now. Get his secret project (free but only for people watching live): [put your unique live link]"* — one line, link last.
- **Friend Text Scripts (2)**:
  - **V1 Direct** — "Alex is doing a huge book launch Aug 16th… giving away something he's worked on 4 years (free, live only). Plus if we get 10+ to register, free prizes we can all use. I'm going. Here's my link."
  - **V2 Benefit-Focused** — "Quick question — wanna come with me to Alex's next book launch? Secret project + crazy bonuses. This is my referral link so we get even more bonuses."
- **Social Media Group Posts (2)**:
  - **V1 Value-First** — "biggest entrepreneur event of the year… the Money Models that get customers to spend more, faster, repeatedly… secret project to everyone live. Tickets free. **Link in comments.**"
  - **V2 Social Proof** — "Over a city of people have registered… 100% free… **Link in bio.**"
- **Slack Channel Messages (2)** — team/coworker framing:
  - **V1 Team Benefit** — "Found something that could help our revenue goals… 'Money Models' = how to get customers to spend more money *with us*… our sales/marketing folks might want to check it out."
  - **V2 Casual Invite** — "Alex Hormozi (the *$100M Offers* guy) is doing a huge book launch… *(If we pool our sign ups we unlock more free stuff.)* Comment below if you're in."
- **Organic Social Posts (2)** — personal-brand framing:
  - **V1 Personal Story Hook** — "Been following Alex for a while and his stuff actually works… I'm definitely tuning in… Link in my bio or DM me."
  - **V2 Value-Forward** — "FREE training this Saturday that could change how you think about making money… I'm registered. Anyone else joining? Link in comments."

## Key Concepts
- **Channel-appropriate link placement**: comments / bio / DM for social; inline for texts and Slack — the kit specifies where the link goes per platform.
- **Relationship-graded tone**: friends get casual "come with me"; teammates get a revenue/business justification; followers get a credibility + personal-results frame.
- **The "I'm going / I'm registered" device**: the affiliate's own attendance is the proof and the invite ("if you wanna come with").
- **Pooling language for teams**: "if we pool our sign ups we unlock more free stuff" adapts the prize ladder to a coworker context.

## Mental Models
- Think **invite, not broadcast**: every short asset is phrased as a personal invitation from someone who's attending, not a brand announcement.
- Use the **"$100M Offers guy" shorthand** to establish Alex's authority fast with audiences who may not know the name.

## Anti-patterns
- **Same wording on every channel**: the kit deliberately varies tone per channel — copying the friend text into a Slack channel reads wrong.
- **Leading with the affiliate's self-interest**: open with what *they* get (free event + secret project); fold the referral link in as "so we both get the bonuses."
- **Link in the wrong place**: posting the link in the body where the platform suppresses reach — follow the bio/comments guidance.

## Key Takeaways
1. Short-form assets ship in **two versions each** — pick by tone and relationship.
2. Cover **SMS, friend texts, group posts, Slack, and organic social** — one message per surface.
3. **Where the link goes is channel-specific** (comments, bio, DM, inline).
4. Frame as a **personal invite** from someone already attending; team versions adapt the prize ladder to "pool our sign-ups."
