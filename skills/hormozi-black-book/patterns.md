# Patterns — Affiliate Black Book (Launch Promotion Playbook)

## The Affiliate Referral Loop
**When to use**: any affiliate-driven launch where you want referrals to recruit more referrals.
**How**: lead with what the viewer gets (free event + free secret project), then add the affiliate bonus as a *shared* upside ("when *we* hit 10, we both get…"). Close every asset with one CTA to register through the affiliate's link.
**Trade-offs**: shared-prize framing recruits co-promoters but requires real, conditional tiers — overpromising ("you've already won") erodes trust.

## The Prize Ladder (tiered referral incentives)
**When to use**: to give affiliates an escalating reason to push harder.
**How**: stack milestones with rising value — 10 referrals (audiobook + drawing) → Top 50 (guaranteed in-person day) → Top 10 (1:1 fireside chat). Restate the ladder in every email and outreach script; narrate progress ("we're climbing the leaderboard").
**Trade-offs**: more tiers = more motivation but more fulfillment complexity; keep tiers few and concrete.

## One Event, Six Angles (ad split-test set)
**When to use**: paid promotion where you don't yet know which message converts.
**How**: write the same offer six ways — Secret Project (curiosity), Money Models Explained, Social Proof, Problem/Solution, Urgency, Direct Benefit. Each = hook headline + single-angle body + one-line free CTA. Launch several, scale the winner.
**Trade-offs**: testing costs spend up front; running one angle is cheaper but blind.

## The Countdown Email Sequence
**When to use**: time-boxed events with a fixed date.
**How**: 6 emails on a tightening schedule (21 / 14 / 7 / 4 / 1 days, then live). Hold the offer constant, raise urgency, narrate prize progress, pair every send with subject + preview, end on the link. Make the go-live email near-instant: one link, one reason.
**Trade-offs**: high send frequency risks fatigue/unsubscribes; the constant-offer/rising-urgency structure mitigates it.

## The Two-Version Swipe File
**When to use**: multi-channel short-form outreach (texts, social, Slack).
**How**: ship each asset in two tones — Direct and softer/benefit/social-proof — and let the affiliate pick by relationship. Specify link placement per platform (inline for texts/Slack; comments/bio/DM for social).
**Trade-offs**: two versions add choice but require the affiliate to judge tone; the personal-invite framing keeps it authentic.

## The Curiosity Anchor (un-named giveaway)
**When to use**: when a free incentive's *mystery* is more persuasive than its description.
**How**: define value by comparison without naming the thing ("better than an NFT, less than a Bitcoin"), gate it to live attendance ("you literally can't get it any other way"). Never describe it in the asset.
**Trade-offs**: drives clicks but must pay off live or it damages credibility.

## Live-Only Scarcity
**When to use**: live/virtual events where attendance is the goal.
**How**: make the headline reward (secret project) available *only* to people watching live; reinforce with "this only happens once" / "miss it and you miss it forever."
**Trade-offs**: maximizes live turnout but forfeits replay/long-tail conversions by design.

## Timestamped Video Outline
**When to use**: affiliate-filmed promo videos.
**How**: give a minute-by-minute beat map with a hook in the first 30 seconds, a teach-a-little/reveal-the-rest-live middle, an explicit Affiliate-Benefits beat, and a persistent on-screen + description link. Offer two angles: problem/teaching and personal/authority.
**Trade-offs**: structure ensures pacing but constrains the creator's natural style; the hook-first rule is non-negotiable.

## The Hormozi Editing Pass (8 writing guidelines)
**When to use**: final tightening of any copy in the kit.
**How**: run each line through — 3rd-grade reading level, present voice, active voice, no adverbs (they signal weak verbs), short sentences (two-comma test), positive language, remove redundant words, remove unnecessary words.
**Trade-offs**: aggressive cutting can flatten personality; the rules prioritize clarity and conversion over voice.
