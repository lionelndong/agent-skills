# Chapter 9: Section C — Free With Alternate Revenue Stream · Lifetime Upgrades · Lifetime Discounts · Discount + One-Time Fee

*Source: "$100M Lost Chapters" — Section C, the cut upsell and continuity money models.*

## Core Idea
One upsell model and three continuity models cut from $100M Money Models. The upsell (**Free With Alternate Revenue Stream**) turns "no" into a sale on a *different* product. The continuity models — **Lifetime Upgrades** (bonuses to stick), **Lifetime Discounts** (price-lock to stick), and **Discount + One-Time Fee** (made-up fees to stick) — all "get them to stick": a good offer gets them to *start*, good bonuses/locks get them to *stay*.

## Frameworks Introduced

### Free With Alternate Revenue Stream (Upsell) — "the no sale sale"
Provide one service/product **free** and monetize with **something else**. "People want the same problem solved in different ways." Two buckets:
- **Paired Upsell** — "I give you thing A free, in exchange for buying thing B" (free storage month + the only lock that fits your unit).
- **Independent Upsell** — "I give you thing A free, and I encourage you to buy thing B" (free 28-day program → $400 supplement package at orientation).
- **Key rule**: works only if A has **low incremental cost**. With cold traffic, close a **trial + card on file** first, then upsell product on the second visit (avoid no-shows). **The biggest predictor of back-end conversion** is whether they buy the first upsell — "It's not a nice-to-have. It's a must-have." Frictionless upsells can hit **90%+ take rates**. (Origin: a gym owner gave a free USB to every "no," then booked a nutrition orientation where ~100% showed and spent 50% more on supplements than regular members.)

### Lifetime Upgrades (Continuity) — get them to stick with bonuses
Pair a **"when"** with a **"what"**:
- **When**: **Delays** (time/payments passed) or **Milestones** (something they do/achieve).
- **What**: **One-Time bonus** (extends tenure once), **Variable bonus** (different thing each time, on a schedule), or **Lifetime Upgrade** (permanent feature/service upgrade).
- **Important notes**: customers must *know* about bonuses (tell them, and tease the next one); keep the *type* known but the bonus itself a surprise; keep bonuses **variable** unless permanent and huge; make milestones things that make the customer successful *and/or* advertise you; **combine** whens and whats; give **status & bragging rights** on unlock (rename customer → "VIP"/"lifer"/"elite"); celebrate status changes publicly. (Origin: the $42k/yr × 3-yr Gym Launch licensing continuity where Hormozi delivered "a new money-making play every 14 days" — revenue went up 13x.)

### Lifetime Discounts (Continuity) — get them to stick with price
Give a cheaper price **as long as they stay on recurring payments**; they stick because **if they leave, they can't get it back.**
- Add **urgency** (limited time), **scarcity** (limited number), and **believable reasons** (grand opening, beta, founder's). "A Lifetime Discount only works if you actually charge more when this offer ends."
- **Three ways to display**: (%) off retail, ($) off retail, or **fixed price (price protection)** — the first two stay flexible; fixed price demands you know your numbers. Limit price protection to a fixed period, not forever. (Origin: "The Opener" advertised a 14-day trial into a **founding member discount** — only for pre-opening signups; "We open every location with 400+ recurring members, or we don't open.")

### Discount + One-Time Fee (Continuity)
Charge a **discounted first period** plus one or more **made-up one-time fees** (setup/enrollment) you can waive, charge, or split. "When people pay, they pay attention."
- **The higher the one-time startup fee, the lower the churn** — "the higher the barrier to entry, so too the higher the barrier to exit." (John's tanning chain: $100 sign-up + $10/mo churned far less than $19 down + $19/mo. A friend charges $5,000 to start + $267/mo → 2-year+ average vs. the normal 4-month fitness client.)
- **Four steps to a one-time fee**: 1) Pick the fee name, 2) Pick the price, 3) Pick the "reason why," 4) Start charging / discounting / waiving it.

## Key Concepts
- **The Sunk Cost Fallacy** — people keep investing where they've already put time/money ("we've come this far"). Big up-front payments weaponize this for stick. ("The bigger the head, the longer the tail.")
- **Initiation fees** — up-front payments that buy a lower rate and dramatically extend tenure ("they'll call before their card changes to keep that lower rate").
- **Greater up-front vs. scheduled payments → higher completion** — collect more up front; remind customers of forfeited cash and lost price near purchase/cancel.
- **"Never waste a crisis"** — real life events (good or bad) are the most believable reasons for a discount.

## Mental Models
- **A good offer starts them; bonuses/locks keep them** — improving monthly *stick* means adding extra value (or lock-in) well after the first payment, and often.
- **Loss aversion as retention** — bonuses you'd lose, discounts you can't get back, fees you'd repay → reasons not to leave.
- **Tangible goods beat services emotionally** — "people were more willing to pay for the products than … the service from white-coated medical professionals."

## Anti-patterns
- **Skipping the first upsell** — its absence is the greatest predictor that the customer won't stay.
- **Fixed-price lifetime discounts without knowing your numbers** — locked-in rates + rising costs = "you've got problems."
- **Re-granting a Lifetime Discount to someone who canceled** — you lose credibility with everyone else; instead downsell a same-price, different-feature offer.
- **Reusing a "one-time only" price on the same thing** — break the promise and lose trust; change what's included instead.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Free With Alternate Revenue Stream** = "the no sale sale": give A free, monetize B (paired or independent); the first upsell predicts back-end conversion.
2. **Lifetime Upgrades** = pair a *when* (delay/milestone) with a *what* (one-time/variable/lifetime), plus status and surprise, to make customers stick.
3. **Lifetime Discounts** and **Discount + One-Time Fee** use price-lock and made-up fees (bigger head → longer tail) to cut churn — but only if you know your numbers.
