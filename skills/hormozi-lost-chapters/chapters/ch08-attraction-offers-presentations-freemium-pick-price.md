# Chapter 8: Section C Attraction Offers — Free Presentations · Freemium · Free Pick Your Price

*Source: "$100M Lost Chapters" — Section C, the cut attraction-offer money models.*

## Core Idea
Three specific attraction money models cut from $100M Money Models for being too niche: **Free Presentations** (educate-to-convert), **Freemium** (a dangerous-but-powerful acquisition strategy for ~100%-margin businesses), and **Free Pick Your Price** (a goodwill play with a sliding scale). Each is "more bullets to add to your potential $100M Money Model."

## Frameworks Introduced

### Free Presentations (Attraction)
Presentations that educate with intent to convert. They excel for complex/expensive things because: (1) **captive audience**, (2) **enough time** to explain and provide value, (3) you make the offer **when they're most motivated**.
- **"The bigger the plane, the longer the runway"** — time spent educating ∝ price and trust needed (a 60-sec ad for a $67 product; a two-day workshop for $100k consulting). Length can run 15 seconds to 15 days.
- **Free education-with-offer formats + benchmarks:**
  - **Free Dinner** (60–90 min presentation) → low offer covers the dinner cost → set 1:1 appts for the high-ticket. *Benchmark:* ~25% of the room to the low offer, ~⅓ of those to the high offer.
  - **Free 90-minute Masterclass** (remote). *Benchmark:* 10% on the call.
  - **Free 5 Day Challenge** (four 60–90 min sessions Mon–Thu, offer Friday). *Benchmark:* 2–5% of signups.
  - **Free 3 Day Virtual Summit** (multiple speakers; pitch end of day 2, repitch day 3 — highest-ticket). *Benchmark:* 10% of final-day attendees.
  - **Free Strategy Call** (step one of a two-step sale; qualify with **BANT** — Budget, Authority, Need, Timing). *Benchmark:* 25% to the second call.
- **The Three Core Limiting Beliefs** every presentation plays to: (1) **results must come easy**, (2) **I must get the best results possible**, (3) **results must lead to attention and approval (status)**.

### Freemium (Attraction)
"Not a business model — it's an **acquisition strategy**." Give away something so valuable people come (via word of mouth) and use it free at ~$0 acquisition cost; then your sales team upsells those "free leads."
- **The three design constraints** — what you give away must: (1) cost ~nothing to fulfill, (2) provide *continuous* value (not one-time), (3) not give away so much of the farm that they never buy.
- **Examples**: Dropbox, Spotify (free + ads), Wistia, Gmail storage (fear of loss). **Works only with 100% free — no discount variation.**
- **Cost math**: cost to service a free user ÷ free→paid conversion % = true CAC (e.g., $0.05/mo ÷ 1% = $5/mo; profitable if ARPU > ~$15/mo).
- **Three roadblocks**: Conversion (too few upgrade), Value (not valuable enough to spread), Cost (fulfillment cost too high vs. paid revenue).
- **Warning**: most who try it (even smart people) do it wrong; nearly all the named examples have funding. "If it's your first rodeo … I would definitely not recommend it."

### Free Pick Your Price (Attraction)
Market the offer as **free**; at checkout, let them **pick their own price**, framed as supporting the staff/families (donations). "It'll seem like they are paying the employees, not the business."
- **Mechanics**: offer bonuses at three payment levels (small/medium/large) to pull people above $0; no max, only rungs; always give the basic level free if they pay nothing.
- **Pre-frame** that it's pick-your-price with bonuses at levels and no obligation — then hit them with confrontational commitment questions ("Are you willing to change X? Stop doing Y? Will you attend all appointments?") to weed out non-stayers. End: "we accept Visa, Mastercard, or XYZ — which would you prefer?" then shut up.
- **Free vs. Discount Note**: does *not* work with a discount wrapper.
- **Paid version** (Pro Tip): an art gallery priced each piece as a *range* ("$149–$299"); most pay above the midpoint to not seem cheap. "Half goodwill, half capitalism."

## Key Concepts
- **BANT** — Budget, Authority, Need, Timing: the strategy-call qualification acronym.
- **"The more time they spend with you, the more money they'll spend with you."**
- **Pick Your Price = goodwill engine** — high conversion, lower average ticket; great in low-trust environments; sets up future upsells.

## Mental Models
- **Long-form converts cold traffic best**, especially one-to-many — value via entertainment, life lessons, and breaking limiting beliefs; retain attention; build goodwill.
- **Freemium as a funnel, not a model** — "free leads" your sales team upsells; it's always easier to upsell than to sell.

## Anti-patterns
- **Freemium without knowing your numbers** — "know your numbers like you know your children's names," or you run a business that loses money servicing free users.
- **Pick Your Price with high-operational-cost giveaways** — give away only low-cost stuff for free; save high-cost fulfillment for payers.
- **A presentation that doesn't force consumption** — you lose the captive-audience advantage.

## Key Takeaways
1. Free Presentations = educate-to-convert; runway length scales with price/trust; hit the 3 core limiting beliefs; qualify calls with BANT.
2. Freemium is an acquisition strategy (free → upsell), only for ~100%-margin businesses that spread by word of mouth — advanced and dangerous.
3. Free Pick Your Price is a high-conversion goodwill play: free offer, pick-your-price with bonus rungs, commitment-screen the buyers.
