# Chapter 4: Free Promotions

*Source: "$100M Lost Chapters" — Section A: Attract. Epigraph: "If it's free, it's for me!"*

## Core Idea
"Free is the most powerful offer of all time and will never expire." At its base it's "something for nothing" / "value in advance." Dr. Dan Ariely's **penny gap**: 9x more people take a free Hershey kiss than one sold for a penny. A free offer is the fastest way to see if anyone wants your thing — and the cheapest, highest-volume source of leads.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **What a failed free offer tells you** (3 diagnoses): prospects (1) **don't want your thing** (change what you give away or how you describe it), (2) **don't believe you** (so always answer **"Why?"** with a true, good reason — "Going out of business, all products must go in 30 days" justifies "90% off"), or (3) **aren't seeing it** — a targeting issue (wrong pond).
- **Pros of Free Offers**: (1) **Highest lead volume** — most leads per eyeball; (2) **Lowest lead cost** — lowest cost per raised hand; (3) **Massive companies go viral by monetizing "free"** (Facebook, YouTube, Dropbox, Uber's free first ride, Netflix's free 30 days, Claire's free ear piercings, European Wax Center, Public Storage's free month).
- **Cons of Free Offers**: (1) **Volume is a double-edged sword** → add friction; (2) **Some people have no intention of buying** → it always comes down to math.
- **The 5 Levers of Friction** (to raise lead quality, find the "sweet spot"):
  1. **Increased Qualifications** — list requirements everywhere (copy, creative, landing pages).
  2. **Increased Information Requirement & Question Type** — more fields, heavier asks (cell/income > first name), open-ended > multiple-choice.
  3. **Increased Number of Steps** — each step drops off prospects.
  4. **Forced Consumption** (Hormozi's favorite) — make them watch a long video before any CTA appears; lets in only the pre-indoctrinated.
  5. **Advertisement Length** — longer ads/copy/video before the CTA self-select higher-value clicks.

## Key Concepts
- **Penny gap** — the disproportionate jump in takers between $0.01 and free.
- **Friction increases lead quality** — "the more hoops … the higher the quality." Goal: "weed out the weirdos but not so much that you lose some lazy whales."
- **Free Brings Broke People Myth** — false. Four independent split tests (10 markets each) of free vs. premium: **close rate and average ticket were the same**; only the *volume and cost* of leads differed (free front ends cut lead cost 5x+).

## Mental Models
- **Free Money Math** — Campaign #1: $1,000 → 500 leads, 250 qualified. Campaign #2: $1,000 → 200 leads, 160 qualified. The team *feels* better about #2, but on dollars and cents **#1 is better** — use the higher volume and let friction "skim the cream."
- **Free Makes More Money (Pro Tip)** — if premium lead cost is 5–10x higher, premium prices should be 5–10x higher. Price, Prospect, Process, Promotion, and Product must ALL match one structure (free *or* premium); "you can't just mix and match."

## Anti-patterns
- **Running a premium offer at free-offer prices** — the #1 mistake when comparing them; an unfair comparison.
- **Letting free overwhelm operations** — give something valuable but don't overextend on fulfillment cost.

## Worked Example: The unbelievable offer
A famous marketer ran a newspaper ad every few years: *"For every $100 you give me I will give you $1,000 back, call 444-444-4444."* No one ever responded — it was so good it was unbelievable. Lesson: a crazy free offer must always answer **"Why?"** with a believable reason.

## Key Takeaways
1. Free = highest volume + lowest cost; close rate ≈ the same as premium.
2. When a free offer fails, it's want / belief / targeting — diagnose, don't abandon.
3. Tune the 5 levers of friction to the "sweet spot," and always justify a crazy offer with a true "Why?"
