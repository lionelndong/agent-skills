# Chapter 6: Section B — The Expensive Customer Problem (CFA)

*Source: "$100M Lost Chapters" — Section B. "The last cut I made to $100M Money Models" — the math behind Customer-Financed Acquisition. Epigraph: "If you can spend more than your competition to get a customer, you will get more customers than them."*

## Core Idea
**Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA): "30 Day Gross Profit > CAC."** If a customer pays you back fast enough, you eliminate cash as the bottleneck to growth. Hormozi's real-life minimum is one better — **30 Day GP > 2x CAC** — so one customer's first-30-day profit pays for two more. Then "you only have to buy your first customer," and customers finance all the rest.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The CFA equation** — `30 Day Gross Profit > CAC` (break-even financing); `30 Day GP > 2x CAC` (Hormozi's minimum standard).
- **The Three Levers of CFA** (to grow: get more customers, make them worth more, do it fast):
  1. **↓ CAC** — Get More Customers → Lower **Cost to Acquire a Customer**. `CAC = (all advertising + sales + supporting costs) / customers acquired`.
  2. **↑ LTGP** — Make Them Worth More → Increase **Lifetime Gross Profit**.
  3. **↓ PPD** — Do It Fast → Decrease **Payback Period** (when `GP > CAC`).
- **The Three Levels of CFA**:
  - **Level 1** — 30-day GP < CAC: you eventually come out ahead, but you float on savings/loans/credit lines (a big risk). Avoid when starting out.
  - **Level 2** — 30-day GP = CAC: a credit card (interest-free for 30 days) becomes your ad budget; pay it off monthly and reuse it. Your credit limit caps customer volume.
  - **Level 3** — 30-day GP > 2x CAC: customers pay for themselves *and* the next one; you can "double your business every month (or faster)" and ditch credit. "CFA removes 'cash' from your list of problems in life."

## Key Concepts
- **30 Day Cash (30D Cash)** — "Alex's Most Prized Metric": gross profit extractable from a new customer in their first 30 days. 30 days matters because it's the typical interest-free-financing window (credit cards). If 30D Cash > CAC, you get "free customers using other people's money."
- **OPM (Other People's Money)** — max a 0%-interest card to acquire customers; pay it off in 30 days from the new customers' GP; repeat. "Crowdfund the growth of your business using your customers."
- **Gross Profit vs. Net Profit** — GP = price − cost of *making/delivering* the thing; net = what's left after *all* costs. Gross margin is GP as a %.
- **Churn** — % of *original* customers who leave between periods (new signups don't change it).
- **LTGP is "the arms race of business"** — "the business that makes its customer the most valuable, wins" (Hormozi's rephrasing of Dan Kennedy: "He who can spend the most to acquire a customer wins"). You can only get CAC to zero, but LTGP can go infinitely high. **CAC is about getting customers; LTGP is about keeping customers.**

## Mental Models
- **CAC is a hard science** — calculate it monthly, by channel; double down on the channel with the best-cost customers. ("That $1,000 sale you thought cost $200 really costs $500.")
- **Speed compounds** — paying back in 1 month vs. 3 months is a 4x difference in growth (double monthly → 8x in a quarter vs. one doubling).
- **LTGP : CAC ratio** — aim for total acquisition cost ≤ 1/3 of lifetime gross profit (LTGP:CAC ≥ 3:1).

## Worked Example: Normal vs. Wonderful Lemonade
Lemonade stand: $10/mo, ~5-month stay, 80% margin → $8 GP/mo, $40 LTGP.
- **Normal business** — CAC $20, payback 2 months: Day 0 −$20 … you don't see cash returned until *month three*, then the client cancels at month five having returned ~$20. "A hard business to grow." This is why "the median small business owner makes $72,489/year."
- **Wonderful business** — CAC $1, payback 7 days: Day 0 −$1 → Day 7 +$7 (cover the $1, keep $6–$7) → reinvest the $7 to get 7 more → 7 more from those, and so on. **Total cash ever spent on marketing: $1.** "Playing with the house's money." Run **CFA Level 3** for 12 months reinvesting all profit → from 1 customer to **4,095 customers**, paying only for the first.

*(Real results Hormozi cites from this math: software $60k→$1.7M/mo in 6 months; consulting $215k→$1.8M/mo in 8 months; e-commerce $80k→$1.5M/mo in 6 months.)*

## Anti-patterns
- **Never having calculated true CAC** — counting only ad spend or assuming content leads are "free"; surprise unprofitability at month end.
- **Floating on Level 1 when bootstrapped** — needs capital you don't have; "it sucks."
- **Settling for 2:1 ROAS as "good enough"** — a few tweaks could let customers fund all growth.

## Key Takeaways
1. CFA: `30 Day GP > CAC` (Hormozi's minimum: `> 2x CAC`) removes cash as the growth constraint.
2. Pull three levers — ↓CAC, ↑LTGP, ↓PPD — and climb to Level 3, where customers pay for themselves and the next.
3. 30D Cash is the prized metric; using OPM (a 0% card paid off in 30 days) gets "free customers for life."
