# Chapter 2: Section A — Attract (Why Free & Discount Promotions)

*Source: "$100M Lost Chapters" — Section A: Attract, opening chapter. Cut from $100M Money Models for being "a little too theoretical." Subtitle: "How To Get People To Engage, Who Otherwise Wouldn't, Using Free & Discount Promotions."*

## Core Idea
Whenever Hormozi enters a new market, he "almost always start[s] with something free or massively discounted." Free and discount offers are **wrappers around the core premium Grand Slam Offer** — they don't change the offer, they make it more attractive to a cold audience so demand starts flowing. "Those who give the most, get the most."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction** (a.k.a. "Generate Flow → Monetize Flow → Increase Friction") — the order of operations for a new market. Generate demand first, *then* figure out how to make money on it; only then add friction. "Too many people try to put the cart before the horse."
- **The promotional wrapper** — the offer is the gift; free/discount is the wrapping paper. "You can wrap a gift in recycled newspaper or … a Chanel bag with matte black paper and a perfectly tied white bow. What's inside may be the same," but the wrapper makes it inherently more attractive. It must answer **"what's in it for me?"**
- **Three reasons to start free/discounted**: (1) you don't fully know what you're doing yet — wiggle room/forgiveness; (2) fastest way to get testimonials; (3) it builds your conviction so you can close the first sales; (4) it makes referrals and demand easy.

## Key Concepts
- **Premium, Free, Discount** — the three Attraction-offer types, covered in that order (Ch 3, 4, 5). Free and discount are "wrappers around the core premium offer."
- **Keep the front end as low as possible** if you can keep building upsells over time — to keep lead flow cranking.

## Mental Models
- Treat a new market like Gym Launch's first offer: give away *yourself* for free. ("I'll fly out, spend my own money on marketing, work your leads, close them, give you the customers for free. I only make what I sell." — Hormozi did this for 33 gyms over 18 months; his first million outside his gyms, all starting with a free offer.) "Sounds like a Grand Slam Offer? Yep."
- **Generate demand before monetizing** — if you want money fast, ironically it often comes fastest from giving things away.

## Anti-patterns
- **Monetizing before you have flow** — adding friction or charging hard before demand exists kills traction in a cold market.
- **Changing the Grand Slam Offer** to make a promotion — the promotion *enhances* the offer; it doesn't replace it.

## Key Takeaways
1. New market → lead with free or a massive discount to benchmark and build from.
2. Order matters: Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Add Friction.
3. Free/discount are wrappers; the Grand Slam Offer underneath stays the same.
