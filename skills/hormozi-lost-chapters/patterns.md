# Patterns — $100M Lost Chapters

## Pattern: Find Your First Avatar (4 steps)
**When to use**: Choosing exactly who to serve (and who to repel); concentrating on the most valuable customers.
**How**: Survey customers (demographics, business stats, aspirations, buying process) → sort to the top 20% (liked most / spent most / stayed longest) → find the fewest qualifiers they share (3–5) → execute: speak only to that avatar in all advertising and reverse-engineer their buying process. (Ch 1)
**Trade-offs**: Short-term revenue dip from the cost of change; long-term higher retention, margins, and LTGP.

## Pattern: Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction
**When to use**: Entering any new/cold market.
**How**: Lead with a free or massive-discount wrapper around your Grand Slam Offer to generate demand; then monetize; only then add friction. (Ch 2)
**Trade-offs**: Giving away value first feels backwards but produces the fastest flow; don't monetize before flow exists.

## Pattern: Premium Promotion (Grand Slam Offer on its own)
**When to use**: You have a proven, very valuable offer + a selling process that demonstrates value, and cash or patience.
**How**: Sell one high-ticket thing; skim the cream with marketing; pack it with bonuses/guarantees/premium support. Use the 9.5x math to justify lower volume. (Ch 3)
**Trade-offs**: Highest cost per lead, demands great copy/sales; not for the under-capitalized or unproven — wrap in free/discount first.

## Pattern: Free Promotion + the 5 Levers of Friction
**When to use**: Maximum lead volume at lowest cost; cold or small markets.
**How**: Make a free offer; if a failure, diagnose want/belief/targeting; always answer "Why?" with a true reason; tune friction via Qualifications, Information Requirement, Number of Steps, Forced Consumption, Advertisement Length to the "sweet spot." (Ch 4)
**Trade-offs**: Close rate ≈ premium; only volume/cost differ. Don't overextend fulfillment; use Free Money Math, not feelings.

## Pattern: Discount Promotion (Four Displays + Two-Step Sale)
**When to use**: Well-understood services, regulated markets, or to eliminate no-shows.
**How**: Use a massive (50%+) discount on a *splinter* of the offer, displayed four ways (% / $ / relative / price) and tested; collect a card on the discounted front end, then upsell the core offer (the second sale). (Ch 5)
**Trade-offs**: Marginal (5–25%) discounts don't move behavior; never habitually discount the core offer; treat buyers as qualified leads.

## Pattern: Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA)
**When to use**: Removing cash as the constraint on growth (esp. bootstrapped).
**How**: Drive `30 Day GP > CAC` (minimum `> 2x CAC`) via three levers — ↓CAC, ↑LTGP, ↓PPD. Use OPM: a 0%-interest card as your ad budget, paid off in 30 days from new-customer GP; reinvest to reach Level 3. (Ch 6)
**Trade-offs**: Level 1 needs capital you may not have; the cap at Level 2 is your credit limit; calculate true CAC by channel.

## Pattern: The Value Grid (vs. value ladder)
**When to use**: Planning the back end; understanding non-linear buying.
**How**: Map all offers in a grid (not a stair-step); sum revenue ÷ prospects to read LTV, CAC, and 30D Cash; spot same-price offers solving different needs. (Ch 7)
**Trade-offs**: A grid needs real metrics; a ladder is fine to brainstorm before you have data.

## Pattern: Ultimate Offer Stacking + Right Stage/Problem/Way/Time
**When to use**: Maximizing revenue-per-lead so you can outspend competitors.
**How**: Weave Attract → Up Front Cash → Upsell/Downsell → Continuity (then repeat), choosing each offer by Right Stage, Right Problem, Right Way, Right Time; advance every prospect (even "no") to the next stage with downsells. (Ch 7)
**Trade-offs**: Each added offer adds operational complexity — only add high-profit / low-cost ones; 1:1 selling enables this best.

## Pattern: Free Presentations (educate-to-convert)
**When to use**: Complex/expensive offers, cold audiences, one-to-many.
**How**: Pick a format by ticket/trust (dinner, 90-min masterclass, 5-day challenge, 3-day summit, strategy call w/ BANT); play to the three core limiting beliefs (easy results, best results, status); hit the benchmark conversion. (Ch 8)
**Trade-offs**: "The bigger the plane, the longer the runway" — high-ticket cold needs long runways; force consumption to keep the captive-audience edge.

## Pattern: Freemium (acquisition strategy)
**When to use**: ~100%-incremental-margin businesses (software/media) that spread by word of mouth.
**How**: Give away something costing ~$0 to fulfill, with continuous value, but not the whole farm; upsell free users; compute CAC = service cost ÷ conversion %. 100% free only. (Ch 8)
**Trade-offs**: Easy to do wrong; most named examples are funded; "know your numbers like your children's names" or steer clear.

## Pattern: Free Pick Your Price
**When to use**: Low-trust environments; goodwill + cash + leads with high conversion.
**How**: Market free; at checkout offer pick-your-price with bonus rungs (small/medium/large), framed as supporting staff; pre-frame, then commitment-screen with confrontational questions; ask for the card and shut up. (Ch 8)
**Trade-offs**: Lower average ticket; only give away low-ops items free. No discount variation.

## Pattern: Free With Alternate Revenue Stream ("the no sale sale")
**When to use**: Turning every "no" into a sale; great internal play / referral generator.
**How**: Give thing A free, monetize thing B (Paired: A in exchange for B; Independent: A free + encourage B). Cold traffic: trial + card on file first, product upsell second. (Ch 9)
**Trade-offs**: A must have low incremental cost; the *first upsell* is the greatest predictor of back-end conversion — a must-have, not nice-to-have.

## Pattern: Lifetime Upgrades (stick with bonuses)
**When to use**: Continuity service businesses; extending tenure.
**How**: Pair a "when" (delay or milestone) with a "what" (one-time, variable, or lifetime-upgrade bonus); tell customers it exists, keep the bonus a surprise, keep it variable unless huge/permanent; add status/labels and public ceremony. (Ch 9)
**Trade-offs**: Lifetime upgrades are permanent commitments; variable bonuses keep flexibility and novelty.

## Pattern: Lifetime Discounts (stick with price-lock)
**When to use**: Strong attraction + retention; grand openings, betas, founders.
**How**: Discount the recurring rate for as long as they pay; add urgency + scarcity + a believable reason; display as (%) off, ($) off, or fixed price; limit price protection to a fixed period. (Ch 9)
**Trade-offs**: Only works if you actually charge more later; fixed-price locks are dangerous if costs rise — know your numbers; never re-grant to someone who canceled.

## Pattern: Discount + One-Time Fee (stick with made-up fees)
**When to use**: Any recurring or defined-end program; offsetting acquisition cost; cutting churn.
**How**: Discount the first period; add one-time fee(s) — 1) name it, 2) price it, 3) give a "reason why," 4) charge/discount/waive it. Higher up-front fee → lower churn (bigger head, longer tail). (Ch 9)
**Trade-offs**: Flexible and powerful, but must give a clear reason; charge it on those who must do work to succeed.

## Pattern: Get Employees Like Customers (3Ds + Performance Diamond)
**When to use**: Scaling lead-getting beyond yourself; building a sellable asset.
**How**: Recruit via the Internal Core Four; train with Document → Demonstrate → Duplicate (a stranger should get your results from the checklist); diagnose dropped performance via the Performance Diamond (Communication, Training, Motivation, Circumstances). (Ch 10)
**Trade-offs**: Employees take work, just less than doing it yourself; reward in training, never punish; fix one step at a time. Protect makers' large time blocks.
