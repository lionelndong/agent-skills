# Glossary — $100M Lost Chapters (+ Secret Chapter, First Avatar, Journal)

**30 Day Cash (30D Cash)** — "Alex's Most Prized Metric": the gross profit extractable from a new customer in their first 30 days. 30 days = the typical interest-free financing window. (Ch 6)

**Absolute Amount Off** — Display a discount as a dollar amount: "$181 Off First Week." Best for understood products. (Ch 5)

**Accommodating Resistance** — Exercise-physiology analogy for selling: match ticket price/value to each customer's buying ability without adding business complexity. (Ch 7)

**Advanced Offer Stacking** — Layering a series of Grand Slam Offers to raise lifetime gross profit ("the back end"). (Ch 7)

**BANT** — Budget, Authority, Need, Timing — the qualification checklist for a Free Strategy Call. (Ch 8)

**The Back End** — The series of offers after the front end that raise how much a customer is worth; "the back end informs the front end." (Ch 7)

**CAC (Cost to Acquire a Customer)** — All advertising + sales + supporting costs ÷ customers acquired; "a hard science," tracked monthly by channel. (Ch 6)

**Churn** — % of *original* customers who leave between periods; new signups don't change it. (Ch 6)

**Core Four** — The lead-getting activities (warm outreach, cold outreach, post content, paid ads) from $100M Leads; applied internally to hiring (Internal Core Four). (Ch 10, 12)

**Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA)** — When 30-day gross profit from a customer exceeds CAC (`30 Day GP > CAC`); Hormozi's minimum is `> 2x CAC`. Removes cash as the growth bottleneck. (Ch 6)

**Discount + One-Time Fee** — Continuity model: a discounted first period plus made-up one-time fee(s) you charge, waive, or split; higher up-front fee → lower churn. (Ch 9)

**The 3Ds (Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate)** — Hormozi's training model: write the checklist, do it in front of them, have them do it in front of you. (Ch 10)

**Five Lead Stages** — Uncontacted → Contacted → Engaged → Qualified → Sold. (Ch 10)

**Five Levers of Friction** — Qualifications, Information Requirement, Number of Steps, Forced Consumption, Advertisement Length — raise lead quality from a free offer. (Ch 4)

**Forced Consumption** — Hormozi's favorite friction lever: require watching long content before any CTA appears, admitting only the pre-indoctrinated. (Ch 4)

**Four Steps To Picking The Right Offer** — Right Stage, Right Problem, Right Way, Right Time. (Ch 7)

**Four Ways To Display Discounts** — Percentage Off, Absolute Amount Off, Relative Equivalent Off, Simply the Discounted Price. (Ch 5)

**Free Pick Your Price** — Attraction model: marketed free, pick-your-price at checkout with bonus "rungs," framed as supporting staff/families. (Ch 8)

**Free Presentations** — Attraction model: educate-to-convert (dinner, masterclass, 5-day challenge, summit, strategy call), each with conversion benchmarks. (Ch 8)

**Free With Alternate Revenue Stream** — Upsell model ("the no sale sale"): give thing A free, monetize thing B (Paired or Independent Upsell). (Ch 9)

**Freemium** — An *acquisition strategy* (not a business model): give away something so valuable it spreads at ~$0 CAC, then upsell; 100% free only. (Ch 8)

**Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction** — The order of operations for a new market (also "Generate Flow → Monetize Flow → Increase Friction"). (Ch 2, 5)

**Grand Slam Offer** — Hormozi's core irresistible offer (from $100M Offers); free/discount promotions are *wrappers* around it. (Ch 2, 3)

**Independent Upsell** — "I give you thing A free, and encourage you to buy thing B." (Ch 9)

**Infinite Returns (power of)** — Price can only go to $0 at the bottom but has no ceiling; raise value to raise price. (Ch 3)

**Initiation Fee** — An up-front fee that buys a lower rate and dramatically extends tenure ("the bigger the head, the longer the tail"). (Ch 9)

**Levels of CFA** — Level 1 (30-day GP < CAC), Level 2 (= CAC), Level 3 (> 2x CAC; customers fund all growth). (Ch 6)

**Lifetime Discounts** — Continuity model: a cheaper price as long as they stay on recurring payments; they can't get it back if they leave. Three displays: (%) off, ($) off, fixed price. (Ch 9)

**Lifetime Upgrades** — Continuity model: pair a "when" (delay/milestone) with a "what" (one-time/variable/lifetime-upgrade bonus) to make customers stick. (Ch 9)

**LTGP (Lifetime Gross Profit)** — Total gross profit over a customer's lifespan; "the arms race of business." Recurring: `GP / churn`. Products: `GP × avg transactions`. (Ch 6)

**Maker** — A worker who *makes* things; needs large uninterrupted blocks (1–2/day), works "open to goal," maximizes blank calendar space. (Ch 10)

**Manager** — A worker who directs others in 10–20 small daily blocks; "an empty time slot is a lost opportunity." (Ch 10)

**Paired Upsell** — "I give you thing A free, in exchange for buying thing B." (Ch 9)

**Payback Period (PPD)** — Time for a customer's gross profit to exceed acquisition cost (`GP > CAC`); the speed lever. (Ch 6)

**Penny Gap** — Dr. Dan Ariely's finding: 9x more people take a free item than one sold for a penny. (Ch 4)

**Performance Diamond** — Four reasons employee performance drops: Communication, Training, Motivation, Circumstances. (Ch 10)

**Premium Promotion** — Presenting the Grand Slam Offer on its own at high ticket; fewest clients, highest profit, simplest math. (Ch 3)

**Promotional Wrapper** — Free/discount packaging that makes the same Grand Slam Offer more attractive ("recycled newspaper vs. a Chanel bag"). (Ch 2)

**Proprietary 5-Minute Appointment Method** — Have an admin handle the first visit; the expert pops in 5 min to set the real sales appointment — eliminates no-shows, opens slots. (Ch 5)

**Relative Equivalent Off** — Display a discount in comparison terms: "Save A Steak Dinner" / "Less Than Going Out To Lunch." (Ch 5)

**Right Stage / Problem / Way / Time** — The four-step filter for adding any offer to a Money Model. (Ch 7)

**Splinter (the offer)** — Discount only a sliver/component of the offer, not the whole "farm." (Ch 5)

**Sunk Cost Fallacy** — People keep investing where they've already spent time/money; weaponized for stick via big up-front payments. (Ch 9)

**Three Core Limiting Beliefs** — (1) results must come easy, (2) I must get the best results, (3) results must lead to attention/approval (status); presentations play to all three. (Ch 8)

**Three Levers of CFA** — ↓ CAC (more customers), ↑ LTGP (worth more), ↓ PPD (do it fast). (Ch 6)

**Two-Step Sale** — Discounted front end → card on file → upsell the core offer (the "second sale," where the money is). (Ch 5)

**Value Acceleration Method (VAM)** — Acquisition.com's method (rooted in the Vista PE approach): keep more high-value customers, cut low-value channels. (Ch 1)

**Value Equation** — Hormozi's offer-value formula (from $100M Offers); referenced in the Journal and the bonus-design logic. (Ch 9, 12)

**Value Grid** — Hormozi's alternative to the value ladder/stair-step: a grid showing non-linear buying that visualizes LTV, CAC, and 30D Cash. (Ch 7)

**Vista Method** — Private-equity approach (score customers by who stays longest/pays most; cut low-value channels) that seeded the avatar process and VAM. (Ch 1)

**Your First Avatar** — The four-step process (Survey → Find biggest spenders → Find shared qualifiers → Execute) to find and serve the highest-value customer. (Ch 1)

**Zeigarnik Effect** — The tendency to remember open loops; for a maker, a pending meeting eats the whole work block. (Ch 10)
