## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### Your First Avatar (the most important "where to start")
**Survey customers → find the top 20% (liked most / spent most / stayed longest) → find the fewest qualifiers they share (3–5) → execute**: speak only to that avatar in all advertising, and reverse-engineer the buying process your best customers went through. "You make more because of who they are, not because of who you are." Repel bad customers on purpose — it raises quality, quantity, and average value at once. (Acquisition.com's Value Acceleration Method, from the Vista PE approach.)

### Attract: Free & Discount as wrappers (Section A)
**Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction.** Free and discount are *wrappers* around your Grand Slam Offer (recycled newspaper vs. a Chanel bag) — they make it more attractive to cold audiences without changing it.
- **Premium** — Grand Slam Offer on its own, high ticket; fewest clients, highest profit, simplest math (the 9.5x). Needs a proven offer + sales process + cash.
- **Free** — highest volume, lowest cost; the **penny gap** (9x more take free than a penny). Close rate ≈ premium; only volume/cost differ. Tune the **5 levers of friction** (Qualifications · Information · Steps · Forced Consumption · Ad Length). Always answer "Why?" with a true reason.
- **Discount** — use **massive (50%+)** discounts on a *splinter* of the offer, displayed **four ways** (% / $ / relative / price). Best for understood services; powers the **Two-Step Sale** (card on file → upsell) and kills no-shows.

### Customer Financed Acquisition — CFA (Section B)
**`30 Day Gross Profit > CAC`** (Hormozi's minimum: **`> 2x CAC`**) removes cash as the growth bottleneck. Pull **three levers**: ↓ **CAC** · ↑ **LTGP** (the "arms race"; recurring = `GP / churn`) · ↓ **PPD (Payback Period)**. **30 Day Cash** is his prized metric; using **OPM** (a 0%-interest card paid off in 30 days) gets "free customers." **Three Levels**: L1 (GP<CAC, float on loans) → L2 (=CAC, credit limit = ad budget) → L3 (>2x CAC, customers fund all growth: 1 → 4,095 in 12 months).

### Advanced Offer Stacking — the Value Grid (Section C)
The **back end informs the front end.** Think in a **Value Grid**, not a value ladder — customers buy non-linearly; sum the grid to read LTV, CAC, and 30D Cash. **Stack: Attract → Up Front Cash → Upsell/Downsell → Continuity** (then repeat). Pick each offer by **Right Stage · Right Problem · Right Way · Right Time.** Advance *every* prospect (even a "no") to the next stage with downsells. (Example: 30D Cash $75 → $1,763 = ~14–15x revenue/lead.)

### The cut money models (Section C)
- **Attraction**: **Free Presentations** (educate-to-convert; BANT; the 3 core limiting beliefs) · **Freemium** (an *acquisition strategy*, 100%-margin businesses only) · **Free Pick Your Price** (goodwill sliding scale).
- **Upsell**: **Free With Alternate Revenue Stream** ("the no sale sale" — give A free, monetize B; the first upsell predicts back-end conversion).
- **Continuity (get them to stick)**: **Lifetime Upgrades** (when × what bonuses) · **Lifetime Discounts** (price-lock + urgency/scarcity/reason) · **Discount + One-Time Fee** (bigger up-front fee → lower churn; sunk cost).

### Employees & time (Section D)
**Getting employees = getting customers** (same Five Lead Stages, same **Internal Core Four**). Train with the **3Ds: Document → Demonstrate → Duplicate.** Diagnose dropped performance with the **Performance Diamond**: Communication · Training · Motivation · Circumstances. **Maker vs. Manager**: a "got a min?" costs a maker **10x** — protect makers' large blocks. "You get rich from what you make; you become wealthy from what you own."
