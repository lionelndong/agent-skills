# Cheatsheet — $100M Lost Chapters

## The two equations to memorize
- **CFA**: `30 Day Gross Profit > CAC` — break-even financing. **Hormozi's minimum: `30 Day GP > 2x CAC`.**
- **Three levers**: ↓ **CAC** (more customers) · ↑ **LTGP** (worth more) · ↓ **PPD** (do it fast).

## New-market default
**Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction.** Almost always open a new market with a *free* or *massive-discount* wrapper around the Grand Slam Offer; benchmark, then add friction.

## Which Attraction wrapper?
| Want… | Use | Tell |
|---|---|---|
| Most leads, lowest cost | **Free** | Close rate ≈ premium; only volume/cost differ |
| Compliance / kill no-shows / card on file | **Discount** (50%+, on a *splinter*) | Use for *well-understood* services |
| Fewest, richest clients | **Premium** | Only if proven offer + sales process + cash |
*Match the whole structure (Price, Prospect, Process, Promotion, Product) to ONE strategy — never mix free-offer prices with a premium offer.*

## Friction dial (free offers) — pick to hit the "sweet spot"
Qualifications · Information Requirement · Number of Steps · **Forced Consumption** (favorite) · Advertisement Length. *Weed out the weirdos, don't lose the lazy whales.*

## Discounts — four displays (test all four)
% Off · $ Off · Relative Equivalent ("Save a Steak Dinner") · Simply the Price. Use **absolute prices for understood products**; "50% off agency retainer" fails (no reference price).

## CFA levels (where are you?)
- **L1**: 30-day GP < CAC → floating on loans/credit. Avoid bootstrapped.
- **L2**: 30-day GP = CAC → credit limit = ad budget.
- **L3**: 30-day GP > 2x CAC → customers fund all growth (1 → 4,095 in 12 mo).

## Stacking — build order & the 4-filter
**Attract → Up Front Cash → Upsell/Downsell → Continuity** (then repeat). Add any offer only if it passes: **Right Stage · Right Problem · Right Way · Right Time.** Advance *every* prospect (even a "no") to the next stage with downsells.

## Thresholds & benchmarks
- **LTGP : CAC ≥ 3:1**; CAC within **3x industry average** is "good enough" → then raise LTGP.
- **Discounts**: skip 5–25% (margin loss); use **50%+**. Paid front-ends → **85–90%+ show rates**.
- **Free Presentation conversions**: dinner ~25% to low offer / ⅓ of those to high · masterclass 10% · 5-day challenge 2–5% of signups · summit 10% of final day · strategy call 25% to 2nd call.
- **Freemium**: only ~100%-margin businesses; CAC = service cost ÷ conversion %.
- **First upsell take rate** (frictionless) can be **90%+** — and is the #1 predictor of back-end conversion.

## Continuity stick (pick the lever)
- **Lifetime Upgrades** — pair a *when* (delay/milestone) × *what* (one-time/variable/lifetime); add status labels.
- **Lifetime Discounts** — cheaper rate while they pay; + urgency + scarcity + believable reason. *Only if you charge more later.*
- **Discount + One-Time Fee** — bigger up-front fee → lower churn ("bigger head, longer tail"; sunk-cost).
*Know your numbers before any fixed-price lock. Never re-grant a lifetime discount to someone who canceled.*

## CAC problem? One question
**"Do my engaged leads have the problem I solve and the money to spend?"**
- No → **advertising** problem (unqualified).
- Yes but too few → **advertising** problem.
- Yes but not buying → **sales** problem. *(Don't fire the sales guy for an ad problem.)*

## Employees: train & diagnose
- **3Ds**: Document → Demonstrate → Duplicate. "Could a stranger get your results from the checklist alone?"
- **Performance Diamond** (why output drops): **Communication** (didn't know THAT) · **Training** (didn't know HOW) · **Motivation** (don't WANT) · **Circumstances** (something's stopping them). Usually it's something *you* missed.
- **Maker vs. Manager**: a "got a min?" costs a maker **10x** (breaks one work unit). Protect makers' big blocks; managers fill slots, makers empty them.

## Tells & smells
- Removed qualification steps → more leads, **less money**.
- A crazy free offer with no "Why?" → unbelievable → zero response.
- Habitually discounting the *core* offer → trains buyers to wait.
- A prospect who skips the first upsell → unlikely to stay.
- "If you want it done right, do it yourself" → false belief that caps growth.
