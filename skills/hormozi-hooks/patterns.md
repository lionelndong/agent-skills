# Patterns — $100M Playbook: Hooks

## Pattern: Build the Hook (Call Out + Condition for Value)
**When to use**: Writing any hook — ad, content, email, outreach, webinar, training.
**How**: (1) Write a **Call Out** that makes the prospect think "This is for me" (harness the cocktail party effect — be the "name" that pierces the noise). (2) Attach a **Condition for Value** — explicit ("If [target] [does/consumes this], they get [reward]") or implicit (show the result, then "Look how I did it"). Make it specific enough for the right people, broad enough for the most people.
**Trade-offs**: A great call out with no value promise grabs attention but doesn't convert; a value promise no one notices never gets read.

## Pattern: Verbal Hook Types (the 8 templates)
**When to use**: You need hook variety fast for a platform/avatar.
**How**: Take ONE message and write it as each type — **Labels, Questions (Yes/Open), Conditionals, Commands, Statements, Lists/Steps, Narratives, Exclamations.** (See the chiropractor set: same offer, seven formats.)
**Trade-offs**: Some types fit some platforms better; you won't know which wins until you test — so produce several.

## Pattern: Nonverbal Hooks
**When to use**: Video/visual platforms that allow sound or imagery.
**How**: Open with a sound or visual that signals either an **embarrassing disaster** (dropped tray) or **important news** (champagne-flute *cling*) — both make people need to know what's next. Pair with a verbal hook when the platform allows.
**Trade-offs**: Easy to be gimmicky; the nonverbal must still set up a value promise (banana → "the value is bananas").

## Pattern: 70-20-10 Hook Allocation
**When to use**: Planning every batch of ads/content.
**How**: **70%** proven winners ("more of what works," from your spreadsheet) · **20%** "Winner Adjacent" (model outliers from other niches) · **10%** wild new formats/hooks. Document losing experiments; graduate winning 10% experiments into the 70%.
**Trade-offs**: Too much 10% destabilizes baseline performance (payroll!); too little 10% and you stop finding new winners.

## Pattern: The Hook Spreadsheet (swipe library)
**When to use**: Continuously — your system of record for hooks.
**How**: One tab per platform you advertise on; columns **name, hook, views, link.** Review your best hooks of all time before making any content. Your best X posts become hooks for shorts/captions.
**Trade-offs**: Requires discipline to log every result; pays off because the hook then dictates the content.

## Pattern: Volume Testing by Content Type
**When to use**: Translating your hook data set into production.
**How**: **Ads** — record ~10 hooks per 1 ad. **Short content** — reuse each winning tweet as a short, then expand on the hook. **Long content** — record 3–4 hooks; pick best in post. **Email** — split-test every campaign; log winners separately.
**Trade-offs**: More upfront recording/effort, but you stop guessing which hook wins — the data decides.

## Pattern: Let the Data Do the Teaching (cold start)
**When to use**: No past content, or no mentor.
**How**: Make a bunch with no idea what'll happen → see what happened → keep the **top 10%** (make more like it), drop the **bottom 10%** (make less like it) → repeat forever, applying 70-20-10 going forward.
**Trade-offs**: Early output will be rough, but "some of it will be in the top 10%" — that's your seed.

## Pattern: Run the Hook Checklist
**When to use**: Operationalizing the whole playbook each cycle.
**How**: **First time** — study others' top performers, isolate hooks, write your favorite 50, use them all. **Every time after** — take your top 5 (top 10%), make 70% of next batch from them, 20% permutations (visuals + words), 10% completely new. Verify each hook has a call out + implied value.
**Trade-offs**: It's deliberately repetitive; the discipline is the point.

## Pattern: Email Subject Line = Hook
**When to use**: Any email campaign or launch sequence.
**How**: Treat the subject line as the call out + value promise; use curiosity ("Btw… (I have a favor to ask)"), exclusivity ("Only open this if you have a business and want to scale"), or a named method ("Revealed: My Whisper-Tease-Shout Method"). Split-test every send.
**Trade-offs**: Clickbait that under-delivers trains subscribers to ignore you; the promise must be real.
