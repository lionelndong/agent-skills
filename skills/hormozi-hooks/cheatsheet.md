# Cheatsheet — $100M Playbook: Hooks

## The one definition
**A hook is whatever gets the attention of your audience** — what they see/hear first. "If nobody notices your ad, then nobody buys your stuff. Nothing else matters."

## The anatomy (every hook = 2 parts)
| Part | Job | Test |
|---|---|---|
| **Call Out** | Make them think "This is for me" | Does it pierce the noise (cocktail party effect)? |
| **Condition for Value** | Promise value from consuming/doing | Explicit ("if X → reward") or implicit (show it) |
*Design rule: specific enough to get the RIGHT people, broad enough to get AS MANY as possible.*

## Effort allocation (Ogilvy's 80 cents)
"After you've written your headline, you've spent eighty cents of your advertising dollar." → **If you have 10 hours to advertise, spend the first 8 on hooks.**

## The 8 verbal hook types (one message → eight hooks)
| Type | Example (chiropractor) |
|---|---|
| **Conditions/Conditionals** | "If you're a chiropractor, this video will get you more leads." |
| **Labels** | "Chiropractors I have a gift for you." |
| **Questions** (Yes / Open) | "Would you rather struggle to get patients for life or fix it in 6 weeks?" |
| **Commands** | "Watch this if you want to get more patients." |
| **Statements** | "The top 1% of chiropractor clinics follow these rules…" |
| **Lists or Steps** | "11 ways Chiropractors get more patients without paid ads." |
| **Narratives/Stories** | "All of a sudden, my phone starts ringing off the hook…" |
| **Exclamations/Provocative** | "Ahhhhh… this is the blueprint to becoming a millionaire." |
*+ **Nonverbal**: sounds/visuals — signal a disaster (dropped tray) or important news (champagne *cling*); combine with verbal when the platform allows.*

## 70-20-10 Innovation Rule (per batch)
| Bucket | % | What | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core** | 70% | "More of what works" — proven hooks | Stabilizes performance / payroll |
| **Emerging** | 20% | "Winner Adjacent" — model other niches' outliers | Paste outlier + repackage ideas |
| **Big New Ideas** | 10% | Wild new formats/hooks | Doc losers; winners graduate → 70% |

## How many hooks to record (volume rules)
| Content type | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Ads** | ~**10 hooks** per 1 ad |
| **Short content** | Reuse each winning tweet as a short, then expand the hook |
| **Long content** | **3–4 hooks**; pick best in post |
| **Email** | **Split-test** every campaign; log winners separately |

## The Hook Checklist
- **First time**: study others' top performers → isolate hooks → write favorite **50** → use them all.
- **Every time after**: top **5** (top 10%) → **70%** of next batch from them → **20%** permutations (visuals + words) → **10%** completely new.
- **Every good hook contains**: a call out + an implied value.

## Decision rules / tells
- **Hook bombing despite great everything-else?** It's the hook. Rewrite only the first 1–2 sentences (Dean's Larry King fix sold millions).
- **Writing about yourself?** Stop — "they don't care." Make the *audience* feel important.
- **Don't know what content to make?** Wrong question — **the hook dictates the content.** Harvest hooks first.
- **No past data / no teacher?** Make a bunch → keep top 10%, cut bottom 10% → repeat. "Let the data do the teaching."
- **Email?** The subject line IS the hook.

## Payoff vs. cost (why bother)
**Nail hooks** → more & better clicks, lower cost per customer, new markets, colder traffic still converts (2x–5x–10x overnight). **Suck at hooks** → wasted ad spend, worse customers, capped scale. Master hooks alone and you can 5x / 10x / 100x attention with nothing else changed.
