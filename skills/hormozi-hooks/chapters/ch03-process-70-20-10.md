# Chapter 3: My Process For Finding, Creating & Testing Hooks

## Core Idea
Hooks aren't guessed — they're harvested and tested. Hormozi's creation process follows **Google's 70-20-10 Innovation Rule** for allocating effort, then runs a platform-specific testing protocol. The punchline: **"the hook dictates the content, and not the other way around"** — once you have big data sets of winning hooks, you never wonder what content to make again.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Google's 70-20-10 Innovation Rule** (Larry Page & Sergey Brin's resource-allocation strategy, applied to hooks):
  - **70% — Core Business**: "more of what works." Use hooks already proven in your own content or others'. Stabilizes all advertising and guarantees a baseline of performance. *(e.g., best-performing X posts get reused as hooks for shorts/captions.)*
  - **20% — Emerging Business**: promising but less proven — **"Winner Adjacent."** If something works in another niche, model the concept. *(Subscribe to outlier-finding tools, or search your own platforms/topics for top content; paste the outlier into a deck with ideas to repackage/reuse the formula.)*
  - **10% — Big New Ideas**: risky, totally new things; "not scared to try and fail here." New visual formats and hooks (e.g., **catching a banana** in an ad). If an experiment loses, document it so you don't repeat it; if it wins, it graduates into the 70% "more of what works" bucket.
  - When to use: every content/ad batch — split effort across the three buckets.
  - How: most effort on proven winners, some on winner-adjacent models, a little on wild experiments.
- **The Hook Spreadsheet (Core Action Step)** — build a swipe library: one tab per platform you advertise on; columns across the top — **name, hook, views, link.** "Every time I go to make content, I review my best hooks of all time."
- **Testing protocol by content type** — how to translate the data sets into recording:
  1. **Ads** — record **10 or so hooks for every 1 piece of ad content** (yes, 10x).
  2. **Short content** — reuse every winning tweet (from any platform) as a short, then finish the short by expanding on the hook.
  3. **Long content** — record **3–4 hooks**; team picks the best in post-production.
  4. **Emails** — run active **split tests** on every campaign; document winners in a separate file.

## Key Concepts
- **70-20-10** — Core / Emerging / Big New Ideas effort split.
- **"More of what works"** — the 70% core bucket; proven hooks.
- **Winner Adjacent** — the 20%; model concepts that won in another niche.
- **Outlier** — a high-performing piece of content (found via tools or platform search) worth modeling.
- **Graduation** — a winning 10% experiment becomes a 70% mainstay "until something else tops it."
- **Let the data do the teaching** — improve without a teacher by copying your own top 10% and avoiding your bottom 10%.

## Mental Models
- Treat **the hook as the unit of production, not the content** — gather hooks first; content follows.
- Use 70-20-10 to **stay profitable now while staying ahead** — baseline performance funds payroll; experiments find the next winner.
- **Volume is the strategy**: 10 hooks per ad, 3–4 per long video, split-test every email — you can't pick the winner, so let the data.

## Anti-patterns
- **One hook per piece of content** — you've given the data nothing to choose from. Record many.
- **Skipping documentation of losers** — you'll repeat failed experiments.
- **"I have no past content" as an excuse** — see Worked Example; starting out is *easier*.
- **Promoting a specific tool dependency** — Hormozi deliberately names none; any outlier tool (or free platform search) works.

## Worked Example: Let The Data Do The Teaching (starting from zero)
The whole process seems to assume you already have past content to mine. Hormozi's Author Note flips it: **"If you're starting out, it's even easier."**
1. Make a bunch of content with **no idea what's gonna happen.**
2. See what happened.
3. Follow 70-20-10 going forward.

His promise: "no matter how bad your content is, some of it will be in the top 10% of that content. Look at the hooks for that content. Make more like that. Look at the bottom 10%. Make less like that. **Repeat until the end of time.** This is how you get better without a teacher. Let the data do the teaching."

## Key Takeaways
1. Allocate hook effort **70% proven / 20% winner-adjacent / 10% wild experiments**; winners graduate into the 70%.
2. Build the **hook spreadsheet** (tab per platform; name, hook, views, link) and review it before every batch.
3. Record **volume** — 10 hooks per ad, 3–4 per long video, split-test every email; let the data pick.
4. **The hook dictates the content** — harvest hooks first, then produce.
5. No past content? Make a bunch, keep the top 10%, drop the bottom 10%, repeat. **Let the data do the teaching.**
