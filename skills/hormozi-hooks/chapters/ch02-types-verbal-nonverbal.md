# Chapter 2: Types Of Hooks — Verbal And Nonverbal

## Core Idea
"In the broadest sense, anything that gets someone to notice your ad is a hook." Hooks split into two categories: **verbal** (words that get attention) and **nonverbal** (visuals and sounds). The eight verbal types below are templates you can fill for any avatar; "if the platform allows, good advertisers use verbal and nonverbal hooks together."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Verbal Hooks — the 8 types** (all examples below are Hormozi's actual all-time best hooks):
  1. **Labels** — words your avatar identifies with. *"Local business owners, I have a gift for you."*
  2. **Questions** — two kinds:
     - **Yes questions**: *"Would you pay $1,000 dollars to have the business of your dreams in 30 days?"*
     - **Open questions**: *"Which would you rather be?"*
  3. **Conditionals** — a scenario/condition leading to a result, learning, or command. *"If you're working all the time and your business isn't growing, you're working on the wrong sh*t."*
  4. **Commands** — direct commands or suggestions telling the audience to do something. *"Read this if you're tired of being broke."*
  5. **Statements** — *"The smartest thing you can do today…"* / *"How to get ahead of 99% of people."*
  6. **Lists or Steps** — *"In this video I'm going to talk to you about the 28 ways to stay poor."*
  7. **Narratives** — stories and anecdotes. *"One day I was in the back and this old lady comes in and she was piss angry."*
  8. **Exclamations / Provocative Statements** — express strong emotion (surprise, sadness). *"Ahhhhh… This is the blueprint to becoming a millionaire and I'm going to walk you through the levels."*
  - When to use: pick the type that best frames your call out + value promise for the platform and avatar.
  - How: take ONE message and rewrite it across multiple types (see Worked Example) to generate variety fast.
- **Nonverbal Hooks** — noises or visuals that grab attention. Back at the cocktail party: a dropped tray of dishes grabs attention (signals embarrassing disaster), and so does *cling*cling*cling* of a knife on a champagne flute (signals important news). Different reasons — "in either case, everyone still wants to know what happens next."

## Key Concepts
- **Verbal hook** — words that get attention (the 8 types).
- **Nonverbal hook** — a sound or visual that gets attention (e.g., catching a banana on camera, a dropped tray, a champagne-flute *cling*).
- **Avatar** — the specific target person whose identity a Label speaks to.
- **Combine modes** — use verbal + nonverbal together when the platform allows.

## Mental Models
- Think of the **8 verbal types as interchangeable wrappers** for the same core promise — one idea becomes eight hooks.
- Use a **Label** when you want a precise call out (the cocktail-party "name"); use a **Question** to pull the prospect into answering.
- Two nonverbal triggers, same result: **disaster signal** vs. **important-news signal** — both make people need to know what happens next.

## Anti-patterns
- **Defaulting to one hook type** (usually a flat Statement) and missing the variety that finds outliers.
- **Ignoring nonverbal hooks** on platforms that reward them (video/visual) — you're leaving half the attention-grab on the table.
- **A clever line with no call out or no value promise** — it may be witty but it won't make the prospect think "this is for me."

## Worked Example: One Message, Every Format (the chiropractor set)
From the Hook Checklist, Hormozi puts the *same* example in each format so a team can spin variety on demand:
- **Conditions**: "If you're a chiropractor, this video will get you more leads."
- **Labels**: "Chiropractors I have a gift for you."
- **Questions**: "Would you rather struggle to get patients for the rest of your life or fix it in 6 weeks?"
- **Commands**: "Watch this if you want to get more patients."
- **Statements**: "The top 1% of chiropractor clinics follow these rules…"
- **Lists Or Steps**: "11 ways Chiropractors get more patients without paid ads."
- **Stories**: "All of a sudden, my phone starts ringing off the hook…"

Same offer, one avatar, seven hooks — that's how you generate the volume the 70-20-10 process needs.

## Key Takeaways
1. Two families: **verbal** (8 types) and **nonverbal** (sounds/visuals); combine them where the platform allows.
2. The 8 verbal types: **Labels, Questions (yes/open), Conditionals, Commands, Statements, Lists/Steps, Narratives, Exclamations.**
3. **Rewrite one message across all formats** to manufacture variety fast (the chiropractor set).
4. Nonverbal triggers work by signaling either **disaster** or **important news** — both make people need to know what's next.
