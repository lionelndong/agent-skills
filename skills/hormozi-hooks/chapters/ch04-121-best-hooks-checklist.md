## Key Concepts
- **Swipe file** — a reusable library of proven hooks, separated by platform with a page break "so you can tear it out."
- **Platform buckets** — ads, YT content, IG, email, Twitter each have distinct winning patterns.
- **Whisper-Tease-Shout Method** — an email cadence Hormozi names in a subject line (build-up across a launch sequence).
- **Email subject line = the hook** — for email, the subject line is the call out + value promise.

## The Hook Checklist (reproduced)
**FIRST TIME THROUGH:**
- ☐ Look at top-performing ads or content of other people
- ☐ Isolate the hooks
- ☐ Write down your favorite 50
- ☐ Use all of them in ads and content

**EVERY TIME AFTER:**
- ☐ Look at top 10% performers (top 5)
- ☐ Make 70% of your next batch of ads using those top 5 hooks
- ☐ Make 20% doing permutations of those hooks (visuals AND words)
- ☐ Make 10% completely different hooks (model "FIRST TIME THROUGH")

**GOOD HOOKS CONTAIN:**
- ☐ Some way to catch their attention (call out)
- ☐ Some implication of value if they consume it

**CREATE MORE VARIETY USING** (same chiropractor example in every format): Conditions · Labels · Questions · Commands · Statements · Lists Or Steps · Stories.

## Mental Models
- Use the swipe file as your **70% "more of what works"** input — start by modeling proven winners, not inventing.
- **Sort by performance, copy the top, cut the bottom** — the checklist is 70-20-10 made literal.
- For email, **obsess over the subject line** the way you'd obsess over a video's first 5 seconds.

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating the list as a museum piece** — read once, never deploy. "They're meant to be used."
- **Copying lines verbatim across a different avatar** without re-targeting the call out.
- **Skipping the "write down your favorite 50"** step — you need volume before you can find your top 5.

## Worked Example: The Price-Anchor "Yes" Hook (Ad #5)
"Would you pay **$1,000** to have the business of your dreams in 30 days? Well, how about **$100** to have the business of your dreams in 30 days? Well… how about **free**?" — a single Yes-question hook that anchors high, drops twice, and lands on "free," pulling the prospect through three escalating yeses before the content even starts. It's a Question type (Ch 2) carrying an explicit Condition for Value (Ch 1): *business of your dreams in 30 days.*

## Key Takeaways
1. The 121 hooks are a **ready swipe file** — bucket your own the same way (per platform) and tear out the page.
2. **Run the Hook Checklist**: first time → harvest 50 and use them all; every time after → 70% top-5, 20% permutations, 10% new.
3. Every good hook still must contain **a call out + an implied value** — the checklist enforces Ch 1.
4. **Email subject lines are hooks**; apply the same rigor as video openings.
