# Cheatsheet — The New Secret Chapter (Section A: Attract)

## The one sequence (memorize this)
**Generate Flow → Monetize Flow → Increase Friction.** In that order. Start every new market free or massively discounted. "Those who give the most, get the most."

## Promotion = wrapper, not gift
Free/discount are **wrappers around the core premium (Grand Slam) offer**. Goal: enhance the offer, **never change it**. In cold markets, the wrapper answers "what's in it for me?"

## Choose your front-end offer
| Offer | Lead volume | Lead cost | Cash up front | Close rate / ticket | Pick when… |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Premium** (standalone) | Lowest | Highest | Full ticket | Highest % (buyers expect to be sold) | Proven offer + sales process + cash; or as the **2nd** offer after free demonstrated value |
| **Free** | **Highest** | **Lowest** (~5x cheaper) | None | Same as premium* | Need max volume; small/local market; testing demand; want fast referrals/testimonials |
| **Discount (50%+)** | High (within the law) | Low | A little (don't rely on it) | High; ~85–90%+ show | Free is non-compliant; want a **two-step sale**; **well-understood** service with a known price |
*Split-test proven: free vs. non-free had the **same close rate & ticket** — only lead cost/volume differed.

## Premium offer — rules of thumb
- **Infinite returns**: nothing below $0, upside is uncapped → buy traffic on a "$25,000/hr budget."
- **9.5x math**: 190 @ $50 = $9,500 vs. (5% × 190 = 9.5) @ $9,500 = **$90,250** → ~9.5x profit, ~10 clients not 190.
- Needs: (1) something very valuable, (2) a selling process that demonstrates it. New/low-volume/high-CAC → **wrap in free/discount first**, remove later.
- Existing clients → premium restructure can **3–5x prices without changing services**.

## Free offer — friction is the volume knob
**Penny gap**: ~9x more take "free" than "$0.01". Free fails for one of three reasons → fix accordingly:
| Failure | Fix |
|---|---|
| Don't want your thing | Change *what* you give away (or how you describe it) |
| Don't believe you | **Answer "Why?"** |
| Aren't seeing it (wrong pond) | Fix targeting |

**5 friction levers** (each ↑quality, ↓volume — find the sweet spot: weed out weirdos, keep "lazy whales"):
1. **Qualifications** (state them; repeat everywhere)
2. **Info requirement & question type** (more/heavier fields; open-ended > multiple-choice)
3. **Number of steps** (each step sheds people)
4. **Forced consumption** (long video before any CTA — best when eyeballs are cheap; Hormozi's favorite)
5. **Advertisement length** (longer pre-CTA content)

**Free Money Math**: judge by **absolute qualified leads**, not % qualified. $1k→500 leads (250 qualified) **beats** $1k→200 leads (160 qualified) even though #2 *feels* cleaner.

## Discount offer — rules of thumb
- **Massive only (50%+)**; **marginal (5–25%) is a trap** — just cuts margin.
- **Splinter the offer**: discount one *component* ("a sliver"), never the core offer (or you train buyers to wait for sales).
- A discount buyer is a **qualified lead, not a customer** (avoid the Groupon trap — structure the offer to auto-qualify).
- A discount only lands on a **well-understood** thing with a reference price.

## The Two-Step Sale (the biggest reason to discount)
**Ad → Opt in → promo call, set appt → shows up for promo → gets value → schedules follow-up → shows up → sold the big offer.**
- Captures the **card on file** → upsell is frictionless ("use the card on file?").
- People **show up for what they pay for** (~85–90%+) → kills no-shows; charge a penalty to enforce steps.
- Eliminate the "card-out" moment (Amazon one-click, Disney wristbands) → more buyers.
- Example: **$19 heavy-metals consult → $2,100 10-week detox plan.**

## Brass tacks
Not enough response? **Make the front end more appealing** — give people who otherwise wouldn't respond a reason to. The whole thing works because **you know your business and product better than your customers do** (information advantage) — surface their future problems, monetize via upsells.
