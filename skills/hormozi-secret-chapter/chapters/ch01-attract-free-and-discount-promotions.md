# Section A: Attract — How To Get People To Engage Using Free & Discount Promotions

## Core Idea
A promotion is a **wrapper around your core premium offer** — free or discount packaging that makes a Grand Slam Offer more attractive to a cold audience so people who *otherwise wouldn't* respond, do. The point of a promotion is **to enhance your grand slam offer, not change it.** When entering any new market, Hormozi "almost always start[s] with something free or massively discounted."

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction** (the master sequence): Generate demand first, *then* figure out how to make money on it, *then* add friction to monetize more aggressively. "Too many people try to put the cart before the horse."
  - When to use: every new market entry, every new acquisition channel.
  - How: lead with a free/discount offer to get lead flow cranking → start transacting/monetizing → add friction to raise quality and keep your front end as low as possible while you build more upsells behind it.
- **The Promotional Wrapper**: free and discount are "wrappers around the core premium offer we created." Same gift inside (the Grand Slam Offer); different wrapping paper (recycled newspaper vs. a Chanel bag) makes it "more inherently attractive."
  - When to use: cold markets where "you must give people a reason to move towards you" — it must answer *what's in it for me*.
- **The Three Front-End Offer Types** (covered across this Section): begin with a **standalone (premium) offer**, a **free offer**, or a **discount offer** — "the core of my 'get new clients' strategy." Covered in that order in ch02–ch04.

## Key Concepts
- **Grand Slam Offer** — the valuable core offer (from Acquisition.com Volume I) that the free/discount wrapper makes more attractive; never altered by the promotion.
- **Demand flow / lead flow** — the stream of interested prospects a free/discount front end generates; generate it *first*.
- **Front end** — the first offer prospects encounter; keep it "as low as possible" if you have upsells behind it.
- **Friction** — anything that makes the offer harder to take; added *after* flow to raise lead quality and monetize.
- **Cold market / cold audience** — prospects who don't know you; need a reason (the wrapper) to move toward you.

## Mental Models
- **"Those who give the most, get the most."** Counterintuitively, "if you want to make money fast… many times it comes fastest from giving things away."
- Think of free/discount as **wrapping paper, not the gift** — you change attractiveness, not the underlying value.
- **Generate demand first, monetize second** — flow is the constraint to solve before profit.

## Anti-patterns
- **Putting the cart before the horse** — trying to monetize hard before you've generated demand flow.
- **Changing the core offer to run a promotion** — the wrapper enhances; it must not water down or replace the Grand Slam Offer.
- **Leading with a hard-to-take offer in a cold market** — gives a cold audience no reason to move toward you.

## Worked Example: The First Gym Launch Offer (the "give me away for free" play)
March 2016: a 26-year-old Hormozi, unknown, in a neon-green fitness shirt, gives a bare-slides talk on how he pre-sold each gym to full capacity with no money out of pocket (the Free 6 Week Challenge). He gets mobbed — 60 minutes to walk 30 feet to the bathroom; over 100 business cards ("John M - Guy with beige jacket - supplements"). He reaches out a week later and, never having sold a course, **creates his first offer by giving the most valuable thing away for free: himself.**

The offer: *"I'll fly out to your gym. I'll spend my own money on marketing, I'll work your leads. I'll close them. Then I get the cash that I collect, and I'll give you the customers for free… I only make what I sell."* "Sound like a Grand Slam Offer? Yep." He did this for **33 gyms over 18 months** — "easy to sell. After all, it was free." It made his **first million dollars** outside his gyms — "all, starting with a free offer."

## Key Takeaways
1. Start new markets free or massively discounted — it gives wiggle room while you're still rough, generates testimonials fast, builds your conviction to close, and seeds referrals/demand.
2. The promotion is a **wrapper**: enhance the Grand Slam Offer, never change it.
3. Sequence is law: **Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Add Friction**, in that order.
4. Keep the front end as low as possible *if* you can layer in more Grand Slam Offers to upsell over time.
