## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The master sequence: Get Flow → Monetize Flow → Then Add Friction
"Too many people try to put the cart before the horse." **Generate demand first**, then figure out how to monetize, *then* add friction to monetize more aggressively — **in that order**. Keep the front end "as low as possible" if you can layer Grand Slam Offers to upsell behind it. On entering any new market, Hormozi "almost always start[s] with something free or massively discounted." Why: wiggle room while you're still rough, fast testimonials, conviction to close the first sales, and easy referrals. **"Those who give the most, get the most."**

### The Promotional Wrapper
Free and discount are **wrappers around the core premium (Grand Slam) offer** — "wrapping paper, not the gift." The point of a promotion is **to enhance your grand slam offer, not change it.** In a cold market the wrapper makes the offer more inherently attractive and answers *what's in it for me*. Three front-end offer types live "at the core of my 'get new clients' strategy": **Premium, Free, Discount.**

### Premium Offers (Grand Slam Offer on its own) — ch02
Sell the client **the best outcome available**, high-ticket, standalone (no discount/trial). Rests on **the power of infinite returns**: "there is no cap on the upside, but you can only go down to zero." So you "buy traffic with your '$25,000/hr' budget" — far more profit per sale at far lower volume.
- **9.5x math**: 190 sales @ $50 profit = $9,500 vs. (5% × 190 = 9.5 sales) @ $9,500 = **$90,250** ≈ **9.5x as profitable**, and you deal with ~10 clients, not 190.
- **Two requirements**: (1) something very valuable to provide; (2) a selling process that demonstrates it.
- **If starting over**: do NOT lead with premium — add a free/discount money model first, remove it as reputation grows. Premium works well as the **"second" offer after a free offer** (only if value was demonstrated first). With existing clients, restructuring to premium can **3–5x prices without changing services**.
- Cons: most expensive lead, slow break-even, least efficient market capture; demands avatar-specific copy and real sales skill.

### Free Offers — ch03
**"The most powerful offer of all time and will never expire"** — "value in advance." Highest lead volume, lowest cost per lead, and the fastest way to test if anyone wants your thing.
- **The penny gap** (Dr. Dan Ariely): ~9x more people take a free item than one priced at $0.01.
- **Three failure modes** (free as a diagnostic): don't-want-your-thing (change the offer/description) · don't-believe-you (**answer "Why?"**) · wrong-pond (targeting). A too-good offer must always answer **"Why?"** ("$100 → $1,000 back," nobody called).
- **Friction = the volume knob** (5 levers): **Qualifications · Information requirement & question type · Number of steps · Forced consumption · Advertisement length.** Each raises quality, cuts volume. Find the sweet spot — "weed out the weirdos but not… lose some lazy whales."
- **"Free Brings Broke People" is a myth**: four split-tests (10 markets each) → **same close rate & ticket** free vs. non-free; only lead **cost/volume** differed (free leads often **5x cheaper**).
- **Free Money Math**: judge by *absolute* qualified leads, not % qualified.

### Discount Offers — ch04
Free and discount work similarly (both create a **perceived value discrepancy**). Use **massive discounts (50%+)**, not **marginal (5–25%)** ones ("just cuts into margin"). A discount is usually a **"piece of the thing," not the entire thing**.
- **Splinter the offer**: discount one core *component* ("a sliver"), never the core offer — or you train customers to buy only on discount ("Giving Away The Farm").
- **The Two-Step Sale** (the biggest benefit): a discounted first step captures the **card on file** + trust and kills no-shows (people show up ~85–90%+ for what they pay for); then upsell the expensive offer frictionlessly. *Ad → Opt in → promo call/appt → shows up → gets value → schedules follow-up → shows up → sold* (e.g. **$19 heavy-metals consult → $2,100 detox plan**).
- A discount buyer is a **qualified lead, not a customer** (avoid the Groupon trap). Use discounts when free is **non-compliant**, or for **well-understood** services with a known reference price (dentists, chiropractors, gyms, haircuts).

### Brass tacks
Not enough response? **Make the front end more appealing** — give people who otherwise wouldn't respond a reason to. It all works because **you know your business and product better than your customers do** (an information advantage): surface their future problems, then monetize via upsells.
