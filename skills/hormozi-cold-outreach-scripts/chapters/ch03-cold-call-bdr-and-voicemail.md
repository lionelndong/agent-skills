# Chapter 3: The Cold Call BDR "Set" Script + Voicemail Script

## Core Idea
A cold-call script for a **BDR/business consultant** to open a stranger, surface an industry pain, and **book a free 20–30 min consultation** — plus a short, curiosity-driven voicemail for no-answers.

## Terminology
- **Positive reply** = the answer we're looking for (they have pain / express a need).
- **Negative reply** = not the answer we want (no pain, "doing great").

## The Cold Call "Set" Script (flow)
1. **Open**: "Hi [NAME]? … this is [BDR NAME]. I'm a business consultant here at (COMPANY). How ya been?"
2. **Reason for the call (industry data)**: "I'm reaching out real quick because we've worked with about (# of avatar)… and based on the conversations/data, they're struggling to (X industry problem) and (Y industry problem), which have been tough for a lot of (avatar). So I'm curious — how's your current situation / how's the past year been?"
3. **Branch**:
   - **Positive reply** → continue.
   - **Negative reply** (no pain) → "Which one is your biggest struggle right now — (X) or (Y)?" then "hats off to you… what are you doing for (X) right now?" → relate ("a lot of avatar don't have upfront capital for paid ads — same boat?").
4. **Offer the free consult**: "What we're doing right now is **30-minute consultation calls completely free** — showing how we generate clients (outcome) in their first (time) without (pain). Whether we work together or not, I think you'll take things you can implement today. How open would you be to that tomorrow or [day after]?"
5. **Branch on the booking**:
   - **YES** → book the meeting.
   - **NO** → fall back to email: "The best email I have is [EMAIL] — is that best? I'll send a quick doc on what we do + free training resources… but honestly it's surface-level… we'd just need **20–25 minutes**. Let's get it on the calendar — what time are you typically free tomorrow or [day]?"
   - Still **NO** → "I'll get the info over to you today — when's a good day to touch base?" (book the follow-up).
   - Dodges → "I'll follow up in a week or so."
6. **Always end with the referral ask**: "Before I let you go — do you know any (avatar) that would like (goal) over the next (time)?"

## The Voicemail Script (reference a customer/competitor)
> "Hi (FIRSTNAME), it's (YOUR NAME). I'm calling in reference to **(a current customer / their competitor)**. Please give me a call back at (NUMBER)."
*Real example*: targeting Adidas with Nike as your customer → "Hi John, it's Alex. I'm calling in reference to **Nike**. Please call me back at…"

**Why it works**: (1) **short** (no overthinking); (2) **relevant** (you named a competitor); (3) **opens a loop** (humans hate open loops).

**Voicemail don'ts**: don't say "This is (Firstname)"; don't mention your company name; don't fail to follow up.

## Key Takeaways
1. Open with industry data + pain, offer a **free 20–30 min consult**, branch to email if they decline, and **always ask for a referral**.
2. Voicemail = name a competitor + open a loop, kept short.
3. The B2B "responded-lead" setter call (when they reply) is in **`hormozi-warm-outreach-scripts` ch01**.
