# Chapter 2: Cold Email & DM Scripts + The Follow-Up Sequence

## Core Idea
Short, curiosity-driven written messages whose only job is to **get a reply / book a quick call**. Then a relentless multi-day, multi-channel follow-up sequence.

## The "Still Looking" Message (Hormozi's money message)
> **"Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?"**
No images. No frills. No links. Just a question. "This message is money for getting leads to engage — among the first things I do when I invest in a new business."
- Examples: *…buy your dream home? …get more sales leads? …tone up your arms? …open an online store? …get that six pack? …boost your credit score?*
- **Pro tip**: use **"still"** when reaching back out to a list you have; **remove "still"** when emailing a list cold for the first time.

## Cold Email: "Are you open?"
> Subject: **Are you open?** — "Hey FIRSTNAME, Are you guys open right now? … We specialize in helping **(avatar)** get **(goal)** in under **(time)** without **(pain)**. I know that's a bold claim, but we've helped **(x clients)**… generate over **(results)**. Some clients include **(notable names)**. The best part is **(irresistible offer)**. Does it make sense to hop on a quick **15-minute call**? Here's my link: [LINK]."
- Attach **5–12 case studies**.
- Close with a choice: "1) Do nothing… 2) Risk it and get paid (irresistible offer). **Click my link, pay $1 to make $15k**…" + a P.S. opt-out ("just reply 'no'") + a P.S. linking the CEO's content.

## Reply Handlers
- **Positive reply**: "Thanks for your reply. Initially we find it best to have a quick **3½-minute chat** to see whether there's a good fit. Here's my calendar: [LINK]."
- **Negative reply**: "Hey [name], so sorry to have bothered you — hope you enjoy your day!"

## Curiosity / Value-First Cold Emails
- **"I'm watching you :)"** — "was checking out your site and noticed a cool opportunity with your email opt-in… I put together a **short video** showing exactly what I found that might **double your email revenue**. Do you have 15 minutes?"
- **"THEIR NAME <> YOUR NAME"** — a personalized value pitch ("I have an idea that will make your life as a [role] easier… click here to experience (goal)… available Wednesday for a virtual cup of coffee?").

## The Follow-Up Sequence (multi-channel, by day)
- **Day 1**: Call → Email ("[Their Co || Your Co]" — "my assistant emailed you about helping with [goal]…") → Text ("I just sent an email but thought I'd follow up — when are you free for 5 min?").
- **Day 2**: Call.
- **Day 3**: Email (reply within the same thread — "still have plans in [year] to [goal]?").
- **Day 4**: Text ("just wanted to bump this — free today or tomorrow morning for a 7-min chat?") → Call (Day 4–7).
- **Day 8**: Email — **"Are you busy..?"** — "this is the 3rd email, I'm sure you are busy! (this is good). **Problem**: [goal] takes a TON of time. **Solution**: 5 minutes and I'll explain our process to expedite it."

## Key Takeaways
1. Lead with a tiny, curiosity-only question ("Are you still looking to…").
2. The cold email = bold claim + proof (5–12 case studies) + irresistible offer + easy CTA.
3. Follow up **multiple times, multiple ways, by day** — and reply to every positive AND negative.
