# Chapter 1: The Cold DM / Messenger "Setter" Script

## Core Idea
A direct-message script for strangers (cold) or lukewarm followers (warm). **Objective: a ~10-minute warm-up call** — "this is the entire point of the process: get on the phone." Get the whole process done in **48 hours or less**.

## The Golden Response Rules (for EVERY message)
1. **Acknowledge / Encourage** — build rapport with the first statement; **make them feel good**.
2. **Ask the next question** in the setter sequence.
*Example* — Acknowledgement: "Oh cool, my aunt is an accountant — you must be very detail-oriented." Question: "Does sitting a lot of the day make it hard to work out?"

## The Strategy (sequence)
**Open → Relate/Rapport → Transition (to your topic) ASAP → Clarify Goals → Label With Problem → Overview Past Pains → Create Curiosity → CALL THEM.**

1. **Open**: "Hey Name — what's going on in your world today?"
2. **Relate** (key: relate everything back to the core problem, e.g. *not having time to eat healthy/work out*):
   - **Warm** (follows/engages you): "I saw you just commented on/liked my post (thank you!) and we haven't connected… looks like you're into skydiving — are you super active?"
   - **Cold** (don't know you — seem like a real person, not creepy): "I see you have kids — do they keep you busy?"
3. **Transition**: "What do you do for work? … Does it keep you busy? … Oh wow, does it make it hard to (eat healthy / work out)?"
4. *(Sales conversation begins — Acknowledge & Compliment, then next question.)*
5. **Clarify Goal**: "So what are you trying to do right now (fitness-wise)? What are your goals?"
6. **Label With Problem**: "Got it, so you're trying to lose a few lbs… What's been your biggest struggle/hurdle?"
7. **Overview Pain**: "What have you tried before? How'd that work? For how long?"
8. **Create Curiosity**: "I have a bunch of clients who were in the same boat. Def have some suggestions for you (if you even want to hear them). I just like to ask before spurting out advice lol."
9. **Get on the phone**: "It'd take me 23,498 pages to type out haha — whenever we both have a minute we can hop on a quick call and I can explain. Would you be up for that? (no pressure)."

## Two Ways to Get the Call
1. **Call them right now** (preferred): "Cool, here's my cell 555-555-5555, what's yours?"
2. **Set a time** (way less preferable — it's a 5-min call, do it THAT day): send the scheduling link and **leave an open loop**: "I'll keep my chat open — once you grab a time, let me know so I can confirm on my side."

## Key Concepts
- **TKAD — Time Kills All Deals.** Be fast as f*** on responses; finish the whole process in ≤48 hrs.
- **The first interaction's job is to get an interaction**, then move to a call.
- **Leave an open loop** when they go to book — it pulls them back.

## Follow Up
- "Hey, any issues?" / "Saw you didn't grab a time — everything good?" Keep moving people along.

## Key Takeaways
1. Golden Rules: make them feel good, then ask the next question — every message.
2. Open → Relate → Transition → Clarify → Label → Overview → Curiosity → Call.
3. Get them on the phone fast (TKAD); call now beats scheduling.
