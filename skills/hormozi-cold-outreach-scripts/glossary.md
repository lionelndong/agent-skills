# Glossary — Cold Outreach Scripts

**BDR** — Business Development Rep; the role running the cold-call "set" script (a "business consultant"). (Ch 3)

**Cold vs. Warm (in DMs)** — Cold = strangers who don't know you (relate to seem like a real person, not creepy); Warm = people who follow/engage you. (Ch 1)

**Create Curiosity** — The step that makes the prospect want the call: "I have suggestions for you (if you even want to hear them)…" (Ch 1)

**Free consultation (30-min)** — The cold-call offer: a free call that delivers value "whether we work together or not." (Ch 3)

**Golden Response Rules** — For every message: (1) Acknowledge/encourage (make them feel good), (2) ask the next question in sequence. (Ch 1)

**Irresistible offer** — The "best part" lever in the cold email (e.g., "pay $1 to make $15k"). (Ch 2)

**Open loop** — Deliberately leaving something unresolved (a scheduling reply, a voicemail) because humans hate open loops. (Ch 1, Ch 3)

**Setter** — The person whose job is to get the prospect on a ~10-minute warm-up call. (Ch 1)

**"Still" (vs. not)** — Use "Are you **still** looking to…" for a list you've contacted; drop "still" for a first-time cold list. (Ch 2)

**TKAD** — "Time Kills All Deals" — be fast on responses; finish the process in ≤48 hours. (Ch 1)

**The "Still Looking" message** — "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?" — a no-frills question that's "money for getting leads to engage." (Ch 2)

**Voicemail (reference) script** — Short voicemail naming a customer/competitor to open a loop; never state your company. (Ch 3)
