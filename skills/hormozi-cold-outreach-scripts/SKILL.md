## Core Scripts & Frameworks

### Cold DM / Messenger Setter — objective: a 10-min warm-up call
**Golden Response Rules** (every message): make them feel good → ask the next question. **Sequence**: Open → Relate (cold = seem human; warm = reference their engagement) → Transition → Clarify Goal → Label With Problem → Overview Pain → **Create Curiosity** → **call them** (call now > schedule; leave an open loop). **TKAD — Time Kills All Deals**; finish in ≤48 hours.

### Cold Email & DM Scripts
- **The "Still Looking" money message**: *"Are you **still** looking to [4-word desire]?"* — no images/links/frills (drop "still" for a first-time cold list).
- **"Are you open?" cold email**: bold claim → proof (**5–12 case studies**) → irresistible offer ("**pay $1 to make $15k**") → 15-min CTA → opt-out P.S.
- **Curiosity emails**: "I'm watching you :)" (a short video that could "double your email revenue"); "THEIR NAME <> YOUR NAME."
- **Reply handlers**: positive → "quick 3½-min chat, here's my calendar"; negative → "sorry to bother you."
- **Follow-up sequence** (multi-channel, by day): Day 1 Call+Email+Text → Day 2 Call → Day 3 Email (same thread) → Day 4 Text + Call → Day 8 "Are you busy..?" (Problem/Solution). Reply to **every** positive and negative.

### Cold Call BDR "Set" Script + Voicemail
- **BDR script**: open → industry data + the two common pains → branch (positive/negative) → offer a **free 30-min consultation** ("value whether we work together or not") → if no, fall back to email + book a touch-base → **always end with a referral ask**.
- **Reference voicemail**: "Hi (name), it's (your name). I'm calling **in reference to (their competitor/a customer)**. Call me back at (number)." — short · relevant · opens a loop. Don'ts: don't say "this is (firstname)," don't name your company, don't fail to follow up.
