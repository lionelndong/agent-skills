# Patterns — Cold Outreach Scripts

## Pattern: Cold DM / Messenger Setter
**When to use**: DMing strangers or lukewarm followers to get a quick call.
**How**: Every message follows the **Golden Response Rules** (make them feel good → ask the next question). Run the sequence: Open → Relate (cold = seem human; warm = reference their engagement) → Transition → Clarify Goal → Label With Problem → Overview Pain → Create Curiosity → **get on the phone** (call now > schedule). Leave an open loop if scheduling.
**Trade-offs**: TKAD — be fast; aim to finish in ≤48 hrs. First message's only job is a reply.

## Pattern: The "Still Looking" Message
**When to use**: First touch to a list, to spark engagement.
**How**: "Are you **still** looking to **[4-word desire]**?" — no images, links, or frills. Drop "still" for a truly cold first-time list.
**Trade-offs**: It's a question, not a pitch — maximizes replies; you sell on the call.

## Pattern: The "Are You Open?" Cold Email
**When to use**: Cold email to a business avatar.
**How**: Bold claim ("we help (avatar) get (goal) in under (time) without (pain)") + proof (**5–12 case studies**, notable clients) + **irresistible offer** ("pay $1 to make $15k") + a 15-min-call CTA + opt-out P.S.
**Trade-offs**: Requires real proof; the offer does the persuading.

## Pattern: Multi-Day Follow-Up Sequence
**When to use**: After any cold first touch.
**How**: Day 1 Call+Email+Text → Day 2 Call → Day 3 Email (same thread) → Day 4 Text + Call → Day 8 "Are you busy..?" email (Problem/Solution). Reply to **every** positive AND negative.
**Trade-offs**: Most replies come from persistence across channels; stop on request.

## Pattern: Cold Call BDR "Set" Script
**When to use**: Cold-calling a business avatar.
**How**: Open → industry data + the two common pains → branch on positive/negative → offer a **free 30-min consult** ("value whether we work together or not") → if no, fall back to email + book a touch-base → **always end with a referral ask**.
**Trade-offs**: Lead with their industry's pain, not your pitch.

## Pattern: The Reference Voicemail
**When to use**: No-answer cold calls.
**How**: "Hi (name), it's (your name). I'm calling in reference to **(their competitor / a customer)**. Call me back at (number)." Short · relevant · opens a loop.
**Trade-offs**: Don't say "this is (firstname)," don't name your company, and always follow up.
