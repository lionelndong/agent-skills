# Glossary — Marketing Machine

Terms, named nodes, and phrases from the $100M Marketing Machine playbook. (Ch N) = chapter where it's covered.

- **6-Month Influx** — the quarterly wave of proof from running events + competitions between each other (~every 12 weeks with 1 comp + 2 events/yr) (Ch 4).
- **6-Point Testimonial Script** — Internal struggle → External struggle → Skepticism → Overcome → External victory → Internal victory; the standard arc for every recorded testimonial (Ch 2).
- **9 Components** — the one-page tear-out: Competition, Chat scrape, Community scrape, Reviews scrape, Social saves, Bonus unlocks, Award unlocks, Events, Lifecycle (Ch 1, Ch 4).
- **After moment** — the victory/result clip pulled from an upsell, milestone, or testimonial call (Ch 2).
- **Award Testimonials** — recognition tied to an aspirational goal, given in exchange for testimonials; can yield FOUR forms of proof per person (Ch 3).
- **Before moment** — the rock-bottom/starting clip pulled from the initial sales call (Ch 2).
- **Bonus Unlock** — a sexy giveaway you normally charge for, given free in exchange for a testimonial video (Ch 3).
- **Camera slave** — Hormozi's term for a founder forced to perform on camera full-time to get customers; the machine ends this (Ch 1, Ch 4).
- **Communication & Communities Scrape** — node that harvests proof from support/rep chats, community posts/wins, and reviews (Ch 3).
- **Competition** — a raw iPhone-style testimonial contest judged by the submission with the most conversions over 30 days; run 1–2×/year max (Ch 3).
- **During moment** — the in-progress clip pulled from 1–2 support calls (Ch 2).
- **Dynamite stick of proof** — many small captured moments bundled into one compelling ad (Ch 3).
- **Event Ads** — three types captured at gatherings: stage ads, professional testimonials, man in the street (Ch 2).
- **Four Forms of Proof** — from one award: group post w/ numbers (PROOF 1), emailed video (PROOF 2), unboxing video (PROOF 3), on-stage presentation (PROOF 4) (Ch 3).
- **Grand slam ad** — an outlier winning ad; volume of customer creative raises the odds of finding one (Ch 4).
- **Handler** — the named person with the clipboard hit list who pulls customers for testimonials at events; paid +$50 per off-list capture (Ch 2).
- **Hit list** — the printed clipboard list of pre-selected testimonial customers, checked off as captured (Ch 2).
- **Hook (first 3–5 sec)** — the raw reaction or strongest line that opens an ad; "the most important part of the testimonial"; tested first (Ch 2).
- **"If I got hit by a bus today…"** — the diagnostic that surfaces founder-dependency: what would keep the business growing without you? (Ch 1).
- **Key-term list** — win-indicating search words (e.g. PIF, Sales, $, Profit, Revenue, close, leads, show, scheduled, win) used to surface wins in chats/communities/reviews (Ch 3).
- **Keyman risk** — founder-dependency that makes a business a risky investment; the machine removes it (Ch 1).
- **Lifecycle Ads** — ads that show a customer's before/during/after journey, clipped from recorded calls; most editing-heavy node (Ch 2, Ch 4).
- **Man in the street** — quick one-question soundbite interviews at events, cut into fast-hitting highlight reels (10 core questions, 10+ answers each) (Ch 2).
- **Marketing Machine** — a checklist of nodes, each a reliable stream of customer-generated videos/images/words to use anywhere you sell (Ch 1).
- **Node** — one switch-on-able proof source in the machine (Lifecycle, Social Media, etc.) (Ch 1).
- **One > None** — author note: don't get overwhelmed; install one node now, add more later; capturing more proof never loses money (Ch 1).
- **Professional testimonials** — event testimonials recorded in a side room with a dedicated camera team, using the 6-point script (Ch 2).
- **"Putting It All Together"** — the operating cadence: weekly start-of-week checklist + quarterly waves (Ch 4).
- **Social Media Scrape** — node that collects tagged/posted media (via a hashtag/saying) and reuses the best as ad creative (Ch 2).
- **Stage ads** — celebratory on-stage photos/selfies/video showing event size and scope; shot end of day 1 or before lunch day 2 (Ch 2).
- **Start-of-week checklist** — the Monday ritual that forces the always-on nodes to actually run (Ch 4).
- **Studs vs. duds** — separating outperforming organic media into its own folder for ad reuse (Ch 2).
- **The bottom of the list feeds the top of the list** — delivery/success activities (bottom) generate the advertising material (top) (Ch 1).
- **"Upon Delivery" Ads** — local-business ribbon-cutting reaction ads (eyes-closed reveal, scale, veneers, "you got the job!") used as hooks (Ch 2).
- **Weekly Proof Checklist** — the 10,000-ft list of what marketing should have every week (names, social, chats, community, reviews, bonuses, awards, reactions) (Ch 4).
