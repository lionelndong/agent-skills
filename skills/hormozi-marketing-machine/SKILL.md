## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What a Marketing Machine is
**"A checklist. Where every item you check off the list represents a reliable stream of videos, images, and words you can use to advertise your business."** Goal: **More ads. Better ads. New ads — without using your face, and without doing anything you weren't already doing.** Use the outputs anywhere you sell (paid ads, content, outreach, sales pitches, webinars, VSLs). It produces customer-generated creative that, at Gym Launch, outperformed Hormozi's best direct-to-camera ads — and made the business sellable ($46.2M).

### The two diagnostics that built it
- **"If I got hit by a bus today… what would keep the business growing tomorrow?"** Surfaces founder-dependency. List everything you do; systematize it.
- **"The bottom of the list feeds the top of the list."** Customer-delivery and success moments (the bottom) are the raw material for advertising (the top). You **capture** testimonials, you don't "generate" them. Put the customer in the spotlight, not you (80% of his top-50 ads had no Hormozi face).

### One > None
Don't get overwhelmed. "One or two of these processes can completely change your life and marketing." Install one node now, run it, come back and add another. **"You won't make less money by capturing more proof that you're good."**

### The 9 Components (the nodes / the wall tear-out)
A switch-on-able set of proof sources; turn on the ones that fit and add your own:
1. **Lifecycle Ads** — clip a **before** (sales call) + **during** (1–2 support calls) + **after** (upsell/milestone) moment from recorded calls to show the customer's journey. Local version: **"Upon Delivery" ads** (raw reveal reactions as the hook).
2. **Social Media Scrape** — give a hashtag/saying, grab tagged media everywhere, **separate studs from the duds**, run the best with proven copy.
3. **In-Person Events** — three ad types: **stage ads**, **professional testimonials** (side room, dedicated camera team, hit list, handler), **man in the street** (10 questions × 10+ answers).
4. **Communication & Communities Scrape** — support/rep chats, community "wins," and reviews; search a **key-term list**, screenshot, file, **reward the turn-in**.
5. **Bonus Unlocks** — a sexy paid giveaway, unlocked **free for a testimonial video**.
6. **Award Testimonials** — recognition tied to an aspirational goal that yields **FOUR forms of proof** per person.
7. **Competitions** — a raw iPhone testimonial contest judged by **most conversions over 30 days** (1–2×/yr max).
   *(The book also names **Delivery** in the node list and presents Lifecycle as the final compiled "lifecycle video" — together these round out the 9-component tear-out.)*

### The 6-Point Testimonial Script (used everywhere)
Internal struggle → External struggle → Skepticism → Overcome → External victory → Internal victory. **The hook (first 3–5 sec) is the most important part** — test a few; have them **say their name first.**

### Award → FOUR forms of proof
**PROOF 1** group post w/ real numbers + tag owner · **PROOF 2** emailed video · **PROOF 3** unboxing video · **PROOF 4** on-stage presentation. Make the award **big, cool, community-tied** ("don't do a boring thing"; Gym Lords got a **sword & shield**).

### Cadence & install order
- **Weekly start-of-week checklist** sweeps the always-on nodes (names, social, chats, community, reviews, bonuses, awards, reaction videos).
- **~Every 12 weeks** events + competitions deliver a wave (run competitions *between* events; 1 comp + 2 events/yr).
- **Install order (effort rises):** Competition → Chat scrape → Community scrape → Reviews scrape → Social → Bonus unlocks → Award unlocks → Events → **Lifecycle (last — most editing)**.
- **Run it or it's worthless:** "Adding stuff to folders isn't gonna get you more customers — use it in your advertisements." Volume → **grand slam ads.**
