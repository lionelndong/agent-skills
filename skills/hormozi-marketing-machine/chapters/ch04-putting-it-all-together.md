# Chapter 4: Putting It All Together (Cadence, Order & the 9 Components)

## Core Idea
Run the nodes on a fixed cadence so customer-generated creative arrives continuously: a **weekly start-of-week marketing checklist** for the always-on sources, plus a **~6-month influx** from events and competitions. "Customer generated ads will give you more creative and copy than you'll know what to do with… a very good chance of finding **grand slam ads.**"

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Weekly Proof Checklist (10,000-ft view)** — every week your marketing team should have:
  - A **list of names** of people who got great results.
  - **Scraped social media** (you, tags, customers — images and videos).
  - **Chat/messaging screenshots** from account reps.
  - **Community posts & comments** (screenshots, videos, comment-section screen recordings).
  - **All new 5-star reviews** on external sites (screenshots + scroll recordings).
  - **Bonus unlocks** (post screenshots + videos to inbox).
  - **Award deliveries / recognition** (screenshots, emailed raw testimonials).
  - **Customer reaction videos** (unboxing, first-time user, long-time user, demonstrations).
  - Make checking all of the above the team's **start-of-week checklist.**
- **The 6-Month Influx** — events and competitions feed in waves. Run **testimonial competitions between your events**: with **1 competition + 2 events per year, you get a big flow every ~12 weeks.** "For some, this is all the ad content they'll ever need."
- **Recommended Install Order** (if starting out): **1) Competition → 2) Chat/messaging scrape → 3) Community scrape → 4) Reviews scrape → 5) Social media scrape → 6) Bonus unlocks → 7) Award unlocks → 8) Events → 9) Lifecycle.** Rationale: competition gives a **big influx**; the next four use stuff you **already have with minimal editing**; bonuses/awards take a *tiny* bit more effort (you must make the thing to give); events are a lot of work but "more than pay for themselves in proof alone"; lifecycle is last because it **takes the most editing horsepower.**

## Worked Example: The Combined Cadence
With one competition and two events spread across the year, plus the always-on scrapes running every week, content lands roughly every **12 weeks** in big waves and **every week** in a steady trickle. The marketing team's Monday checklist sweeps social saves, chat/community/review screenshots, bonus and award submissions, and reaction videos; the quarterly waves drop stage videos, pro testimonials, man-in-the-street reels, and (when run) the competition winners — formatted as ads and shipped. The net effect: you are never "figuring out the next ad."

## The 9 Components (the one-page tear-out)
The book's final reference lists **9 ad-type components**, each with sub-assets:
1. **Testimonial Competition** (6-point script) — post screenshots; videos in email.
2. **Chat/Messaging Scrape Ads** — screenshots; screen recordings of high-volume reply threads.
3. **Community Scrape Ads** — screenshots; screen recordings; screen-record the "wins" tab; search-a-term-then-record.
4. **Reviews Scrape Ads** — screenshots; screen recordings.
5. **Social Media Saves** — save your own images/stories; search & save customers' and their tags.
6. **Bonus Unlock Ads** — videos in email; screenshots.
7. **Award Unlock Ads** — videos in email; unboxing videos; screenshots.
8. **Event Ads** — stage videos/pics; pro testimonials (6-point script); man-in-the-street compilations.
9. **Lifecycle** — cut award-winners / milestone-hitters / ascended customers into one "lifecycle" video.

## Key Concepts
- **Grand slam ad** — an outlier winner; more creative volume → higher odds of finding one.
- **Start-of-week checklist** — the recurring ritual that forces the always-on nodes to actually run.
- **Wave vs. trickle** — events/competitions = quarterly waves; scrapes/social = weekly trickle.

## Mental Models
- Think **portfolio of creative**: volume + variety is how you reliably hit grand slams.
- Sequence by **effort-to-influx ratio**: do the big-influx, low-edit nodes first; save the high-edit ones (lifecycle) for last.
- A node only counts if it's **on a checklist someone owns** — otherwise it silently stops.

## Anti-patterns
- **Capturing without running** — the recurring warning: folders don't make money; ads do.
- **No cadence/ownership** — without a start-of-week checklist, the always-on nodes lapse.
- **Doing competitions too often** — they're a periodic wave, not a weekly tool.
- **Starting with lifecycle** — most editing-heavy; demoralizing as a first node.

## Key Takeaways
1. Put the always-on nodes on a **weekly start-of-week checklist**; let events/competitions deliver **~quarterly waves**.
2. Install in the order **Competition → scrapes → social → bonuses → awards → events → lifecycle** (effort rises down the list).
3. Volume of customer-generated creative is what surfaces **grand slam ads** — and more customers.
4. Use the **9-component tear-out** as a wall reference so the machine keeps feeding itself.
