# Chapter 3: Scrapes, Bonuses, Awards & Competitions

## Core Idea
The highest-volume, lowest-effort proof comes from **scraping** interactions you already have (chats, communities, reviews) and from **engineering** proof on purpose with **bonus unlocks**, **award testimonials**, and **competitions** — the last of which judges winners by actual ad performance.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Communication and Communities Scrape** — "weaponizes all those great little moments… then bundles them into a dynamite stick of proof." Three sources: **Support/Account-Rep Chats · Community Posts/Wins · Reviews & Comments.**
  - How (chats): build a **list of win-indicating key terms** ("PIF, Sales, $, Profit, Revenue, Monthly, close, closed, leads, show, scheduled, win") → give reps a shared folder → set a checklist reminder to search the terms, **sort by date, screenshot the new ones**, add to the folder (optional subfolders by type) → **reward reps who turn in screenshots** ("if you don't reward them… they won't do it"); optional **$100 gift card** weekly for anyone whose screenshot becomes a winning ad.
  - How (community): create a **"wins" post category** and folder → every **Friday** prompt "wins of the week" → **Monday** screenshot weekend wins → search the same key terms inside the group, screenshot the best → **BONUS: screen-record scrolling through the wins** for yet another ad.
  - How (reviews): same method, new source — list your review sites (**Glassdoor, Yelp, Google, Facebook, Trustpilot, Amazon**), sort by newest, screenshot the good ones, add to a folder, and reward the person who does it.
- **Bonus Unlocks** — "Create some giveaway that you charge separately for… make it big and sexy" (e.g. sales scripts, platform ad training). Attach a price and make it for sale — then offer it **free in exchange for a testimonial video.**
  - How: pitch how great it is (record the pitch where all customers see it) → tell them to **send a <5-min video hitting the 6 points** to `proof@yourco.com`, give a reference example, **post the video in the group with stats** (a second written testimonial), tag the community manager, and include scrape-able keywords.
- **Award Testimonials** — "If you do it right, you can get **FOUR forms of proof** from one award per person." Requires an **aspirational goal** (gym owners: a $1M/year gym) and an award **tied to your community, made big and cool** (Gym Lords got a **sword and shield**).
  - How (the 4 proofs): customer makes a **group post** with actual numbers using your template (**PROOF 1**) + tags the owner → **emails the video** to `proof@yourco.com` (**PROOF 2**) → records an **unboxing video** (**PROOF 3**) → is **recorded on stage** receiving it in front of the community (**PROOF 4**). Mail a personalized/engraved trophy; present a generic one on stage.
- **Competitions** — "God this is good. But people tire of them, so… once or twice a year max." Run a **raw, iPhone-style testimonial competition** judged by ad performance: **the submission with the most conversions over 30 days wins.**
  - How: set announce + deadline dates → pick a **crazy award** (e.g. **6 months free service**) → give submission instructions (fill a results template, attach video, tag the community manager, email the raw file to `proof@yourco.com`) → share **last time's winner** and the 6-point script → announce on all internal channels and **personally invite/follow up the top ~50** → ask them to film **in the moment** ("the raw-er the better") → cut off submissions, format as ads, run them → set a **30-day reminder** to count results and announce the winner.

## Worked Example: One Award → Four Forms of Proof (Gym Lords)
Aspirational goal: hit a **$1M/year gym**. The award is a **sword and shield** ("swords are common in the fitness world as trophies… people associate them with prestige — and our community was called Gym Lords"). A qualifying owner: (1) posts their real sales numbers in the group on the testimonial template and tags the owner → **PROOF 1**; (2) emails the video to `proof@yourco.com` → **PROOF 2**; (3) films themselves **unboxing** the engraved sword/shield mailed to them → **PROOF 3**; (4) is **recorded on stage** being presented a generic one at the next event → **PROOF 4**. One award ceremony, four reusable assets per person.

## Key Concepts
- **Key-term list** — the search words that surface wins inside chats/communities/reviews.
- **Dynamite stick of proof** — many small captured moments bundled into compelling ads.
- **Bonus unlock** — a paid-looking offer "bought" with a testimonial instead of money.
- **Reward-or-it-won't-happen** — every scrape/capture task needs a manager reward, or the team stops doing it.

## Mental Models
- Treat scraping as a **standing weekly chore with a key-term query**, not an occasional hunt.
- Treat awards as **status engineered for proof** — "People like to get recognized in meaningful ways for their meaningful results."
- Judge competition entries the only way that matters: **does it perform as an ad?**

## Anti-patterns
- **Boring awards** — "don't do a boring thing"; if people don't crave it, you get no proof.
- **Running competitions too often** — audiences tire; cap at 1–2×/year.
- **Just giving awards and leaving** — instead, require a testimonial to receive the award.
- **No reward for the team** — screenshots and turn-ins dry up.
- **Hoarding screenshots in folders** — they make no money until run as ads.

## Key Takeaways
1. Scrape chats, communities, and reviews with a **key-term list**; screenshot, file, reward.
2. **Bonus unlocks** trade a sexy paid offer for a 6-point testimonial video.
3. **Award testimonials** can yield **four forms of proof** from one recognition.
4. **Competitions** (≤2×/yr) are judged by **30-day ad conversions**, max raw, top-50 follow-up.
