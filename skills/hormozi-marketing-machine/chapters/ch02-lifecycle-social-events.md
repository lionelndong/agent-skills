# Chapter 2: Lifecycle, Social Media & Events

## Core Idea
Three of the most powerful nodes capture *moments* you're already creating: the customer's journey on your calls (**Lifecycle**), what you and your customers post (**Social Media Scrape**), and the proof you harvest at gatherings (**In-Person Events**). Each is a hand-off checklist that turns raw moments into ad-ready creative.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Lifecycle Ads** — "harness the *moments* that occur in normal conversations in your business." Record all calls, then clip a **before** moment (initial sales call), a **during** moment (1–2 support calls), and an **after** moment (upsell/milestone/testimonial call) to "show rather than tell a client's entire journey through time."
  - When to use: any business with recorded calls (especially remote/SaaS/coaching).
  - How (the checklist): **Legal** adds consent/permission language to contracts → **IT** records all calls (sales, onboarding, support, ascension, milestone) and **buys extra storage / turns off auto-delete** → **All departments** create calendar events titled with the client's name → **Sales** sends happy-customer calls to marketing weekly → **Marketing** cross-references calendar events by name/date, clips the before/during/after moments, builds a **friendly labeling system**, then "Run 'em and print money."
- **"Upon Delivery" Ads** (local businesses) — a "ribbon cutting" moment: eyes-closed reveal of the new kitchen, hopping on the scale, seeing new veneers, "you got the job!" reactions. The **raw reaction becomes the hook (first 3–5 sec)** of the ad; make per-reaction ads and **highlight reels of only the reactions** ("these crush").
- **Social Media Scrape** — systematically collect tagged/posted media from you and customers and reuse the best as ad creative.
  - How: give the community a **saying or hashtag** and a profile to tag (e.g. `#gymlord #gymlaunch #gymowner`; `#poweredbyprestige`; `#neverskipdessert`) → set **regular reminders** to grab tagged media on every platform (especially 24-hour stories) → tag/save with searchable names → **"Separate studs from the duds"** (a folder for media that outperformed typical posts) → run the best images/videos as creative with already-performing ad copy.
  - Tell: **"Good organic performance correlates highly with good ad and outbound performance."**
- **In-Person Events** — "If you're an in-person business you have an insane advantage… Every day is an in-person event." Do events **1–2× per year** even if you sell nothing there; document everything for marketing assets. Three event ad types: **Stage ads · Professional testimonials · Man in the street.**

## The Three Event Ad Types (how)
- **Stage ads** — a picture on stage with people celebrating behind you (visual proof of size/scope); multiple selfies + a video; "you only get one take." Do it at **end of day one or before lunch on day two.**
- **Professional testimonials** — pull customers to a side room and record. Reserve a **separate recording room** (or 2–3 extra hotel rooms); hire **≥1 extra camera team** (put the better team on testimonials — they matter more); set up lighting/sound and **"TEST. TEST. TEST."**; pick customers ahead of time and confirm in writing; tell them it won't clash with the keynote (you'll give notes); use a **clipboard hit list**; announce the handler at the event open; **incentivize** testimonials (bonus to the customer; **+$50 to the handler for every off-list person** captured).
- **Man in the street** — quick soundbite "one-question interviews" during breaks. Hire a **second two-man team**; prepare **10 core questions**, get **10+ answers each**, and cut **fast-hitting highlight reels** of everyone answering one question (e.g. "How many new customers since you started?", "What were you making before X vs now?", "How much weight have you lost?").

## The 6-Point Testimonial Script (used in pro testimonials)
A single script walks every testimonial through the customer's arc:
1. **Internal struggle** — rock bottom before; what you hated doing; what you wanted but couldn't.
2. **External struggle** — your objective metrics (then).
3. **Skepticism** — main concerns about buying/clicking/joining.
4. **Overcome** — what made you do it anyway.
5. **External victory** — your objective metrics now.
6. **Internal victory** — best moment since; what you can do now you couldn't; what you no longer have to do.
Rules: **the hook is the most important part** — be explicit, test a few hooks first; have them **say their name at the start** (so the camera team can match names to videos). Then "Save 'em. Upload 'em. Add music. And run 'em."

## Key Concepts
- **Before / During / After** — the three lifecycle moments that tell the journey.
- **Hook (first 3–5 sec)** — the raw reaction or strongest line; tested and prioritized.
- **Studs vs. duds** — separate outperforming organic media for ad reuse.
- **Hit list / handler** — the named person + clipboard that ensures testimonials actually get captured at events.

## Mental Models
- Treat recorded calls as a **gallery of content** to clip, not as one-time conversations.
- Treat your hashtag as a **free always-on testimonial collector.**
- At events, **"squeeze every last drop of marketing power"** — the media is often the only ROI when you sell nothing on stage.

## Anti-patterns
- **Failing to capture big moments** — "Many businesses have these big *moments* but people fail to capture them."
- **Skimping on testimonial production** — losing audio/video for an entire event ("you will want to kill everyone"); putting the weaker camera team on testimonials.
- **Pulling people during the keynote** without warning or notes.
- **Not incentivizing** capture — without a bonus, the handler/team won't grab off-list people.

## Key Takeaways
1. Record everything; clip **before/during/after** into one lifecycle journey.
2. Give a hashtag, scrape relentlessly, and **separate studs from the duds.**
3. Run events with a recording room, a dedicated camera team, a hit list, and incentives.
4. Run every testimonial through the **6-point script**; nail the hook; get the name first.

## Connects To
- **Ch 1**: these are the "bottom of the list" moments feeding the top.
- **Ch 3**: the 6-point script reappears in bonus unlocks, awards, and competitions.
- **Ch 4**: events are the ~6-month influx; lifecycle is installed last (most editing).
