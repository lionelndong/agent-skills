## Core Idea
The second half of the journal is Hormozi drafting **$100M Leads**. The legible pages show **the book's skeleton (re-drawn many times), the Core Four, the Four Lead Types / Lead Getters, the lead-stage pipeline, the Hook → Retain → Reward content unit, the LTGP-to-CAC math, and the Rule of 100.** As in the Offers half, he sketches the same Section I–VI outline repeatedly and re-orders it, and labels guiding quotes from **Feynman** and **Franklin**.

## Frameworks Introduced (exact legible names)
- **The Core Four** — the four ways to get leads: **Warm Outreach · Cold Outreach · Post Content · Run Paid Ads.** Drawn on a 2×2 of **People Who Know You vs. Don't** × **Private (one-to-one) vs. Public (one-to-many)**: Warm Outreach (know you / private), Cold Outreach (don't / private), Post Content (know you / public), Paid Ads (don't / public).
- **Lead Getters / The Four Lead Types** ("Others tell people about your stuff") — **Customers · Affiliates · Employees · Agencies.** (Plus **Referrals** as the customer-driven engine.)
- **Engaged-Lead / Acquisition-Channel Lead Stages** — **Stranger → Uncontacted → Contacted → Engaged → Qualified → Sold** (with the activities **Advertise → Incentivize → Nurture → Sell**, and a NO branch).
- **The Advertising Cycle** — **Offer · Avatar · Medium → Advertise → Acquisition Channel · Platform · Audience → Engaged Leads.**
- **Content Unit: Hook → Retain → Reward** — the atomic structure of every piece of content (labeled "Hook / Retain / Reward," abbreviated H-R-R or M-M-R/E-R on some pages), nested from short-form up to a **Book → Chapters → Paragraphs**.
- **Three Retention Structures** — **Lists · Steps · Stories** (the ways to retain attention inside one piece of content).
- **The Five-Step Lead-Getting process** (the "Start Here" Leads page) — **Step 2 · Step 3 · Step 4 · Step 5: LEARN (L) / LOSE (O)** … built on **Problem → Solution → Magnets → Advertising → Core Four → More / Better / New**, ending in the **Four Lead Types** (Customers, Affiliates, Employees, Agencies).
- **The Rule of 100** — **do 100 primary actions per day for 100 days** across **Warm Reach Outs · Cold Reach Outs · Content · Paid Ads** (measured in minutes/dollars). **Bonus: do the 100/day on more than one of the Core Four.**
- **Internal vs. External Core Four** — the same Core Four applied to **getting customers** (External: Customers/Affiliates/Employees/Agencies) and to **getting employees** (Internal: ask your network, cold outreach, post-to-promote your job, recruiters/in-house recruiting).
- **The Performance Diamond** — diagnose dropped performance via **Communication · Training · Motivation · Circumstances.**

## Worked Example (a concrete one the journal walks through)
**The LTGP / CAC math (the New Way vs. Old Way cash-flow graphs).** Across several legible pages Hormozi draws a customer at **Price 15, Cost 5, Gross Profit 10**, paying for **~25 months → ~250 LTGP**, and contrasts:
- **Old Way:** spend ~30 to acquire, only ~10 gross profit in month 0 → **cash flow goes negative** before it recovers (the dip below the break-even line).
- **New Way:** add an **upsell** so first-purchase profit exceeds CAC → **cash flow stays positive** from the start.

He then states the rule explicitly (legible): **"If LTGP = 30 and CAC = 10, it has a 3:1 LTGP:CAC ratio … is it profitable?"** — i.e. compare **LTGP to CAC** to decide whether a channel funds its own growth. *(This is the journal's draft of the book's LTGP-to-CAC / Customer-Financed-Acquisition logic — for the worked numbers and thresholds use the `hormozi-100m-leads` and `hormozi-lost-chapters` skills.)*

A second concrete sketch: the **"You → Lead Getter → Lead Getters → Recruiter"** leverage diagram, showing **1 you getting 100 leads → 1 lead getter getting 100 → many lead getters → a recruiter who recruits lead getters**, i.e. how Lead Getters compound (the "1× → 4×" leverage note).

## Mental Models
- **Lead Flow = Attention × Pressure, through Filters.** Every lead-gen page reduces to generating attention, applying the right pressure/persuasion, and filtering down to qualified leads (Fishing / Gold Panning / Diamond Mining).
- **One platform first, then expand.** The skeleton repeatedly says start on one platform/channel, make it work, *then* go More/Better/New/Cheaper and Broader/Up/Down/Adjacent.
- **"How most see the market vs. how it really is."** A recurring diagram: a beginner thinks "I'm 1 of 4 advertisers on the one channel I know, so I own ~1/4 of the market and can't grow" — when the real market spans all of the Core Four × every platform × every Lead Getter. The point: you are almost never as big as you think.
- **Referrals compound; churn decompounds.** The Golden Ratio (referred ÷ churned) determines whether the business grows on its own.

## Anti-patterns
- **Don't read the dense numeric pages as data.** The CAC/LTGP figures, "Rule of 100" tallies, and lead-list mockups OCR to noise; trust the *labeled frameworks*, not the handwritten numbers.
- **Don't treat the re-drawn skeletons as different systems.** Sections I–VI appear many times with the order shuffled — that *is* the concept evolution, not multiple frameworks.
- **Don't substitute the journal's shorthand for the book.** "Hook/Retain/Reward," "Core Four," and "LTGP:CAC" are real and legible, but their full definitions live in the finished book.
