# Chapter 1: What This Journal Is — The "Dear Reader" Note

*Source: "100M Journal" (266 scanned pages; ~40K chars of sparse OCR, most of it illegible handwriting). Copyright © 2023 by Alex Hormozi, Acquisition.com.*

## Core Idea
This is **Hormozi's private handwritten journal** for the making of **$100M Offers** and **$100M Leads** — scanned to PDF. It is **not a teaching book and not a fill-in workbook**. It is a curated artifact of **concept evolution**: page after page of him drawing, re-drawing, and re-ordering the skeletons of two books before they were written. He explicitly says he picked "only the most legible" of his notes — and even so, the vast majority of the handwriting is not reliably readable. What *is* legible are the **printed section labels, the book outlines/skeletons, and the named frameworks**, which match the published books exactly.

Use this chapter to understand *what the document is and how to treat it*. The actual frameworks are documented in ch02 (Offers journal) and ch03 (Leads journal).

## The "Dear Reader" note (legible, front of the journal)
Reproduced from the legible opening pages:

> **"DON'T JUDGE ME FOR MY 100 BAD IDEAS. JUDGE ME FOR MY 1 GOOD ONE — BECAUSE IT'S THE ONLY ONE THAT MATTERS.**
>
> Please find enclosed inside my private journal for both [$100M] Offers and [$100M] Leads. These books have consumed a lot of my time & effort over the past few years. **I curated only the most legible of my notes and I grouped them in a way that made some sense of the concept evolution.** As you look through these notes you will see me wrestle with ideas in front of your eyes. You'll see how they evolved and maybe get a deeper understanding as a result. **You'll also see some ideas I cut from the books but still hold merit in their own right.** I hope you enjoy both these notes and the books as much as I enjoyed making them for you.
>
> Your biggest fan, **Alex Hormozi**
>
> **P.S. YOU CANNOT LOSE IF YOU DO NOT QUIT.**"

## Key Concepts
- **"100 bad ideas / 1 good one"** — Hormozi's framing for the journal: the value of the creative process is the rare winning idea; the rest is the cost of finding it. This is *why* he's comfortable publishing messy, wrong, and abandoned notes.
- **Concept evolution, grouped by book** — He organized the notes by the two books and "in a way that made some sense" of how each idea developed. The journal shows him re-sketching the same skeleton many times (e.g., the same Sections I–IV re-drawn page after page with the order changing).
- **Cut ideas that "still hold merit"** — Some sketched concepts never made the published books. The journal is the only place several of them appear (some overlap with the material in the **Lost Chapters**).

## Mental Models
- **Treat it as a structural/historical artifact, not a source of teaching.** It *confirms the skeletons and named components* of two books; it does not re-teach them.
- **Legible-only rule.** Only printed labels and clearly-readable headings can be trusted. Do not infer meaning from illegible strokes — the honest answer to "what does this scribble say?" is usually "unknown."

## Anti-patterns
- **Reconstructing teaching from handwriting.** The bulk of the pages OCR to noise (fragments like "DAI DAI DIS DEY," "Ee Ee Ere," "ÉÉÉ"). Anything not plainly legible is **not** captured anywhere in this skill and should not be guessed.
- **Expecting new frameworks.** The journal predates and underlies the books; it does not contain a hidden additional system. For the real content, go to the books' skills.

## Key Takeaways
1. This is Hormozi's **private journal** documenting the *making of* $100M Offers and $100M Leads — a behind-the-scenes look at his thinking, not a how-to.
2. Only the **printed labels, skeletons, and framework names are legible**; most handwriting is not.
3. Its value is **historical**: it shows the genesis and evolution of the Grand Slam Offer, the Value Equation, Client/Customer Financed Acquisition, the Core Four, and the rest — drafted, re-drafted, and re-ordered in his own hand.
