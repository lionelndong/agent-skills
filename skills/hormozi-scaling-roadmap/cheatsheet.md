# Cheatsheet — $100M Scaling Roadmap

## The master table — find your stage by its CONSTRAINT, then do the ONE thing
| Stage | Name | Role | Headcount | Constraint (Bottom Line) | Graduate by (the ONE objective) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | IMPROVISE | Researcher | N/A | Nothing is happening | Get people to try your stuff for free |
| **1** | MONETIZE | Starter | 1 | Your business makes no money | Make your first sale |
| **2** | ADVERTISE | Doer | 1 | New customers are inconsistent | Let more people know about your stuff |
| **3** | STABILIZE | Trainer | 1 to 4 | There's too much for one person to do | Get help |
| **4** | PRIORITIZE | Manager | 5 to 9 | Trying to be everything to everyone | Niche down to serve only people like your best customers |
| **5** | PRODUCTIZE | Director | 10 to 19 | Not making enough per customer. Low LTV:CAC. | Make and sell your second premium product to customers |
| **6** | OPTIMIZE | Leader | 20-49 | Everything is inefficient | Doing better rather than doing more |
| **7** | CATEGORIZE | Executive | 50 to 99 | Every system is overwhelmed. Business is disorganized. | Triage appropriately |
| **8** | SPECIALIZE | President | 100 to 249 | No one is good enough to know everything | Create dedicated people and teams for specific tasks |
| **9** | CAPITALIZE | Chairman | 250 to 500 | Not sure where the next big growth comes from | Make a big bet on the future |

**Decision rule**: match your pain to a **Constraint**, not your headcount. Work only that stage's **Graduate by** until it's true. Then re-diagnose.

## The constraint ladder (what breaks next, in order)
No demand → can't charge → inconsistent demand → too much for one person → serving everyone → too little per customer → inefficiency → disorganization → no one knows everything → no next growth.

## "Better" beats "More" (Stage 6) — the swap table
| DON'T | DO INSTEAD |
|---|---|
| Hire more people | Train current people better |
| Add more products | Improve current products |
| Get more customers | Serve current customers better |
| Add more tools | Use current tools better |

## Owner role progression (what YOU become)
Researcher → Starter → Doer → Trainer → Manager → Director → Leader → Executive → President → Chairman.
Layers of management appear at: Stage 4 (one layer) → 7 (three) → 8 (four) → 9 (five, incl. CEOs).

## Tells you're at the wrong focus
- Still doing everything for **free** → you're stuck in Stage 0/1; charge.
- Marketing **only when slow** → Stage 2 failure; run the Rule of 100 daily.
- Fixing **everything at once** → Stage 3 failure; pick the #1 problem.
- Saying **yes to every paying customer** → Stage 4 failure; niche down.
- Growing by **selling more of the same** → Stage 5 failure; build a second product.
- Adding **more** instead of **better** → Stage 6 failure.
- Running on **one acquisition channel** → Stage 7 risk; add a second.
- "**Jack of all trades**" org → Stage 8 failure; specialize.
- Tinkering with the **cash cow** → Stage 9 failure; make a separate big bet.

## Recurring rules across all stages
- **One thing at a time** (biggest problem / one improvement / specific teams on specific features).
- **Scan 6 areas** every stage: Product · Marketing · Sales · Customer Service · Tech · People/HR & Money.
- **Formalize money as you grow**: entity (0) → payroll (3) → P&L + insurance (4) → budget/forecast (5) → balance sheet + quarterly taxes (6) → departmental budgets (7) → audited books + bankers (9).
- **Don't rush** — measure progress in quarters; foundations make the next stage easier.
