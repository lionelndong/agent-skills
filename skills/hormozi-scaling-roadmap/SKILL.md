## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The Roadmap itself — a constraint ladder of 10 stages
Scaling is a fixed sequence of stages, each defined by **one binding constraint** ("Bottom Line") and **one objective to graduate**. As the owner, your **role** changes at each stage (Researcher → Chairman). **Diagnose by the constraint, not the headcount** — find the stage whose Bottom Line matches your pain, then work *only* its "Graduate by" until it's true, and re-diagnose.

| Stage | Name | Role | Headcount | Constraint (Bottom Line) | Graduate by |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | IMPROVISE | Researcher | N/A | Nothing is happening | Get people to try your stuff for free |
| 1 | MONETIZE | Starter | 1 | Your business makes no money | Make your first sale |
| 2 | ADVERTISE | Doer | 1 | New customers are inconsistent | Let more people know about your stuff |
| 3 | STABILIZE | Trainer | 1 to 4 | Too much for one person to do | Get help |
| 4 | PRIORITIZE | Manager | 5 to 9 | Trying to be everything to everyone | Niche down to serve people like your best customers |
| 5 | PRODUCTIZE | Director | 10 to 19 | Not making enough per customer. Low LTV:CAC. | Make and sell your second premium product |
| 6 | OPTIMIZE | Leader | 20-49 | Everything is inefficient | Doing better rather than doing more |
| 7 | CATEGORIZE | Executive | 50 to 99 | Every system is overwhelmed; disorganized | Triage appropriately |
| 8 | SPECIALIZE | President | 100 to 249 | No one is good enough to know everything | Create dedicated people and teams for specific tasks |
| 9 | CAPITALIZE | Chairman | 250 to 500 | Not sure where the next big growth comes from | Make a big bet on the future |

### The 6-area scan (every stage, rising complexity)
Each stage diagnoses the same areas at higher difficulty: **Product · Marketing · Sales · Customer Service · Technology · People/HR & Money**. "Protect the business / formalize money" recurs throughout: entity (S0) → payroll (S3) → P&L + insurance (S4) → budget/forecast (S5) → balance sheet + quarterly taxes (S6) → departmental budgets (S7) → audited books + bankers (S9).

### "One thing at a time" (the recurring discipline)
Don't fix everything at once. **S3**: fix the **#1 biggest customer problem** first. **S6**: run the optimization loop **one improvement at a time** (find → why → better way → test → train → monitor; make it stick). **S7**: specific teams on specific features.

### The named methods (preserve exactly)
- **S2 — Rule of 100**: spend **100 minutes daily on marketing**, every day (not just when slow). **Double down** on the one channel that works; don't diversify early. Fix things in an **"unscalable way"** (valuable, not scalable yet). Scripts: **"CLOSER"** (convert leads), **"Angry Boat"** (upset customers), **"Epiphany Script"** (testimonials).
- **S1 — V1 ("Version 1")**: a product *good enough that people will pay*, not perfect.
- **S4 — Niche down + say "no"**: pick a best-customer type, reshape the product, **raise prices**; say no to wrong customers/projects. Start measuring (lead→sale, CSAT, response time).
- **S5 — Second premium product**: extend **LTV** by selling a new, higher-priced product to existing customers; start small, test with best customers. The new LTV funds ads + better hires.
- **S6 — "Better" beats "More"**: train current people / improve current products / serve current customers / use current tools **better**, before adding new ones.
- **S7 — Categorize + second channel**: sort employees, applicants, data, customers, money, leads into categories; add a **second acquisition channel** (e.g. referral program) so you're not a "one-engine boat"; add **lead scoring**; prevention over firefighting; ATS + HRIS.
- **S8 — Hyper-specialization + brand**: dedicated specialist teams (no "jack of all trades"); shift to **brand-building** as ads decay (clear message, free value, association with trusted brands); best leads → best closers; trim "Swiss Army knife" features.
- **S9 — The big bet**: **M&A** (buy a company) or **R&D** (build something new), big enough to move the needle (1-year+ payoff). Protect the **cash cows**; strategic workforce planning; founder-led recruiting; audited books + investment bankers.

## Scope & Limits

This skill covers the **ACQ $100M Scaling Roadmap** (Stages 0–9) — diagnosing a business's binding constraint by stage and the single objective that unlocks the next one. Stages 0–9 span ~no-revenue to 250–500 employees; beyond Stage 9, each company's path becomes unique. For offer *creation* see `hormozi-100m-offers`; for offer *sequencing / LTV* see `hormozi-money-models`; for lead generation see `$100M Leads`. The roadmap references named methods (CLOSER, etc.) without fully teaching them — it points to where each is taught. Hormozi's examples (restaurant, food truck, computer repair, gym) are preserved as written; apply the frameworks to your own context without altering them.
