# Stage 9: CAPITALIZE

## Core Idea
With 250–500 employees you're a **"Chairman"** with five layers of management under you (including CEOs). You've **hit a plateau** — your "cash cows" get worse if you change them, but you need new growth. You're **not sure where the next big growth comes from**. The job: **make a big bet on the future** (big enough to move the needle). This is the **last stage** in the roadmap; beyond it, companies enter the world of large corporations and each path becomes unique.

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Chairman
- **Headcount**: 250 to 500
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Not sure where the next big growth comes from
- **Graduate by**: Make a big bet on the future

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The big bet: M&A or R&D** — your successful products ("**cash cows**") get worse when you try to change them, but you need new products to grow. Solve it by either:
  - **Buy another company (M&A — Mergers & Acquisitions)**, or
  - **Build something totally new (R&D — Research & Development)**.
  Either way it's a big bet that takes **at least a year** to pay off. Small bets won't move the needle anymore.
- **Capitalize on success** — take advantage of your success to make big moves: buying companies, creating new products, entering new markets, or making major investments.
- **Strategic workforce planning** — turnover is up and you lack the people to grow. Plan the org structure, plan for people leaving, fill skill gaps, have **backups for important roles**, and turn generalists into specialists.
- **Founder-led recruiting for top roles** — the best people don't respond to normal job postings; they only want to talk to the founder/CEO. The founder must directly recruit the highest-level positions, sell them on the vision/growth, and be patient (can take **6+ months**).

## Key Concepts
- **Cash cows** — your famous, profitable products; changing them risks upsetting regulars, so growth has to come from new bets alongside them.
- **Brand first, then sell + multichannel attribution** — you can't tell which marketing is working anymore; build brand first, use multiple channels, and look at how all marketing works **together**, not separately. Add a compliance process so all marketing follows the rules.
- **Specialize sales by product/customer** — salespeople are overwhelmed by too many lead/product types; have different salespeople for different products and customer types, different processes per situation, and sort leads by product or location.
- **Audit-ready finances + investment bankers** — your financial records aren't good enough to convince others to fund big investments; get books **audited**, talk to **investment bankers**, set up the capital structure properly, protect against big risks, and possibly **raise money** for acquisitions or faster growth.
- **Enterprise systems** — old tech can't handle the load; hire specialized tech people, separate/update systems, possibly adopt enterprise-level software.

## Mental Models
- **A ship in calm waters** — successful but not growing much; you must choose a new heading.
- **The restaurant owner's choice** — open new locations, buy other restaurants, create a new brand, or start a food product line; whatever you pick must be **big enough to matter** at your size.

## Anti-patterns
- **Tinkering with the cash cow** — changing famous products makes them worse and upsets loyal customers.
- **Small bets** — won't move the needle for a company this size.
- **Unaudited books** — block your ability to raise capital for big moves.

## Key Takeaways
1. The constraint is **not knowing where the next big growth comes from** — make a **big bet** (M&A or R&D), expecting a 1-year+ payoff.
2. Protect the **cash cows** while betting on new products, markets, or acquisitions.
3. Do **strategic workforce planning** and **founder-led recruiting** for the highest roles.
4. Get **audited finances**, talk to **investment bankers**, and possibly raise capital to fund the bet.

## Connects To
- **Stage 8 (Specialize)**: hyper-specialization created the "outsized returns to capitalize on" with this bet.
- **Stage 7 (Categorize)** & **Stage 6 (Optimize)**: multichannel marketing and efficiency themes culminate here at enterprise scale.
- **End of roadmap**: beyond Stage 9, each company's path becomes unique to its industry and situation.
