# Stage 6: OPTIMIZE

## Core Idea
With 20–49 employees you're a **"Leader"** (a full team of managers under you). You have two products, but **everything is inefficient** — more people hasn't made things work better. The challenge isn't doing **more**, it's doing everything **better**. Goal: **doing better rather than doing more.** The real secret: **"Better" beats "More."**

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Leader
- **Headcount**: 20-49
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Everything is inefficient
- **Graduate by**: Doing better rather than doing more

## Frameworks Introduced
- **"Better" beats "More"** (the secret to success) — the explicit Don't/Do swaps:
  | DON'T DO THIS | DO THIS INSTEAD |
  |---|---|
  | Hiring More People | Train Current People Better |
  | Adding More Products | Improve Current Products |
  | Getting More Customers | Serve Current Customers Better |
  | Adding More Tools | Use Current Tools Better |
  "Success isn't about doing more — it's about getting more for what you do."
- **The optimization loop** (one improvement at a time): Find what's not working → figure out why → create a better way → test it → train people → monitor results. "You can't rush optimization." Make sure changes **stick**.
- **Cohort your customers** — group customers by **when they joined and how they use products**; track more detailed customer-success metrics (churn rises with more products).

## Key Concepts
- **CAC is rising** — ads don't work as well with colder audiences and marketing costs (CAC) go up; you need a better way to create **ads at scale**.
- **Sales needs real training** — your core product isn't closing as well as it used to.
- **Real cybersecurity** — with people joining and leaving, company data/access is at risk; set up real security, better passwords, and proper tech onboarding/offboarding.
- **Talent wants benefits** — you can't find enough good people through your network; high-level talent wants full benefits packages, retirement plans.
- **Real financials** — money seems missing/wasted; you need a real **balance sheet**, more detailed financial data, and to pay taxes quarterly.

## Mental Models
- **Patience over rushing** — making things better takes time and investment without immediate payoff; people resist change.
- **Leverage what you have** — squeeze more output from existing people, products, customers, and tools before adding new ones.

## Anti-patterns
- **Just doing more** — the tempting wrong move; adding people/products/customers/tools without fixing efficiency.
- **Rushing optimization** — skipping the find→why→better-way→test→train→monitor loop.
- **Changes that don't stick** — optimizing once without documenting and reinforcing.

## Key Takeaways
1. The constraint is **inefficiency**; the answer is **"Better" beats "More."**
2. Train current people, improve current products, serve current customers, and use current tools **better** — before adding new ones.
3. Run the **optimization loop one improvement at a time** and make changes stick (document everything).
4. Add real infrastructure: cybersecurity, benefits, a balance sheet, customer cohorts/metrics.

## Connects To
- **Stage 5 (Productize)**: the two products you now run are what need optimizing.
- **Stage 7 (Categorize)**: optimization gives way to organizing an overwhelmed, larger business.
- **Stage 3 (Stabilize)**: "one thing at a time" recurs — here as "one improvement at a time."
