# Stage 3: STABILIZE

## Core Idea
You have your first 1–4 full-time employees and there's **too much work for one person**. Going from chaos to stability. Your role changes from **"doer" to "trainer"** — you now teach others how to help run the business. Goal: **get help**.

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Trainer
- **Headcount**: 1 to 4
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: There's too much for one person to do
- **Graduate by**: Get help

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Fix the ONE biggest problem first** — you can't fix everything customers complain about. Pick the **#1 thing** that bothers customers most, fix just that really well, then move to the next. "Don't try to fix everything at once."
- **Define a "good" potential customer** — make a list of what makes someone a good lead, so you stop wasting time on people who aren't serious about buying. (Early lead-qualification.)
- **A proper onboarding / welcome path** — new customers feel lost when they start; create a proper way to welcome and guide them so they get results quickly.
- **Document & train (SOPs)** — write down how you do important tasks, train the team, give them the tools, and check their work.

## Key Concepts
- **Build trust over time** — leads buy slower now because they don't know you as well; start sending emails and creating content to build trust.
- **Tool consolidation** — you bought too many software tools; figure out which you actually need and cancel the rest (extend free trials / get discounts on the keepers). Get a basic system to track your sales pipeline.
- **Formalize money & payroll** — stop paying people informally; set up proper payroll processing, basic bookkeeping, and proper invoice/payment management.
- **Hire properly** — learn to write good job descriptions and review resumes; get a payroll service to handle employee taxes and fees correctly.

## Mental Models
- **One problem at a time = stability** — chaos comes from fighting every fire; stability comes from sequencing the biggest fix first.
- **Owner as trainer** — your leverage now is teaching, not doing.

## Anti-patterns
- **Trying to fix everything at once** — the defining Stage 3 mistake; it perpetuates chaos.
- **Paying people informally** — set up real payroll and handle employee taxes correctly.
- **Talking to unqualified leads** — define a "good" customer and stop wasting time on the rest.

## Key Takeaways
1. Pick the **#1 customer complaint**, fix it well, then move to the next.
2. Get your money in order: real payroll, accounting software, invoice/payment systems.
3. **Document tasks and train the team** so they can help you (and check their work).
4. Create a proper customer **onboarding** path and start building trust with content/email.
5. Graduate when you've moved from a one-person show to a small team that helps run the business.

## Connects To
- **Stage 2 (Advertise)**: consistent demand is what overwhelmed one person.
- **Stage 4 (Prioritize)**: with a team, the next constraint is trying to serve everyone — niche down.
- **Stage 7 (Categorize)** & **Stage 6 (Optimize)**: "fix one problem at a time" recurs at larger scale.
