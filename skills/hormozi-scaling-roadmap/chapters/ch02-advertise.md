# Stage 2: ADVERTISE

## Core Idea
You've made your first sales; now **tell more people**. The constraint is inconsistent customer flow ("packed on weekends but empty on weekdays"). Goal: **let more people know about your stuff** in a consistent way. You're a "doer" — actively running the business and starting to use freelancers/part-time help.

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Doer
- **Headcount**: 1
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: New customers are inconsistent
- **Graduate by**: Let more people know about your stuff

## Frameworks Introduced (named methods — preserve exactly)
- **Fix things in an "unscalable way"** — do whatever it takes to make customers happy even if it's inefficient (work longer, double-check everything, extra service at no charge, fix problems immediately). The point is to be **valuable, not scalable yet**. (E.g. driving ice cream to a customer's house at 2AM.)
- **The "CLOSER" method** — the method for converting leads / a consistent sales script. Practice the pitch until it's natural; track what works.
- **The "Rule of 100"** — spend **100 minutes daily on marketing**. Make marketing a daily priority (get ahead), not just something you do when slow.
- **The "Angry Boat" method** — the method for handling upset customers.
- **The "Epiphany Script"** — the script for getting testimonials from happy customers.

## Key Concepts
- **Double down, don't diversify** — test different ways of reaching people, then stick with the one that works best; don't diversify in the beginning. Do more of what works (volume).
- **Track actions & results** — keep track of what brings in customers.
- **Getting Help → full-time** — part-timers aren't enough; start converting them to full-time, post job ads, look for people who want steady work.
- **Managing People** — set up employment paperwork (W2s and 1099s), basic training, work schedules, clear responsibilities.
- **Money management** — set up QuickBooks (or similar), save for taxes, check the bank account daily, track all expenses.

## Mental Models
- **Valuable before scalable** — at this stage you earn loyalty and reputation by over-delivering manually; efficiency comes later.
- **Consistency is the product** — the business problem isn't "can I get a customer?" but "can I get them *predictably*?"

## Anti-patterns
- **Marketing only when slow** — guarantees the feast/famine cycle; market every day (Rule of 100).
- **Diversifying channels too early** — spreads you thin; pick the winner and pour volume into it.
- **Sacrificing quality for speed** — never; fix problems immediately and keep improving.

## Worked Example
Inconsistent flow is the enemy. The fix is operational discipline: every day, put **100 minutes** into the one marketing channel that's working (Rule of 100 + double down), run sales through a consistent script (**CLOSER**), and when a customer is upset, run the **Angry Boat** method; when they're delighted, run the **Epiphany Script** to capture a testimonial. Meanwhile keep customers happy in an **unscalable way** until the flow is steady.

## Key Takeaways
1. Make marketing a **daily** habit (Rule of 100 = 100 minutes/day), not a reaction to slow periods.
2. Pick the one channel that works and **double down**; don't diversify yet.
3. Use named playbooks: **CLOSER** (convert leads), **Angry Boat** (upset customers), **Epiphany Script** (testimonials).
4. Serve in an **unscalable way** — be valuable now, scalable later.
5. Graduate when quality is consistent, new customers come regularly, systems work, money is organized, help is reliable, and marketing is steady.

## Connects To
- **Stage 1 (Monetize)**: you've proven people pay; now make the *flow* of buyers consistent.
- **Stage 3 (Stabilize)**: consistent demand creates "too much work for one person" — the next constraint.
