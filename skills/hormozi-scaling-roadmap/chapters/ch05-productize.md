# Stage 5: PRODUCTIZE

## Core Idea
With 10–19 employees you're a **"Director"** (2–3 managers under you). Your original product is doing well, but **one product isn't enough** — customers buy once and then have nothing else to buy (and may churn). The constraint is **low LTV:CAC** — you're not making enough per customer. Goal: **make and sell your second premium product to existing customers** to extend LTV.

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Director
- **Headcount**: 10 to 19
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Not making enough per customer. Low LTV:CAC.
- **Graduate by**: Make and sell your second premium product to customers

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Extend LTV with a second premium product** — "you need to make more per customer." This gives you the cash to **scale advertising** and **recruit higher-level people**. Listen to what else customers want, build something new they can buy, make it high quality, and **price it well (usually higher than the first product)**.
- **One-Hit Wonder → Multiple Products** — you can't grow just by selling more of the same thing. Reduce risk and build a more valuable company by having multiple products and a path for customers to buy more.
- **Balance** (the key to Stage 5) — keep the first product running well while creating the new one; stay profitable while investing in growth; keep current customers happy while attracting new ones. Successful companies **start small** with the second product and **test it with their best customers**.
- **Connect customer service to product development** — let what customers say drive what you build next.

## Key Concepts
- **LTV:CAC** — the ratio of lifetime value to customer acquisition cost; raising per-customer value (LTV) is the lever at this stage.
- **Churn** — customers leaving because they need things you don't offer; a second product reduces it.
- **Stop overpromising in sales** — salespeople overpromising causes refunds and bad reviews; create sales materials and record customer calls for training.
- **Company-owned tech & dashboards** — move off employees' personal computers/software (they take info when they leave); get company equipment, a way to track leads/customers, and dashboards for key numbers.
- **Real budget & forecasting** — you don't know how much you can reinvest; build a real budget and simple financial forecasting; promote good employees from within; create proper termination policies.

## Mental Models
- **Sell the next thing to people who already trust you** — the cheapest growth is more value to existing customers.
- **Balancing act** — protect the cash cow while building the next engine; don't sacrifice one for the other.

## Worked Example
Original product sells well but customers have nothing else to buy, so LTV:CAC is low and some churn. The Director listens to what else the best customers want, **builds a second, higher-priced premium product**, tests it small with those best customers, and creates a clear path to move customers up. The extra per-customer revenue funds more advertising and better hires — restarting growth.

## Anti-patterns
- **Selling more of the same** — won't grow you; you need a *new* product, not just volume.
- **Overpromising in sales** — causes refunds and bad reviews; build proper sales materials.
- **Letting employees use personal computers/software** — they take company info when they leave.

## Key Takeaways
1. The constraint is **low LTV:CAC** — make more per customer.
2. Build and sell a **second premium product** (priced higher than the first) to existing customers.
3. **Start small and test** the second product with your best customers; keep the first one running smoothly.
4. The extra LTV funds **advertising and higher-level hires** — fueling further growth.

## Connects To
- **Stage 4 (Prioritize)**: a clearly-defined best customer is who you build the second product for.
- **Stage 6 (Optimize)**: now with two products, the next constraint is inefficiency — make them *better*, not more.
- **$100M Money Models**: this stage's "second offer to existing customers" is the roadmap's nod to upsell/continuity LTV mechanics.
