# Stage 1: MONETIZE

## Core Idea
You've graduated from giving stuff away free — now **start making actual money**. The single goal: **make your first sale** (and then consistent first sales to different customers), proving people will pay for what you offer. Moving "from cooking free meals for friends to opening a small food stand where people actually pay."

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Starter
- **Headcount**: 1
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Your business makes no money
- **Graduate by**: Make your first sale

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The "V1" Product (Version 1)** — your product/service isn't good enough to pay for yet. Make a "Version 1": not perfect, just **good enough that people will pay for it**. (A sandwich good enough to sell at a food truck, not yet restaurant quality.)
- **"Hidden costs"** — find the things that make customers unhappy even if they don't tell you directly (slow service, leaky packaging). Your job is to figure out *why* people might not like or use your product.
- **The challenge areas at Stage 1**: Product (the main challenge — make it worth paying for), Marketing, Sales, Customer Service, Technology, Getting Help, Managing Money, Protecting Yourself, The Human Side.

## Key Concepts
- **Sell from proof and problems** — use results from free customers as proof ("look how much this helped them"); talk to people about their problems; start with people who already liked your free stuff; be honest about what it costs.
- **Paying customers ≠ free customers** — they have higher expectations, complain more, need more attention, want better results.
- **Managing Money basics** — set up payment collection (Square/PayPal), actually run a real payment through the system, get the first customer's money into your bank, track who has/hasn't paid.
- **Getting Help** — you can't do everything; reach out to people you know, use Upwork/Fiverr for freelancers, start small with part-time help.

## Mental Models
- **Use the free crowd first** — your warmest, most forgiving buyers are the people who already liked the free version.
- **"Good enough to pay for" beats "perfect"** — perfection delays the only thing that matters here: the first sale.

## Anti-patterns
- **Still doing everything for free** — a sign you need more work; you must make the free→paid leap.
- **Waiting for a perfect product** — V1 just needs to clear the "worth paying for" bar.
- **Losing track of payments / chaos** — a warning sign; keep simple money records from day one.

## Key Takeaways
1. Make your **first sale** — then make consistent first sales to different customers.
2. Build a **V1**: good enough to pay for, not perfect.
3. Set up real payment collection and keep track of money from the start.
4. You're ready for Stage 2 when you're consistently making sales, people pay your prices, basic systems exist, money is tracked, you understand customers, and you're ready to advertise.

## Connects To
- **Stage 0 (Improvise)**: the free users you served become your first buyers.
- **Stage 2 (Advertise)**: once first sales are reliable, the next constraint is *consistent* new customers.
