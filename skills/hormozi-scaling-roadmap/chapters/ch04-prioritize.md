# Stage 4: PRIORITIZE

## Core Idea
With 5–9 employees you become a real **"manager"** (one layer of organization between you and the front line). You've said "yes" to anyone who'd pay, and now you're **trying to be everything to everyone**. The key skill is learning to say **"no."** Goal: **niche down to serve only people like your best customers**.

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Manager
- **Headcount**: 5 to 9
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Trying to be everything to everyone
- **Graduate by**: Niche down to serve only people like your best customers

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Pick your ideal customers / Niche down** — your product is pulled in too many directions. Choose a **specific type of customer** to focus on, update the product to serve just those ideal customers, and **raise prices to match your specialized service**.
- **Learning to say "no"** — no to customers who aren't right, no to projects that don't fit, no to doing everything yourself. (Saying "no" means turning down money short-term to build something bigger long-term.)
- **Start measuring things** — begin tracking basic numbers: how many leads turn into sales, customer satisfaction, lead-response speed, and basic financials (P&L).
- **Make it professional** — write an employee handbook, set up proper insurance, create basic processes; "start running your business like a real company."

## Key Concepts
- **Attract the right people** — too many unqualified leads; create better free content to attract the right people; make the sales team follow up faster.
- **Centralize customer info (CRM)** — customer information is scattered; get a proper CRM for notes and interactions, and start tracking customer satisfaction.
- **Hire properly** — stop interviewing the wrong people; learn to interview properly and check references; create an employee handbook (time off, dress code, etc.).
- **Money reality** — "one-time" expenses keep surprising you; running a business costs more than you thought. Get proper P&L statements and basic business insurance.

## Mental Models
- **Solving the right problem for the right person** — "a lot of businesses do lots of activity and don't grow because they're solving the wrong problems — for the wrong person."
- **Focus is subtraction** — you grow by choosing who *not* to serve.

## Anti-patterns
- **Saying yes to every paying customer** — the cause of being pulled in too many directions.
- **No measurement** — you can't manage lead→sale conversion or satisfaction you don't track.
- **Staying a generalist offer** — refusing to niche keeps prices low and the product diluted.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Figure out who your best customers are** and reshape the product (and price) around them.
2. Be willing to **say "no"** to poor-fit customers and projects.
3. Start **measuring**: lead→sale rate, satisfaction, response time, basic financials.
4. Professionalize: employee handbook, insurance, CRM, basic processes.
5. Graduate when the business is focused and professional — serving specific customers really well.

## Connects To
- **Stage 3 (Stabilize)**: a stable team is the precondition for choosing a focus.
- **Stage 5 (Productize)**: once you serve a clear best customer, the next move is a *second product* for them.
- **Stage 8 (Specialize)**: niching the customer here mirrors specializing the org later.
