# Stage 0: IMPROVISE

## Core Idea
You're one person with an idea but **no actual business**. The whole job: **get people to try your stuff for free** and find out if they actually want it — like a cook serving friends before opening a restaurant.

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Researcher
- **Headcount**: N/A
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Nothing is happening
- **Graduate by**: Get people to try your stuff for free

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The 6 focus areas at Stage 0** — the lens for every stage, applied here at the simplest level:
  - **Product**: make something you can give away for free (a service, information, or an actual product) — the point is that it's free so people will try it.
  - **Marketing**: tell people about your free stuff; start with people you know (friends, family, your email list, social followers).
  - **Sales**: you don't know how to sell yet, so just ask people to try the free stuff, then ask what they think and what would make it better.
  - **Customer Service**: take really good care of free users — answer fast, help them use it, be super nice; they're helping you learn.
  - **Technology**: just the basics — computer, internet, phone, email. Like a hammer and screwdriver when building a house.
  - **Basic Setup**: create a business entity (e.g. an LLC) to separate personal and business money; open a business bank account; track spend for write-offs.

## Key Concepts
- **Practice Mode / Tutorial Level** — Stage 0 is the tutorial level in a video game. Learn the basic moves and get comfortable before the real game (charging money) begins.
- **Protect Your Business** — the recurring foundational task: entity + bank account + bookkeeping basics.
- **Free as a research tool** — free removes the price barrier so you get the honest feedback that tells you what people actually want.

## Mental Models
- **Building a house with no tools or materials yet** — you're at the very beginning; don't expect structure to exist; your job is to start.
- **Cook before the restaurant** — get people to taste the food (free) before charging anyone.

## Anti-patterns
- **Trying to make money yet** — don't; focus on helping people and getting feedback.
- **Spending money / buying fancy tools** — use free tools; keep everything simple.
- **Refusing to change** — be willing to change things based on what people tell you.

## Key Takeaways
1. The only goal is: enough people try your free stuff **and like it**.
2. You graduate when people say good things and **come back for more** — that's the simple signal you're ready to charge (Stage 1).
3. Keep it simple, be patient, learn from every interaction, and protect yourself with a basic legal/financial setup.

## Connects To
- **Stage 1 (Monetize)**: graduating = people love the free version → now charge for it.
- **The 6 focus areas**: this is the first appearance of the Product / Marketing / Sales / Customer Service / Technology / Money(+Setup) lens used at every stage.
