# Stage 7: CATEGORIZE

## Core Idea
With 50–99 employees you're an **"Executive"** with three layers of leadership under you. Everything feels messy and disorganized — **every system is overwhelmed** ("Organized Chaos"). The job: start **sorting things into categories** and build a system to handle each category. Goal: **triage appropriately.**

## Stage Card (verbatim)
- **Your Role**: Executive
- **Headcount**: 50 to 99
- **Bottom Line (the constraint)**: Every system is overwhelmed. The business is disorganized.
- **Graduate by**: Triage appropriately

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Sort the chaos into 6 categories** (the core of "Categorize") — organize and segment, for what comes next:
  - **Your Employees** (who does what)
  - **Your Job Applicants** (which ones to interview)
  - **Your Data** (where everything is stored)
  - **Your Customers** (which ones need the most attention)
  - **Your Money** (what to spend on what)
  - **Your Leads** (which to focus on)
- **The "one engine boat" — add a second acquisition channel** — you only have one main way of getting customers; if it breaks, you're stuck. Add a **second way to get customers** (e.g. a **referral program** where happy customers bring in new ones). Get better at **lead scoring**.
- **Lead scoring** — score leads so you know which ones are worth pursuing; create a follow-up checklist; reward salespeople who get customers to show up.
- **Prevention over firefighting** — customer service is always putting out fires; create a plan that **prevents** customer problems before they happen and focus on renewals/buying more.

## Key Concepts
- **Specialized teams on specific features** — the product feels outdated and slow to improve; have specific teams fix one problem/feature at a time instead of everyone fixing different things.
- **Centralize data in the cloud** — info, documents, and passwords are scattered; move everything to the cloud with a central store.
- **ATS (applicant tracking software)** — hiring takes too long and candidates accept other jobs; get software to track candidates and use professional recruiting firms.
- **HRIS (Human Resource Information System)** — employee info is scattered; get an HRIS as "one big digital filing cabinet."
- **Spending controls** — money is spent carelessly; create **quarterly budgets per department**, spending limits, and approval for big purchases; improve insurance and forecasting.

## Mental Models
- **Organizing a messy garage** — first sort everything into piles (tools, sports gear, decorations), then build proper storage for each pile. Same with the business.
- **Don't run on one engine** — single points of failure (one channel, one process) are existential risks at scale.

## Anti-patterns
- **Relying on one acquisition channel** — if that engine breaks, the whole business is in trouble.
- **Firefighting customer service** — fix the cause (prevention + renewals), not just the symptoms.
- **Scattered data and uncontrolled spending** — centralize data (cloud) and put budgets/approvals on money.

## Key Takeaways
1. Triage by **sorting into categories**: employees, applicants, data, customers, money, leads.
2. Add a **second acquisition channel** (e.g. referral program) so you're not a one-engine boat.
3. Implement **lead scoring**, prevention-focused customer service, and renewal focus.
4. Put in real systems: cloud data, **ATS**, **HRIS**, quarterly departmental budgets with approvals.

## Connects To
- **Stage 6 (Optimize)**: optimized parts now need organizing at larger headcount.
- **Stage 8 (Specialize)**: categorized work is the precondition for assigning specialists.
- **Stage 9 (Capitalize)**: "use multiple channels" deepens the second-channel idea introduced here.
