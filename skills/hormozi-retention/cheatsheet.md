# Cheatsheet — Retention / Churn Checklist (Hormozi)

## The Churn Checklist (run in order; skip what doesn't fit)
1. Activation points → 2. Onboard → 3. Incentivize activation → 4. Community linking → 5. Correct/fire bad customers → 6. Annual options → 7. Cancellation call/video → 8. Survey twice/yr → 9. Customer journey.
> "No magic bullet… 100 tiny things that all save a few people each."

## Decision rules
- **Where to start?** → **Activation points.** "All roads lead to activation"; biggest effect on churn. Retest every 6–12 months.
- **More value or lower price?** → **More value.** Keep value > price; prefer adding value over cutting price.
- **Add features to reduce churn?** → **No — subtract.** Overwhelm is the #1 churn cause. Cut to the **core 2–3 things**. "Value per second, not seconds of value."
- **Raise price?** → **Yes, if doubling price loses <20% of deals** (all else equal). Then test intermediate points (he tested $39–$59).
- **Onboarding format?** → ladder down only as cost forces: **custom > generic > | personal > group > | live > recorded > | carrots > sticks > | some beats none.** Expensive product → 1-on-1.
- **Connect customers to whom?** → **To each other, not you.** "Hard to leave a relationship."
- **Annual: mandatory or optional?** → Phone/webinar sales → can make **mandatory** (fewer sales, less churn, often more money). Website checkout → **optional**.
- **Bad customer?** → correct (explain + model good), **3 strikes**, then remove.
- **Cancellation: discount to save them?** → **Never "just because."** Save with a **Redo** or an **Upsell** (credit toward higher program).
- **Budget for retention?** → set it at **~1/5th of CAC** per customer.
- **Spend on keeping vs getting?** → **Keeping.** Retention is **5–25x** cheaper than acquisition.

## Key math (memorize)
- **Churn = leavers ÷ original pool** (new signups never change it).
- **10% → 3% churn = 3.3x LTV** (10 months → 33 months). (9% → 3% also = 3.3x.)
- **5% retention increase → 25–95% profit increase** (Harvard Business Review).
- **Referrals > churn ⇒ unstoppable compounding growth** (10% vs 10% = flat).
- **Big head, long tail**: $6,800 upfront + $199/mo → LTV **$6,800 → $12,800**.

## Tells & smells
- **Attendance/usage dropping** → churn is coming. Intercept now (gym: drop to 2 sessions; SaaS: **usage churn** = subscribed but not using).
- **Month-1 churn spikes after you start contacting people** → that's **"shaking the three,"** not failure. Keep going; it falls in months 2–3.
- **Customers in the bottom two engagement tiers** → your cancellations live here. Most retained customers are top-tier.
- **You're "always marketing, never growing"** → a leaky-bucket business; fix churn first.
- **You keep having to "create new stuff" to stay alive** → high churn is eroding your reputation tailwind.
- **A customer who just bought again** → least likely to churn → push **ascension**.
- **Wealthier customers** → want **less** handholding, not more.

## Cancellation-call script beats
- Let them vent; **out-agree** them — "only one person can be in the angry boat at the same time."
- "That's completely ridiculous. You're 100% correct. Will you give me the opportunity to make it right?"
- "If these things happened, you'd be happy?" → that's your fix roadmap.
- Remind them what they **lose**: custom URLs, posts, access, founder pricing, data/work.

## Customer journey (four milestones)
**Activate → Testimonial → Refer → Ascend.** Activate first; rest in any sensible order; incentivize each. **Always ask them to buy again.**

## ACA reach-out
**A**cknowledge what they did · **C**omplement them · **A**sk a question.

## Survey questions (find your core 2–3)
1. "If I removed everything on this list but one, what would you want to keep the most?"
2. "If I kept everything on this list but one, which would bother you the least to see me remove?"
