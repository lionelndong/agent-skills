# Patterns — Retention / Churn Reduction (Hormozi)

Every named technique in this playbook, as a reusable pattern. Keep his names exactly.

## Common Factors Analysis (DoD Process)
**When to use**: solving any retention/ops problem where people already win at it.
**How**: Ask experts (plural) → get all answers → keep only the factors that appear across most of them. Research your *lowest-churn* customers/operators; interview each on everything they do; narrow to ~5 common factors.
**Trade-offs**: needs enough data/experts to find overlap; reveals the core driver and removes individual bias.

## The 5 Horsemen of Retention (gyms)
**When to use**: high-touch / location or coaching businesses.
**How**: (1) Track attendance — reach out when sessions drop to two; (2) Reach out 2x/week (praise + solve little problems); (3) Handwritten cards (made yourself); (4) Member events (every 21/42/63 days, "regular to you, random to them"); (5) Exit interviews (set at onboarding; trigger on cancel notice).
**Trade-offs**: labor-intensive ("nobody does it… because it's work") — which is exactly the moat.

## Churn = Leavers ÷ Original Pool
**When to use**: any time you measure retention.
**How**: `(start − retained) ÷ start`. Count only the first-timepoint cohort; ignore new signups.
**Trade-offs**: simple and unambiguous; people miscount by adding new customers to the denominator.

## The "Churn 100%" Inversion
**When to use**: designing a retention plan from scratch.
**How**: list everything that would make customers leave (ignore, break promises, miscommunicate, treat poorly, set unrealistic expectations, hide progress, isolate, make it hard) → do the exact opposite of each.
**Trade-offs**: fast, comprehensive, obvious — "but nobody does it."

## Test the Price (Price/Churn Table)
**When to use**: choosing the most profitable price point.
**How**: hold traffic constant; chart price → conv rate → sales → churn → LTV → total return. Pick the highest total return ("most sales at the highest LTV"). If doubling price loses <20% of deals, raise it. Then test intermediate points (he tested $39–$59).
**Trade-offs**: conversion and churn rise with price but not proportionally — so "test, test, test."

## Provide On-Going Value ("value per second")
**When to use**: any recurring-revenue/membership business.
**How**: cut to the core 2–3 things and make them great; reduce effort and sacrifice so customers actually consume; when consumption stops, contact them more. "There is wisdom in deletion."
**Trade-offs**: adding features feels like progress but usually *raises* churn (overwhelm).

## Activation Points
**When to use**: first/highest-priority churn lever for any business.
**How**: gather churned-customer data → keep those who stayed ≥3 months → sort by spend, take top 20% → learn everything about them → narrow to 5 common factors → onboard everyone to that behavior; update messaging to attract that avatar. Retest every 6–12 months.
**Trade-offs**: data-dependent; "all roads lead to activation" — biggest effect on churn.

## Onboarding to the Activation Point
**When to use**: every new customer (and sales prospects, to lift close rate).
**How**: Outline how to get value → drive to activation point. Resell the value (in their goals), show what unlocks over time, teach communication norms, follow up (always schedule the next touch). Ladder: custom > generic, personal > group, live > recorded, carrots > sticks, **some beats none**. Optional: sell onboarding ("earn the right").
**Trade-offs**: 1-on-1 is high-effort but high-return (one co.: +25% ascensions; $2M/mo → $2M/wk).

## Incentivize Activation (+ stay past churn points)
**When to use**: when you know the activation behavior and want adoption.
**How**: reward the behavior (unlocking courses, calls, event tickets, badges, status, free lifetime access at a hard level). Place unlockables *just past* major churn points (e.g. unlock on month 4 if people leave at month 3). Watch **usage churn** and intercept non-users.
**Trade-offs**: build incrementally — each incentive helps on its own.

## Community Linking
**When to use**: memberships/communities at any scale.
**How**: connect members to each other (group events, manual intros, community podcast, elevate micro-celebrities). "Connect members to each other, not you."
**Trade-offs**: more scalable than a single tie to you; "hard to leave a relationship."

## Correct or Fire Bad Customers
**When to use**: communities where bad actors degrade everyone's experience.
**How**: delete bad posts + explain + model good; 3 strikes then remove; pin best 1–2 posts daily; create categories incl. Wins/Fun/Discovered/Meetups.
**Trade-offs**: short-term loss of a customer for long-term health of the base.

## Annual Payment Options
**When to use**: extend average stay, especially phone/webinar (mandatory) or checkout (optional) sales.
**How**: "buy 10 months, get 2 free" (16% off) → 10–20% uptake; steeper discount → more. Price annual above avg months if <12, else buy-10-get-2-free.
**Trade-offs**: mandatory lowers sales but cuts churn (can net more).

## Big Head, Long Tail
**When to use**: offers with large one-time value + ongoing consumable value.
**How**: big upfront (education/setup) priced to that value + small recurring (e.g. $6,800 + $199/mo → LTV $6,800 → $12,800). **Bundle the recurring with the upfront on one sale** to keep the anchor.
**Trade-offs**: splitting into two sales destroys the retention-driving anchor.

## Cancellation Call / Video
**When to use**: any churning customer (call for higher price points; video for high-volume/low-price).
**How**: tell new customers it's required. On the call: let them vent, out-agree them ("only one person in the angry boat"), then **Save with a Redo** or **Save with an Upsell** (credit toward the higher program). Remind them what they lose (URLs, posts, access, founder pricing, data).
**Trade-offs**: saves ~half; the other half gives feedback gold. Never discount "just because."

## Survey Twice a Year + Regular Reach-Out (ACA)
**When to use**: protecting your core 2–3 things and showing customers you care.
**How**: ask "If I removed everything but one, what would you keep most?" and "If I kept everything but one, what bothers you least to remove?" Between surveys, reach out 1-on-1 every 2–3 weeks via **ACA** (Acknowledge, Complement, Ask). Wealthier customers want less handholding.
**Trade-offs**: light effort, compounding goodwill.

## Customer Journey (Four Milestones)
**When to use**: turning retention into a planned system.
**How**: Activate → Testimonial → Refer → Ascend; incentivize each (pairs with unlockables). Activate first; the rest in any sensible order. **Ask them to buy again** (ascension).
**Trade-offs**: just-purchased customers churn least — ascension reduces churn AND adds revenue.
