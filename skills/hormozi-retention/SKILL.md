## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What churn is, and the goal
**Churn = customers who leave over a period** = `leavers ÷ original customer pool` — and it counts **only** the first-timepoint cohort (new signups never change it). A business that loses customers as fast as it gets them is a **"leaky bucket business"… hardly a business at all.** Target: **< 3% monthly churn** ("I have yet to see a business with less than 3% churn that does not make great money"). Keep **value > price** and customers stick; prefer **adding value** over cutting price.

### Why it's the highest-return work
- **10% → 3% churn = 3.3x LTV** (a customer stays 33 months instead of 10).
- Retention is **5–25x cheaper** than acquisition — budget **~1/5th of CAC** to keep a customer.
- **HBR**: a **5% retention increase → 25–95% profit increase**.
- **Referrals > churn ⇒ unstoppable compounding growth.**
- Existing customers already trust you, got value, gave a card, cost nothing to acquire, and are your ideal buyer.

### Provide On-Going Value To Get On-Going Customers
**"Think value per second, not seconds of value. Less but better is better than more and decent. Overwhelm is the #1 reason for churn."** Cut to the **core 2–3 things** and make them great; reduce effort/sacrifice so customers actually **consume** the value; when consumption stops, contact them more. "There is wisdom in deletion." Design retention by **inversion**: list what would make customers leave (ignore them, break promises, miscommunicate, treat poorly, set unrealistic expectations, hide progress, isolate them, make it hard), then **do the exact opposite**.

### The 5 Horsemen of Retention (the gym answer)
Found by interviewing the lowest-churn gyms and keeping the **common factors**: **(1) Track attendance** (≥3 sessions/wk sticks; reach out when it drops to two) · **(2) Reach out 2x/week** (praise + solve little problems) · **(3) Handwritten cards** (made yourself) · **(4) Member events** ("regular to you, random to them," every 21/42/63 days) · **(5) Exit interviews** (set at onboarding; saves ~half of cancellations). Expect **"shaking the three"** — churn spikes in Month 1 (quitters + already-churned-still-billed), then falls in months 2–3.

### The Churn Checklist (the cross-industry system — run in order, skip what doesn't fit)
1. **Figure out activation points** — leading indicators of retention: *"Every customer that does (X) or gets (Y) stays longer than those who don't."* Find them: churned-customer data → keep those who stayed **≥3 months** → sort by spend, take **top 20%** → narrow to **5 common factors**. "All roads lead to activation."
2. **Onboard customers** = teach them to hit the activation point. **Custom > generic, personal > group, live > recorded, carrots > sticks, some beats none.**
3. **Incentivize activation** (unlockables, status, access); place rewards **just past major churn points**; watch **usage churn** (subscribed but not using).
4. **Community linking** — **"connect members to each other, not you."** Events, manual intros, community podcast, elevate micro-celebrities.
5. **Correct or fire bad customers** — explain + model good; **3 strikes**; pin best posts.
6. **Add annual payment options** — "buy 10 months, get 2 free" (16% off) → 10–20% take it. **Big head, long tail**: big upfront + small recurring on **one sale** (keep the anchor).
7. **Cancellation call / video** — saves **~half**: **Save with a Redo** or **Save with an Upsell** (credit toward the higher program). Never discount "just because."
8. **Survey twice a year** to find your core 2–3 things; between surveys reach out 1-on-1 every 2–3 weeks via **ACA** (Acknowledge, Complement, Ask).
9. **Make a customer journey** — **Activate → Testimonial → Refer → Ascend**; just-purchased customers churn least, so **ask them to buy again**.

> "I have never not made more money by going through and applying this checklist."
