# Chapter 3: The Churn Checklist (9 Steps)

## Core Idea
The repeatable, cross-industry system: boil churn-reduction down to **simple activities that work across many businesses**. This is "the exact checklist we walk through and apply" with every new portfolio company. "There is no magic bullet for churn. It's 100 tiny things that all save a few people each." Not all steps apply to every business — do the ones that fit. Skool is the running example.

## Frameworks Introduced

### The Churn Checklist — the 9 steps (keep all nine, in order)
1) **Figure Out Your Activation Points** · 2) **Onboard Your Customers** · 3) **Incentivize Customer Activation** · 4) **Community Linking / Events** · 5) **Correct or Fire Bad Customers** · 6) **Add Annual Payment Options** · 7) **Cancellation Call / Video** · 8) **Survey Customers Regularly** · 9) **Make a Customer Journey**.

---

**#1 — Activation Points** = leading indicators of retention. Template: **"Every customer that does (X thing) or gets (Y result) stays longer than customers who don't."** (Skool: hosts who get **≥3 group members** stick far longer — so *help* them get 3, don't hope.)
- **How to find it**: gather data on churned customers → of those, find who **stayed ≥3 months** (3 months is just a convention) → order by **most money spent**, take the **top 20%** → learn everything about them (demographics, psychographics, income, business size, revenue) → do common-factors analysis, narrow to **5 candidate factors**, work down the list.
- **Bonus — update messaging**: advertise *to* those biggest-spending, longest-staying customers; update paid ads, organic, and onboarding to select for them. (Gym Launch: same ad cost as a competitor, but he targeted only the highest-LTV avatar → his customers worth **$42,000 vs the competitor's $5,000** in year one → **70x more profit**.)
- Common activation points: **B2B service** = first time they get leads; **Software** = first login / sees dashboard; **Physical product** = first use of the consumable.
- "All roads lead to activation" — make finding activation points your **top priority**; retest every 6–12 months.

**#2 — Onboard Your Customers** = "teach customers how to hit the activation points." Guidelines, in order of power: **Custom > generic. Personal > group. Live > recorded. Carrots > sticks. Some beats none.** Method = **Outline how to get value → drive them to the activation point**. Also: **resell the value** (framed in *their* goals), tell them what unlocks as they stay, teach how to communicate with you/team/others, and **follow up** (always tell them when you'll speak next). Optional: **sell onboarding** (e.g. 6 PT sessions before group classes — "earn the right" to enter the base).

**#3 — Incentivize Customer Activation** — make it worth it to do the activation behaviors: unlocking courses, recognition, free/discounted products, more access to important people. Most Skool cancellations come from the **bottom two tiers of engagement**; most retained customers are **top tier** → the more they use it, the longer they stay. Also **incentivize staying past major churn points**: "If most customers leave after the third month, put something cool that unlocks on month 4."
- **Pro Tip — Usage Churn**: still subscribed but no longer using → intercept *the moment they stop using it* and get them using again to retain them.

**#4 — Community Linking** — **"Connect members to each other, not you."** Multiple deep peer ties are more scalable than one tie to you. "It's easy to quit a membership, it's hard to leave a relationship." / "They come for the bikini. They stay for the community." Tactics: **group events**, **manually connect** members who'd benefit from each other, **start a community podcast** (interview members), **elevate micro-celebrities** (praise them publicly for their specific expertise).

**#5 — Correct or Fire Bad Customers** — "they make it bad for everyone else." Incentivize good behavior, give feedback on bad: **delete bad posts and say why + show what good looks like**, **3 strikes** then remove, **pin the best 1–2 posts daily** (elevates people, models rewarded behavior, helps them reach level 3). Create post categories, including these four: **Wins** (→ testimonials), **Fun** (keeps it light), **Discovered** (share findings with data/proof), **Meetups** (connect on/offline).

**#6 — Add Annual Payment Options** — "Customers who pay for longer stays… stay longer." Make annual **mandatory** (fewer sales, lower churn — can net more; good for phone/webinar sales) or **optional** (website checkout). Typically **10–20%** take annual at **"buy 10 months, get 2 free"** (16% off); steeper discount → more uptake.
- **Pricing the annual**: price it **above your avg # of months if < 12**; if avg > 12 months, use **buy-10-get-2-free**. (Avg 6 months at $1,000/mo = $6,000 → price annual above $6,000.)
- **Alt 1 — "Big head, long tail"**: big one-time upfront (education/setup, priced to *that* value) + small recurring (consumable value). E.g. **$6,800 upfront + $199/mo** → ~30 months → LTV from $6,800 to **$12,800**. **Keep the recurring fee bundled WITH the upfront fee** — don't make it a second sale, or you lose the price anchor that drives retention.
- **Alt 2 — Founder rates**: an owner discount (a **50%** cut is a great start, if margins hold) to convert more people and keep them longer.

**#7 — Cancellation Call / Video** — "You can usually save **half** of the people who hop on a cancellation call." Half say "F you" (great feedback); the other half you save two ways: **Save with a redo** ("Let me make it right") or **Save with an upsell** (credit their payment toward the higher program they should've been in — "that's on me"). Phone beats email/text. Let them vent — **"only one person can be in the angry boat at the same time"**: agree harder than they do ("That's completely ridiculous. You're 100% correct. Will you give me the opportunity to make it right?"), then "if these things happened, you'd be happy?" becomes the roadmap. Remind them what they **lose** (custom URLs, posts, access, founder pricing, data). High-volume/low-price? Use a **cancellation video** that resells them and reminds them what they risk losing.

**#8 — Survey Customers Regularly** (twice a year) — list everything you provide and ask: **"If I removed everything on this list but one, what would you want to keep the most?"** and **"If I kept everything but one, which would bother you the least to remove?"** This finds your **core 2–3 things**. (A portfolio company learned customers loved their *events* → events became a top product line.) Between surveys, reach out **1-on-1 every 2–3 weeks** using the **ACA framework: Acknowledge** (what you've seen them do), **Complement**, **Ask** a question. Counterintuitive: **wealthier customers want less handholding**.

**#9 — Make a Customer Journey** — four milestones every customer should hit: **Activate → Testimonial → Refer → Ascend** (incentivize each; pairs well with unlockables). **Ascension** is emphasized: customers "get an itch to buy more over time… from you or the guy down the street," and "if someone just bought another thing from you, they're the least likely to churn." So **ask them to buy**. Besides activation (first), 2-3-4 can happen in any order — but plan for all four.

## Key Concepts
- **Activation point** — the X-behavior / Y-result that predicts longer retention.
- **Usage churn** — subscribed but not using; a leading cancellation signal.
- **ACA framework** — Acknowledge, Complement, Ask (the check-in reach-out script).
- **Big head, long tail** — large one-time upfront + small recurring; keep them on one sale to preserve the anchor.
- **Founder rate** — owner discount to convert + retain.
- **Major churn points** — the months where customers tend to leave; place unlockables just past them.
- **Four milestones** — Activate, Testimonial, Refer, Ascend.

## Mental Models
- **Help them hit the activation point — don't hope they do.** "Roll the dice and hope they get 3 members, or… help them get 3 members."
- **More personalized onboarding = better retention** (one portfolio co. went group → 1-on-1 onboarding for a **25% boost in ascensions**, contributing to **$2M/month → $2M/week**).
- **Connect members to each other, not you** — relationships beat memberships.
- **The least-likely-to-churn moment is right after a purchase** — so build ascension into the journey.

## Anti-patterns
- **Hoping for activation** instead of engineering it.
- **Generic group onboarding** when personal/1-on-1 is feasible — "custom > generic, personal > group, live > recorded."
- **Tolerating bad customers** — they ruin it for everyone; 3 strikes and out.
- **Splitting the upfront and recurring into two sales** — kills the price anchor that drives retention.
- **Discounting via cancellation calls "just because"** — instead save with a *redo* or an *upsell*, not a giveaway.
- **Skipping ascension** — letting customers buy their next thing from a competitor.

## Worked Example: Finding the activation point (the Gym Launch "Fast Cash Play")
Gym Launch churn was **8%**, target **4%**. They asked **"When do people first get value from the product?"** The leavers "didn't make their investment back quickly"; the longest-stayers "made their investment back in the first 30 days." So they built **"The Fast Cash Play"** and pushed every client to recoup their investment in the first 30 days → **churn went 8% → 3% within 6 months.** That recouped-investment moment *was* the activation point — and onboarding was redesigned to drive everyone to it.

## Key Takeaways
1. Run the **9-step checklist**; "I have never not made more money by going through and applying this checklist."
2. **Activation points first** — find the X-behavior/Y-result of your top-20% spenders who stayed, then **onboard everyone to it** and **incentivize** it.
3. **Connect members to each other**, correct/fire bad ones, and **survey twice a year** to protect your core 2–3 things.
4. **Annual options** ("buy 10 get 2 free") and the **big-head-long-tail** structure extend average stay — keep upfront + recurring on one sale.
5. **Cancellation calls save ~half** (redo or upsell, never just discount); build the **Activate→Testimonial→Refer→Ascend** journey and **ask them to buy again**.
