# Chapter 1: The Warm Setter Call Script (B2B — Responded Lead)

## Core Idea
A phone script for calling a **warm lead who responded** to your email/LinkedIn/referral. The whole objective: learn their situation, plant the solution, and **book the next call within ~36 hours** (and get every decision-maker on it).

## The Script Flow (in order)
1. **Open / permission**: "Hey (name)? … it's (name) from COMPANY. How have ya been? … I'm just calling because you responded to [referral source] about getting help with your (PROBLEM YOU SOLVE). **Is now a bad time?**" (If yes → "I'll make this quick"; if no → continue.)
2. **State the purpose**: "…just to learn a little more about your company, where you're at with your (goal), and share more about our process to see if we can help."
3. **Let them overview themselves**: "I've done a little research on (company)… in your own words, give me a quick **20–30 second high-level overview** of what you do and what makes you unique." → then a **custom compliment**.
4. **Find the trigger**: "Was there something in particular that piqued your interest in responding?"
5. **Overview past actions**: "Have you (step to reach goal) yet? … What have you done so far?" (Compliment progress.)
6. **Get the big goal + timeline**: "Zooming out, what is the big goal? … How soon are you looking to do this by?" — *the timeline is the lever to motivate action; if they don't volunteer it, ask.*
7. **Recap + surface pain**: recap current state / goal / date / what they're doing → "Seems like things are going fairly well… what made you reach out again?" → have them **highlight pain points**.
8. **Ask permission to advise**: "Do you mind if I share my .02?"
9. **Diagnose + tease the solution**: briefly explain why what they're doing isn't working and hint at the fix (e.g., "low-ticket offer → not enough day-one cash → … what would your business look like if we **doubled the front-end cash collected**?").
10. **Assign the video (homework)**: "I have a video walking you through exactly how to do this — two options: watch it on our next call, or I send it to watch in between… which do you prefer?" → *they take ownership → more people watch.*
11. **Book within 36 hours**: "I have a slot at X tomorrow or X the day after?"
12. **Get all decision-makers**: "What's your title — are you the founder? … Is there anyone else beneficial to cc — co-founders? We like to work with all the founders." → add their email to the invite.
13. **Lock it down**: confirm they got the invite + the video, then: "Our calendars are slammed — **is there any reason why I won't see you at X time X day?**"

## Key Concepts
- **Book within 36 hours** — speed protects the booking.
- **The timeline is the motivation lever** — surface *why* the date matters.
- **Homework (the video) increases show + close** by getting buy-in before the call.

## Anti-patterns
- Leaving the goal/timeline vague (no lever to pull).
- Not getting co-founders/partners on the call.

## Key Takeaways
1. Permission → their overview → trigger → goal+timeline → pain → advise → video → book in 36h → cc all founders → confirm.
2. The timeline is the lever; the video is the buy-in.
3. End every call with a booked next step and a "any reason I won't see you?" lock.
