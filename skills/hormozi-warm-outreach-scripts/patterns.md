# Patterns — Warm Outreach Scripts

## Pattern: Warm Setter Call (B2B responded lead)
**When to use**: Calling a warm lead who replied to an email/LinkedIn/referral.
**How**: Permission ("is now a bad time?") → their 20–30s overview + compliment → trigger → big goal + **timeline (the lever)** → recap + surface pain → "share my .02?" → diagnose + tease fix → assign the **video** → **book in 36 hours** → cc all founders → confirm invite/video + "any reason I won't see you?"
**Trade-offs**: Book fast (speed protects the booking); always get every decision-maker on the call.

## Pattern: Inbound Assessment Call (B2C front-end offer)
**When to use**: A consumer who took your front-end offer.
**How**: Open as qualification → PAIN/DIG/future-pace discovery → tie to best-result clients → book the assessment with scarcity → **take a card on file** to hold the spot → confirm with a text + selfie.
**Trade-offs**: Card-on-file lowers raw bookings slightly but **eliminates no-shows** and raises show rate dramatically.

## Pattern: The Three Card-on-File Overcomes
**When to use**: They hesitate to give a card to hold the slot.
**How**: (1) Normalize ("like booking hair/nails — you reserve the time"); (2) Reason-why ("only ~10% showed without it"); (3) Joking tone ("afraid I'll go on a shopping spree? or that you won't show up?").
**Trade-offs**: Use the lightest overcome that works; keep it warm.

## Pattern: Organic DM → Call
**When to use**: Followers who engage but never act on your CTAs.
**How**: DM = Hey + how-you-found-them + compliment + question (**3 separate messages**) → respond with **A-C-A** (use voice memos) → deploy the short **fill-in offer** → move to a call ("speed is king").
**Trade-offs**: First message's only job is to get a reply — never pitch in message one.

## Pattern: The Short Fill-in Offer
**When to use**: Any low-space channel (DM/text/email/in-person).
**How**: "I help (ideal customer) get (dream outcome) in (time) without (effort & sacrifice) and (increased perceived likelihood). Does anyone you like come to mind?" → if no, "Anyone you hate? (ha)."
**Trade-offs**: It asks for referrals, not a hard yes — low pressure, high engagement.
