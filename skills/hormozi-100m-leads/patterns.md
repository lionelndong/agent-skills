# Patterns — $100M Leads

## Pattern: Create an Engaging Lead Magnet (7 steps)
**When to use**: Leads aren't engaging with your core offer (especially higher-priced stuff).
**How**: (1) Pick a narrow problem whose *next* problem your core offer solves; (2) choose a type — Reveal Problem / Sample-Trial / One Step of Multi-Step; (3) deliver via Software / Information / Services / Physical Product; (4) test the name (headline > image > subheadline); (5) make it easy to consume (every format); (6) make it darn good (give away the secrets, sell the implementation); (7) CTA (what to do + reason now).
**Trade-offs**: Even free has a time cost; a weak lead magnet damages your reputation. Default to a lead magnet over the core offer when unsure.

## Pattern: Run the Core Four
**When to use**: You need engaged leads — pick by what you have more of.
**How**: Warm Outreach (1-to-1 to people who know you), Post Content (1-to-many warm), Cold Outreach (1-to-1 strangers), Paid Ads (1-to-many strangers). More time than money → content/cold outreach; more money than time → cold outreach/ads.
**Trade-offs**: Each trades time, money, or both. You only need one — then max it; they compound.

## Pattern: Warm Outreach to First Five Clients
**When to use**: Starting out, or launching a new product line.
**How**: List → platform → personalize → 100 reach outs/day (3x) → A-C-A → Value-Equation offer → make it free ("first five free") → exhaust platforms → start charging once referrals come → keep warm (9-word email).
**Trade-offs**: Reliable but limited by time and the size of your network. Objective early = learn, not earn.

## Pattern: Build Audience-Growing Content
**When to use**: One-to-many to a warm audience; the audience is the compounding asset.
**How**: Make content units (Hook with topics/headlines/format; Retain with lists/steps/stories; Reward = value per second). Make it for strangers. Do more. Link units for longer content.
**Trade-offs**: Less personal (fewer respond); you compete with everyone; success copied. Audience growth tells you it's working.

## Pattern: Monetize an Audience (Give:Ask)
**When to use**: You have an audience and want revenue without killing growth.
**How**: Over-give; "give until they ask" (give in public, ask in private). Ask via Integrated (keep ratio high) or Intermittent (occasional ask piece). One CTA at a time. Scale depth-then-width or width-then-depth.
**Trade-offs**: Asking too often shrinks the audience ("don't kill the golden goose").

## Pattern: Cold Outreach Machine
**When to use**: You want faster/more predictable leads beyond your network.
**How**: Build a list (scrape / buy / elbow grease) → personalize + Big Fast Value (look like a warm reach-out; 3rd-grade reading level) → volume (automate delivery + distribution, follow up more times/more ways, restart after 3–6 months). Count in 100s for 100 days.
**Trade-offs**: Labor-intensive, slow to scale (≈a year), but reliable, private, hard to copy, and makes a sellable business. Aim ≥3x LTGP:CAC.

## Pattern: Make a Paid Ad (Call Out + Value + CTA)
**When to use**: One-to-many to cold; fastest scale.
**How**: Pick platform → target (lookalike + factors) → Call Out (first 5 sec; verbal + nonverbal; Infinite Ads = 30 callouts × 10 ads) → Value (What-Who-When) → CTA (spell it out) → capture on a simple landing page.
**Trade-offs**: Riskier (pay first); a game of efficiency. Don't confuse a sales problem with an ad problem.

## Pattern: Scale Paid Ad Spend
**When to use**: Deciding how much to spend.
**How**: Track → Lose (budget 2x 30-day cash to test; 10x down on winners) → Print (reverse budget from "how many customers can I handle?"). Watch LTGP:CAC; use 3x industry-avg CAC to diagnose ads vs. model. Use Client Financed Acquisition (upsells to recoup in 30 days).
**Trade-offs**: You'll lose more often than you win — but small losses, big wins.

## Pattern: More, Better, New
**When to use**: Doing the Core Four but want more leads.
**How**: More (Rule of 100) → Better (test the constraint, one test/platform/week, log it) → New (new placements > new platforms > new Core Four). Flip to "Better, More, New" above $1M/yr profit.
**Trade-offs**: "New" is hardest — exhaust more+better first.

## Pattern: Engineer Customer Referrals
**When to use**: To get the cheapest, exponential leads (referrals must beat churn).
**How**: Build goodwill with the six "give value" ways (better customers, expectations, results, faster wins, better product, sell-next) → ask via the seven ways (one/two-sided, ask at purchase, negotiation chip, events, ongoing, unlockable bonuses). Treat the ask as an offer.
**Trade-offs**: Requires a genuinely remarkable product; most businesses fail on product quality or never ask.

## Pattern: Get Lead Getters (Employees, Agencies, Affiliates)
**When to use**: You've run out of hours; you want leverage.
**How**: **Employees** — Internal Core Four; trade doing for managing; build a sellable asset. **Agencies** — hire to *learn* a method/platform (6-month purpose + deadline), run your team alongside until it beats them, then drop to consulting. **Affiliates** — 6 steps: find ("who's got my leads?") → offer (a way to make money) → qualify (customer/expert) → pay (tiered, max CAC) → launch (Whisper-Tease-Shout) → integrate.
**Trade-offs**: Each is higher leverage but more advanced; lead getters can also get lead getters.

## Pattern: Commit With the One-Page Advertising Checklist
**When to use**: To actually start and not drown in planning.
**How**: Pick lead type (Customers/Affiliates/Employees/Agencies) → pick Rule of 100 or Open To Goal → fill the checklist → do it daily until you can hire it out → repeat (employees become the new target).
**Trade-offs**: One page leaves no room for excuses; volume is usually the missing ingredient.
