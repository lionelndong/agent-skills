# Chapter 6: Core Four #2 — Post Free Content, Part I (Build an Audience)

## Core Idea
Posting free content is one-to-many to a warm audience. The key reframe: **"the content you create isn't the compounding asset — the audience is."** Content may disappear; the audience keeps growing. "Volume works."

## Framework: The Content Unit — Hook, Retain, Reward
All audience-growing content does one thing: **rewards** the people consuming it. To do that you must: (1) **Hook** attention (get them to notice), (2) **Retain** attention (get them to consume), (3) **Reward** attention (satisfy the reason they consumed). The smallest material that does all three = **a content unit** (can be a tweet, meme, or sentence — all three can happen at once).

### 1) Hook — get them to notice
- **Topics** (5 categories): **Far Past** (past lessons — "give them the story without the scar"), **Recent Past** (do stuff, then talk about it; testimonials/case studies), **Present** (record ideas as they happen; note publicly), **Trending** (go where the attention is), **Manufactured** (create the experience, then show it — biggest payoff, highest cost). *Manufactured monetizes best; documenting keeps volume high and is more sustainable.*
- **Headlines** — the news's meta-analysis components (include ≥2): **Recency, Relevancy, Celebrity, Proximity, Conflict, Unusual, Ongoing**.
- **Format** — match the popular format of the platform so it looks like stuff they've already liked.

### 2) Retain — keep them consuming
Drive **curiosity** by embedding unresolved questions. Three favorite vehicles: **Lists** (themed: "Top 10 Mistakes"), **Steps** (ordered actions → explicit reward), **Stories** (real or imaginary → "what happens next"). Interweave them freely.

### 3) Reward — satisfy the reason they came
"Think **value per second**." *"There is no such thing as too long, only too boring."* You can't guarantee a reward, but you increase its odds by hooking the right audience, retaining with lists/steps/stories, and clearly satisfying the hook. **How you know you succeeded: your audience grows.**

## Key Concepts
- **Long = more content units linked together.** Shorter content hooks/retains/rewards fewer times; longer does it more (more skill). Start small.
- **Make all content for strangers** — assume the consumer has never heard of you; then strangers share it and your audience grows faster. (Avoid inside jokes.)

## Worked Example: "Just Do More, Bro"
A $120,000 influencer's only "secret": *more*. He posted 3x/day on Instagram and 5x/day on LinkedIn to Hormozi's 1x. Over the next six months Hormozi put out 10x the content — and added **1.2M people** to his audience. "Volume works. Content works. A growing audience is the result."

## Anti-patterns
- Treating the content (not the audience) as the asset.
- Littering content with inside jokes that exclude strangers.

## Key Takeaways
1. Build content from content units: Hook, Retain, Reward.
2. Hook with topics/headlines/format; retain with lists/steps/stories; reward with value per second.
3. Make it for strangers, do more, and let audience growth tell you it's working.
