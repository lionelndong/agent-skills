# Chapter 11: Core Four on Steroids — More, Better, New

## Core Idea
"There are more leads out there than you can possibly imagine." If you're doing the Core Four and still want more engaged leads, there are exactly three ways to boost any of them: **More, Better, New.** (1) Do more of what you're doing. (2) Do it better. (3) Do it somewhere new.

## More — The Rule of 100
"Even with no improvements at all, if you double your inputs, you'll get more engaged leads." **The Rule of 100**: do **100 primary actions every day, for 100 days in a row**. Applied to each Core Four:
- **Warm Reach Outs**: 100 reach outs/day.
- **Post Content**: 100 minutes/day making content (≥1 post/day).
- **Cold Reach Outs**: 100 reach outs/day (expect lower response; automate).
- **Paid Ads**: 100 minutes/day making ads, 100 days straight (aim for Client Financed Acquisition).
*"Commit to the rule of 100 and you will never go hungry again."* (Facebook found the top 0.1% of advertisers test **11x more creative** — "you just can't scale a mediocre ad profitably.")

## Better — Testing & Constraints
You only get better by **testing**. Do more until it breaks, then make it better. Find the **constraint** — the step with the biggest drop-off, where the smallest improvement creates the biggest boost. *Worked example*: improving a 5% "apply" step by 5 points = +100% leads (2x), vastly beating the same improvement on a 30% or 50% step. **Test one thing per week per platform** (so you know what worked; steps affect each other; forces prioritization; runs long enough for data). Log every test. **Front > Back** (the lowest-percentage, highest-leverage steps are usually at the front).

## New — New Places, New Ways
Combats **The Size of the Pie Fallacy**: businesses market on one platform, one way, to one tiny audience, see a few competitors, and assume that slice is the whole market. (A chiropractor at $2M/yr on one platform thought he'd "saturated" a $15.1B industry.) **When to do new**: when more+better return less than a new placement/way would. Order: **New Placements > New Platforms > New Core Four** — "what gets the most leads for the amount of work."

## Key Concepts
- **More, Better, New ordering flips with size**: under $1M/yr profit → "more" first (not enough volume for % gains to matter); over $1M/yr → "better, more, new."
- "What's stopping them from doing 10x what they're currently doing?" — the first question before investing.

## Mental Models
- *Volume negates luck* — do twice as many and you get good in half the time. *"Replicate before you iterate."*
- *More + better work together* — do more until it breaks, fix the constraint, repeat.

## Key Takeaways
1. More = the Rule of 100 (100 actions/day × 100 days).
2. Better = test the constraint, one test per platform per week, log it.
3. New = new placements → new platforms → new Core Four (beware the Size of the Pie Fallacy).
