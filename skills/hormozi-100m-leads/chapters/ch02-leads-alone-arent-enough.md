# Chapter 3: Leads Alone Aren't Enough

## Core Idea
Words matter because they shape what we do. The whole book started because Hormozi couldn't define "a lead." The precise definitions: **a lead is a person you can contact**; what you actually want is an **engaged lead** — a person who *shows* interest in the stuff you sell.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Lead** — "a person you can contact." A bought email list, numbers in your phone, people on the street: if you can contact them, they are leads.
- **Engaged Lead** — a lead who *shows* interest (gives contact info on a website, follows you and is contactable, replies to your campaign). **"Engaged leads are the true output of advertising."**

## Key Concepts
- **The point of the book**: get *more engaged leads*. (It couldn't be titled "engaged leads" because no one would get it — but now you do.)
- The next question the book answers: **How do we get leads to engage?** → Offers and Lead Magnets.

## Mental Models
- *If words have us thinking the wrong way, we'll do the wrong stuff.* Precision in "lead" vs. "engaged lead" changes what you optimize for.

## Key Takeaways
1. A lead = a person you can contact (that's all).
2. The goal is engaged leads — people showing interest — not raw contacts.
3. Engaged leads are the true output of advertising.
