# Bonus Chapters: Promotions — Premium, Free & Discount

> *From the 2 (really 3) bonus chapters Hormozi shipped with $100M Leads — early drafts of a later book. They cover how to **promote** your Grand Slam Offer to generate demand.*

## Core Idea
A promotion is **wrapping paper around your core Grand Slam Offer**, not a change to it. "You can wrap a gift in recycled newspaper or put it in a Chanel bag… What's inside may be the same, but we make it more inherently attractive." Promotions matter most for **cold markets**, where you must give people a reason to move toward you (answer "what's in it for me?"). **"Get Flow. Monetize Flow. Then Add Friction."** — when entering a new market, Hormozi almost always starts with something **free or massively discounted**. "Those who give the most, get the most."

## Premium Promotions — Presenting the Grand Slam Offer on Its Own
- **The power of infinite returns**: "There's nothing below $0… but you can go infinitely higher." Premium buyers let you buy traffic on a "$25,000/hr budget" while competitors use a "$6.75/hr budget" — and you need far less volume.
- **Premium math (worked example)**: Business #1 makes $50 profit/sale, Business #2 makes $9,500/sale. Offer the same 190 prospects the $10,000 offer and close ~5%: 5% × 190 = 9.5 sales × $9,500 = **$90,250** vs. 190 × $50 = $9,500 → **9.5x more profitable**, with ~10 clients instead of 190.
- **Pros**: simplest math/fewest moving parts; only quality customers (skim "the cream, not all the milk"); high lead quality (no incentives).
- **Cons**: most expensive cost-per-lead / slow to break even; must be a good copywriter who knows the avatar; must be good at sales (a "good old-fashioned sale"); least efficient way to capture a market; must have an extremely compelling offer (bonuses, guarantees, premium support).
- **Rule**: if you're new or low-volume, **start with a free or discount offer, prove results, then restructure to premium**. Premium works well as the *second* offer after a free one (only if the first demonstrated value).

## Free Promotions
- **"Free is the most powerful offer of all time and will never expire"** — at its base it's "something for nothing" / value in advance.
- **The Penny Gap** (Dr. Dan Ariely): **9x more** people took a free Hershey's Kiss than one priced at $0.01. Lowering from one cent to free can be "a landslide."
- A free offer is the fastest way to test if anyone wants your thing. **If it doesn't work**, the prospect either: (1) **doesn't want it** (change what/how you give it away), (2) **doesn't believe you**, or (3) **isn't seeing it** (wrong pond / targeting).
- **Crazy free offers must answer "Why?"** — the famous "$1,000 back for every $100" newspaper test got *zero* responses because it was too good to be believed. Always give the reason why.

## Discount Promotions
- The third wrapper: a price reduction used to push fence-sitters over the edge. Like free, it gives more **margin for error on your copy** because the offer itself does some of the persuading.
- Still a *wrapper* — the discount enhances the Grand Slam Offer; it doesn't replace it. Pair with a reason why, scarcity, and urgency.

## Why Start Free/Discounted (Hormozi's reasons)
1. You don't know what you're doing yet — wiggle room/forgiveness while it's rough. 2. Fastest way to get **testimonials**. 3. Builds your **conviction** to close the first sales. 4. Easy to get **referrals** and generate demand. (His original Gym Launch offer was free: "I'll fly out, spend my own money, work and close your leads, give you the customers free — I only make what I sell." A Grand Slam Offer that made his first million.)

## Key Takeaways
1. Promotions are wrappers (Premium / Free / Discount) around the core Grand Slam Offer — enhance, never change it.
2. Premium maximizes profit-per-sale with low volume; free maximizes engagement (the penny gap) but must answer "why?"; discount pushes fence-sitters.
3. New or low-volume? Start free/discounted to get flow and proof, then move to premium.
