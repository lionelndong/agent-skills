# Chapter 12: Lead Getter #1 — Customer Referrals (Word of Mouth)

## Core Idea
Referrals are "the lowest cost, highest profit, and best quality leads out there." A **lead getter** is someone who gets *you* leads; the best referrals come from customers. "The best advertising is a happy customer. An amazing product turns every customer into a lead getter."

## Why Referrals Are Special
- **Worth more (higher LTGP)** and **cost less (lower CAC)** — a referred customer is often free. (4:1 LTGP:CAC becomes 12:1 if each customer brings two more.)
- **Exponential, not linear** — one brings two, two bring four. "Nothing scales like word of mouth."
- **Referral Growth Equation**: referrals (in) − churned customers (out). If **referrals > churn**, you grow with no other advertising; if **< churn**, you're on the "hamster wheel of death."

## Two Reasons Most Businesses Don't Get Referrals
1. **Their product isn't as good as they think.** ("Why are my customers too embarrassed to tell everyone about my product?") 2. **They don't ask.**

## Goodwill = Value − Price
Price is what you charge; value is what they get; the gap is **goodwill** (economists' "customer surplus"). Goodwill → word of mouth → referrals. You build it by **giving more value**, not lowering price ("any fool can sell something for less").

## Framework A: Six Ways to Get Referrals by Giving More Value (maps to an ad)
1. **Call Outs → Sell Better Customers** (target your lowest-churn, highest-value "perfect fit" customers).
2. **Dream Outcome → Set Better Expectations** (underpromise, overdeliver — lower promises until close rates dip, then stop).
3. **Perceived Likelihood → Get More People Better Results** (find what your best customers did; make new customers repeat it; tie your **guarantee** to those success actions).
4. **Time Delay → Make Faster Wins** (deliver in smaller increments; updates are wins; **BAMFAM** = Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting; deliver early, never late).
5. **Effort & Sacrifice → Keep Making Your Stuff Better** (monthly: survey → fix the most common problem → test → roll out).
6. **CTA → Tell Them What To Buy Next** (keep selling; customers who fall off don't refer).

**One Question to Rule Them All**: if you lost all customers but one and could only get new customers from that one — how would you treat them? Then do that.

## Framework B: Seven Ways to Ask for Referrals
Treat asking like an **offer** (show the value the customer gets when they refer). The three components: how you give the incentive, what you incentivize with, how you ask.
1. **One-Sided Benefit** (pay your CAC to referrer *or* friend). 2. **Two-Sided Benefit** (split CAC between both — Dropbox/PayPal). 3. **Ask Right When They Buy** ("Who else could you do this with?" — not *if*, but *who*). 4. **Referrals as a Negotiation Chip** (discount in exchange for 3 intros). 5. **Referral Events** ("Bring-a-Friend" promotions). 6. **Ongoing Referral Programs.** 7. **Unlockable Referral Bonuses.**

## Worked Example: Dropbox & PayPal
- **Dropbox** gave free storage to both the customer and the referred friend → went viral, **39x'd in 15 months**.
- **PayPal** gave $10 to both sides → 1M users within two years, 100M in six. "We do what they did. We ask for it."
*Pro tip:* asking for referrals at signup routinely added **25–30% more sign-ups** — often covering the entire ad cost (since they run above 3:1 LTGP:CAC).

## Key Takeaways
1. Referrals are the cheapest, highest-quality, exponential leads — referrals must beat churn.
2. Build goodwill (Value − Price) with the six "give value" ways.
3. Capitalize on it by asking — treat the ask as an offer (seven ways).
