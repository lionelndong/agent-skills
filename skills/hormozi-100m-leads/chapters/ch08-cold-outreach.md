# Chapter 8: Core Four #3 — Cold Outreach

## Core Idea
Cold outreach = private one-to-one contact with strangers (cold audiences). "Quantity has a quality all of its own." It's "the more advanced cousin of warm outreach," no longer limited by who you know. The key difference vs. warm: **trust** (strangers don't have it).

## The Three Problems Strangers Create (solved in order)
1. **You don't have a way to contact them** → **Build a List**.
2. **Even if you can, they ignore you** → **Personalize, then Big Fast Value**.
3. **Even with attention, they're not interested** → **Volume**.

### Problem #1 — Build a List
Three ways: **Scrape** (subscribe to lead-scraping software, test a sample), **Buy** (list brokers, test samples), **Elbow grease** (manually pull from groups/communities). *Interest groups are the warmest cold audience* (concentrated pools looking for a solution). Self-assembled lists are "freshest" (fewer prior reach-outs).

### Problem #2 — Personalize, Then Big Fast Value
- **Personalize (act like you know them)** — make a cold reach-out *look like a warm one*; 1–3 facts a friend might know, plus a compliment. (Re-writing scripts below a 3rd-grade reading level → **+50% responses**.)
- **Big Fast Value** — strangers give you far less time; "we're not trying to tickle their interest, we're trying to blow their minds in under thirty seconds." Give away something they'd *actually pay for*. (Swapping a "game planning session" lead magnet for real free service **3x'd take rates**.)

### Problem #3 — Volume
- **Automate delivery** (pre-recorded voice memos, templated emails/texts — record once, send to all). You have far more strangers than warm contacts, so don't fear "burning through" them.
- **Automate distribution** (robo-dialers, blasts). You generally trade personalization for scale — the fewer leads, the less automation. *Personalization tech* lets you do both.
- **Follow up — more times, more ways.** People respond to different methods; an unanswered email is a reason to call. After exhausting the list, start back at the top, then **wait 3–6 months and repeat** (circumstances change). Don't contact anyone who asks you to stop.

## Key Concepts
- **Count in 100s** — "Don't set a daily goal below 100. And don't stop for 100 days minimum."
- **Benchmarks**: aim for ~3% list → engaged leads; you do well when **LTGP is ≥ 3x what it costs to get a customer** (the bare minimum; portfolio examples hit 30:1).
- **Low-ticket fix**: if your ticket is too low for cold outreach to pencil, use cold outreach to recruit **affiliates** instead (one affiliate sale = many customers).

## The 7 Benefits of Cold Outreach
1) No content/ads needed (one perfect message). 2) Competition can't see it (private/secret). 3) Incredibly reliable (input → output, like clockwork). 4) Fewer platform changes. 5) Less compliance pain. 6) No spokesperson → a **sellable** business. 7) Hard to copy (many steps are invisible).

## Worked Example: 9 Months of Torture, Then Millions
A new rep cold-outreached for Hormozi during COVID: Sept 0 sales → Oct 2 → Dec 4 → Jan 6 → Feb 10 → Mar 14 → Apr 20 → May 30 sales ($480k). "Cold outreach generates millions per month for our businesses." Lesson: it's reliable but **takes a long time** (he expected 12 weeks; it took ~a year).

## Key Takeaways
1. Solve the three stranger-problems in order: list → personalize+value → volume.
2. Make a cold reach-out look like a warm one, then give big fast value.
3. It's a numbers game — count in 100s, 100 days; reliable but slow to scale.
