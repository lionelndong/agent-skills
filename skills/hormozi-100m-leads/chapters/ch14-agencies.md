# Chapter 14: Lead Getter #3 — Agencies

## Core Idea
Advertising agencies are lead-getting service businesses. But the high-leverage move isn't to *outsource* to them forever — it's to **use them to learn**. "It comes from playing a different game." (His first agency taught him to run Facebook ads in 8 hours for $6,000 — "a skill that's made me millions.")

## How Agencies Want You to Think They Work (the trap)
Step 1 they excite you; 2 a valuable-feeling onboarding; 3 their "best" senior rep on your account; 4 you see results; 5 they move the senior rep to the newest client; 6 a junior rep takes over; 7 results suffer; 8–9 you complain, cancel; 10–11 you find another agency and repeat the "cycle of insanity."

## Framework: How to Use Agencies (the Hormozi way)
**Hire an agency to learn new methods and new platforms** (only once you have money — good agencies cost money). Start every relationship with a purpose and a deadline. The script:
> "I want to do what you do in my business, but I don't know how. I'd like to work with you for 6 months to learn how you do it. I'll pay extra for you to break down *why* you make the decisions you do. Then I'll train my team on it, and once they can do it well enough, move to a lower-cost consulting arrangement. Are you opposed to this?"

- Hire one **"good enough"** agency to learn the ropes of a new platform, and a more **elite** one (even at 4x) to learn how to maximize it.
- Run **both teams** until *your team beats the agency*, then drop to consulting only and put the money into scaling what you learned.
- Your team improves (full-time focus) while the agency's attention dilutes with each new client.

## How to Pick the Right Agency (10 signals)
1) Someone you know got results with them. 2) Prominent companies got results. 3) A waiting list. 4) A clear sales process with realistic expectations. 5) Long-term strategy, no short-term hacks. 6) They tell you exactly what they need, when, and why. 7) Regular meeting cadence + progress updates. 8) Simple terms with clear cost-vs-results tracking. 9) A good **offer** (run them through the Value Equation: dream outcome, likelihood, time, effort). 10) They're expensive ("all good agencies are expensive, but not all expensive agencies are good").

*Pro tip:* talk to 5–10 agencies to become an informed buyer; ask good ones for referrals to other good ones.

## Key Concepts
- Both sides win: the agency gets a customer; you get a money-making skill for life.
- Budget for a window where you pay *both* the agency and your team to do the same thing — "totally worth it when you get it right." (It went from ~12 months to beat an agency down to <6.)

## Key Takeaways
1. Use agencies to **learn** new methods/platforms, not to depend on them forever.
2. Set a purpose + deadline; run your team alongside until it beats them, then drop to consulting.
3. Pick agencies on word-of-mouth, waiting lists, realistic expectations, a good offer — and yes, expensive.
