# Section III Intro: The Core Four (Advertising Methods)

## Core Idea
There are only **four ways** one person can let other people know about anything. Combine two audience types with two communication modes and you get the Core Four. "If you aren't getting as many leads as you want, you're not doing the core four with enough skill or with enough volume."

## The Framework — Two Axes
- **Two Audiences**: **Warm** = people who know you / gave permission to contact them (your audience). **Cold** = strangers / other people's audiences.
- **Two Communication modes**: **One-to-One** (private — a call, an email) vs. **One-to-Many** (public — a post, a billboard). *Automation doesn't change the nature: a 10,000-person email blast is "one-to-one really fast by a machine."*

## The Core Four (the 2×2)
| | One-to-One (private) | One-to-Many (public) |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm** (know you) | **Warm Outreach** | **Post Free Content** |
| **Cold** (strangers) | **Cold Outreach** | **Paid Ads** |

- 1-to-1 to a Warm Audience = **Warm Outreach**
- 1-to-many to a Warm Audience = **Posting Content**
- 1-to-1 to a Cold Audience = **Cold Outreach**
- 1-to-many to a Cold Audience = **Paid Ads**

"These are the only four things you can do to let other people know about the stuff you sell."

## Key Concepts
- Each method trades **time, money, or both** for reach.
- You only need to do **one** to get engaged leads — pick one, then max it out.
- The methods **compound**: every combination boosts the others (content makes outreach and ads convert better, etc.).

## Mental Models
- *The Core Four is your "get out of jail free" card* — master one and you can feed yourself for life.
- *If leads are short, it's a skill or volume problem in the Core Four* — not a mystery.

## Key Takeaways
1. Audience (warm/cold) × communication (1-to-1 / 1-to-many) = the only four advertising methods.
2. Low leads = not enough skill or volume in the Core Four.
3. Pick one, max it, then add the others — they compound.
