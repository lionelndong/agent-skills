# Chapter 7: Core Four #2 — Post Free Content, Part II (Monetize Your Audience)

## Core Idea
Monetize a warm audience without becoming a spam monster by mastering the **give:ask ratio**. "Give-give-give… until they ask."

## Framework: The Give:Ask Ratio
- Gary Vaynerchuk's "jab, jab, jab, right hook" — deposit goodwill with rewarding content, then withdraw by making offers. More goodwill → more attention, more compliance.
- Benchmarks: TV ≈ 3.5:1 give:ask; Facebook ≈ 4:1. Mature platforms give less and ask more. **Growing** platforms over-give and under-ask.
- **Hormozi's tweak: "Give until they ask."** Give enough and people start asking *you* (DMs, email). **Give in public, ask in private.** "The moment you start asking for money is the moment you decide to slow down your growth."

## How to Ask (two strategies)
- **Integrated** — weave an ask into every piece of content while keeping the give:ask ratio high (e.g., 3×30-sec ads in a 60-min podcast). The **PS statement** is one of the most-read places to ask.
- **Intermittent** — many pure "give" pieces, then occasionally an "ask" piece (e.g., 10 gives, then promote on the 11th). Dominates on short-form platforms; integrations win on long-form.
- When you ask, advertise either your **core offer** or your **lead magnet** (default to the lead magnet — lower risk). **Only one CTA at a time** — "a confused mind doesn't buy."

## How to Scale It
- **Depth then Width** — maximize one platform, then add the next. (Compounds faster; risk = single-channel dependency.)
- **Width then Depth** — get on every relevant platform early, then maximize all at once. (Broader reach + repurposing; costs more labor.)

## The 7 Lessons From Making Content
1. Switch from **"How to" → "How I"** (share experience; "no one can question you").
2. **"We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught."** Keep repeating yourself.
3. **Puddles → Ponds → Lakes → Oceans** — narrow your content focus, become king of the puddle, then expand.
4. **Content creates tools for salespeople** — keep a "greatest hits" list to send before/after calls.
5. **Free content retains paying customers** — buyers consume your free content and factor it into perceived ROI.
6. **People don't have shorter attention spans — they have higher standards.** "No such thing as too long, only too boring."
7. **Avoid pre-scheduling posts** — manual posting gets a tighter feedback loop and better effort.

## Worked Example: Free Content Was Nurturing the Demand
When paid ads "stopped working," the real change was that Hormozi *stopped making gym content*. A survey revealed most gym owners had consumed his free content before booking a call — the content was nurturing the demand all along. "Even if it's hard to measure, free content gets you better returns on all advertising methods."

## Benchmarks
Measure **total followers/reach** (how big) and the **rate of growth** (how fast), monthly. Control inputs (posting + ask cadence); measure outputs. "Do. Not. Stop." (His podcast took 4 years to crack the Top 100.)

## Key Takeaways
1. Over-give; "give until they ask," in public.
2. Monetize via integrated or intermittent asks — one CTA at a time.
3. Scale depth-then-width or width-then-depth; measure size and growth rate.
