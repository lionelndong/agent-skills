# Chapter 4: Engage Your Leads — Offers and Lead Magnets

## Core Idea
To get leads to engage, you advertise one of two things: your **core offer** (straight for the sale) or a **lead magnet** (a free/low-cost offer to see who's interested). "You have to give people something they want." A lead magnet is **a complete solution to a narrow problem**.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Offer** — what you promise to give in exchange for something of value. **Core offer** = your product/service for money (the direct path to money — try this first).
- **Lead Magnet** — "a complete solution to a narrow problem." Low-cost or free; reveals a *next* problem that your core offer solves. (The salty-pretzels-at-a-bar model: pretzels solve hunger → reveal thirst → drink revenue covers the pretzels.) Leads who take a free offer now are more likely to buy a related paid offer later.
- **Even free stuff has a cost** — people give time before money; if your lead magnet isn't worth their time, it's overpriced. "If they think your lead magnet is worth their time, they'll think your core offer is worth their money."

## The Seven Steps to Create an Effective Lead Magnet
1. **Figure out the problem to solve + who for** (Problem-Solution cycle: every problem solved reveals a new one — pick a narrow problem whose *next* problem your core offer solves).
2. **Figure out how to solve it** → the **Three Types of Lead Magnets**: (1) **Reveal Their Problem** (diagnosis — speed test, posture analysis, termite inspection), (2) **Samples & Trials** (full but brief access — "be a drug dealer," give the strongest hit first), (3) **One Step of a Multi-Step Process** (give one valuable step free). *The dressing room is all three at once.*
3. **Figure out how to deliver it** → **Four Delivery Mechanisms**: Software, Information, Services, Physical Products. (3 types × 4 deliveries = up to 12 lead magnets per problem.)
4. **Test what to name it** — the headline matters most (Ogilvy: "you've spent 80 cents of your dollar"). Test headline, then image, then subheadline. (He A/B-tested this book's title via polls.)
5. **Make it easy to consume** — offer every format (video/text/audio/etc.); 2–4x take rates.
6. **Make it darn good** — "Give Away The Secrets, Sell The Implementation." Provide more value than the cost of your core offer *before* they buy. Sucky fluff destroys your reputation.
7. **Make it easy to tell you they want more** — a **Call To Action (CTA)**.

## CTA: What To Do + Reasons To Do It Now
- **What to do**: clear, simple, direct ("call now," not "don't delay").
- **Reasons to act now**: (a) **Scarcity** (limited amount — use honest/real scarcity), (b) **Urgency** (limited time — his favorite: time-limited bonuses you rotate weekly), (c) **Fraternity Party Planner** — make up a reason ("because…" — any reason beats no reason).

## Worked Example: Lead Magnet Cuts CAC 3x
Advertising the core offer: $1,000/call → close 1 in 3 → **$3,000/customer**. Advertise a free lead magnet (costs $25 to deliver) instead: 10x engagement → $100/call; if 1 in 10 buys → **$1,000/customer**. Same budget, **triple the business**. "The goal is to print money, not just make our 'fair share.'"

## Key Takeaways
1. Advertise a core offer or a lead magnet; lead magnets win for higher-priced stuff.
2. A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem whose next problem your core offer solves.
3. Make it (Step 6) so good people feel obligated to pay you — a Grand Slam Offer works for free stuff too.
