# Chapter 9: Core Four #4 — Run Paid Ads, Part I (Making an Ad)

## Core Idea
Paid ads = one-to-many to cold audiences. You "rent eyeballs or earballs" — trade money for guaranteed reach. The fastest way to get the most people to see your stuff. Because reach is guaranteed but profit isn't, paid ads are **a game of efficiency**, not reach. "The question isn't 'do ads work?' it's 'how well can you make them work?'"

## The Four Problems Paid Ads Solve
1. **Knowing where to advertise** → **Pick a Platform** (you've used it; you can target your people; you can format for it; you can afford the minimum). *Place ads where your competitors place ads to start.*
2. **Getting the right audience to see it** → **Target them**: (1) **Lookalike audiences** (upload your customer list → platform finds similar, bigger), (2) **Factors of your choosing** (age, income, gender, interests, location). "The right message to the wrong audience falls on deaf ears."
3. **Making the best ad** → **Call Out + Value + CTA** (below).
4. **Getting permission to contact them** → a simple **landing page** (continuous "click to close" experience — match the ad's look and promise).

## The Ad = Call Out + Value + CTA
### 1) Call Out — get them to notice (the most important part)
"My advertising became 20x more effective when I focused the majority of my effort on the first five seconds." Harness the **cocktail party effect**.
- **Verbal callouts**: **Labels** (Clark County Moms, Gym Owners — local + type of person is a favorite), **Yes-Questions**, **If-Then Statements**, **Ridiculous Results**.
- **Nonverbal callouts**: **Contrast** (color/sound/movement that sticks out), **Likeness** ("quack like a duck" — look the part; mascots), **The Scene** (visually show the yes-question/if-then).
- **Infinite Ads**: record ~30 first-sentence callouts × 10 main ads = **300 variations** in hours.

### 2) Value — get them interested (the What-Who-When Framework)
Built on the **Value Equation**. Know **8 key things** about your product — how it delivers the four value drivers and avoids their four opposites:
- **Dream Outcome / Nightmare**, **Perceived Likelihood / Risk**, **Time Delay (Speed) / Slow**, **Effort & Sacrifice (Ease) / Hard**.
- **Who** — humans are status-driven; show how the value changes how *others* treat the customer (spouse, kids, colleagues, rivals).
- **When** — run it through their timeline (past → present → future).
*"Longer ads use more angles; shorter ads use fewer"* — keep the callout and CTA the same.

### 3) CTA — tell them what to do next
S-P-E-L-L it out (click the button, call this number, scan this code). "Assume the audience has no idea who you are… they're in a rush… and they have a 3rd-grade education." Apply urgency/scarcity/bonuses.

## Key Concepts
- **Get unlimited inspiration**: search "[platform] ad library"; an ad running a month+ is probably profitable — break down 50 and copy the structure.
- **You don't need to become a copywriting expert** — just explain your offer clearly with What-Who-When; get good enough to be profitable, then scale.

## Key Takeaways
1. Paid ads = a guaranteed-reach efficiency game; pick platform → target → make the ad → capture contact.
2. Every ad = Call Out (notice) + Value via What-Who-When (interest) + CTA (action).
3. Focus the most effort on the first five seconds (the callout).
