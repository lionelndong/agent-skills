# Section V: Get Started — Open To Goal, The Roadmap, A Decade in a Page

## Core Idea
"Advertising is an inputs and outputs game." Most people dramatically underestimate the **volume** it takes. "The right action in the wrong amount still fails." You input advertising effort; your output is engaged leads. Period.

## Framework: Open To Goal (the Rule of 100 "for the big kids")
The Rule of 100 commits you to a number of *actions*. **Open To Goal** commits you to the work until you hit a number of *outcomes — no matter what.* (A gym chain made managers sign up five new members per day, however long it took.) "Give up the idea of 'doing your best.' Instead, do what is required." Hormozi's high-ROI habit stack: sleep early, get right to work (no rituals), **no meetings until noon** (a long block of uninterrupted work after uninterrupted sleep). *"Do more than they do, and you will have more than they have."*

## Framework: The One-Page Advertising Checklist
1. **Pick the type of engaged lead to get**: Customers, Affiliates, Employees, or Agencies.
2. **Pick Rule of 100 or Open To Goal** — commit to your daily advertising actions.
3. **Fill out the advertising checklist** for that daily action.
4. **Do the daily action** until you can afford to pay someone else to do it.
5. **When you can, go back to Step 1** — make *employees* your new target lead type, and repeat.
"Skip the atrocious waste of time that is a hundred-page plan. One page leaves little room for excuses."

## Framework: The Roadmap — Zero to $100,000,000 (7 Levels)
- **Level 1**: Your friends know about your stuff. *(Warm outreach.)*
- **Level 2**: You consistently let everyone you know know. *(Warm outreach + post content.)*
- **Level 3**: You get **employees** to do more advertising.
- **Level 4**: Your product is good enough for consistent **referrals** (aim 25%+). *("This is where most people mess up — they let their product slip.")*
- **Level 5**: You advertise in more places, more ways, more people (new audiences, placements, platforms, lead-getters).
- **Level 6**: You **hire killers** — veteran executives who grow advertising departments without you. *("Hire experience, not potential.")*
- **Level 7**: "I'll come back and edit this once I cross a billion."

## A Decade in a Page (the whole book)
1. Define a **lead** (engaged leads, not just leads). 2. Turn leads into engaged leads with an **offer or lead magnet**. 3. **The Core Four** — warm outreach, post content (hook/retain/reward, give until they ask), cold outreach (lists, personalization, big fast value, volume), paid ads (targeting, callouts, What-Who-When, CTAs, client financed acquisition). 4. **More, Better, New**. 5. **The Four Lead Getters** — Customers, Employees, Agencies, Affiliates.

## Worked Example: "You Either Win or You Learn"
After a mentor gave him "permission to fail" (set aside a % of budget to test with no expected return), Hormozi tripled his ad budget: $400k (June) → $780k (July). New audiences, then text-blast follow-up ($1.8M), then more/better ads ($2.5M), then an affiliate program (+$1.5M → $4M/mo). "Test until you find something that works. Double down until it breaks. Then test until you find the next thing."

## Key Takeaways
1. Volume is the usual missing ingredient — work **Open To Goal**.
2. Commit on **one page**: pick a lead type, pick Rule of 100 / Open To Goal, do it daily, then hire it out.
3. Scale through the 7 Levels; protect the product at Level 4; hire experienced executives at Level 6.
