# Chapter 10: Core Four #4 — Run Paid Ads, Part II (Money Stuff)

## Core Idea
"Efficiency matters more than creativity." All advertising works; the only thing that differs is how well. Paid ads are about return on investment — put X dollars in for views, get Y dollars out. "Good enough is good enough" to scale.

## Framework: The Three Phases of Scaling Paid Ads
1. **Track Money** — before spending a dollar, set up accurate tracking. "If you don't track, you're gonna get cleaned out."
2. **Lose Money** ("investing in a money-printing machine") — you pay first, so your bank account drops before it rises. **Budget 2x the 30-day cash collected** from a customer to test a new ad; shut it off at 1x if it gets no leads. Lose often but small (know when to stop); win rarely but big (know when to hit the gas) — 10x down on winners.
3. **Print Money** — once ads break even or better, stop asking "how much should I spend?" and ask **"how many customers can I handle?"** Reverse the budget from your sales goal (+~20% pad). "If the number terrifies you, you're doing it right."

## Framework: LTGP : CAC
Measure efficiency as **Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)** to **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)**.
- **LTGP** = all the money a customer ever spends minus all the money it takes to deliver it. (Hormozi measures LTGP, not "LTV.")
- Two levers: **lower CAC** (better ads) and **raise LTGP** (better business model). Prefer both.
- **Diagnosis rule**: research your industry-average CAC. If your CAC is **below 3x** the average → your ads are fine, fix your **business model (LTGP)**. If **above 3x** → fix your **advertising (CAC)**. "Costs can only approach zero; how much you make can go up to infinity."

## Framework: Client Financed Acquisition (CFA)
The profit from a first purchase is often less than CAC, and full LTGP takes months — a cash-flow trap. **CFA**: if a customer pays you *more than it costs to get and fulfill them within the first 30 days*, you can recycle cash to get the next customer — **limitless scale**. (30 days = any business can get interest-free money via a credit card.) Fix it by **immediately selling them more** (upsells). *"It's how I've scaled every company past $1M/mo in the first twelve months — without outside funding."*

## Worked Example: The $100 Upsell That Breaks Even
$30 CAC, but only $10 gross profit on the first sale → cash-flow negative for two months. Add a $100 upsell (100% margin) that 1 in 5 take → +$20 average → $10 + $20 = **$30 in <30 days = free customers**. Every $10/mo after is "gravy."

## The 3 Personal Lessons
1. **Don't confuse sales problems with advertising problems** — if the right leads (with money) aren't buying, your ads work fine; you have a sales problem. (One company gave up "six inches from gold," ~$30M in enterprise value.)
2. **Your best free content can make the best paid ads** — especially **UGC** (user-generated testimonials/reviews).
3. **If you say you suck at something, you will probably suck at it** — "I'm not techy" cost him four years; reversed in four hours.

## Key Takeaways
1. Track → Lose → Print; budget 2x 30-day cash to test, 10x down on winners.
2. LTGP:CAC is the scoreboard; use 3x industry-average CAC to diagnose ads vs. model.
3. Client Financed Acquisition (recoup get+fulfill cost in 30 days, via upsells) is the key to limitless scale.
