# Chapter 13: Lead Getter #2 — Employees

## Core Idea
Lead-getting employees are people you train to get you leads using the Core Four. "More lead-getting employees means more engaged leads — and less work you have to do." Trade doing-time for managing-time: "trade forty hours of doing for four hours of managing."

## Why Employees Make You Wealthy
A business that runs *without you* does two things: turns a risky "job" into a sellable **asset**, and makes you wealthier. Same $2M/yr profit is worth ~$0 if it needs you, or **$10M+** if it doesn't (investors will buy it). **"You get rich from what you make. You become wealthy from what you own."**

## New Beliefs About Talent
Hormozi spent years believing "if you want it done right, you gotta do it yourself" — and it never made him more money. The replacements:
- "If you want it done right, **get someone to spend all their time doing it**."
- "If I can do it, **someone else can do it better**."
- "**Everyone is replaceable, especially me**." → *Replace yourself as soon as you can*, then make yourself useful elsewhere.
He couldn't do *everything* better than *all* his employees; trusting them made his time more valuable.

## Framework: The Internal Core Four
You get employees the **same way** you get customers — reframe "let potential *customers* know" to "let potential *employees* know":
| Customers | Employees |
|---|---|
| Warm Outreach | Asking Your Network |
| Cold Outreach | Recruiting |
| Post Content | Posting Job Openings |
| Paid Ads | Promoting Job Postings |
| Customer Referrals | Employee Referrals |
| Affiliates | Associations, Guilds, Listservs |
| Agencies | Staffing Firms |
"When you need new talent, you just advertise to get it. And when you need more, you do more."

## Worked Example: The Hiring Constraint
Cold-outreach sales missed goal two quarters running. The team blamed the method. Tracing it: reps churned every ~4 weeks, hired 1 of 4 candidates, 1 qualified per 10 screens → ~40 interviews per frontline worker. The **constraint was one-on-one screening**. Fix: interview in groups, screen out "crazies," push everyone with work ethic + basic social skills to sales. Within six weeks hiring outpaced churn and **cold-outreach sales doubled**. "The issue wasn't our method, skills, or offer — we just didn't have enough people doing it."

## Key Takeaways
1. Employees do the Core Four for you — trade doing for managing.
2. A business that runs without you is a valuable, sellable asset (you become wealthy from what you *own*).
3. Get employees with the Internal Core Four — same skills as getting customers.
