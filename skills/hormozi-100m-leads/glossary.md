# Glossary — $100M Leads

**A-C-A Framework** — Warm-outreach conversation method: Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask. (Ch 5)

**Advertising** — "The process of making known" — letting strangers know about what you sell. (Ch 1)

**Affiliate** — An independent business that tells its audience to buy your stuff in exchange for money/free stuff; the highest-leverage lead getter. (Ch 15)

**BAMFAM** — "Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting"; never leave a customer in no man's land. (Ch 12)

**Big Fast Value** — In cold outreach, blowing a stranger's mind in under 30 seconds by giving away something they'd actually pay for. (Ch 8)

**Call Out** — Whatever you do to get the audience's attention (the most important part of an ad); verbal (Labels, Yes-Questions, If-Then, Ridiculous Results) and nonverbal (Contrast, Likeness, The Scene). (Ch 9)

**Client Financed Acquisition (CFA)** — Making more than the cost to get and fulfill a customer within the first 30 days, so cash recycles into the next customer; the key to limitless scale. (Ch 10)

**Cocktail Party Effect** — Even amid noise, one thing (your name) catches attention; the basis of callouts. (Ch 9)

**Cold Audience** — Strangers who haven't given permission to contact them (other people's audiences). (Ch 4)

**Constraint** — The step with the biggest drop-off, where the smallest improvement creates the biggest boost. (Ch 11)

**Content Unit** — The smallest material that hooks, retains, and rewards attention (can be a sentence/meme/tweet). (Ch 6)

**Core Four** — The only four ways to advertise: Warm Outreach, Post Content, Cold Outreach, Paid Ads (audience × communication). (Ch 4)

**Core Offer** — Your main product/service sold for money; the direct path to money. (Ch 3)

**Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)** — What it costs to get a customer. (Ch 10)

**Engaged Lead** — A lead who *shows* interest in what you sell; the true output of advertising. (Ch 2)

**Four Lead Getters** — Customers (referrals), Employees, Agencies, Affiliates. (Section IV)

**Give:Ask Ratio** — How often you give value vs. make an offer; over-give to grow ("give until they ask"). (Ch 7)

**Give Until They Ask** — Give so much value (in public) that people ask *you* to buy (in private). (Ch 7)

**Goodwill** — Value minus Price (customer surplus); the source of word of mouth and referrals. (Ch 12)

**Hook, Retain, Reward** — The three components of every content unit. (Ch 6)

**Internal Core Four** — Getting employees the same way you get customers (Asking Your Network, Recruiting, Job Postings, Promoted Postings…). (Ch 13)

**Lead** — A person you can contact. (Ch 2)

**Lead Getter** — Someone who gets *you* leads (a customer, employee, agency, or affiliate); higher leverage than doing it yourself. (Section IV)

**Lead Magnet** — A complete solution to a narrow problem (free/low-cost) that reveals a next problem solved by your core offer. (Ch 3)

**Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)** — All the money a customer ever spends minus all the money it takes to deliver it (Hormozi's preferred metric over "LTV"). (Ch 10)

**Lookalike Audience** — A platform-built audience similar to (and bigger than) a list you upload. (Ch 9)

**More, Better, New** — The three ways to boost any Core Four method. (Ch 11)

**Nine-Word Email** — Dean Jackson's re-engagement message: "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?" (Ch 5)

**Open To Goal** — Committing to the work until you hit a number of *outcomes* (not just actions), no matter what. (Ch 16)

**Penny Gap** — Dan Ariely's finding that 9x more people take "free" than something priced at one cent. (Ch 17)

**Promotion** — A free/discount/premium "wrapper" that makes the Grand Slam Offer more attractive without changing it. (Ch 17)

**Rule of 100** — 100 primary advertising actions per day, for 100 days straight. (Ch 11)

**Size of the Pie Fallacy** — Assuming the tiny slice you advertise to is the whole market. (Ch 11)

**Warm Audience** — People who know you / gave permission to contact them. (Ch 4)

**What-Who-When Framework** — Ad-value framework built on the Value Equation: the 8 What elements (4 value drivers + 4 opposites), Who (status from others' view), When (past/present/future). (Ch 9)

**Whisper-Tease-Shout** — The launch method (Call Out → Value → CTA over time) used to activate affiliates. (Ch 15)
