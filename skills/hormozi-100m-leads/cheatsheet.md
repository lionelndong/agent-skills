# Cheatsheet — $100M Leads

## The one definition that changes everything
**Lead** = a person you can contact. **Engaged lead** = a person who *shows* interest = the true output of advertising. Get more engaged leads.

## The Core Four (the only ways to advertise)
| | One-to-One (private) | One-to-Many (public) |
|---|---|---|
| **Warm** (know you) | Warm Outreach | Post Free Content |
| **Cold** (strangers) | Cold Outreach | Paid Ads |
*Low leads = not enough skill or volume in the Core Four. Pick one, max it; they compound.*

## Decision rules
| When… | Do… | Because… |
|---|---|---|
| Higher-priced offer, leads won't engage | Lead magnet first | Lowers CAC; reveals the next problem your core offer solves |
| More time than money | Warm outreach → post content | Cheapest reach |
| More money than time | Cold outreach / paid ads | Buy reach |
| Doing Core Four, want more | More → Better → New | Volume first; % gains need scale |
| Under $1M/yr profit | "More" first | Not enough volume for % changes to matter |
| Over $1M/yr profit | "Better" first | % gains now cheapest/highest-return |
| Right leads (with money) not buying | Fix sales, not ads | It's a sales problem, not an ad problem |
| CAC < 3x industry avg | Fix the business model (LTGP) | Ads are fine |
| CAC > 3x industry avg | Fix the ads (CAC) | Model is fine |
| Low ticket, cold outreach won't pencil | Recruit affiliates instead | One affiliate = many customers |
| Entering a cold market | Wrap offer free/discounted | Get flow, monetize flow, then add friction |

## Lead magnet — 7 steps
Problem+who → how to solve (Reveal / Sample-Trial / One-Step) → deliver (Software/Info/Service/Physical) → test name → easy to consume → darn good (give secrets, sell implementation) → CTA.

## CTA = what to do + reason now
Reasons: **Scarcity** (real/honest), **Urgency** (time-limited bonuses, rotated), **Fraternity Party Planner** (make up a reason — any reason beats none). One CTA at a time.

## Warm outreach (10 steps, compressed)
List → platform → personalize → **100/day (3x)** → **A-C-A** (Acknowledge/Compliment/Ask) → Value-Equation offer → **first five free** → top platform first → start charging → 9-word email ("Are you still looking to ___?"). Benchmark: ~1 customer / 100 reach outs.

## Content unit = Hook · Retain · Reward
- **Hook**: Topics (Far Past/Recent Past/Present/Trending/Manufactured) · Headlines (Recency, Relevancy, Celebrity, Proximity, Conflict, Unusual, Ongoing — use ≥2) · Format (match platform).
- **Retain**: Lists · Steps · Stories (embed curiosity).
- **Reward**: value per second. "No such thing as too long, only too boring."
- **Give:Ask** → "give until they ask" (give public, ask private). Integrated vs. Intermittent.

## Cold outreach = 3 problems
1) **List** (scrape / buy / elbow grease; interest groups = warmest cold). 2) **Personalize + Big Fast Value** (look like a warm reach-out; 3rd-grade reading = +50%). 3) **Volume** (automate delivery + distribution; follow up more times/ways; restart in 3–6 mo). Count in 100s × 100 days. ≥3x LTGP:CAC.

## Paid ad = Call Out + Value + CTA
- **Call Out** (first 5 sec): verbal (Labels, Yes-Questions, If-Then, Ridiculous Results) + nonverbal (Contrast, Likeness, The Scene). Infinite Ads = 30 callouts × 10 ads.
- **Value = What-Who-When**: What (4 drivers + 4 opposites: Dream/Nightmare, Likelihood/Risk, Speed/Slow, Ease/Effort) · Who (status via others) · When (past/present/future).
- **CTA**: spell it out.

## Paid ad money
**Track → Lose → Print.** Test budget = 2x 30-day cash; 10x down on winners. **LTGP:CAC** is the scoreboard. **Client Financed Acquisition** = recoup get+fulfill cost in 30 days (upsells) → limitless scale.

## More · Better · New
**More** = Rule of 100 (100 actions/day × 100 days). **Better** = test the **constraint** (biggest drop-off), one test/platform/week, log it, front > back. **New** = placements > platforms > Core Four (beware Size of the Pie Fallacy).

## The Four Lead Getters
| Getter | Key move |
|---|---|
| **Customers (Referrals)** | Goodwill (6 give) + ask (7 ways); referrals must beat churn |
| **Employees** | Internal Core Four; trade doing for managing; sellable asset |
| **Agencies** | Hire to *learn*, run your team alongside, then beat them |
| **Affiliates** | 6 steps; offer = "a way to make money"; Whisper-Tease-Shout |

## Referrals — 6 give / 7 ask
Give: Sell Better Customers · Set Better Expectations · Get Better Results · Faster Wins (BAMFAM) · Better Product · Sell Next. Ask: one-sided · two-sided (Dropbox/PayPal) · at purchase ("who," not "if") · negotiation chip · events · ongoing · unlockable bonuses.

## Affiliates — 6 steps
Find ("who's got my leads?") → Offer (way to make money) → Qualify (customer/expert) → Pay (3-tier: 25/50/100% CAC) → Launch (**Whisper-Tease-Shout**) → Integrate (give magnet → promote → sell core).

## Get started
**Open To Goal** (work until the *outcome* is hit). One-page checklist: pick lead type → Rule of 100 / Open To Goal → daily → hire it out → repeat. **Roadmap (7 Levels):** friends know → consistent → employees → referrals (protect product!) → more places/ways → hire killers (experience, not potential) → (billion, TBD).

## Laws & tells
- **Double your leads → double your business.**
- **You either win or you learn** (set aside a % of budget to test).
- **Volume negates luck. Replicate before you iterate.**
- **You get rich from what you make; wealthy from what you own.**
- **The right action in the wrong amount still fails.**
