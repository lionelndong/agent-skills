# Cheatsheet — Fast Cash Playbook

Decision-ready rules from *$100M Playbook: Fast Cash* (Alex Hormozi). Numbers are his.

## The one-line recipe
Sell **unscalable value** to your **warm base** at **10x–50x** price, **cap the spots** (5–10%), promote in **≤7 days** adding **a bonus per message**. Run **every 90 days**.

## Thresholds & defaults
| Lever | Default |
|---|---|
| Price | 10x–50x average cart ("add a zero," optionally ×5) |
| Spots / cap | 5–10% of customers (you must sell out) |
| Offer structure | 1–3 core components + 3 bonuses |
| Promo window | 7 days or less, or until spots run out |
| Cadence | Every 90 days (quarterly); twice a year fallback |
| Gross margin | ≥90% |
| Audience | Current + past customers + engaged leads only |
| 10x The 10% | 10% pay 10x → revenue doubles (minus delivery → bottom line) |

## Decision rules
- **Need cash now or just want profit?** Either → run the play. The cash is best made *before* you need it.
- **How do I sell it?** High price → **1-on-1 consult**. Too many customers / big brand that sells premium without a consult → do what already works.
- **Consult vs. automated checkout?**
  - Limited salespeople (often just you) → **Push to Consult**: buyable day one, but drive *bookings* to spread consults across the window.
  - Big list + working checkout → **Push to Automated Checkout**: build tension, open cart to all at once, first come first serve (stampede).
- **Price reveal?** Weak at sales or hot list → state it ("four-figure investment," "at least $2000," "more than a year of service"). Otherwise hold it for the consult.
- **No card on file?** Have them reply "in," then call to collect the card immediately. Card on file → charge with permission, book, or both.
- **Sold out but someone wants in?** Waitlist them; if a spot opens and they had payment ready, notify + charge.
- **List gone cold (6+ months no contact)?** Exclude from this play; warm them for the next.
- **Cutting "unscalable" parts to save effort?** Don't — at high prices the unscalable parts *justify* the price.
- **Cadence too aggressive?** Weekly fails (fatigue); yearly underperforms. If 12 weeks isn't enough to cool the list, drop to twice a year.

## Bonus-drop order (save the best for last)
| Sequence point | What to add |
|---|---|
| Recap / early | First bonus (e.g. Status — better parking) |
| 2 days out | Second bonus (e.g. Secret — special training) |
| 24h warning (consult) | **Best** bonus — worth **more than the whole price** (e.g. Access — luxury retreat) |
| Cart open, 5 min (checkout) | **Best / most-immediate** bonus (e.g. Experience — your personal cell) |
| 6h post (checkout) | One more unmentioned bonus to close out (e.g. Convenience — extended hours) |

Rule: sell every bonus with **good stuff it gets** + **bad stuff it avoids**.

## Unscalable value menu (pick to build the offer)
Attention / Many-on-1 · Personalization · Convenience · Status · Duration · Speed · Experience (White Glove) · Access · Network · Secrets.

## End-of-play checklist (harvest)
Send sequence → collect cash → deliver → **collect the 4Rs (Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells)** → cooldown the list → set next date → rinse & repeat. (And pay your taxes.)

## Tells & smells
- Only one fixed-price offer → you're capping spend; you're leaving 10x-The-10% money on the table.
- Afraid to price high ("scaredy cat") → worst case you sell few and *still* make money; you're "no worse off."
- Didn't sell out by day 3–4 → the offer's too weak; **add to it** before sending the rest.
- Dismissing it as "not scalable" → it is; the extra cash is exactly what funds scaling.
- Small % of revenue → can still be a **large % of profit** (early on ~15% of revenue ≈ half of profit).

## ROI proof (model)
$25k/mo gym (166 × $150), 25% margin → $75k/yr net. Add quarterly Fast Cash (≥90% margin): Q1 $60k, Q2 $65k, Q3 $48k, Q4 $56k = **$229k extra**. → revenue $300k→$529k (**+76%**), net income $75k→$281k (**~4x**), **+22 customers**.
