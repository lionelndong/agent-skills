## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What a Fast Cash Play is
**"Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality."** A **Fast Cash Play** is a *limited-time offer advertised to your warmest audiences* — current customers, previous customers, and engaged leads (everyone who gave explicit permission to be contacted) — run **~4x/year, on top of** whatever they already pay you. *"A quick nickel beats a slow dime."* It's designed to make the most money in the shortest time from the people most likely to buy. Treat it like **"winning lottery tickets you cash in once a quarter"** — not lead nurture, not value-add content.

### The four levers
A Fast Cash Play is limited in spots (**scarcity**), high touch (**exclusivity**), sold for lots of money (**big ticket**), for a limited time (**urgency**). Profit comes from two places: the leads are already acquired (no added cost to sell) and bigger price tags carry better margins.

### 10x The 10%
**"If you get 10% of your customers to pay 10x the price… you double your revenue."** Since you already spent to acquire them, the extra revenue (minus delivery) **drops straight to the bottom line** — sometimes 2x–4x revenue from one in ten buying the more expensive thing. To make the 10% show up, **limit the number of buyers** so the offer selects for the big-spending group. *"Price like you mean it."*

### What to sell — Unscalable Value
Sell the most valuable things you can do that are **"unscalable"**: **Attention** (1-on-1; or Many-on-1) · **Personalization** · **Convenience** · **Status** · **Duration** · **Speed** · **Experience** (White Glove) · **Access** · **Network** · **Secrets**. Structure: **1–3 core components + 3 bonuses**, layered in one at a time across the sequence to widen the price-to-value gap. At crazy-high prices, *keep* the unscalable parts in — they justify the price. "Any fool can do less and charge less."

### What to charge & how many
**What to charge**: take your average cart/transaction value, **"add a zero"** (optionally **×5**) → **10x to 50x** your current price. The price *selects* for purchasing power. **How many**: cap so you **absolutely sell out** — **5–10% of customers** (100 → 5–10); 1000+ → fulfillment sets the cap. Selling out fast makes the next play sell out faster.

### How to sell & promote (two sequences)
High prices deserve a **1-on-1 consultation**. Promote for **≤7 days, or until spots run out**, adding a new bonus per message (sell each with good stuff it gets + bad stuff it avoids):
- **Push to Consult** — make it buyable day one but drive **bookings** so a small sales team spreads consults across the window. Save the **best bonus (worth > the whole price)** for the 24-hour warning.
- **Push to Automated Checkout** — build launch-style tension (see $100M Leads), **open the cart to everyone at once, first come first serve** (stampede); drop the most-immediate bonus the moment the cart opens.
Cards on file → reply "in," charge with permission. No card → call to collect immediately. Sold out → **waitlist** and charge if a spot opens.

### Cadence — every 90 days (Cash Calendar)
Run **once a quarter** (twice a year as a fallback). Weekly fatigues the audience; yearly underperforms. Five reasons: cleans the pipeline, increases customer value, keeps things fresh, sets a cadence, and drops to the bottom line. Target **≥90% gross margins**. End every play by collecting the **4Rs — Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells** — then cool down and set the next date. This is one play inside the broader **Cash Calendar** (with Reactivation, Referral, and Easy Cash plays). *"Little pay raises you give yourself."*

### The proof
Despite being **~15% of revenue early on, Fast Cash Plays were nearly half his profit.** Model: a $25k/mo gym (25% margin, $75k/yr net) adding quarterly Fast Cash (≥90% margin) → **+$229k cash → revenue +76%, net income ~4x ($75k→$281k), +22 customers** — "by just sending a few emails a quarter." (And: don't forget to pay your taxes.)
