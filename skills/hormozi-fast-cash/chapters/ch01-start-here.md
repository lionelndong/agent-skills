# Chapter 1: Start Here

## Core Idea
**"Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality."** As long as you have cash you're still in business — so you want lots of ways to generate lots of cash, really fast. The Fast Cash Play was born when Gym Launch gyms got hit with surprise tax bills and needed money *by next week* to stay current.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality.** — the governing mantra of the whole playbook.
  - When to use: any time you're tempted to optimize top-line revenue or even profit at the expense of *cash on hand*.
  - How: judge a business by whether it has cash to survive shocks, not by its revenue headline. "You can have the best business in the world, but if you've got no cash, your days are numbered."
- **The high-ticket-from-a-giveaway move** — the original Fast Cash Play: take a promo first conceived as a giveaway and turn it into a high-ticket offer sold to your warm base, fast.
  - When to use: when a business (yours or a client's) needs a lump of cash within days.
  - How: package an offer 5–10 buyers will take at a high price (the gyms targeted $5,000), include sales training for the offer, market it to current + recently-canceled members, start the same day, cash in days.

## Key Concepts
- **Cash Play**: a promotion run to your existing base to rip cash out of a business fast.
- **Unexpected cash drain**: the recurring shock (taxes, broken equipment, an extra pay period) you should always *expect* even though you can't predict its source.
- **"Get out of jail free" card**: what a Fast Cash Play in your back pocket functions as.
- **Warm base**: current customers, past customers, and recently-canceled members — who you market the play to.

## Mental Models
- Think of cash as oxygen: "you can make it through all kinds of mess ups if you've got cash." Profit and revenue don't keep you alive in a crisis; cash does.
- The best time to build a cash-generating play is *before* you need it — "the only thing better than making extra cash when you need it, is making even more when you don't."

## Anti-patterns
- **Spending it all when the money's good**: the gyms "moved up a few brackets," never set cash aside, then couldn't pay the tax man. Making more revenue without holding cash put them at risk.
- **Treating taxes / shocks as "unexpected"**: "since when are taxes unexpected?" Expect the unexpected cash drain.
- **Blowing leftover cash on depreciating junk**: Hormozi steered gyms with leftover cash to prepay the year / reinvest in the business rather than "some stupid exercise machine no one in their gym cares about."

## Worked Example: The Gym Launch Tax-Bill Rescue (April 2019)
Gym Launch cancellations spiked. The cause: gyms had made so much money via Gym Launch they jumped tax brackets and got unexpected tax bills — and had spent the cash. They were happy with the service; they just couldn't pay both Gym Launch and the IRS.

Hormozi's fix: a high-ticket promo (originally a giveaway idea) the gyms could run *immediately*. Looking at their numbers, he expected **5–10 people at $5,000 a pop**. He added specific sales training (most gyms had never sold anything expensive), told them to slot all high-ticket clients on Sundays, wrote the promotional materials himself, scheduled a big launch call for the next day, and told them to invite even recently-canceled members.

Result a few days later: "It's CRUSHING." The **average business collected an extra $30,000 in cash**; many "chickened out" and sold at $3,000, but the few who held at $5,000 "all raked in $50k or more." Every gym renewed and returned to good standing. Leftover cash was redirected to prepaying the rest of the year. The director's takeaway: "should I just save this for when a gym needs cash?" — Hormozi: "When does a business not need cash?" → added to the playbooks.

## Key Takeaways
1. Cash, not revenue or even profit, is what keeps a business alive — build ways to generate it fast.
2. The Fast Cash Play started as an emergency tax-bill rescue and works precisely because it's sold to a warm base at a high price, fast.
3. Expect an unexpected cash drain; keep a few of these "get out of jail free" cards ready before you need them.
4. Run it even when you *don't* need the cash — that's when it's most profitable.
