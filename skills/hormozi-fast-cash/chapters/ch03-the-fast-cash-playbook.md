# Chapter 3: The Fast Cash Playbook

## Core Idea
The full execution: **who** you advertise to, **what** you sell, **how many** to sell, **how** to sell it, **what to charge**, and **how to promote it** (two layouts depending on how you sell). Works for both B2B and B2C. The recipe: sell unscalable value at 10x–50x your normal price, cap the spots, and promote for seven days or less.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Who to advertise to**: existing customers, previous customers, and your engaged-leads list — because (1) they're most likely to buy and (2) they're the lowest cost (often free) to reach. This shortens the "letting them know" and "getting them to buy" steps.
- **What to sell — the Unscalable Value list**: the most valuable things you can do that are *"unscalable."* Hormozi's categories:
  - **Attention** — 1-on-1 from you or senior people; **Many-on-1** (a team supporting one customer — with a top expert, even more valuable than 1-on-1).
  - **Personalization** — customized versions of your standard solutions.
  - **Convenience** — 24/7 support, extended/exclusive hours.
  - **Status** — special parking, perks, badges, public recognition.
  - **Duration** — longer than standard (six months to a year).
  - **Speed** — first in line, priority, fast-response promises (<10 min).
  - **Experience** — "White Glove" service, personal cell access, concierge line, nice dinners/drinks, gifts.
  - **Access** — in-person experiences, retreats, normally-restricted areas, behind the scenes.
  - **Network** — connection with other top customers.
  - **Secrets** — exclusive bonuses, trade secrets, vetted vendors ("secret menu," "black book").
- **Offer structure: 1–3 core components + 3 bonuses.** Layer the bonuses in one at a time to raise buy-likelihood with each addition (new info widens the price-to-value discrepancy and "pushes them over the edge"). All bonuses go to all buyers regardless of when they buy.
- **How many to sell (the cap)**: cap it so you'll *absolutely sell out* — **5–10% of your customers** (100 customers → limit 5–10). With 1000+, fulfillment may constrain you before the 5–10% window — fine. Selling out fast makes the next play more compelling and sell out even faster.
- **What to charge**: *"Take whatever your average cart value — or transaction size is, and add a zero. And if you want to get frisky, multiply it by five."* → **10x to 50x your current price tag.** Go beyond comparison to your other products; the high price *selects* for customers with the purchasing power to buy. The only thing that should shock them as much as the price is the value.
- **How to sell it**: high price points deserve a **one-on-one consultation**. The more personal you make the sale of a personal product, the more bites. If you can't (too many customers, or a big brand that can sell premium without a consult), do what already works.

## Key Concepts
- **Unscalable value**: high-touch things that don't scale — exactly what justifies ultra-premium price tags. "You. Will. Work. More. For. These. Customers."
- **Bonus layering**: dropping a new bonus into each message gives a fresh reason to buy and confirms the choice for those who already bought.
- **Sell-out cap**: deliberately limiting spots to guarantee a fast sellout (and compounding future demand).
- **Cards on file**: if not tech-savvy, just have customers reply "in"; with permission, charge their card / book their appointment; if no card on file, call to collect it immediately after they respond.
- **Cooled-off leads**: neglected leads (no contact for 6+ months) — exclude from this play; start warming them for the next one.

## Mental Models
- **"Any fool can do less and charge less. But we're trying to do more so we can charge more."** Ultra-premium value → ultra-premium price.
- **It's not literally about cell-phone access** — it's about offering crazy value. Balance what *your* customers find crazy-valuable, how many of those things you'll do, and whether they justify a price that "overflows your bank account in a few days."
- **How you sell changes how you promote** — consults get a book-an-appointment flow; automated checkout gets a cart-stampede flow.

## The Two Promotion Sequences
Promote for **seven days or less, or until spots run out.** Add a new bonus with each message; sell each bonus with the good stuff it gets them and the bad stuff it removes.

### PUSH TO CONSULT SEQUENCE
- **7 days out** — text: something big is coming via email tomorrow. That's it.
- **6 days out** — Announcement email (list 1–3 core components; if weak at sales or hot list, give a price range: "four-figure investment," "willing to invest at least $2000," or "costs more than a year of service"). Text to draw attention to it. CTA = schedule a consult.
- **5 days out** — Recap (bullets from yesterday for those who missed it). Add **first bonus** (e.g. Status — better parking).
- **3 days out** — Sales update (how many sold, how many left, first-come-first-serve; restate good stuff it gets / bad stuff it avoids). Text reminder, CTA to reply.
- **2 days out** — "X Spots Left." Add **second bonus** (e.g. Secret — special training); explain benefits. Text reminder.
- **24 hours out (AM)** — Warning. Add your **best bonus** — worth more than the entire price tag — to sell out (e.g. Access — in-person two-day retreat at a luxury location). Text reminder.
- **12 hours out (PM)** — Sold out. Text.
- **(Optional) Back out one spot left** — if someone backs out. Text.

### PUSH TO AUTOMATED CHECKOUT SEQUENCE
Build tension launch-style (see $100M Leads) and cause a stampede by opening the cart to everyone at once, first come first serve.
- **5 days out** — Primer text: something big tomorrow.
- **4 days out** — Announcement email (1–3 core components; include price if weak at sales + hot list). Text to draw attention.
- **2 days out** — Cart opens in 48 hours; first come, first serve. Text.
- **24 hours out** — Add **first bonus** (e.g. Personalization). Tell them to set an alarm — expect it to sell out in minutes. Text.
- **60 min out** — Text: set an alarm, payment method ready.
- **5 min — Cart open** — Add the **best bonus** here, the moment you say cart's open (e.g. Experience — your personal cell); make it the most immediate benefit. Text.
- **60 min post** — Sales update (how many sold, how many left). Text.
- **6 hours post** — "X spots left." Add **one more bonus** not yet mentioned (e.g. Convenience — extended hours) to close it out. Text.
- **12 hours post** — Sold out. Text (shows the promo was justified).
- **Next day** — Back out one spot left, if applicable. Text.
> Note: prefer to sell out and then **waitlist** people who tried to buy; if they were just late with payment ready, get permission to notify them if a spot opens — and charge them if it does.

## Anti-patterns
- **Cutting "unscalable" things from a high-price offer**: at crazy-high prices, *keep* the unscalable solutions in — they're what justify the price.
- **Pricing timidly**: failing to go 10x–50x leaves the big-spending group's money on the table.
- **Including cooled-off leads**: the play works best with warm audiences; neglected lists can make you "seem like a shill" and turn off good customers.
- **Smashing all consults together with too few salespeople**: instead, make it buyable day one but drive people to *book* appointments so you can spread consults across the window.

## Worked Examples: Ultra-Premium Offers
"Think about the craziest thing you can offer. Then, offer it." (Use the Offer Creation Process Part I & II in $100M Offers.)
- **B2C — Fitness, $10,000**: "1 on 1 Year-Long 'Total Transformation' Package" ("Look like a Greek god or goddess by next year"). Core: tailored supplements, meals, workouts, photoshoot. Bonus 1: year-long nutrition plan + intro to a meal-prep company. Bonus 2: 1-on-1 workouts 3x/week. Bonus 3: 24/7 personal cell access.
- **B2B — Marketing Agency, $50,000/yr**: "1 Year Personal Brand Explosion" ("Get Famous" Package). Core: script, film, edit, post all content, 4x/year. Bonus 1: additional social platform. Bonus 2: lead-magnet creation. Bonus 3: organic funnel with VSLs to convert max leads.
- **Recurring Max Cash — Monthly Price × 12**: "1 Year to [ULTIMATE RESULT]." Bonus 1: custom work on their struggle. Bonus 2: thing that makes it faster. Bonus 3: thing that makes achievement "virtually guaranteed (that's not a guarantee)." *Note: does exceptionally well — offer it to the existing base as added value for prepaying the year; typically need little beyond the bonuses to get 10% to say yes. "Highly, highly recommend."*

## Key Takeaways
1. Sell unscalable value (attention, access, speed, status, experience…) as 1–3 core components + 3 layered bonuses.
2. Cap at 5–10% of customers so you sell out fast; price at 10x–50x to select for big spenders.
3. High prices deserve 1-on-1 consults; pick the consult sequence (book appointments) or automated-checkout sequence (cart stampede) and promote in ≤7 days, adding a bonus each message.
4. Exclude cooled-off leads; waitlist rather than oversell.
