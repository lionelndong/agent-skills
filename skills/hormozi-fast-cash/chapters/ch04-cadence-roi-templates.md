# Chapter 4: Cadence, ROI & Templates

## Core Idea
Run Fast Cash Plays **once a quarter (every 90 days)** — the sweet spot. Weekly doesn't work; yearly barely works "if you don't hate money." Quarterly cleans your pipeline, raises customer value, keeps things fresh, gives time to deliver and reset, and drops nearly all the extra warm-lead revenue to the bottom line. The chapter proves it with ROI math and gives a ready-to-use checklist plus a full sample email/text campaign.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **5 Reasons to run every 90 days**:
  1. **Cleans the pipeline** — urgency + scarcity gets qualified-but-on-the-fence leads over the edge → more customers than you'd otherwise get.
  2. **Increases customer value** — people who buy more often keep buying (if delivery doesn't suck).
  3. **Keeps things fresh** — always something new and interesting going on; shakes things up; keeps customers engaged.
  4. **Sets a cadence** — quarterly gives time to deliver and reset before the next play.
  5. **Drops to the bottom line** — if you're break-even or better, additional warm-lead revenue is nearly all profit; small % of revenue can be a large % of profit.
- **The 4Rs** — collect at the end of every play: **Reviews, Results, Referrals, and Resells.**
- **Cash Calendar (Author Note)**: this playbook is one playbook inside his **Cash Calendar** playbook — layering plays (Reactivation, Referral, Easy Cash, Fast Cash) generates referrals, new customers, and retention "all without any added costs… all cash all year round."
  - Other named plays referenced: **Reactivation Plays, Referral Plays, Easy Cash Plays** (Fast Cash is the one that makes the most money fastest).

## Key Concepts
- **90-day cadence / "little pay raises you give yourself"**: quarterly Fast Cash Plays as recurring self-funded raises.
- **ROI Benchmarks**: Fast Cash Plays should run **at least 90% gross margins** ("yours should too").
- **Twice-a-year fallback**: if you need longer than twelve weeks to cool the list between promos, run it twice a year instead — "still free money."
- **The 4Rs**: the post-play harvest (Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells).
- **Cooldown**: after the play, get back to providing value before the next date.

## Mental Models
- **"These are little pay raises you give yourself… every year."** Layer them and you get cash all year round.
- **"Worst case… run it twice a year instead. No big deal — it's still free money."** The downside of trying is near zero.
- Don't dismiss it as not "scalable" — "most people don't because they think stuff like this isn't 'scalable.' But it is. And… all the extra money you just made" is what helps you scale it.

## Anti-patterns
- **Running it weekly**: "it doesn't work" — fatigues the warm audience.
- **Running it only yearly**: barely works "if you don't hate money."
- **Implementing it incorrectly**: you "can seem like a shill, turn off good customers, and make room for better competitors."
- **Forgetting taxes**: "once you make all this extra cash… don't forget to pay your taxes." (The whole origin story was a tax shock.)

## Worked Example: The ROI Benchmark (a $25k/mo gym)
A gym doing **$25,000/mo recurring** (166 members × $150/mo), running 25% margins → **$6,250/mo profit = $75,000/yr.** Add a Fast Cash calendar at **≥90% gross margins**:
- **Q1**: 10 buy at $6,000 = **$60,000** (+3 new customers)
- **Q2**: 13 buy at $5,000 = **$65,000** (+2 new customers)
- **Q3**: 8 buy at $6,000 = **$48,000** (+1 new customer)
- **Q4**: 14 buy at $4,000 = **$56,000** (+2 new customers)
- **Total Extra Cash: $229,000**

Old: $300,000 revenue / $75,000 net income. New: **$529,000 revenue / $281,000 net income.** That's a **76% increase in revenue that yields a quadrupling of net income and 22 new customers** — "by just sending a few emails a quarter."

## The Fast Cash Checklist (tear sheet)
1. Pick and assemble the list (past + present customers).
2. Pick the offer — max out unscalable value; model the examples.
3. Pick a price to match — **10x to 50x** for maximum cash. Be bold.
4. Choose sell method: consults (open cart immediately, book calls + buy before deadline) **or** automated checkout (build hype, open cart, quickly close).
5. Prep emails and texts with CTAs that match the offer: Announcement → Recap → Two days out → 24 hours out → Cart open → Sales update → Two spots left → Sold out → Back out one spot left.
6. Send the sequence. Collect cash.
7. Deliver the stuff.
8. Collect the **4Rs** at the end: Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells.
9. Cooldown your list — get back to providing value.
10. Set your next date. Rinse & repeat.

## Worked Example: The Sample 7-Day Campaign
*"A good example has twice the value of good advice."* A **seven-day campaign** so you meet/close everyone (longer loses urgency; shorter misses sales). Pair texts with emails — texts draw attention, emails give details. Fill the **[BRACKETS]** with your bonuses/features. If the offer's right, you sell out by day three or four; if not, add to the offer before sending the rest.

**Schedule (consult version)**: Sun (7 days out) opener text → Mon (6 days out) email + AM/PM texts → Tue (5 days out) email + meme text → Wed (4 days out) "won't happen again" text → Thu (3 days out) email → Fri (2 days out) "last couple spots" text + email → Sat (last day) AM "12 hours out" email + PM "last shot" email → Sun (only after sellout) "sold out" text.

**The 6 sample emails** model: (1) Mon "We need to talk" — frame the deal as a thank-you that funds an investment benefiting everyone, 20% off for a year + premium speed/ease/access features, limited to 10, book a call OR reply "I'm in. Yes, charge the card on file…"; (2) Tue "best. deal. ever." recap + "about half the spots are already gone"; (3) Thu "over half the spots are gone" social proof; (4) Fri "2 spots left + game changer" reveals a saved bonus; (5) Sat AM "Saving $XXX ends tonight" — the "what if there is no NEXT ONE?" close + adds **Bonus #5**; (6) Sat PM "someone backed out → great for YOU, can I call now?" ## Key Takeaways
1. Run Fast Cash Plays every 90 days (twice a year as a fallback); it's the cadence that compounds.
2. Target ≥90% gross margins; a small share of revenue becomes a large share of profit (the $25k/mo gym 4x'd net income).
3. End every play by collecting the 4Rs (Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells), then cooldown and set the next date.
4. Prep all emails/texts and bonuses in advance; the included tear sheet + 6-email campaign are model-and-fill templates.
