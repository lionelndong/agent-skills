# Chapter 2: Fast Cash Plays — What / How / Why

## Core Idea
**"A quick nickel beats a slow dime when you know how to use it."** Fast Cash Plays are limited-time offers advertised to your warmest audiences, run ~four times a year. They print profit because the leads are already acquired (no extra cost to sell) and bigger price tags carry better margins — and because **10x The 10%** doubles revenue from just one in ten customers.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Fast Cash Play (definition)**: a limited-time offer advertised to your warmest audiences (current customers, previous customers, all engaged leads — everyone who gave explicit permission to be contacted).
  - When to use: once a quarter, *on top of* whatever those customers already pay you, to make the most money in the shortest time from the people most likely to buy.
  - How: make it limited in spots (**scarcity**), high touch (**exclusivity**), sold for lots of money (**big ticket**), for a limited time (**urgency**).
- **10x The 10% rule**: *"If you get 10% of your customers to pay 10x the price… you double your revenue."*
  - When to use: whenever you only have one fixed-price offer and are capping how much a customer can spend.
  - How: introduce an ultra-premium offer so the 10% who *can* spend more actually do. Because you already paid to acquire them, all the extra revenue (minus delivery) "drops straight to the bottom line" — sometimes 2x, 3x, even 4x revenue from one in ten buying the more expensive thing.
- **The four levers of a Fast Cash Play**: **scarcity** (limited spots) · **exclusivity** (high touch) · **big ticket** (sold for lots of money) · **urgency** (limited time). These four properties are what make the play work.

## Key Concepts
- **Warmest audiences**: current customers, previous customers, and engaged leads — most likely to buy and lowest (often zero) cost to advertise to.
- **Two sources of profit**: (1) leads already acquired → no added cost to make the sale; (2) bigger price tags → much better margins.
- **Cap on spend**: a single fixed-price offer caps how much any one customer can give you; more offers let high-intent buyers spend more.
- **"1 out of 9 Americans is a millionaire"**: the proof that buyers *can* afford ultra-premium prices if you back them with value.
- **Unexpected cash drain**: broken equipment, leaky pipes, new tech, cyber attacks, legal fees, or an extra pay period in a 30-day window — the reason to keep these plays handy.

## Mental Models
- Treat Fast Cash Plays like **"winning lottery tickets you cash in once a quarter"** — not a constant promotion and not lead-nurture content.
- **"Price like you mean it."** To get a big-spending offer noticed, attract the big-spending group by *limiting* the number of buyers.
- Worst case is fine: "if one out of ten customers buys something for 10x the price… you double your revenue." If not many buy, you still make money and are "no worse off."

## Anti-patterns
- **Confusing the play with lead nurture**: Fast Cash Plays are *different* from nurturing new leads about your main offer and from long-term value-add content (e.g. content emails) used to keep lists warm.
- **Only having one fixed-price offer**: you cap how much willing buyers can spend — "this only happens if you have stuff for them to buy."
- **Being a "scaredy cat" about premium prices**: owners forget how fast ultra-premium prices "add up to mountains of cash," or are simply afraid to charge them.

## Worked Example: 10x The 10% Math
Hormozi asks, "How do I get these [millionaire] people to give me some of their money?" — which births the 10x The 10% rule. The logic: if **10% of your customers pay 10x the normal price, you double your revenue.** Since you already spent to acquire those customers, nearly all of that extra revenue (minus delivery) drops to the bottom line — so depending on margins you can 2x–4x revenue just by getting one customer in ten onto a more expensive offer. The mechanism that makes the 10% show up: limit the number of buyers so the offer selects for the big-spending group.

## Why It Matters
Despite Cash Plays being only **~15% of his annual revenue in the early days, they were nearly half his profit** — meaning doing them "doubled my take home income… and that was before I got good at 'em." You never know when you'll need extra cash, so these plays are "get out of jail free" cards. And the best version is making the cash *before* you need it.

## Key Takeaways
1. Fast Cash Plays = limited-time, high-ticket offers to your warmest audiences, run ~4x/year, on top of what they already pay.
2. The four levers are scarcity, exclusivity, big ticket, and urgency.
3. 10x The 10%: get 10% of customers to pay 10x the price and you double revenue — and most of it drops to the bottom line.
4. A small share of revenue can be a huge share of profit (~15% revenue → ~half of profit early on).
