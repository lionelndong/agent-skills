# Patterns — Fast Cash Playbook

Concrete, reusable plays and tactics from *$100M Playbook: Fast Cash* by Alex Hormozi. Keep names exact.

## The Fast Cash Play (master pattern)
**When to use**: you need a lump of cash fast, or quarterly to harvest profit from your warm base.
**How**: advertise a limited-time, high-ticket offer to existing customers + past customers + engaged leads. Make it scarce (capped spots), exclusive (high touch), big ticket (10x–50x price), and urgent (≤7 days). Structure as 1–3 core components + 3 bonuses; layer the bonuses in across the sequence. Cap at 5–10% of customers so you sell out.
**Trade-offs**: you'll work much more per customer; done wrong (or to cold lists) you can "seem like a shill" and turn off good customers.

## 10x The 10%
**When to use**: you have only a single fixed-price offer capping customer spend.
**How**: introduce an ultra-premium offer so the ~10% who can spend more do; getting 10% to pay 10x doubles revenue, and most of it (minus delivery) drops to the bottom line.
**Trade-offs**: limiting buyers is required to attract the big-spending group; worst case you "just don't sell that many" and still make money.

## Unscalable Value offer (what to sell)
**When to use**: building the offer itself.
**How**: pick from Attention / Many-on-1 / Personalization / Convenience / Status / Duration / Speed / Experience / Access / Network / Secrets — the high-touch things that don't scale, because those justify the price. At crazy-high prices, *keep* the unscalable elements in rather than cutting them.
**Trade-offs**: maximum personal labor; that labor is what earns the premium.

## "Add a zero (×5)" pricing
**When to use**: setting the Fast Cash price.
**How**: take average cart/transaction value, add a zero, optionally multiply by five → 10x–50x. Go beyond comparison to your other products; the price selects for purchasing power.
**Trade-offs**: fewer buyers by design; the value shown must shock as much as the price.

## The Sell-Out Cap
**When to use**: choosing how many spots.
**How**: cap at a number you'll *absolutely* sell out — ~5–10% of customers (100 → 5–10). With 1000+, let fulfillment set the cap. Selling out fast makes the next play sell out even faster.
**Trade-offs**: caps total cash per play; compounds future demand and credibility.

## Push to Consult Sequence
**When to use**: high price points; you sell via 1-on-1 consults.
**How**: 7→6→5→3→2 days, 24h, 12h cadence. Make it buyable day one but drive bookings so a small sales team (often just you) can spread consults across the window. Add a new bonus each message, saving the best (worth > the whole price) for the 24-hour warning.
**Trade-offs**: bounded by sales-team capacity; booking spreads the load.

## Push to Automated Checkout Sequence
**When to use**: big list and/or a brand that sells premium without a consult.
**How**: build launch-style tension (see $100M Leads), open the cart to everyone at once, first come first serve. Primer (5d) → Announcement (4d) → cart-in-48h (2d) → first bonus + "set an alarm" (24h) → 60-min text → cart open + best/most-immediate bonus (5 min) → updates and bonus-driven closes after.
**Trade-offs**: needs working checkout + tech; creates a stampede that can sell out in minutes.

## Bonus Layering
**When to use**: throughout any Fast Cash sequence.
**How**: make all 3 bonuses available to every buyer, but reveal one at a time; each new bonus gives fresh info that widens the price-to-value gap. Sell each with the good stuff it gets and the bad stuff it removes.
**Trade-offs**: requires bonuses prepped in advance; rewards early buyers by confirming their choice.

## Waitlist on Sellout
**When to use**: you've hit the cap and someone still wants in.
**How**: prefer to sell out, then waitlist those who tried; if they had payment ready and were just late, get permission to notify them if a spot opens — and charge them if it does.
**Trade-offs**: captures overflow demand without breaking the scarcity promise.

## Quarterly Cadence (Cash Calendar)
**When to use**: ongoing, not just emergencies.
**How**: run every 90 days (twice a year as a fallback if the list needs longer to cool). Target ≥90% gross margins. End each play by collecting the 4Rs (Reviews, Results, Referrals, Resells), cool down the list, set the next date, repeat. This play is one inside the broader Cash Calendar (with Reactivation, Referral, Easy Cash plays).
**Trade-offs**: too frequent (weekly) fatigues the audience; too rare (yearly) leaves money on the table.

## Exclude Cooled-Off Leads
**When to use**: assembling the list.
**How**: drop leads neglected for 6+ months; stick to existing and past customers in good standing; start warming the neglected ones for the next play.
**Trade-offs**: smaller send list now; protects deliverability and goodwill.
