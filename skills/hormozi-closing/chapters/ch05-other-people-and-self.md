# Chapter 5: When They Blame Other People & Self

## Core Idea
The last two layers of the onion. **Other People** = "I don't want to buy because someone else prevents me." **Self** = "I avoid buying because I'm scared of making a mistake." For Other People: *"there are no social obligations, only social consequences — they don't need permission, they need support."* For Self: a past failure should never make them miss future success — **the biggest mistake of all is not making a decision** (it guarantees they stay the same).

## Frameworks Introduced

### Other People — Decision Makers (Authority/Permission)
Best prevention: **have decision makers on the call ahead of time** (part of qualification; reschedule if they won't be). If you must close anyway, run this **sequence** ("someone can agree at any point"):
1. **What if they say no?** "What if XXX doesn't give you permission?" If they say "I'd probably do it anyway" → "Awesome, let's just get you started!"
2. **What would they object to?** Get *around* them making the decision — divert to a different obstacle you can overcome (price? program? me?).
3. **If they know you're not happy, why would they stop you?** Argue the partner already approves of similar things, so "this is just like those."
4. **Ask for support, not permission** — "they are their own person and should make the decision for themselves."
- **Role reversal (Nudge):** "If your husband needed this to make *his* dreams happen, would you support him? Then why wouldn't they do the same for you?"
- **"Support not permission" reframe:** "It's your life, not theirs. If you give that power away and two years from now you're [bad thing], who are you going to blame? Them. That's when resentment sets in — and that's not fair to you or them."
- **Pro Tip — Three-Day No-Sweat Guarantee:** "Sign up, I'll delay the payment; if your partner says you should stay poor/fat/stupid/lazy, call me — first I'd like to talk to them, second I'll tear up the contract." Locks the spot, preserves time to talk. (Downsell: card down, delay payment 24–72 hrs.)
- **Humorous extreme** as the last layer: the "Cheetos / sweatpants" hypothetical — get them laughing so they see the silliness of blaming others. *(Nudge: "Sometimes it's better to ask for forgiveness than permission, right?")*

### Other People — Bad Experiences (Trust/Skepticism)
- **"Don't Let It Burn You Twice"** — "It burned you once when you made the poor investment; it'll burn you twice if that bad decision prevents you from making a good one." (The "bad eighth-grade boyfriend → all men are bad" analogy: there are good coaches and bad coaches.)
- **Delivery matters as much as the script** — "unless you've got big rapport and correct tonality you will flop."

### Self — Preferences ("I want your results, but I need to get them *my* way")
What to do: their preferences probably *led* to their current problems, so those preferences get in the way of the result; help them choose which matters more — the result or the preference. **"Isolate And Solve. Always start here":** "What specifically would make it for you vs. not for you?"
- **"We Vote With Our Dollars About The Things That We Care About"** — the Sephora story: a new identity brings new priorities; "draw the line in the sand" between the old you and the you that you want to be. (Has a B2B alternate: "people who want to be rich invest in their skill sets.")
- **"Pain Of Change Is Less Than The Pain Of Staying The Same"** — "We change when the pain of staying the same is higher than the pain of change."
- **"You Gotta Change To Change"** — "What you have been doing has been getting you what you've been getting." ("That breakfast makes you look like you.")
- **"Change The Variables — Change The Outcome"** — "We know these variables, in this order, create this outcome… I wouldn't feel ethical offering [the change] to you."
- **"The Mechanic Close" / "Surgeon Secretary Close"** — for hyper-specific questions you can't answer on the spot: answering would be like a mechanic diagnosing your car over the phone before it's in the shop, or "a secretary at a heart surgeon's telling you what the doctor's gonna do." Run the other way from anyone who *does* answer; "the only way we'd be able to answer it is after we get to know [you/your body/your business] better."

### Self — Rushed Decisions / Avoidance ("Let me think about it")
- **"What Would Make It A No?"** — get the indecisive to decide what makes it a *no*; weak reasons → "you're simply unsure… just like every other great customer."
- **"Don't Repeat The Same Mistake That Got You Here"** — "maybe you're in this situation because you've delayed making the decision?"
- **"How Much Has Not Making This Decision Cost You?"** — the cost of *inaction*; "we never regret the things we did; we regret the things we didn't do."
- **"Another Year of Almost"** — "another year of *almost* hitting your goals, *almost* retiring your spouse… what about a *'this is my year'* kinda year for a change?"
- **"It Doesn't Take Time To Make Decisions, It Takes Information"** — walk the decision variables together now; "I'm the only source of information you got, so let's talk."
- **The "Gameplan" Close** — three questions they'll all answer yes to: (1) Will this get you closer to your goals? (2) Do you have access to the money to start? *(if no → who has access? resourcefulness > resources)* (3) Can we help you do this better than on your own?
- **"This Isn't A Fast Decision"** — "you kinda made this decision years ago… You saw an ad. Clicked. Opted in. Showed up. You're six inches from gold — you don't wanna stop digging now."

### Guarantee Closes (only work if you offer a guarantee)
- **"Both Options Are Risk Free"** — "you can't make an informed decision until you've actually tried it… you can try it risk free… If after 30 days I didn't fulfill my promise, you let me know, I'll give you the money back."
- **"Decadere"** — *decide* comes from Latin *de-cadere*, "to kill off / cut off." "Doing this is a decision. Not doing this is a decision. So which future are you cutting off?"
- **"Closer Or Further"** — "If we keep making decisions that get us closer, we'll get there eventually… We don't need to be snipers; we need to be directionally right."

## Key Concepts
- **No social obligations, only social consequences** — the philosophical core of the Other People overcome.
- **Support, not permission** — the goal of every decision-maker close.
- **Isolate And Solve** — always the first move on a Preferences/Fit objection.
- **Cost of inaction** — the central lever for rushed-decision/avoidance.

## Mental Models
- **Burned-twice framing** — a past bad investment only truly costs you if it stops a future good one.
- **You gotta change to change** — keeping the same inputs guarantees the same outputs; the prospect must trade either the result or the preference.
- **Six inches from gold** — the prospect has *already* invested (ad → click → opt-in → call); quitting now wastes all of it.

## Worked Example: The Decision-Maker sequence end-to-end
Prospect: "I have to talk to my spouse."
Rep: "What if they don't give you permission — what if your spouse says no?"
Prospect: "Then I wouldn't do it."
Rep: "What part specifically do you think they wouldn't approve of? The price? The program? Or me? (ha)"
Prospect: "I just don't make decisions without my spouse."
Rep: "Does your spouse approve of your *current* struggle? If not, why would they be against you fixing something they already don't approve of? *(Role reversal nudge)* If your spouse needed this to make their dreams happen, would you support them? … I think you should ask for **support, not permission**. It's your life — if you hand that power over and two years from now things are worse, who do you blame? Them. That's not fair to either of you."
*(Still no?)* "Tell you what — sign up, I'll delay the payment; you lock in your spot but still get time to talk it over. **Three-day no-sweat.** Fair enough?"

## Anti-patterns
- **Forcing a genuinely household-altering purchase** — "if the purchase materially affects their household… you'll need partner buy-in; there's no point in forcing it."
- **Answering a hyper-specific question you can't truly answer yet** — use the Mechanic/Surgeon Secretary close; don't bluff.
- **Treating "let me think about it" as a real reason** — convert it to "what would make it a no?"

## Key Takeaways
1. Other People: they need **support, not permission** — "no social obligations, only social consequences."
2. Have decision makers on the call up front; if not, run the 4-step sequence + three-day no-sweat.
3. Bad experience → **"Don't let it burn you twice."**
4. Self/Preferences → **Isolate And Solve**, then "you gotta change to change."
5. Rushed decisions → the **cost of inaction**; "it doesn't take time to make decisions, it takes information."
6. Guarantee closes (Decadere, Closer Or Further) make *not* deciding the only real risk.
