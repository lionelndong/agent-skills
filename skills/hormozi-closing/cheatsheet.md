# Cheatsheet — $100M Playbook: Closing

## The one definition
**Closing starts *after* you ask for the sale. Its goal isn't the sale — it's giving the prospect the power to decide.** The money is in the **maybes**.

## Advertising → three buckets
| Bucket | Aka | Job |
|---|---|---|
| **YES** | Laydown | Don't mess it up |
| **NO** | Unqualified (bad fit) | Let them go (it's an *advertising* problem) |
| **MAYBE** | On the fence | **This is why we train** — close them |

## Route the objection (the Onion of Blame)
"Anything that's not yes" → which layer?
- **Circumstances** ("out of my control") → **Time · Money · Fit**
- **Other People** → **Authority/Permission · Trust/Skepticism**
- **Self** → **Preferences · Fear/Avoidance (rushed)**
*Expect objection swaps — that means you're closing correctly. Peel one layer at a time.*

## The universal wrapper (every close)
**Repeat back → acknowledge (never disagree) → reframe.** Assume "totally understand" before every response. Get permission before getting real: *"Can I be a coach instead of a friend for a second?"*

## All-Purpose (80/20) closes — use on ANY non-yes
| Close | The line |
|---|---|
| **What's Your Main Concern** | "What's your main concern? What are you afraid of happening?" |
| **Reason** | "The reason you're telling yourself not to do this is the reason you should." |
| **Hypothetical** | "If this were perfect, would you do it? Then what's the difference?" |
| **Zoom Out** | "You want X. We sell X. Is overanalyzing the reason you haven't made it happen?" |
| **1 to 10** | "<10? What would it take to get you to a 10?" |
| **Best Case Worst Case** | Every option is risk-free except walking out the door |
| **Card Not On Me** | Banking app → account number; rep Googles routing number |

## Money (4 flavors → move)
| Flavor | Sounds like | Master move |
|---|---|---|
| **Not enough value** | "It's too expensive" | **"Price too high" = "value too low"** — shift to value |
| **Can't afford it** (rare) | "I can't afford it" | **Resourcefulness not resources** |
| **Others do it for less** | "Guy down the street is cheaper" | "If we were the same price, which would you pick? Why?" |
| **I'd buy it for less** (haggle) | "Can you do it for less?" | **"We could do it for more (ha)"** |
*Value stack: It's Good That It's A Lot · Good Things Aren't Cheap · Would You Believe Me If It Were Less · You Pay/Spend The Money Either Way · Some Now Or More Later.*

## Time → priorities
"Too busy / I'll start when I have time" → **Better To Start When You're Busy · You're Gonna Get Busy Again · It's Not About Timing It's About Priorities · Smartphone Close · When/Then.** Core: *the best time to start is now, busy.*

## Other People → "support, not permission"
*No social obligations, only social consequences.* Best fix: **decision makers on the call up front** (reschedule if not).
1. What if they say no? → ("I'd do it anyway") → close.
2. What would they object to? (price/program/me) → off-ramp it.
3. Why would they stop you if they know you're unhappy?
4. **Ask for support, not permission.** + role reversal + **Three-Day No-Sweat** (delay payment 24–72 hrs).
Burned before → **"Don't let it burn you twice."**

## Self → result vs. preference / cost of inaction
- **Preferences** ("not for me"): **Isolate And Solve** first ("what specifically would make it for you vs. not?") → We Vote With Our Dollars · Pain Of Change · **You Gotta Change To Change** · Change The Variables.
- **Unanswerable specific Q**: **Mechanic / Surgeon Secretary Close** (don't bluff; defer).
- **Rushed/avoidant** ("let me think about it"): What Would Make It A No · **How Much Has Not Deciding Cost You** · Another Year Of Almost · It Doesn't Take Time, It Takes Information · Gameplan Close.
- **Guarantee closes**: Both Options Are Risk Free · **Decadere** (decide = cut off) · Closer Or Further.

## STAR — qualify before you *sell*
**S**ituation (problem) · **T**iming (time to solve) · **A**uthority (to decide) · **R**esources (money). No STAR = advertising problem, not a close.

## Thresholds & defaults
- **Two or three closes per obstacle** — get good, don't become an encyclopedia.
- **Stack closes**: if one doesn't land, hit it from another angle (a second often works better).
- **Three-day no-sweat / delay payment 24–72 hrs** as the decision-maker downsell.
- **Take ACH** so a missing wallet never kills a sale.

## Tells & smells
- "It's expensive" / price shock is often an **observation, not an objection** — "I agree," let them process. *Unless they say there's a problem, assume they're thinking out loud.*
- A prospect **swapping objections** = progress, not rejection.
- **Weak reasons for a "no"** = they're simply unsure (great-customer behavior).
- "If you feel like you're always in a battle, look at what happens *before* the call and at your **scripting before the ask**."

## Iron rules (don't break)
- **Never change price on the spot** to win a sale.
- **Once they say yes — shut up** (the rule of law).
- **Only ask for the sale when you think you've got 'em**; else keep probing.
- **Selling before the ask; closing after.** Diffuse problems *before* asking for money.
- **Expect and plan for no** — it's expected, not failure.
- **Kind, not nice**; the person who cares most about the prospect wins the sale.
