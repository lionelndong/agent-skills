# Glossary — $100M Playbook: Closing

**ACH** — Automated clearing house (bank) payments; Hormozi urges taking them: lower costs, fewer expirations/chargebacks, and you can sign a client even without their wallet. (Ch 3)

**All-Purpose Closes** — The 80/20 closes (the 20% that work 80% of the time); usable on "anything that's not yes" to force a decision or surface a real obstacle. (Ch 3)

**Avoidance treadmill** — The waffling state of an unclosed maybe; leaving a prospect on it "lets them down." (Ch 1)

**Best Case Worst Case Close** — Frame the options as all risk-free except walking out the door (the only guaranteed loss). (Ch 3)

**Blame** — The power-stealing thing a prospect points at to avoid a decision; sorts into three sources. Also called an obstacle/objection. (Ch 2)

**Both Options Are Risk Free Close** — Guarantee close: "you can't make an informed decision until you've tried it… and you can try it risk free." (Ch 5)

**Card Not On Me Close** — Handle "I forgot my card": pull up the banking app for the account number; rep Googles the routing number. (Ch 3)

**Cheap Comparison Close** — "If we were the same price, which would you pick? Why?" — let them list your advantages. (Ch 4)

**Champions close** — Winners score the points that matter (Federer won 54% of points but 80% of matches). (Ch 1)

**Circumstances** — The External blame layer: "stuff out of my control." Splits into Time, Money, Fit. (Ch 2, Ch 4)

**Closer Or Further Close** — Guarantee close: "We don't need to be snipers; we need to be directionally right." (Ch 5)

**Closing** — The most important part of selling; starts *after* you ask the prospect to buy. Its goal is to give the prospect power to decide. (Ch 1)

**Cost of inaction** — "How much has it cost you to *not* decide up to this point?" — the lever for rushed/avoidant decisions. (Ch 5)

**Decadere** — Latin *de-cadere* ("to kill off / cut off"), root of *decide*; "which future are you cutting off?" (Ch 5)

**Decision avoidance** — "Maybe yes / maybe no" = neither; the state closing exists to break. (Ch 1)

**Decision Makers** — The Authority/Permission obstacle; best handled by having them on the call up front. (Ch 5)

**Don't Let It Burn You Twice** — Bad-experience close: one bad investment only truly costs you if it stops a good one. (Ch 5)

**Fit** — The Circumstances sub-blame about product details ("not right for me"); overlaps with Self/Preferences. (Ch 2, Ch 5)

**Future Favor Close** — "Do future you a favor and have her look back on these next 10 years really proud." (Ch 4)

**Gameplan Close** — Three yes-questions: closer to goals? access to money? can we help better than on your own? (Ch 5)

**Good Fast Cheap** — Comparison close: you can't have all three "done right." (Ch 4)

**Good Things Aren't Cheap — Cheap Things Aren't Good** — Value close: "How many free PDFs have you downloaded and made no more money?" (Ch 4)

**Guarantee Closes** — Closes that only work if you offer a guarantee, because not buying carries the greater risk. (Ch 5)

**Hypothetical Close** — "If this were perfect, would you do it? Then what's the difference between perfect and what we've got?" (Ch 3)

**Isolate And Solve** — First move on a Preferences/Fit objection: "What specifically would make it for you vs. not for you?" (Ch 5)

**It's Good That It's A Lot** — Value close: a big investment makes them take it seriously. (Ch 4)

**It's Not, Not The Money** — "If this were free, would you do it?" → strips every non-price objection so you isolate the real one. (Ch 4)

**Kind, not nice** — "Kind people want to genuinely help… sometimes that means calling them on their BS in a tactful way." (Ch 2)

**Laydown** — A YES; advertising already closed them ("you just have to show up to accept payment"). (Ch 1)

**Mechanic Close** — For unanswerable specific questions: like a mechanic diagnosing your car over the phone before it's in the shop. (Ch 5)

**Money (blame)** — A Circumstances sub-blame in four flavors: not enough value, can't afford it, others do it for less, I'd buy it for less. (Ch 4)

**Nudge** — A one-liner (not a full overcome) used when someone is right on the edge to tip the deal over. (Ch 2)

**Obstacle / Objection** — The familiar names for the "power-stealing stuff people blame." (Ch 2)

**Onion of Blame** — The model that every objection sorts into three layers: Circumstances, Other People, Self. (Ch 2)

**Other People** — The blame layer where someone else (or distrust of you) prevents the purchase; Authority/Permission + Trust/Skepticism. (Ch 2, Ch 5)

**Pain of change** — "We change when the pain of staying the same is higher than the pain of change." (Ch 5)

**Power** — "The ability to direct or influence people"; the missing ingredient in most buying decisions. Closing gives the prospect power to decide. (Ch 1)

**Price too high = value too low** — The master money reframe; immediately translate one into the other. (Ch 4)

**Reason Close** — "The reason you are telling yourself not to do this is the reason you should do it." (Ch 3)

**Resourcefulness Not Resources** — "Can't afford it" close: a self-made millionaire's only edge when they had nothing was how resourceful they were (Phil Knight/Nike). (Ch 4)

**Role reversal** — Decision-maker nudge: "If your spouse needed this for their dreams, would you support them?" (Ch 5)

**STAR** — Qualification: **S**ituation (problem), **T**iming (time to solve), **A**uthority (to decide), **R**esources (money). Unqualified prospects are an advertising problem, not a sales problem. (Ch 4)

**Stacking closes** — Hitting the same objection from another angle/analogy if the first doesn't land. (Ch 2)

**Support not permission** — The goal of every Other People close; "no social obligations, only social consequences." (Ch 5)

**Surgeon Secretary Close** — Variant of the Mechanic Close: "like a secretary at a heart surgeon's telling you what the doctor's gonna do." (Ch 5)

**Three Buckets** — Advertising splits an audience into YESes, NOs, and MAYBEs. (Ch 1)

**Three-Day No-Sweat Guarantee** — "Sign up, I'll delay the payment; if your partner objects, call me — I'll talk to them, then tear up the contract." (Ch 5)

**Transference of belief over a bridge of trust** — Hormozi's definition of selling. (Ch 2)

**Unqualified** — A NO; a genuine bad fit ("people in Ohio who opt in to a gym offer in Florida"). (Ch 1)

**We Could Do It For More Close** — Haggling close: "Uhh… we could do it for more (ha)." (Ch 4)

**We Vote With Our Dollars** — Preferences close (the Sephora story): a new identity brings new priorities. (Ch 5)

**What Would Make It A No?** — Rushed-decision close: get the indecisive to decide what makes it a *no*. (Ch 5)

**What's Your Main Concern Close** — "What's your main concern? What are you afraid of happening?" — Hormozi's most-used phrase. (Ch 3)

**When/Then Close** — Time close: "I'll do it when I have time" flips the order, like "When I get healthy, then I'll go to the doctor." (Ch 4)

**You Gotta Change To Change** — Preferences close: "What you have been doing has been getting you what you've been getting." (Ch 5)

**You Pay The Price Either Way** — Value close: pay with years of your life or a payment today; "the currency we value least." (Ch 4)

**Yellows are golds / be a trashman** — Practice closing on unqualified leads to get more reps. (Ch 2)

**Zoom Out Close** — "You want [X]. We sell [X]. Is overanalyzing the details the reason you haven't made it happen yet?" (Ch 3)

**1 to 10 Close** — "Where do you see yourself?" → if <10, "What would it take to get you to a 10?" (Ch 3)
