## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### What closing is
**Closing — the most important part of selling — starts *after* you've asked the prospect to buy.** "If you don't close, you don't make money." The goal of closing **isn't to get the prospect to buy**; it's to give the prospect **enough power to decide** one way or the other. Close properly and you win either way: best case they buy; worst case they don't, and you dodge an unqualified purchase. The only failure is leaving a maybe on the **avoidance treadmill**.

### Advertising splits your audience into three buckets
- **YESes (Laydowns)** — advertising already closed them; don't mess it up.
- **NOs (Unqualified)** — a genuine bad fit (an *advertising* problem).
- **MAYBEs (On the fence)** — value it but have reservations; **most** interested people. **"This is why we train." The money is in the maybes.**

### Power — the missing ingredient
People on the fence "have too little power to influence their own decision," so they waffle ("maybe yes / maybe no" = neither). **Closing gives people the power to decide.** Giving decision-making power = (1) understand what they want, (2) **put what they want next to the options they have to get there**, (3) make sure they reach a genuine decision. *Ethical assumption: "the difference between help and manipulation is intention."*

### Blame — what robs people of their power (the Onion of Blame)
When people avoid a decision, they **blame** something that *looks* reasonable. Every objection sorts into **three sources** (peel one layer at a time; **expect objection swaps** — that means you're closing correctly):
1. **Circumstances (External)** — "stuff out of my control." → **Time · Money · Fit**
2. **Other People** — someone else / distrust of you prevents it. → **Authority/Permission · Trust/Skepticism**
3. **Self** — "I'm scared of making a mistake." → **Preferences · Fear/Avoidance (rushed)**

### The universal wrapper (before every close)
**Repeat back what they said → acknowledge or agree, but NEVER disagree → reframe.** Assume "totally understand / that's a great point" before every response. **Get permission before getting real**: *"Can I be a coach instead of a friend for a second?"* You sit on the **same side of the table** — "you can never convince someone; you guide them to convince themselves."

### All-Purpose (80/20) Closes — use on anything that's not yes
- **What's Your Main Concern** — "What's your main concern? What are you afraid of happening?" (the workhorse).
- **Reason** — "The reason you're telling yourself not to do this is the reason you should."
- **Hypothetical** — "If this were perfect, would you do it? Then what's the difference?"
- **Zoom Out** · **1 to 10** ("What would it take to get you to a 10?") · **Best Case Worst Case** (everything's risk-free except walking out the door) · **Card Not On Me**.

### Closes by blame source
- **Time** → it's *priorities*, not timing; **the best time to start is now, busy** (Better To Start When You're Busy, You're Gonna Get Busy Again, Smartphone Close, When/Then).
- **Money** (four flavors): not enough value · can't afford it · others do it for less · I'd buy it for less. **Master move: "price too high" = "value too low."** Can't afford → **Resourcefulness Not Resources**. Haggling → **"We could do it for more (ha)."**
- **Other People** → they need **support, not permission** ("no social obligations, only social consequences"). Put decision makers on the call up front; else run the 4-step sequence + **Three-Day No-Sweat**. Burned before → **"Don't Let It Burn You Twice."**
- **Self** → Preferences: **Isolate And Solve** → "You Gotta Change To Change." Rushed/avoidant: the **cost of inaction** (How Much Has Not Deciding Cost You, Another Year Of Almost, "It doesn't take time to make decisions, it takes information"). **Guarantee closes**: Both Options Are Risk Free, **Decadere** (decide = "cut off"), Closer Or Further.

### STAR — qualify before you *sell*
**S**ituation (problem) · **T**iming (time to solve) · **A**uthority (to decide) · **R**esources (money). No STAR = an advertising problem, not a close to attempt. *(Yet "yellows are golds" — practice closing on unqualified leads to get reps; volume negates luck.)*

### Selling is a transference of belief over a bridge of trust
You only build trust if you genuinely want to help. **Belief and trust are a continuum, not binaries** — not *whether* they believe you, but *how much*. **Be kind, not nice.** The person who cares most about the prospect wins the sale. **Once they say yes — shut up.** **Never change your price on the spot.**
